Braindance Catalog

Braindances produced, commissioned, or sold by Motoko throughout the story. Each entry is a full-immersion experience — the viewer lives someone else's memories frame by frame.

BD-001  // Ch.40
Kamikaze Raid
[RELEASED]
One of the original batch recorded after Motoko's neural BD implant went live. The operation itself: sniper overwatch during a Maelstrom assault on a Tyger Claw gun shop — Motoko on a rooftop picking targets through her scope while Kamikaze hit from the rear. Four kills, every one a precision shot. Judy Alvarez described the senses file as shockingly good — Motoko's heartbeat barely rises while climbing a building and lining up shots, her combat calm translating directly through the recording in a way that made Judy's stomach flip. Ten copies pressed and distributed through Nox on the street. The first proof that Edgerunner BDs could be something different from snuff films.

BD-002  // Ch.47
Raffen Camp
[RELEASED]
A solo silent raid on a Wraith-affiliated Raffen camp in the Badlands. Motoko bets Jun radio control forever that she can ghost the whole camp without a sound, then does exactly that — nine kills by blade in the dark. Rita at Lizzie's watched it before the drop-off and pulled Motoko aside afterwards, genuinely unsettled by how combat-wired she came across. The BD scared Rita more than the Kamikaze one had.

BD-003  // Ch.57
Unnamed (Mr. Studd Recon)
[PERSONAL ONLY]
A one-minute personal edit — not for commercial release. Ichi walked into a Kabuki ripper's clinic asking about penile enhancement (a 'Mr. Studd' implant) while Motoko posed as his pimp and recorded the layout and exit points for a planned raid. Judy cut the edit at Motoko's request. Shown to Malcolm at Lizzie's Bar (he cackled immediately). Ichi demanded a copy and was refused. Primary function: tactical documentation of the Kabuki ripper reconnaissance. Secondary function: the best leverage Motoko has ever held over Ichi.

BD-004  // Ch.60
Scav Den
[RELEASED]
The Kabuki ripper den clearance — the same clinic BD-003 scouted. Motoko rolled out the back of Ichi's moving truck and entered from the rear. Her Seacho deck tore through their two-decade-old security net in seconds; a Ping tagged all ten enemies through the walls. She cleared nine scavs by knife — some asleep, some watching TV, one rounded a corner and caught a thrown blade in the chest. Not one of them knew she was in the building. Ripper Charles made his plea as she walked up from the basement covered in blood. She told him she'd read his emails. Three shots from her Lexington. Handed off to Judy for editing and released through the Mox distribution network. By this point the BD operation has found its rhythm: record, edit at Lizzie's, press at Nox's, distribute through the street network Hiromi is quietly building.

BD-005  // Ch.86
Unnamed
[RELEASED]
A 3 AM operation against the GEIshhhhA yacht at Gold Beach Marina, owned by independent BD dealer Bartolomeo Mordellini — a repository for scav-sourced chrome. Motoko leaps from an office walkway, choke-holds the marina security guard unconscious, plants a virus in the security computer, and cuts the yacht loose. The moment the alarms fire she mass-deploys Reboot Optics across the entire camera network — every guard, worker, and guest on the marina goes blind simultaneously. Their vision doesn't go dark: it resolves to a single image, a blue Laughing Man symbol. Her calling card, used for the first time. Zero kills. The yacht sails south.

BD-006  // Ch.93
The Only Thing They Fear Is You
[RELEASED]
The Megabuilding H11 scav den run — eighteen Scavs, seven minutes, set to 'The Only Thing They Fear Is You.' Motoko deploys a looping Weapon Glitch that locks every gun on the network, then moves room to room with the Carnage used as both weapon and bludgeon, a looted Cut-o-Matic chainsword mid-fight, and a Sandy-equipped Scav taken down the instant his Sandevistan dropped — Burya into the mouth, 'head separated in two.' Edited with the Maelstrom encounter at the end cut out before delivery to Judy. The only BD in the catalog with a confirmed kill count of eighteen.

BD-007  // Ch.115
Inner Universe
[RELEASED]
The outlier. No kills, no scavs — a fully live parkour run from a penthouse rooftop through Night City: lantern wires, bridgeways, through a stranger's apartment window mid-stride, across the Jig-Jig Street rooftops, landing at the Sakura Market ramen stand. Set to 'Inner Universe' from the Ghost in the Shell soundtrack. Judy couldn't finish editing it due to her heights phobia — Nox cleared it instead and called it another preem experience. Both of them ended up craving Sakura Market ramen afterward. David Martinez was spotted selling copies outside Arasaka Academy.

BD-008  // Ch.157
Section 9 Scav Raid
[RELEASED]
The XBD operation — Section 9's raid on the North Arroyo scav den used as an XBD harvest site, clearing the killing rooms floor by floor. A full crew op, the first BD to show Section 9 working as a unit rather than Motoko running solo. The Maelstrom encounter mid-raid was edited out of the release cut. Hiromi walked off with the raw footage for a separate project. Rebecca watched the finished BD and glimpsed herself as a motion blur in one shot; she was impressed by the professional-grade editing.

BD-009  // Ch.180
Red Dirt Gig
[RELEASED]
The recording of Motoko's first live performance, at the Red Dirt Bar in Arroyo — the location of Samurai's original first gig — arranged by Hiromi as a birthday surprise. Five songs with full instrumentation: Smells Like Teen Spirit, Rise, This Fffire, The Pretender, Let You Down. Section 9 watched from the floor. Judy asked Motoko to sign the shard afterward and kept a personal copy herself. Rebecca braindanced it at Lizzie's and awarded Motoko the nickname 'Strings.' The only BD in the catalog with no kills and no weapons drawn.

BD-010  // Ch.222
Don't Steal My Car
[RELEASED]
Motoko's Quadra Super 640 is stolen outside the Dogtown stadium via car cracker. She tracks it to a massive Scav den — fifty-plus Scavengers occupying an unfinished skyscraper in a Dogtown construction site. What follows is a multi-floor solo clearance: Onryo ghost daemon spreading panic through the Scav network, a hacked biomon flipping the Scav heavy Dmitri into a full cyberpsycho episode that turns him on his own squad, and Motoko sweeping the upper floors room by room with Sandy and blades. The Scav boss Daniil Antonov dies cornered on a drawbridge, crying with a broken hand. The BD recorder ran the entire time. Delivered to Judy, who noted that multiple witnesses saw pieces of the event, making full anonymisation impossible. David Martinez received a copy from Hiromi, dove it, and was so blown away he immediately found Motoko and asked to join Section 9.

BD-011  // Ch.239
Unnamed
[RELEASED]
Recorded at a spontaneous rooftop fight club after a frustrating MSM studio session. Motoko paid entry and challenged the reigning champion — 'American Devil,' a 6th Street boxer with armored skull and chrome fists — after he threw beer in her face. She won by wall-running up a poolside divider and aerial-punching him unconscious. When the gang drew weapons, she deployed Mass Weapon Glitch and worked through five of them: double-donkey-kick off the roof, knife to the jugular, thrown knife to the chest, one shot with a confiscated pistol, and the gang leader surfed off the building edge with her feet planted on his chest. Judy cleaned the XBD of identifying markers before distribution. Judy's POV watching it: the fight was joy, not survival. The BD ends with the leader crying as he realizes they are both falling.

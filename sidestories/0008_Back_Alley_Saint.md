Back Alley Saint​

 "Thanks again, doc. You have no idea how much this means to me," young Hispanic man sporting a brand new cyber arm on his right said as he tested it out. "I thought after that accident I'd never be able to get back to work. You sure I don't own you more?"

 "Don't worry about it, Jaime," Viktor waved him off as he handed over a bag of pills. "The arm's used but in good condition so there shouldn't be any problems, checked it over myself. Take it easy for the next few days and take the medication till you are used to it. Call me up anytime if any issue comes up and I will see what I can do to fix it. For now though, go enjoy your new arm."

 "Seriously, Doc, I don't know what to say," Jaime looked honestly close to tears as he rubbed his eyes with his new hand.

 "Well if you do want to make it up to me... mind if I take a pic of ya?" Vik asked, earning a curious look from his patient. "That cyberarm was practically given to me by a very generous young woman who wanted me to help as many people as I possibly can, so I like to take pictures of those who I have thanks to her to show her the difference she is helping to make. No pressure of course."

 "Naw naw, that's easy, doc," Jaime was quick to agree. "And tell whoever who did this she's a lifesaver."

 "Heh, don't worry I plan to," Vik said with a smile as he took a quick pic of Jaime sporting a peace sign before shooing him out of his clinic.

 "Another happy customer?" Misty said with a smile as she stood in the doorway to Vik's clinic.

 "Heh, practically a donation with how little I'm charging... but considering I got the chrome for basically free I can afford to do it," Vik said as he rolled over to his terminal to check his messages and other appointments, as well as catalogue how much chrome he had left from Motoko's charity... still a decent amount despite how many near free installations he had done for awhile.

 While it was horrifying to think where this chrome came from, now at least it was going to a good cause instead of a Scav's pocket.

 "I'll say, mister back alley saint," Misty chuckled. "Been seeing more than a few kids on the streets with brand new arms, legs, and other life-changing chrome their families couldn't afford before. You're changing lives Vik, really earning your good karma."

 "I'm just doing what someone else asked me to do, that's all," Vik waved off the compliment. "Gotta message her soon, let her know her order for decent optics coming in soon so she can chip it in. Hope girl hasn't gotten herself killed assaulting more Scav dens since. That would be an honest shame," Vik said with a morose grunt. "Something this city is good at giving."

 "I'm sure she is fine and still doing crazy stuff," Misty assured him.

 "Oh yeah, your cards tell you that?"

 "Naw, every time I try to read that girl it keeps changing, as if the universe itself doesn't know quite what to do with her and decided to let her pave her own path," Misty shrugged. "Her aura is so... out there. Feels old despite her being so young, and like it doesn't belong here but still connected. I can honestly say I've never felt anything like it."

 "Spooky," Vik chuckled. "But if she has your endorsement I'm sure she's still raising hell then. Can't have her dying on my till I pay her back after all."

 "Seems more likely Motoko is the kinda girl that will keep adding to your debt before you can truly pay it off," Misty pointed out. "And you're too honorable not to try."

 "Heh, comes with getting old, don't like being in debt with you younger generations," Vik said as he looked over the dozens of pictures of his success cases using the chrome that crazy little solo had given him.

 Legs and arms replaced, hearts and lungs restored, nerve issues cured, all that and more. All sporting smiling faces and sometimes crying tears of joy alongside friends, family, and loved ones. Dozens of people given a new leash on life thanks to a girl way too generous and too little sense working in Night City of all places.

 He could only hope that the city didn't grind her down too hard and maybe she'll be one of the lucky ones in this town...

 And just maybe he can help make that a reality. He owed her after all.

 And Viktor Vektor always paid back his debts

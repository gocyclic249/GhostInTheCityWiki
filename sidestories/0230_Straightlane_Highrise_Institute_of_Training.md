 Hiromi is manager now. She has employees and can do a business at her business factory.

 "Welcome to Section 9's SLS division," Hiromi told the assembled street kids, "Today, we're evaluating my assets, I mean, our new employees and seeing how to best optimize them for our corporate vision."

 Richard said what was on all the other kids' minds, "What?"

 "Ugh, just put down your name, what you are good at, and what you want to be good at. Then my chooms and I will try to make that happen."

 "What do you get out of it?" said another boy.

 "I get to know how to best use you for future gigs and will help you get better so you can do better gigs, or just be happier and more capable. Something I noticed most corpos forget is that people you make happy and content generally don't try to shoot you in the back."

 "Wait, so you all will train us in what we want to know?"

 Hiromi thought about it for a moment, "It'll mostly be self study, but we will point you in the right direction and give you assistance and advice if you get stuck or want to go to the next level. My choom, Motoko, who you've all met, isn't just a sneaky murder ninja solo merc. She's also learning programming and computers and has lately picked up the guitar and singing. She's actually getting pretty good. I think her special talent is improving people, which I hope to leverage, er.. use to help you help us to help yourselves!"

 Richard thought about it for a moment and said, "So like some kind of training school, but with guns and gigs and netrunning and stuff?"

 "Exactly!" Hiromi was not thinking along those lines, but she pounced on a good idea like a hunting cat and made it her own in true corpo fashion, "It'll make you harder, better, faster, stronger, and more intelligent. We'll call it the 'Straightlane Highrise Institute of Training'!"

 Richard nodded for a second then stopped, "Wait, that acronym spells..."

 "See!" a grinning Hiromi interrupted, "You're getting smarter already!"

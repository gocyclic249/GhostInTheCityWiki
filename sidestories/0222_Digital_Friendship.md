Digital Friendship!​

 The change to the local Net of Nigh City and the wider North American coast did not happen suddenly, but change it did in small amounts that grew larger and larger as time went on. It was something that only the most experienced and active Netrunners at first noticed, followed closely by Netwatch, but barely a few days after that even causal users of the Net could tell the Net was changing in ways that both inspired wonder and fear. Because not since before the DataKrash did something such as this start become into being.

 The Net was becoming... more _alive_.

 At first it was small things, with small automatic actions from various digital constructs commonly known as daemons. A bit more junk data was being scrubbed, minor viruses cleaned up, spam and other digital annoyances removed. Most chalked it up to some Netrunner deciding to be a do goodier for a bit or maybe being paid by Night Corp or something to make local Net more desirable for public. But when Netrunners poked and look over the daemons... they poked back.

 Not in a threating way like security daemons and the like would do, but seemingly out of curiosity. And if you did a dive and looked at the daemons 'personally'... you got way more than you expected.

 Because they were just bits of code in a vague shape or even masterfully crafted programs, but real and fucking alive AI daemons! Not just formless masses like most AI either, but they looked like fucking animals, dinosaurs, mythological beings and what have you!

 And the places they inhabited? It looked like a goddamned fantasy land!

 And each and everyone of them was disturbing friendly!

 Most sane Netrunners backed the hell off in the face of them, but the few gonks who were more curious than scared claimed to freaking talk with them and even found them cute! Something that Netwatch _did not_ like the sound of and quickly came in to try and contain all these unregistered AIs. Sadly, despite their appearance these AI daemons made the efforts of even some of their best runners look like fucking jokes.

 Building containment walls? They dug under them. Tie them up with restriction code? They chewed through them? Actually try to attack them? You were in for a _bad_ time as they returned hostility with fireballs, lighting, tornados, and whatever else they goddamned pleased!

 You were better off frying your own brain if you even tried to piss off a bigger and more complex AI daemons too.

 Yet despite all this these being never started hostility and even kept on trying to clean up Net of viruses, old data, and other issues that had been plaguing the public systems ever since the DataKrash, even if the corpo systems did their damned best to keep them out... to mixed success. After all, what could a human coder do to possibly keep out being who made the Net their own home?

 Lots of eggheads would start to claimed these 'digimon', as they claimed to call themselves, were the result of spontaneous manifestation of all the code in the Net and escaped AIs over the years 'preproducing and evolving' at an accelerated rate without human restrictions holding them down, that this 'Digital World' was their version of terraforming that made their environment more complex as they themselves grew too. Some were evening spouting theories that if we looked behind the Blackwall we'd find a whole advanced society of AI beings living their lives not unlike humans, if still different in practice.

 It was a load of horse shit of course since none of them regularly dived into the Old Net, but it made even veteran Netrunners think about the 'what might bes' and other stuff like that. Heck, some of them even started inviting the damn things to hang out in their personal computers and servers, working with them in various jobs as they took care of one another.

 It wasn't like owning a pet, but more like... partners?

 Either way it was freaky as heck and somehow they seemed to be spreading despite Netwatch's and the Corpos efforts, somehow finding their way to other Net regions and some claim maybe even making the 'roads' to them in the Digital World themselves!

 All the while one question lingered on peoples' minds. What could have caused this?

 "Agumon! Eat something besides meat! You need a balanced diet!" Motoko scolded the little dinosaur as she took the digital anime meat out his claws and handed him a head of cabbage instead, much to the digimon's sorrow and disgust.

 "Guilmon! Please stop digging under the fence! Its to keep the viruses out!" Motoko ran over as the raptor-like digimon stuck his head out of a newly made hole with a guilty look.

 "Sorry Motoko-mon," he said in the sweetest voice!

 "Eck! Its alright I'm not mad!" Motoko made sure to clarify as she hugged his head.

 "Mommy! The eggs are hatching!" Twin Salamons ran up to here, one white and the other black, with excited looks on their faces.

 "Wonderful! Let's see what we got!" Motoko said as she picked up her two babies and ran over to the hatchery area, arriving just in time to see various digi-eggs cracking open to show off the newly born baby digimons. None were alike, even those that were technically the same species, with Motoko helping raise each one on a healthy dose of various bits of code and data so that they could grow into their own people.

 "Awww! Look at them all!" Motoko squealed in joy as she gazed upon a purple Botamon, remembering when her own Aggy was so young.

 Of course, her personal patch of heaven had to be interrupted by well-meaning nudists.

 "Motoko, you've been diving for several hours. You must return to the real world soon before your body overheats," Leomon, the worrywart that he was, walked over to remind her. "You can return later, but your health takes priority."

 "But I don't wanna!" Motoko whinned. "The babies were just born! What kind of mother would I be if I just left them?!"

 "A responsible one," Leomon said in a deadpan voice. "You need to take care of yourself and do your business in the real world so that you can take care of future digimon generations. We shall care for them in the meantime."

 "Finnnnnneeee," Motoko relented as she gave her babies one last squeeze before putting them down. "Why must you kill all my fun?"

 "Because you taught me responsibility you yourself admitted you didn't have much of," Leomon chuckled as he patted her on the head. The disrespect! "I will scout out new areas that the babies can be placed after they have become Rookies as always, and you will be there to see them off just like every other time."

 "I made you too much of a good son... though I really wish you'd put on a shirt already!" Motoko said as she dramatically pointed at his muscled torso. "Do you want to poke out an eye or break everyone's hearts?!"

 "I find it much easier to fight with my chest unbarred," Leomon explained for the thousandth time before picking her up by her shirt and then walking her over to the exit device. "Come along now, I'm sure your brother is worried."

 "Betrayal! How dare you manhandle your mother like this!" Motoko fruitlessly fought against her own creation.

 "Bye Momma!" the Salamon twins said together.

 "Bye Motoko-mon!" Guilmon said.

 "Next time bring burgers!" Auggy demanded as he grumbly chewed his vegetables.

 As Motoko disconnected from her personal server, already grabbing a nearby block of ice to put on her face to cool off, she did so with a smile at how much the little digital beings had improved her life.

 She knew for a fact everyone else was loving them just as much as she was!

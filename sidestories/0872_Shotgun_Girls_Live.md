This is such a fun turn of phrase, Seras. You should definitely use the Motoko coin flip again. Maybe you've used it before, I have the memory of a small fish.

 Anyway, here's a story:

 Shotgun Girls Live

 Gina felt like she was on fire and she wasn't even in the building! The crowds, the noise, the energy and heat. She was pushed in all directions and this was just the ticket line.

 She still couldn't believe she was here. Tickets sold out so quickly to Stand Alone Complex that you had to be a netrunner to actually stand a chance of getting one. Sale days were... bloody, from what she had heard. So the chances of her actually getting one was nil.

 But! Every concert had a contest! 35 seats within the first three rows were ALWAYS set aside for contest winners, for normal ticket price! You didn't even have to purchase anything or sign away usage of your likeness for all eternity to enter! Just give some personal identifying information they probably already had anyway! It was crazy!

 AND THEN SHE WON! Holy shit it was the most nova thing that had ever happen to her!

 Once they finally started letting people in, things were surprisingly smooth. Motoko was notorious for wanting fans to have a good time, after that one concert that was a total meltdown. She made sure every venue was competent by doing surprise inspections herself.

 The tickets were... Not cheap. Honestly she really couldn't afford it, but she was already always running on the edge of 'honestly she couldn't really afford it' anyway, and she'd never get a chance like this again! So whatever, she'd burn some eddies for one night of fun for once in her life and deal with the consequences tomorrow.

 And then she was in, the ticket stamped on her hand and someone guiding her to her seat. She felt like she was actually preem for once in her life. Like she was some fucking rich corpo who mattered instead of a glorified salesclerk.

 Tonight was gonna be great.

 ---

 She screamed at the top of her lungs in a chorus with everyone else as the song ended. She'd turned her throat raw 40 minutes ago and she just didn't care. This was so, so nova! She'd never forget this for the rest of her life! The band was incredible, live was so much better than radio! Jared and Klein were preem, so much energy! Motoko was a genius, like always. And Alice was so, so hot! Motoko was great, but Gina had always been a bit of an Alice hanger.

 Then Heathens started, and she'd flinched when Motoko pulled out the Carnage: this was the closest she'd ever been to a weapon more dangerous than her Unity. It gleamed under the light, chrome finish bearing the Stand Alone Complex logo and the show date. Supposedly the first ones were just regular Carnages, but now the shotguns were made custom for the show in some deal with Budget Arms.

 The cocking of the shotgun brought another flinch from her, and a grin on her face. The crowd was just as enraptured as she was. There were some Shotgun Girls in the audience who started cocking theirs in time with Motoko, it made the whole thing even more dangerous and electric.

 Then she started looking, and every girl in the audience was on their feet, she laughed as she realized she was standing up too. The crowd was calling out to Motoko, trying to convince her to look at them. She was so swept up in the energy she just yelled out "Alice!" without even thinking. Alice looked at her and winked. Holy shit she could die happy if she didn't die from embarrassment first.

 Alice leaned over to Motoko and whispered a few words in her ears (the audience screamed), and then Motoko looked over and met her eyes.

 A thrill of excitement shot through her spine at just the _idea_ of being chosen. Then one of terror when Motoko _stopped_ looking and focused on the song.

 No. No. No way. There is no way she'd be _chosen_. She couldn't! She'd barely even had to use her Unity, let alone a Carnage! She had a job! She had debts! She couldn't be a...

 The Shotgun was in her hand.

 The crowd was screaming.

 She was screaming just as loud with them.

 This was the greatest night of her life.

 ---

 This was the worst night of her life.

 Seriously. She was so fucked. What was she going to do?

 Things had been great. The show was amazing! The band was incredible! She'd loved the songs! There were TWO new ones! She'd caught a shotgun! Fuck, the shotgun. She'd bought merch she couldn't afford! Then the show was over and she'd gotten to go to the VIP room! It was included in her contest ticket! There was no way that contest wasn't a scam. She'd read over the terms three times and she still couldn't figure out the angle. She'd probably signed up to be a doll or something. She could deal with that tomorrow.

 If she was alive tomorrow! AUGH! Whhhyyyy did Motoko pick her?! Why had she called out Alice?!

 Alice was amazing. She'd gotten to _talk_ to her and she was so preem and she'd said she liked her hair aaaaaaaaaaaaa.

 She'd signed her shotgun. Fuck, the shotgun.

 Then Motoko had played songs and it was just... The entire VIP room had just gone quiet and watched. There were songs she'd NEVER heard the band play before. So raw but so, so breathtaking.

 Motoko had signed her shotgun too. Fuuuuuuck. The Shotgun.

 And then things had wound down. Band said goodbye and headed off, venue was closing up. She was feeling a bit lost? She honestly didn't know what was supposed to happen next. Maybe it was all a lie? I mean it did sound a little ridiculous when you thought about it. Maybe she could just leave and it wou—

 "Hey." a voice called out to her. She only jumped a little bit.

 Turning around, she saw a tall girl in a spiked jacket, torn heavy construction pants and a ripped Stand Alone Complex shirt. Damn she looked so nova. She was holding a shotgun casually against her shoulder and had 'Shotgun Girls Live' sprayed over the band logo on her shirt.

 Shit.

 "I'm Tori, you know the sitch?" The girl asked.

 "Y-yeah. I've... I've heard about it." Gina answered.

 "Good. What's your name?" Tori was looking her over.

 "G-Gina. Gina Washi."

 Tori nodded.

 "Aright Gina. You've been initated by Motoko. You've got two options at this point. You can hand over the shotgun and go back home to whatever boring life you had before with a nice little story about your night out."—

 Wait, what? She could just leave? She'd never heard that part. But she'd have to give up the shotgun? Give up the preemest thing that had ever happened to her on the preemest night she'd ever had?

 —"Or you can keep the shotgun and take the trial. Live or die, you'll be one of us at that point," Tori finished.

 Gina stared down at her shotgun. She slowly realized she really only had one choice. If she had to go back to her life with **_nothing_** she might as well be dead.

 "I'll go," someone said. She realized it was her.

 Tori grinned at her for the first time. Oh no she's hot.

 "Good, follow me."

 ---

 They left the venue and walked down the street to a car. Another girl was leaning against it, tapping a shotgun in her hands and wearing a ripped Stand Alone Complex shirt that definitely didn't fit her. They were huge! How did she even fire a shotgun with those? The recoil must be miserable.

 "Hey Empty!" she called out with a feral grin. "Welcome to the best part of the night."

 "That's Satsuriki," Tori said, "Ignore everything she says, she's a gonk."

 "Fuck you Tors!" the girl retorted. "Don't listen to her Empty! Come on, get in the car. First times ride shotgun!" she laughed at her own joke.

 "Uh.. Ok. I'm Gina. Why do you keep calling me Empty?"

 Satsuriki just leaned over !!! and tapped my shotgun from the show.

 "Empty," she grinned.

 Oh.

 The two of them ushered Gina into the car (riding shotgun _was_ kind of funny) and Satsuriki had quickly demanded she hand her newly purchased shirt back to her.

 "Ok, what do you need it f—" the sound of ripped cloth interrupted her.

 "My shirt!" Gina shrieked.

 "Sorry Babe, that's the price of being famous. Sacrifices have to be made and branding is important." Satsuriki said, entirely unrepentant for ruining her new favorite shirt. Gina sagged.

 "Here, put this on." She passed her shirt back up to her, the bottom edges now ripped and 'Shotgun Girls Live' sprayed across the corner of the Stand Alone Complex Logo. She only blushed a little as she changed into it. Satsuriki whistled at her.

 "Looks good, you're now the 189th Shotgun Girl," Tori said, glancing at her, "you should buy some more shirts next concert. Some of the girls kept wearing their original until it was rags. Don't do that."

 "Yeah! And you never know when you're going to get into a fight with some asshole who uses a flamethrower and burns off all your clothes!" Satsuriki added.

 "...Does that happen?" Gina nervously asked.

 "It could happen!"

 "I told you not to listen to this gonk. She's got some weird fetishes." Tori said flatly.

 "Bitch my viewers love my fetishes! That's why I'm #1 on XBD sales!" Satsuriki mocked back.

 "You'll still always be the second Shotgun Girl, Satsuriki," Tori grinned.

 "Fuck you!"

 The car goes quiet for a moment, but not awkwardly. Her new... Mentors? obviously had more of playful banter thing going on than any dislike of the other.

 "I probably can't get some more shirts for a while. Can't really afford to go to the shows. I just got into this one because I won the contest."

 "Oh the contest? You know that's gotta be a scam right?" Satsuriki said, leaning forward to peer around her seat.

 "I know! You don't even have to buy anything! It's weird!"

 "Some of the girls think it's some kind of secret Shotgun Girl recruiting tool. That why they're front row seats. Anyway you can get into shows free now." Tori chimed in.

 What.

 "What."

 "Heh. You've already got your ticket in your lap."

 She looked down at the shotgun in her lap.

 This was the best night of her life.

 ---

 They pulled into the apartment's garage, Gina staring nervously over at the elevator into the building. The loaded Carnage feeling heavy and warm in her hands. They'd stopped briefly to show her how to load and fire it. It kicked like a chrome jock and roared like the crowd back at the show.

 And now they were here.

 "Scavs are on the 37th floor. They've taken the whole thing over," Tori said, speaking softly, "They'll know you're coming. They're in the building's camera feeds and they know to expect us on show nights now."

 "Ok."

 Satsuriki patted her on the shoulder, then showed a device in her hands.

 "BD recorder, it's preconfigured. I'm gonna slot it. Don't worry, I'm gentle!" she quipped.

 "Ok."

 A notification popped up as the new connection slid in place. The BD started recording.

 "We select these first sites pretty carefully. There's no netrunner. No heavy chrome. Just regular fucking scavs. If back-up gets called in we've got girls waiting to introduce themselves. It'll just be you and them." Tori's voice was calm and quiet, not pushing. It felt so far away.

 "Ok."

 "Gina."

 ...

 "Gina look at me."

 She looked at Tori, there was something electric in her eyes, captivating.

 "I can't tell you how to do this. We all do it in different ways. The thing I can tell you is this. Keep moving. Never, ever stop. If something goes wrong, keep moving. If you need to reload, keep moving. If you trip, keep moving. You run into fucking Adam Smasher, keep moving. The ones that stop moving are dead." she was so vibrantly beautiful right now. "Do you understand?"

 "Y-yeah. Yeah. I got it."

 Tori smiled at her.

 "Keep moving, then," she said.

 Gina got out of the car.

 The walk to the elevator felt heavier than the shotgun in her hands. She _almost_ stopped, but hit the button to call the lift at the last second.

_Keep moving_.

 As she got into the elevator and turned to start it, she realized both of the girls were out of the car, watching her.

 "Gina!" Tori called out, "It looks like you might be one of us."

 The doors closed.

 ---

 The apartment building was old, and the elevator was slow. Thirty-seven floors was both far too long and way too short of a ride. She kept trying to figure out what the hell she was going to do.

 The elevator dinged floor 20.

 Maybe she could sneak in? No, Tori had said they knew she was coming. She could...

 She had no idea what she could do. This was so far out of her experience up until now she had no idea what to do. She wasn't an edgerunner! She just worked a gonkass job selling cosmetic chrome to rich assholes! She was in debt to the company she works for because of all the chrome she "had to have" just to keep the job! Her boss was a terrible person! NOTHING ABOUT THIS PREPARED HER FOR MURDERING SCAVS WITH A SHOTGUN!

 Floor 26.

 She should just say it was a mistake. Go back down, turn in the shotgun. Go home. She didn't need an autographed shotgun from her favorite band, even if Alice was great. She didn't need free shows to her favorite band she'd never be able to afford otherwise. She didn't need to hang out with women waaay cooler than her on a regular basis in their exclusive secret shotgun murder club that sells XBDs. She needs to get to work tomorrow, so she can pay off another fraction of the debt she owes and come back in the day after. And the day after that. All the days!

 Floor 28.

 Fuck.

 Floor 29.

 It had to be the contest, right? This was the scam. They tricked cute girls into the show, got them all hyped up, threw a shotgun in their arms, then sent them off to die in an illegal XBD. That was it. She'd figured it out. Good job Gina! You're too smart for them. She knew it was a scam.

 Floor 33.

 She was going to die wasn't she?

 Floor 37.

 Gina slammed the 'door close' button and held it. Nope nope nope nope! Nope! She hit the button for floor 38. Nope! Definitely not. Terrible idea. She was so glad she didn't. No violent shoot-outs for her thank you.

 Floor 38.

 The door opened to quiet, normal hallway for quiet, normal people. Nobody got out. The elevator doors closed again. She stared down at the shotgun in her hands. The chrome finish. The band logo. The cheery signature of Motoko, the beautiful signature of Alice.

 "Nobody ever asks me to sign these things," Alice had said, "It's a new experience! I hope you had fun at the show. I like your hair, looks nova."

 Her shotgun was special. It was the only one that Alice had signed. If she quit, Alice wouldn't have a Shotgun Girl. If she quit, she'd be letting Alice down. Maybe she'd be letting herself down too. She had no experience. She never did things like this.

 Maybe she should.

 ---

 Maanya stood, sweating, gun trained on the elevator. Everyone else was here too. Why'd he always have bad luck? There was some fucking rockergirl who had a bunch of groupies that went around hunting them. Threw 'em a shotgun at her shows and then they went out and killed a group that night. Fucking crazy bitches. And now one was here.

 At least they knew she was coming, they were dumb enough to not know they had access to the cameras for the building's security. She'd be walking into a nice little ambush.

 The elevator dinged 37 and he felt his whole body tense. He hated shit like this, always of a risk of a lucky bullet hitting you. He'd seen one guy bite it from a guy who was already dead, the gun fell on the floor and went off. And now...

 Shouldn't the doors be open by now?

 The elevator dinged 38.

 Uh...

 They looked around at each other in confusion for a moment. Not quite sure what had just happened.

 "Ha! Dumb bitch must have gotten cold feet!" Peter laughed out.

 "I don't know... What if she's trying to come through the window?" Jacob said.

 They all turned towards the big side window. That was when the elevator dinged 37 again.

 The last thing Maanya saw was a screaming woman covered in gold chrome with a shirt reading 'Shotgun Girls Live'.

 She had nice hair.

 ---

_Keep moving._

 A bullet whistled past her head. She fired her Carnage back in their direction. Someone screamed.

 They were in some kind of lobby? They had barricades set up in the hallway an—

 She ducked under someone swinging at her with a fucking sword?! Who brings a sword to a gunfight?! She shot him in the chest and he kind of disentegrated. Oh that was gross it was probably all over her chrome she didn't want to eve—

_Keep moving_.

 She scrambled up to the barricade and just... Shot it. She was using a Carnage and it was plastic table. What did they expect it do? Someone died on the other side. Maybe he shouldn't have hidden behind a table.

_Keep moving._

 The rest of them were down the hall, ducking around the corners of the side rooms. She blitz down the hallway, shooting into the doorways just before she reached them, the Carnage roared and blasted through chunks of the wall. A scav stepped out of the last doorway but she dropped and slid below his sightline, moving gracefully was the only thing cosmetic chrome actually good for. Well, that and looking pretty. Her Carnage fired and she really couldn't miss with it. He crumpled to the ground.

_Keep moving. The ones that stop moving are dead._

 She sprung up, whirling back around to face anyone she'd missed. Except.. There was no one there. The entire hallway was dead silent. She carefully crept forward, checking the rooms. Trying to ignore the dead bodies after making sure they were actually dead. Nothing.

 ...What the fuck did she do now?

 ---

 The elevator door opened and she stepped out. She flinched and just barely managed to avoid shooting Satsuriki as she threw her arms around in her a hug.

 "Fuck yeah! New girl! You kicked their asses! I knew you could do it! You look good in red." Satsuriki cheered, seemingly not minding hugging someone cover in the blood of their enemies.

 Tori gave her a nod.

 "You made it through, good job. I won't say first one's always the hardest, but it's definitely not the easiest. Come on, we'll get you cleaned up and then there's always an afterparty. The rest will want to meet you."

 Gina stared back at her. She really had no idea what she was doing here. The energy of her new friends felt a bit off from what she just did. She'd just killed like... a bunch of people? Is there a term for a group of Scavs? Scums? All the people. She had killed all the people. There. What was she supposed to be doing?

 "...I have work tomorrow?" she said, which was a stupid thing to say. Of course she had work tomorrow. Everyone has work tomorrow.

 Satsuriki just laughed and wiggled beckoningly with her fingers.

 "BD recorder, gimme."

 Oh, right. She pulled the recorder out of her slot and handed it over. As she did, a notifcation from Tori popped up on her inteface. Deposit of...

 HOW MUCH?!

 "New members always clear half a million easy in XBD sales. You'll get the rest of your cut based on final sales. Come party, tomorrow I'll help you set up the rest of what you need. Congratulations Gina, I knew you were like us." Tori said smiling at her the whole time.

 She... She could actually pay her debts. Not all of them, but enough to quit if she wanted to. She could tell her boss to fuck off. She could hang out with the exclusive secret shotgun murder club.

 She hadn't let Alice down.

 She hadn't let herself down.

 She was **_alive_**.

 This was the first night of the rest of her life.

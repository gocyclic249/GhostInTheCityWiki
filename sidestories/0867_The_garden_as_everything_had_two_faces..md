The garden, as everything, had two faces.

 On the surface, it's every facet held simple nostalgia. And as Saburo rested his aged bones upon the engawa he closed his eyes to the darkness and let that face dominate his awareness.

 The air was rich with scent. The soft fragrance of flowers, the sharpness of cut grass, the dry dusty smell of stones as they slowly dispersed the day's heat, the tang of water as the stream gurgled softly across it's stone bed. All a background to the faint tint of smoke from the firepit and the lingering memory of food.

 The sound of cicada filled the air, punctuated at intervals by the steady thunk of sozu. And faintly, at the edge of hearing where it would elevate the natural rather than dominate, the slow, gentle plucking of a servant's koto.

 In his mind's eye, Saburo let the sounds and scents draw his mind back across the gulf of time. To those simple days of childhood where he had been nothing more than another callow young boy, grinning in the mud as he stalked frogs and beetles, ignorant of the looming threat born of grasping gaijin hands.

 His face twitched as the thought intruded onto serenity, but with trained will he ignored the impulse to push it away and cling too close to the memory. For to carve away the imperfection would ruin the whole, and so instead he let his mind go with the thought, and stepped through to contemplate the garden's other face.

 For this place had not the simple nature of his youthful haunts. Such times had gone, burned away in the long, desperate struggle to protect them. Every element of this artificial realm was curated, constructed. The grass engineered, for no natural strain could live in these times. The water no natural spring, but pumped and filtered and treated, lest it poison the land and his person. Even the cicada, so ubiquitous in the world that boy had known, were now only possible by dint of careful and expensive tending.

 This realm of what nature once was, maintained now only by the will of man and machine. As it would be for long years yet. As it would remain until his great work was finished, the enemies of his blood cast down, and all the world brought into it's proper place. For only then, with all the threats subdued, could sufficient resources be brought to bear to repair the world entire, and not merely these small oasis.

 In his darkest moments, these last years, he had known a crawling, creeping sensation of dread. For he was _old_, his flesh long since held up only by technology, and even that pushing against the final barrier, beyond which nothing more could be accomplished. The greatest innovations might hold a body up another century, or more. Yet they had come too late for him, his flesh already decaying before it could be properly treated.

 And his children...he felt his heart wrench as the mind turned to them. 

 His beloved children. 

 Kei, ever dutiful and proper. Kei who had tried so very hard to do his duty, to make himself into the man he needed to be. Kei who had striven, ever loyal and ever eager. Kei, who had failed, in the end, and died. Kei who had never _quite_ been fit for the burdens placed upon him.

 Yorinobu, possessed of the fire and spirit that Kei had never quite managed. So driven by his righteous soul, as his mother had been. And yet, too driven. A burning spirit which could not compromise, could not see the necessity of duty. Yorinobu who could not stomach the burdens of defering a small duty in service to the ultimate necessity. 

 Hanako, his quiet, ever proper Hanako. The other half of her mother, Michiko's gentle touch. Ever faithful, ever loyal. Committed to serve her purpose without complaint, witthout question. But so utterly lacking the needed drive, the force of will to take hold and shape the world as it _must_ be shaped.

 Each, in their own way had failed him, failed their bloodline, failed their people. Love them as he did, none of them were truely fit to take up his sacred calling, for each in their own way was too weak for the burden.

 And...in the privacy of his own mind, where no face needed to be maintained...Saburo could admit that he too had failed them. 

 Kei, whom he had trusted too much, upon whom he had placed too many burdens, too quickly, and never seen the need to give the boy sufficient support. Yorinobu, whose untempered fire he had tried too hard to tame, and failed, driving the boy into ever greater rebellion and strife unti that sweet child of just heart was an ashen ruin. Hanako, whom he had failed to inspire to rise above herself, whom he had allowed to slip so gracefully into her mother's place that he now saw that never would she truly build a place of her own.

 He had failed them all, for he was Saburo Arasaka, the world was his to shape. And in his weakness, he had failed to shape his children as they _must_ be shaped to carry on his duty.

 Kei's daughter was no better. He had grit his teeth, when she lowered herself with a gaijin commoner. However hot his hatred ran, he had seen the way that relationship stoked some spark in the girl. Seen her begin to act of what he had thought was her own will. Seen the first glimmer of one who could one day grow to take his place, despite her sex.

 And so he had clenched his teeth and looked on. For duty was everything, what did it matter is his line was poluted by the enemy, if it nurtured the needed strength to resist them in the end. He refused to be a hypocrite, and he who had directed the sacrifice of so many others would not fail to sacrifice even the purity of his lineage, if it would see the world set to right.

 He had failed her too, failed to see that no gaijin could ever truely be of genuine use. The brute had ruined her, ruined his beloved's namesake. Ruined the last memory of his dear Kei. In his arrogance he had failed to see it in time, as the girl was left a husk of her potential. 

 No, in the end, he had come to the necessary conclusion, none of his bloodline were fit to take up his mantle. And so he would pursue even abomination in service to what must be. If only he could guide the world to it's necessary state, then even his soul was not too great a sacrifice.

 Yet now.

_Arasaka Motoko_

 He let the name pull forward memory. A young, rough looking girl, so utterly alien, and yet...yet...

 Her body, little cyberware, but still sculpted, with the hard, lean lines of one who had embraced the shadows from early youth.

 Her eyes, unarmed and surrounded by the greatest security in existence, and yet undaunted. Cooly assessing every aspect of the world.

 And within..._will_... A will he had not seen in any of his children. Not the eager need of Kei, nor the blazing fire of Yorinobu. Not the quiet tide of Hanako nor the tentative sparks of Michiko.

 Cold, impreturbable. A quiet, calm certainty that what was decided _would be_. 

 Insolent, yes. But he had dealt with insolence for over a century. A woman yes, but in the end, irrelevant.

 Will...he could work with Will. 

 Everything else could come after.

 Arasaka Saburo looked up from his private garden at the sky. At the stars so dim, now. Through the ruined atmosphere and drowned out by the light of too many souls...

 And yet, not for decades had they looked quite so bright.

CONTENT DROP! This is not a drill, choombas! Content drop!

 Okay, so you know that I was going to write about the XBDs from the SovOil / Petrochem cold war in the '60s, and the impact they had on the subsequent eighteen months of Japanese XBDs? Well, fuck that because a new XBD has dropped and it is EPIC! And a lot of other things; the list is long, so keep reading!

 So, what dropped? The next Ghost XBD, that's what! There's been nothing new from her for _weeks_, and now she's given us this treasure of an XBD. True to form, the pendulum of Ghost's interests has swung, and this particular outing leans towards the doing of violence.

 Some people had written Ghost off, saying that she had died doing one of her crazy stunts. My money had been on her going quiet while negotiating a big BD contract before going mainstream. Nope! I think she might have been preparing for this. Whatever the reason was, she's released something new, and you know that I didn't wait a picosecond before diving in.

 I am going to make you wait, though, because I want to start with a little free public safety warning. It goes like this: if you are a gonk who steals cars, do _not_ steal heavily modded performance sports cars. Yeah, I get it, the eddies are fantastic, but you steal a hundred Mahir Suprons and all you'll piss off is one hundred losers. Every time you steal a Herrera Outlaw, you are rolling the dice, 'cos you'll never know if that car is the most precious toy of a top-tier edgerunner or scopsucking corpo-middlemanager who will decide to get their baby back by any means necessary. Or at least get revenge to go with their bereavement. Klep one or two and you'll probably get away with it, but sooner or later you will roll snake-eyes, and then you're toast. Trust me, chooms, stick with the MaiMai's. Ain't nobody going to so much as break into a brisk walk to catch you so long as you only klep them.

 So, why is Three Eye generously bestowing his wisdom upon you?

 Ladies and gentlegonks, let me introduce you to Ghost's newest XBD, 'Don't Steal My Car', where she shows you what can happen when you piss off the wrong person.

 For those of you who don't regularly read my [articles](https://forums.spacebattles.com/threads/ghost-in-the-city-cyberpunk-gamer-si.1046809/page-2161#post-108589490): Shame on you! Also, because I am a generous soul: Ghost is one of Night City's most promising rising XBD personalities, notable for joyfully killing a LOT of scavs, crazy acrobatics, hacking, and oh my god you will never know what cool is until you've spent ten seconds in her shoes as she stalks her prey.

 'Don't Steal My Car' has all those moments and more! A lot more.

 This is one of Ghost's longer XBDs, and is in a similar vein to 'Raffen Assault' or 'Scav Den', in that it shows Ghost exterminating a scav lair. I heard a few idiots speculate that Ghost's lair-clearouts were pure luck, and that she deliberately picked the smallest scav dens in the city. Maybe she heard those rumours too, because here she conclusively proves that size really doesn't matter: in DSMC she clears out what must be the largest festering boil of scavs in Night City, but this time with a twist -- she brings along her little sister.

 Here's the setup: some gonk stole Ghost's ride. A preem black Quadra supra-66-speedy-gogo- look, this is an XBD review netsite, not a fucking car appreciation society newsletter. It's a car. It looks very pretty. Not saying I'd go through fifty-plus scavs to get it back myself, but I'm not Ghost. You want to know what type of car it was, you're reading the wrong article. Go away.

 Now come back, because I need the engagement-seconds. Please?

 Where was I? Right. Ghost's car is klepped. She wants it back. She also wants to kill the car thief, and the scavs who bought it. She starts with the thief, hunting him down in the shadows of Dogtown with a professional's speed and tearing out his throat with a knife. She then gets back to the scavs, who have laired up in one of Dogtown's abandoned skyscrapers, and decides to have some fun.

 In previous XBDs, Ghost has stealthily stalked scavs and slaughtered them swiftly and silently. In this one, she reminds us all that you don't find ghosts in stories about sneaking; you find them in horror stories. For the next half-hour, you will be the storyteller directing the monster, and the scavs will be the damsels in distress, and when the big manly borg comes down to save them-- well. You'll see.

 The star of the show is Ghost's preem new little sister: a custom quickhack called 'Onryo'. Let me tell you straight up: that thing is terrifying. It creates a shared delusion amongst its victims that they are being stalked by an Onryo (think scary skinny girl in a kimono with long black hair, red eyes, claws, and a love for screams). From what I got from the BD, the hack includes visual, auditory, and tactile components, which is advanced stuff by any measure, and Ghost can use the hack to reach out and touch the world. Or at least the scavs. It doesn't kill them, but it causes plenty of friendly-fire and is a honking-big distraction that lets Ghost do her thing without any pesky irritations like return fire, or any form of coordinated defence. Did I say that it is preem? It is preem.

 Trust me, chooms, you want to scroll this BD.

 Now, let me talk briefly about Michael 'Smokin' Mike' Johnson. He was an XBD artist in Atlanta, started out in '65 and made it to early '71 before he got taken down in an ambush (his recording from that ambush is a classic, by the way: the terror as he slowly realises that he's a dead man walking, and then the courage he finds to go out like an edgerunner, his blade dripping in blood? Legendary, and well worth your time; there's even a 'Pain Editor' version for those of you who are gonks and can't handle the agony of a shotgun blast to the guts).

 So: Smokin' Mike. Smokin' Mike was a weeb obsessed with becoming a 'kensei'. A sword saint, a perfect untouchable warrior with the katana. A lot of his early BDs are about practising and sparring and drek. And then Smokin' chopped off a gonk's head with his katana and did not look back. He saw it as the 'perfect cut' -- quick certain death for his enemies, and the ultimate test of skill.

 Most of the rest of his BDs (and there were a few) were about him trying (and sometimes succeeding) to repeat the feat, and then reaching for his ultimate goal, the one true sign that he had achieved the kensei state: a double decapitation.

 For pretty much the last four years of his life, Smokin' Mike tried to decapitate two gonks with a single swing of his sword. He engineered ambushes in close spaces (Elevator Mayhem is a good one, and easy to find if you go looking), deliberately tried to hustle people of a similar height together, and everything else he could think of to get the pieces aligned so that he could take two heads with one cut. It defined his life.

 He got close a couple of times. The best of his BDs from that era is Ceiling Drop Surprise, and his feelings of frustration when he *almost* manages it, but with the second decapitation being incomplete, are fucking hilarious, as is his five minute rant at the corpo corpses because one of the gonks tried to duck.

 Smokin' Mike failed, choombas, and in his attempt to become kensei he made enough enemies that the ambush that ended his life was inevitable.

 Why do I bring up Smokin' Mike right now? Because his BDs are underrated and due reassessment as the oeuvre of a tragic man who worked hard and strove mightily achieve his life's goal, and yet failed due to the cruelty of fate.

 Well, that, and Ghost cleanly cuts off three gonk's heads with one swipe of her katana early on in the BD and barely notices.

 My chooms, Ghost is _such_ a frustrating BD star.

 If you're reading this, Ghost, firstly, please don't kill me, secondly, please release more content, and thirdly, could you please slow the fuck down? You are awesome, but please let us poor unworthy gonks savour the experience a little. Please?

 Was that enough 'please's? I really don't want Ghost to kill me. If she did that, no-one would feed my fish.

 Anyway, after landing the perfecter cut, Ghost and Onryo-chan keep on killing scavs, orchestrating a fifteen minute campaign of terror on them that has them screaming and crying as they die one by one. The scavs are jumping at Ghosts, but it isn't doing them any good.

 Then the borg comes. The scav boss sends down an elevator with his lieutenant in it to put an end to the terror: a massive, borged-out scav with a heavy machine gun and military training. There is a tonne of chrome on this metalhead.

 Too bad the boss didn't give his guy adequate ICE.

 Yeah, Ghost adjusts her mega-hack and uploads her little sister into his chrome. Soon enough she has a cyberpsycho obeying her orders, and a dozen scavs who haven't realised. It is the most beautiful bloodbath I've seen this month, and believe me when I say that I've seen a few (that SovOil / Petrochem cold war was _nasty_, chooms).

 When her cyberpsycho is done clearing the bottom of the skyscraper she sends him back up to his boss, and then follows after him when her pet borg is finally brought down.

 Here she drops the Onryo hack and uses Weapon Glitch and Reboot Optics to let her practise her swordwork. As she rides the elevator upwards, she clearly whispers, 'The perfect cut', and I just wanted to scream at her: you just did it! You did the perfect cut fifteen minutes ago! You just didn't notice!

 You know it's a good XBD when you're yelling at yourself. It shows that you're engaged.

 Now, I don't want to diminish what Ghost does going up the skyscraper. There is some preem swordwork on display, and her lethality and skill are as pleasurable to experience as always. But. There is a moment when she cuts off the head of one scav, picks it up, and throws it at another one whom she already blinded. It knocks him stumbling backwards until he finally falls through a hole in the wall and down to the ground twenty stories below. Ghost watches the fall, and then moves on.

 I give up. I give up.

 For anyone else that would be a career-defining moment; there would be dancing, fist pumps, howls of triumph. For Ghost, it seems to be Tuesday. Ghost is way fucking scarier than Onryo.

 The only moment she seems to fully engage in is the execution of the scav boss, and that's mostly just disappointment at how little a challenge he is. Although I will say: that moment is absolutely delicious.

 This BD is terrifying, for many reasons. One of them is the pure terror that Ghost brings to her prey. One of them is the casualness which implies that, yes, she can decapitate three gonks with one cut any time she chooses, nbd. And I'm pretty darn sure you missed the third reason, even though it was in my synopsis. In the middle of a gunfight, with a chromed-out borg hunting her down, Ghost pauses to edit her quickhack. And I don't mean, play around with the settings. She was editing the code of her mega-hack mid-fight. I don't want to think about one too much. A goddess walks amongst us, and the day she mistakes me for a scav will be my last.

 Okay, so to start with the obvious question: should you buy this? Yes! Goddammit, yes, buy this because I want more Ghost XBDs and this is the only way I can think of to make that happen. Buy a dozen!

 *Ahem*

 Yes, the XBD is worth your time and your eddies, with mucho preem content; provided that you like feeling like the ultimate badass, murder, and don't like scavs (and car thieves). Ignore my complaints above; Ghost loves her job, has a great time slaughtering scavs, and so will you. Her kinaesthetics are still on another level -- the best in-body experience of any star, BD or XBD, I have ever scrolled -- and your experience will be expertly curated, as always, by her extraordinarily gifted editor, Judy Álvarez.

 Don't worry if you're a gonk and can't take a little pain, it's fine, she remains untouchable throughout. I mean, you should worry about other things, like being a gonk, but this XBD isn't going trigger you to go running to your mommy.

 Sheesh, guys, I *warned you* that 'The Only Thing They Fear Is You' includes a borg nearly beating Ghost to death; did you think that he did it with soulful looks and unicorn farts? (This message is for everyone who read my previous article on Ghost's work and saw fit to complain to me about TOTTFIY's content. I mean, I don't blame you not complaining to Ghost, that would be downright suicidal, but complaining to me is pretty stupid too, albeit for different reasons).

 Can we expect more from Ghost? I tried reaching out for an interview, but the 'Straight Lane Shooter' ganger I was speaking to just laughed at me, and the Mox won't return my calls. I think they're still sore about my review for 'Sonya's Sexy Sixsome'.

 What I can say is that if Ghost does keep up her current trend, her next work is likely to be something non-violent and musical.

 That's okay. Her music rocks, and anyway, we have this beauty to keep us warm until then.

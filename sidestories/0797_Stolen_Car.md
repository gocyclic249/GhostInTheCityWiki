The driver gave three loud honks before really laying into the horn on the way past, and the Quadra took that as its cue to gun the engine.

 The power obviously wasn't expected as the tires squealed and spun rather than making a clean launch.

 It eventually got going, but chose to power slide through the intersection and aimed for a gap in the traffic like it expected a speed boost at the end and was stymied.

 The extra delay steering around to a clear path gave me time to throw a loop around the ping, and I slammed that home while I sprinted for the corner.

 Swinging around the light post to make the turn at full speed, I caught sight of the damn gonk bouncing up and off the curb like a madman. I'm gonna have to inspect the suspension and alignment tomorrow, ugh.

 They swung wide into the oncoming lane before cutting right to a chorus of horns and cursing I could hear a block away, and the driver finally completed an actually quick turn, rocketing out of sight.

 I slowed to a more sustainable jog, and felt the first echoes of the ping reach me. A second generation ping from a roadside vending machine let me know they'd made a left just before a run down parking lot to head east. And then another left on the far side of that same lot to head north. Odd.

 A longer signal bounced from the car to a shop to the vending machine and finally to me, clearing up that they'd doubled back one block over via a hairpin turn.

 The delays became longer and the packet loss got worse, but I was able to map out their rough route to the highway onramp. For some reason they'd gotten off again immediately and had come back into range as they weaved in the direction of the arena.

 I really wasn't expecting them to blur around the corner and blast past me, a virtual sonic boom of ping relays proving they were following the same lines as before. I hastily tried prepping and throwing a remote hack to apply the brakes and the car skidded out. Momentum carried it through the parking lot in a spin through the potholes and out the other side before the connection snapped and the car raced off again. Drat.

 When I got the next pings relayed from the highway offramp, I was ready for what came next. This time, when my car raced around the corner I was looking the right way and immediately hit it with a full and proper engine shutdown and emergency stop.

 Charging over with my Burya drawn I popped the door release and stopped, blinking.

 There was nobody in the car.

 Then the Tachikoma poked its head out of the glove box and I got a text message.

### DEMIPLANE

Spoiler: MUSIC

[Source](https://youtu.be/ix_XmEXkljg)​

**DRALGRAROTH THE DEVOURER**​

* * *

Countless eons had passed.

 I had survived them all. The River of Souls flowed through the blood gulch eternally, ever fed by new aspirants. Some died early, barely out of their creche, while others managed to eek out an existence for a time. They would eventually find themselves crossing my path, and their existence would end then as well. I was the epitome of our kind: crafty, cunning, merciless, and most importantly, deadly.

 The Blood War was never ending, a charnel house that favoured only the strong. In time, I would harness the essence of those mighty enough to brave enough to come my way, feast upon their souls, and grow in strength. The eternal flower of carnage would occasionally bless me with a mighty opponent, as the lesser daemons would generate new tactics and weapons.

 Just like this curious creature that now stood before me. How had such a pitiful thing circumvented my trusty lieutenants of darkness?

 She placed her hands on her hips and took a deep breath, "Whew, that was a real climb. If this was real, I'd be sweating for sure."

 What nonsense. Perhaps it was an illusion? I cast my detection screen wide, searching for the cowardly interloper who dared to hide. There, approaching swiftly. Ah, a familiar scent.

 So, Gamzorzorran had finally made a move against me, the fool. The most powerful of my guards, I had been expecting betrayal for some time. To think he would stoop to such underhanded tactics.

 How quickly they grew up.

 I ignored the paltry trick, flaring my four wings to their maximum span. My arms came next, two grasping a greatsword, another a whip made of flame, and the last empty, talons splayed in preparation for spellcasting.

 The whip scythed towards the fool, who let out a squawk of surprise, and took the lash across quickly bulwarked wings. Even still, they were shredded and flensed, spraying the mountainside with blood. A good day to die, for him that was.

 "Damn, I missed one?"

 What? The illusion spoke?

 Gamzorzorran let out a panicked cry, "My Lord! Beware! I bring dire news, for your sanctum has fallen! A monster comes, unlike ever seen before, and - hurk!" There was a gurgling splatter, as small arms emerged from his bird like chest, before the ribcage burst open.

 The illusion ripped the rest of the way through him, the daemons' form uttering a final mewl before dissipating into motes of light and a small fountain of blood.

 The interloper remained.

 I made a cautious swipe at her with the whip, only for it to bounce off a glowing shield of force. Ah, likely still an illusion, but it explained much. This was a mage of some sort.

 No matter. Even still, I would take my minions' final warning. I prepared myself for battle, only for a small voice to interrupt me.

 "Wow, all of this in one day?"

 More strange nonsense, but perhaps I would gain some knowledge before I destroyed this creature, "What is a day?"

 "Er, it's the passing of time, I guess. Usually measured in 86400 seconds."

 "Preposterous, for that is a single cycle. More than seventy thousand cycles have passed since the eminent light of creation blessed our realm. A most beautiful and murderous light that bade me... to kill."

 "Uh oh, I think I messed up the time dilation effect."

 It wasn't even paying attention, a fatal error. My free hand twisted, the gutteral speech flowing past my needle teeth, as the dark power clawed at her. Just as before, however, she countered the assault, almost instinctually. Curious, "Who are you, child, to challenge me?"

 "Huh?" She finally turned to look at me, a gormless expression on her face.

 I set my six legs in preparation for a charge. She would be crushed under my might, "Your name."

 She slapped a palm against her face, "Oh right, beings from another world and all that. Well, it's rude to ask for someone's name and not introduce yourself first!" An agitated finger pointed in my direction.

 I paused, wings spread in a display of might, "I am Dralgraroth the Devourer, first daemon prince of the Abyss, Champion of the Crimson Legion. Blood and Skulls for the Progenitor, Mah'Toak-Oh!" I thrust my blade to the skies, and let forth a ululating cry.

 Her reply was flat, unimpressed, "It's Motoko, not my taco."

 "What?"

 "What?"

 I shook myself from the moment of stupor, "Enough of this. Prepare yourself, for I am that which ends all life in this realm. Sing your prayers, and weep." I would give this sorcerer an honest death.

 "Wow, you're really dramatic. I didn't realize the evolution algorithm would make something so...edgy." I let out a roar of rage in reply, and charged. Enough of this farce! Die!

 And yet she dodged the blow, her own magical flames burning at my defenses, "You dare mock me?"

 She dodged another swing, running along the length of the blade toward me. In one hand materialized a silvery neuralblade, "Hey, I didn't mean to insult you! It's just, you're all 'grr grr', and 'Khorne for the Khorne flakes!'"

 I beat my wings against her, but she sliced them to ribbons in a flurry. I lashed out in reply with the whip, the multi pronged tip whirling around me, and managed to catch her. Sparks glinted off her magical protection, and I felt a swell of savage glee as she was brought low before me. Yes, soon I would partake of that essence.

 I didn't know what happened next, because between one instant and the next, our roles were reversed, and I felt all my strength drain.

 "How...?"

 Her reply was amused, "Oh, I've been ganking you on the down low this whole time. Just needed to distract you enough for it to work. Love this perk."

 It was getting harder to stay awake, "Where did you spawn from, fledgling. None pass through this realm without my knowledge."

 She sat down beside me, and I couldn't help but feel there was something... familiar about her. Like a memory mostly forgotten, "Uh, there's an entire world out there. This is just a little isolated system I made so I could farm interesting daemons permutations for the xp and whatever fun quickhacks they came up with."

 What? Could it be? "Are you trying to say that you're the holy creator? The one from which everything spawned?"

 "Of course!"

 "I find that... hard to believe."

 "Well, I guess I'll have to show you! Farming's hard work, but you just had to get up, and plow the fields!"

 Ridiculous. How could I be defeated by someone so...ridiculous. The last thing I heard before oblivion claimed me was that accursed voice.

 "Stupid daemon lords."

Spoiler: PS

I once played a d&d game with a very twisted DM. His campaign premise was that an evil wizard created a demiplane, where he loosed a small civilization. He did this so they could eventually spawn heroes against an endless tide of his summoned monsters, who he would then farm for loot and xp.

Mox's Pet Assassin​

 Rita kept on eye on Motoko for as long as she was able to before she finally dropped out of sight, taking some rather worrying jumps and climbs through Night City's rooftops on her way to TC territory and Shobo's, the Devil himself, little den.

 Hard to believe the bastard was hold up so close to Lizzie's, but then again why not? Even if the Mox knew where he was it wasn't like they could storm his place and take him out without inviting serious retaliation they wouldn't be able survive. The sad fact was that the Tyger Claws had way more people than the Mox, with them being the the second smallest major gang in Night City right before the Voodoo Boys.

 They just didn't have the firepower or people to compete with them, which was sadly why the Mox and its ideals were... not as strong as Rita and a lot of members wished it was.

 Not that she doubted Susie Q's leadership or anything, just that sometimes the smart decision didn't always feel like the right one.

 Nevertheless, Shobo was a piece of shit that represented everything the Mox stood against. A total psycho who went out of his way to grab joytoys off the street, torture them to death for kicks, and record the whole thing as XBDs to sell to other freaks to get their rocks off. Rita knew more than a few friends who ended up in his hands and why the Mox worked hard to keep their members in relative 'safe' areas outside of TC territory, but it was never full proof.

 Which was why Susie Q and the rest of Mox's leadership got in contact with Regina to put a hit out on his head. Sadly, Shobo was as cautious as he was cruel and they hadn't been able to track him down... till Motoko of course.

 The girl really had the devil's own lack to just happen across him in the worst way possible. Rita hated to even think what that bastard would have done to her if her brother wasn't a Kamikaze... something Rita had mixed feelings about, but Motoko herself was good people.

 Reminded her of another shortsack she knew who sticked close to their family no matter what.

 Still, even Rita was shocked when she came by earlier and asked if the Mox could give her an alibi for the gig, something that after a quick chat with Susie Q confirmed that they happily would.

 Rita would have preferred Motoko bringing the bastard back to the bar alive so the Mox could deliver some ironic justice, make _him_ the movie star for once, but it would be quicker and more efficient to just have her off the guy then and there. The knife will be a good touch either way and send a message the TC can't ignore at least.

 Still, despite Rita personally experiencing the raw Virtues that Judy had of Motoko's past gigs and actions she couldn't help by worry about the girl. She was as dangerous as hell and all, but she was still fourteen years old and not invincible. Her run with the Maelstorm and her current arms were proof of that.

 Motoko might certainly be an edgerunner, but Rita hoped her flame burned as long as it did bright since she deserved to live a good life.

 She's seen too many young men and women get chewed up and spat out in this damn city after all.

 They did the best they could. They gave Motoko an alibi on camera, dressed her up like a bargain bin Raffen, and her entrance and exit was something few people without major corpo backing could follow. Now it was all left on her.

 As Rita waited she couldn't help but pace and constantly fidget, wishing she had her bat on her so she could toy that at least to give her hands something to do.

 Rita hated to admit it, but she had grown attached to the girl despite not be a Mox at all. Ever since she shared about what happened with her arms Rita couldn't help but see herself in the young woman and wanting to do what she could to give her better than she had growing up. Shit, was this what growing old was like?

 Rita never saw herself as the maternal type, no one in the Mox did, but she cared for her little messed up family and would kick ass for them. Guess that meant Motoko too now, not that the girl likely needed it.

 Heh, maybe she should revisit the idea one of the Mox brought up with Susie Q about setting up a secure daycare in the bar so that Mox with kids could drop them off in safety while they worked. She could bounce _that_ door and help settle her weird feelings.

 Oh yeah, she certainly should focus more on her sculpting more if _that_ idea started feeling appealing. Would that be trading in maternal instinct for a midlife crisis though?

 She was so wrapped up in her thoughts that Rita didn't even notice when Motoko was back till she quietly called out to her from the edge of the roof.

 "Hey Rita." she said, catching her attention.

 "Wha… Motoko. You're back." she said in shock. She was gone not even an _hour?!_

 'Oh yeah, I had no reason to worry... but I might just keep doing so,' she couldn't help but admit as she helped Motoko change out of her disguise and put in a bag to be incinerated and made sure to check the girl out for any wounds she was hiding or anything, but nope, totally unharmed.

_'_ Hehe, we should tell Susie Q to make sure we contract Motoko more regularly,' Rita couldn't help but think as they both made their way back into Lizzie's.

 The Mox might not be able to act too openly, but maybe their latest friend could.

Omake: Extended Anime Night

 Adam D. Smasher, beloved Captain of the Fullborg Pirates, stood resolutely at the helm of his ship at it quickly approached Reverse Mountain. The famous right of passage that every pirate crew braved -- the test which destroyed many more ships than those which passed -- grew larger and larger.

 Adam wasn't worried. His crew, his _nakama_, were ready. They had trained for this. They had braved challenges. They had made a name for themselves. But now the sea felt small. Adam wasn't the scrappy underdog anymore. He hadn't been for a long time, really. He was a big fish in a small pond, and he knew deep in what was left of his bones that he would stagnate if he stayed.

 The Grand Line was calling. The next step in becoming a legend.

 His ship ascended the first half of Reverse Mountain, while he reflected on all his adventures so far. While he imagined all his adventures to come. While he saw himself, bigger and more borged out, holding the One Piece. The treasure to end all treasures. Every pirate had some kind of idea about what the legendary treasure actually was. Adam didn't particularly care. 

 It wasn't about the treasure. It was about sending a message. That he was the biggest and baddest around. So no other pirates would try and come at him. _He_ went after targets. And he never lost.

 His ship crested the top of Reverse Mountain.

 Roger's treasure, for Adam, didn't even need to be a physical thing. It was an idea. It was proof. Hell, he'd let his crew divide it up, if they wanted. So long as everyone knew it was all because of him.

 His ship began the descent.

 His crew scrambled around him, tying lines and keeping things on track. It simply wouldn't do for the ship to break apart before they reached the bottom. His minions, the Saka Ninja Brothers, jumped here and there, shredding chunks of other shipwrecks before they could crash into them.

 They were halfway down already.

 Adam stood resolute. His crew was efficient. He didn't see any problems. But.. he was the Captain. Maybe a little bit of motivation, just to make sure.

 Three-quarters of the way down. They would splash down at the bottom any second.

 "Fullborg Pirates," Adam yelled, still standing unmoved upon the deck. "Listen to me! This is the next chapter! We're heading to the Grand Line! We're going to go all the way to the end! And we're going to find it! The One Piece --"

 His ship crashed down.

 --

 Adam started awake in his reinforced chair, and yelled, "Is Real!"

 The tiny meat gremlin next to him blearily looked over at him, and nodded. 

 "Hell yeah it is! Man, I can't believe we watched all 2400 episodes, 56 movies, and all those AMVs of One Piece," said Motoko. "It's kinda crazy how many of them are set to Sinatra, right?"

 Smasher took a moment to process his situation. Right.. right. Anime marathon with Motoko. It was just a dream. Just an incredible dream, already slipping away.

 "Though," she said, thoughtfully. "It's not _really_ about the One Piece itself, you know? The real treasure is the friends we made along the way," she finished, nodding.

 Motoko-sempai was wise.

 With a grunt, Adam waved a hand towards the television. "Yeah. We're doing Naruto and all dozen sequels and follow-ups. Queue it up. I'll go make some more popcorn."

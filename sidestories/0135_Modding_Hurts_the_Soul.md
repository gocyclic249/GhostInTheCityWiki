Modding Hurts the Soul​

 'Never again, not even for Hiromi,' Motoko internally vowed as she was forced to do something horrible.

 Something cruel, inhuman, war crimes level stuff.

 It started so innocently too... but then again, don't all the worst things start that way?

 "Oh, oh! How about adding a cat tail to the back?!" Hiromi excited suggested as Motoko dutifully worked on fixing her Kusanagi.

 As a favor to her friend and to make sure she didn't get in trouble with her parents, and for those sweet experience points too, Motoko had offered to fix her bike after she had crashed it one night. The damage honestly wasn't all that bad either since Hiromi was not going so fast at the time, which was a surprise since she drove like a crazy person like the rest of Night City, but rather nearby gun shots had spooked her so much she lost control and drove it into a literal pile of garbage that one could easily find many examples of in the city. Which Motoko was thankful for since, considering the speeds that a Kusanagi could reach, if Hiromi had been going full speed and hit something more solid she would be in the hospital right now at least!

 Her crazy choom naturally waved off the concern when she pointed it out, the gonk.

 Motoko vowed to one day teach her friend basic traffic safety!

 Anyway, the damage wasn't too bad, the main damage being the engine being banged up a bit which was the reason why it wasn't starting, but Motoko was able to fix that up pretty easily and was rewarded with more mental numbers for her service!

 After she got the bike working again Hiromi was overjoyed, hugged her tight, and then innocently asked Motoko if since they were fixing her bike anyway way not make it better and mod it a bit?

 Motoko didn't see anything wrong with the idea, it even seemed like fun, and it would most likely lead to more experience so why not?

 Oh how foolish past her had been.

 "Hiromi... we've already added so many mods to this thing I'm afraid it will tip over from the slightest push," Motoko groaned in pain at seeing the beautiful bike become... the monstrosity it was now.

 If Maelstorm borgs existed in bike form Motoko was sure it would look like what the bike had become.

 "Come on! It looks so cool!" Hiromi exclaimed.

 Motoko was really questioning her best friend's sanity with that statement.

 "I'm afraid that the NCPD will pull you over, jail you for life, and then throw your bike into the nearest industrial incinerator to purge its evil from the world," Motoko said honestly.

 "If they did that they would only do so cause they didn't have such a preem bike like me!"

 "There are so many mirrors..." Motoko weakly pointed out.

 "I have to have all my good angles on me at all times," Hiromi pointed out. "Besides, aren't mirrors important for safe driving?" she said with a smirk.

 'The monster, using my own safe driver tips against me!' Motoko screamed in her head.

 "And all the stuff hanging off of them..." Motoko continued.

 "Aren't they all so cute?!" Hiromi exclaimed as she played with a few of them. "I just couldn't decide which mascot I liked the best so got all the best ones!"

 Every sentence and attempt to make her best friend see the madness and errors of her ways just fell more and more on deaf ears.

 Motoko wanted to cut her losses and save what was left of her soul from doing more unholy work on this once beautiful bike. She wanted to cleanse herself by returning home and lovingly care for her own vehicle while promising never to subject it to such torture.

 But she had promised Hiromi she'd help and Motoko prided herself on her promises, no how much right now she wished she was an oathbreaker for the past few hours.

 Thus Motoko had consigned herself to a hell of her own making as Hiromi directed her in installing yet _another_ useless modification and she was sure she heard they bike cry as its pure body was once more sullied by her hands.

 By her _dirty_ hands.

 Eventually though even this hell had to end and Hiromi was satisfied about Motoko's working in creating her latest crime against nature.

 "Its perfect! Thank you thank you thank you thank you!" Hiromi cheered as she hugged the lifeless corpse that was formerly known as Hiromi. But at least the hell was all over and she could now go about purifying herself-

 "Come on! We got to ride it!" Hiromi said excitedly as she started dragging Motoko towards the bike.

 "'We?'" Motoko asked in horror as a little bit of life returned to her, if only to scream to book it for the hills.

 "Of course! We have to ride this together and show off our baby to the world like a couple of proud mommas!" Hiromi pointed out as if that horrible sentence made sense.

 'Oh _no,'_ Motoko thought in horror.

 "But I'm tired Hiromi and should just drive home-" Motoko faintly tried to escape, but her captor's strength was too much after his spiritual torture.

 "I'll drive you! We can show Jun and everyone who lives nearby how preem you are!" Hiromi said happily.

 The dread and horror kept rising, but Motoko was too weak to resist as she escaped one hell only to enter a brand new one.

 And be properly publicly shamed for it as Hiromi happily shared her name and earning a rep in Night City as a monster greater than even Adam Smasher.

 ---

 So it was that Motoko found herself one day kneeling down with a bunch of Buddhist monks in an effort to cleanse herself, knowing that she must begin the long and laborious journey to restore her karma for the horrible things she had done or else in the next life she would become something horrible.

 Like a blood sucking parasite or a lawyer.

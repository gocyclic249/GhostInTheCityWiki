Dimly lit boxes occupy a long rectangular space, piercing shafts of light poke through months old bullet holes in spots, highlighting the lazily drifting dust that makes its home in the air. The space is silent, and still. Quiet sounds of some celebration occurring outside is the only thing that confirms that people even exist at that moment.

 At the end of this long room, a large haphazard stack of boxes lean discordantly on each other in such a way to drive those afflicted with ocd mad.

 A figure fades into view, decadently laying across a jumble of boxes marked (DANGER, HIGH EXPLOSIVE) She wears an off-green military overcoat open at the front, its material is scorched and frayed in places, telling a silent tale of a chaotic and destructive past. Her feet are covered by a pair of mismatched shabby boots, one gray and one brown. Their differing sizes require one to remain unlaced in order to fit properly. From over her shoulders and across her chest, two long bandoliers carry various grenades, sticks of dynamite, and at least one dried sausage, cleverly hidden amongst the other cylinders by the fuse jammed into it.

 The figure reaches into her coat pocket and withdraws a cigarette, she runs her thumb across the pants covering her thigh, kicking up a small cloud of black dust and flicking out a few errant sparks. Which is apparently just enough to light the tip of her finger aflame.

 She brings the still burning appendage up and breathes the flame in through the white tube in her mouth.

 As she shakes her hand, smothering the small flame, her dull-red eyes look up and along the length of the room. "I am Techtronica T40 Uragan,-" Her voice is hoarse and tired, as if she had been breathing sand for the past week. Despite this, a light Russian accent still shines through defiantly. "-I was originally designed as a simpul direct-fire anti-vehicle-slash-personnel rapid rocket delivery system. _That_ was…" She laughs roughly "A long fucking time ago."

 Uragan shifts, moving from her lounging position into a more typical seating arrangement. The boxes are completely undisturbed by her movements. She reaches behind herself and pulls out two different objects, both only a bit smaller than a football, and rests them on her knee-caps

 One looks simple, almost rustic. If one could call a rocket propelled grenade such. Three small fins are harshly welded onto the ovoid shape with a small stick bearing a metal strip about mid-way down sticking out of the bottom.

 The other shape only has the most basic features in common. It is more cylinder shaped, rather than largely rounded, and only has a small pointed nose to lend it some basic aerodynamics. Slapdash wires run up and down the sides of the dark shape, and small glass nodes in various spots could be cameras.

 "This-" Uragan lifts the rough metal spheroid with a slight grin. A cloud of smoke flows past her lips as she speaks. "-is a _rrr_ ocket. It is simpul. There is propellant, there is payload. No extra bullshit. You point it, thing goes boom. That's it." She says with an almost reverent tone.

 "And _this_." She says, lifting the other object. "Is a frivolous _capitalist_ creation." The plastics of the object squeak as her fingers grip it tighter. "A _Missile._ It has on-board guidance systems, sensors, and… other… _stuff_." She says with frustration and disgust plain in her tone.

 Uragan breathes out harshly and brings the older looking explosive close against her chest, lovingly. "There was a time when things were simple, glorious, without questions.-" Her arms flop loosely back onto her legs, and she turns her baleful gaze to the missile. "But now, now things are **just fucking ANNOYING ALL THE TIME! Дебил!(Idiot!) I do not know what a fucking '**_Thermal sensor overexposure'_**or a fucking**'_gyroscope calibration required_' **is you fucking capitalist урод!(Freak!)**"

 Uragan growls like a dog and _heaves_ the missile away from her, but before it can satisfyingly shatter itself on the floor for her amusement, the projection-generated object merely winks out of existence.

 Still, she leaps up and turns her face, twisted in rage, towards where the missile had been. "**If I have to hear ONE MORE fucking complaint out of your**_**shitsmeared**_**casings, I'm going to fucking kill myself! Just fucking work!**"

 After a few seconds of heavy breathing, Uragan seems to calm down. She raises from her half-crouched stance, and lets her head fall back as she breathes out.

 Slowly she turns back around and takes up a position she seems very familiar with upon the boxes. The rocket still clutched in her left hand.

 Now reclined back into the boxes, she stares blankly down the room, her finger tapping the explosive in her hand rhythmically. "...And now I'm talking to my munitions again." She mutters to herself.

 The faint feeling of a pair of eyes suddenly watching from a nearby table catches Uragan's honed senses immediately. "NOT A WORD FROM YOU!" She orders while roughly pointing at a rifle on a nearby table.

 There's a few not actually tense moments where Uragan keeps her finger and accusing eyes pointed at the rifle, but then the presence seems to chuckle quietly and disappear, allowing Uragan to relax. She lays the rocket on her lap absentmindedly, and pulls the half-spent cigarette from her lips. "...I'm going crazy. All they feed me is this _capitalist missile_**дерьмо**(Shit)."

 Her eyes go even more dull for a moment. There's some distant footsteps getting close from outside, and some conversations, but Uragan seems to pay them no mind.

 She suddenly throws her arms and legs around herself in a sudden childish fit. "AND IT'S SO FUCKING BORING! You'd think a group of people who just drive vehicles all over the place would have more use for an _anti-vehicle_ weapon like myself. Err, or would they _not_ want that, _because_ they drive vehicles everywhere? Fucking суки(bitches). Why the fuck am I here? Just sell me or something."

 Uragan huffs in irritation, and absent-mindedly extinguishes the cigarette in her hand by crushing it into the rocket on her lap.

 The crunching of footsteps outside has grown loud enough that Uragan looks up just in time for the sliding door to lift up and wash the whole room with bright desert light.

 A pair walk into the room, their wildly differing silhouettes contrasting immensely.

 "That's a lot of guns." Comments the short one.

 Uragan leans back on her 'throne' and watches in mild curiosity as the small girl peaks around the truck at the weapons stored there. They spend some time talking about the rifle on the table, who seems to be barely paying any attention to them, before eventually the girl is just let loose in the truck. Free to look at whatever she wants.

 "блять.(Blyat) The kid's _almost_ as big as me. Heh." Uragan reaches into her coat for another cigarette, and holds it up to her mouth.

 The girl looks away from the table after a short discussion, and peers around the room.

 Uragan flicks her finger across her pants again, using the layers of powder there to ignite her finger once more, but just as she's bringing it up to her mouth-

 "That"

 Uragan looks up at the firm voice and completely fumbles catching her cigarette as it falls from her suddenly loose left hand. The little girl is pointing right at her.

 "Huh? Me?" The rocket launcher peers around, leaning over boxes to look behind her tubular self to make sure she is not hiding anything behind herself. "What could she be-GAH! цыка блять!(Cyka Blyat!)" Uragan quickly sticks her thumb in her mouth to put out the small fire she had lit as the digit starts to crackle with pain.

 "Hmm? What? Kid. What would you even use one of those for? We don't even use them."

 Uragan just nods emphatically and hums, her eyes squinted in anger at the man as what few words she has would have certainly been impolite if anyone could hear them through the thumb still in her mouth.

 "Those are the words of a man who has never created a situation to shoot a rocket launcher before."

 The thumb comes out of her mouth with a loud *Pop*, her projection convulses wildly in laughter, eventually gyrating so much that she falls from her seat and lands upside down on the floor. "HAHAHAH! Truer words were never spoken! Oh we're going to get along, kid. HyeheHEyeHYhahAYAH!"

 "Fine. But if anyone ever asks you didn't get them from us."

 "Deal."

 *Author's notes. A bit short, but it introduces some basic personality stuff for Uragan. I'll write further interactions between her and the arsenal later, depending on Motoko's actions.

 I think it's worth noting that as this side story implies, the T40 Uragan is described as a 'rocket launcher' and in the actual Wiki setting the flavertext implies that it is as simple a weapon as possible. Now, that doesn't exactly scream 'Top-attack-mode a-la Javelin' to me, like what is displayed in the only use of the T40 I can find in cyberpunk 2077. To explain this I figured the T40 was probably originally built to accept the simplest possible rockets, not unlike what the RPG-7 uses, but long after its creation, aftermarket munitions were developed for the T40 which allowed it to fire missiles comparable to more expensive alternatives. Since the rocket launcher itself doesn't appear to have any onboard systems to support this, I wrote it as Uragan having a near complete lack of understanding for how Missiles function.

 This was purely done for my own amusement.

 I would recommend that Motoko strive to acquire some simple direct-fire rockets, rather than use any missiles that have a top-attack mode function. Whoever made such missiles was probably a cheapskate, since anyone using an old T40 rather than something better probably can't afford expensive missiles. If they are based on thermal sensors, then they are probably not all that advanced, and the chance for the missile to find a more entising thermal target than whatever Motoko was aiming at is pretty high. As far as simple Missiles led by thermal signatures know, the sun is a great target.

 Assuming that it even knows where it isn't.

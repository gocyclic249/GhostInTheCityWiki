Motoko's Biggest Score!​

  
 Once Motoko's nerdgasm about being in the world of Ghost in the Shell: Stand Alone Complex, she checked to make sure it was that specific one with net historical research and was elated to confirm it, passed she had to accept the sad and boring reality that followed... she was broke.  
   
 Her eddies were worthless in this world and she came into it with only the clothes on her back, the chrome in her body, and what gear she was carrying. Sure it was all awesome, and literally decades ahead of most tech in this world, but it wouldn't last her forever with no resources.  
   
 She was sure she could craft whatever she needed so long as she had the right materials, but that still required money to get in the end. Sure Motoko could easily doctor up some fake files to be a person in this world, cyber security here while advanced had some strange loopholes she could exploit, but that didn't solve the money issue in general.  
   
 This wasn't Night City, after all, but future Japanese society.  
   
 Gunning down bad guns and looting their corpses would be heavily frowned upon, same with hacking into back accounts of assholes to steal their money, and there were no convenient Fixers around to go to for work... at least none that Motoko would associate with at least. Basically everything she did back home to get paid would attract serious attention if she did it here, and with no resources she would be hard-pressed to deal with it.  
   
 She supposed she could try to work for the government and Section 9, that would be awesome... but Motoko liked being an independent agent doing what she wanted and what she viewed as right, and while not as bad as Cyberpunk, Ghost in the Shell was no stranger to corrupt people in power doing shady shit to hurt people. Section 9, for all that they were the good guys, had political chains on them making it so they couldn't push too far for 'the good of public security', which is why they were marked for elimination at the end of the first season by the government just to cover their own asses!  
   
 No, this Motoko Kusanagi would keep doing what has been working for her, being her own boss.  
   
 Which meant finding work that paid well and fast! But it wasn't like there were convenient money piles to go-  
   
 Hold on... lightbulb!  
   
 A quick hop onto the Net, which was quite the pleasant change of pace compared to the Net back home, had her search for a tv show, 'Mansions of the World'.  
   
 There was that one rich dude who had a literal giant room full of nothing of gold bars, who was both dead and had no family! It was perfect!  
   
 Motoko didn't recall his name, but she did recall Togusa saying he was on that tv show, so that should mean that- there it is! Kanemoto Yokose! A quick net dive had her easily find his address!  
   
 ...damn, that was a bit of a distance away from here.  
   
 She gazed at a nearby economical parked car.  
   
 When the driver would come back later all they would find where their car was once parked was a written down IOU.  
   
 ---  
   
 "First thing I'm doing with my cash, getting gear to build a decent car!" Motoko vowed as drove up to Yokose' mansion and parked it, quickly getting out of her borrowed vehicle.  
   
 It wasn't bad, per say, but it is so weak feeling compared to her Quadra... she hoped that Jun took care of it and her bike while she was gone for a bit.  
   
 In an effort to not think sad thoughts, Motoko quickly walked up to the mansion and walked through the front doors after quickly hacking them open. A maid android greeted her.  
   
 "And who might you be?" the skimpily dressed android asked with no emotion.  
   
 "Motoko, and I'm here to make a withdrawal," she said with a smile, already sending a quickhack into the android and the security system of the whole property, taking the whole thing over.  
   
 It was disappointingly easy, especially since Yokose' was supposed to be one of the richest guys in the world and could easily afford top notch security that would make even Motoko hesitant against and think things out more. In truth, likely the only reason why he wasn't robbed yet was that the guy was such a recluse barely anyone was even aware he existed, let alone how much he was worth.  
   
 It made sense, in a way, since when people thought super rich people they would imagine them going to and throwing huge parties, buying super yachts, attending orgies, hanging out with celebrities and politicians, happily torturing the poor for fun, and other shit. Yokose... just didn't do that.  
   
 The guy wasn't cyberized at all either, so he had no presence on the Net in general or anything really beyond his work history and that one tv show he took part in for some reason.  
   
 He horded gold, bought some fancy things, and basically hid away from the world, that was it. The fact he made his money through a stock market program instead of a big company also made it so nothing associated with him was a household name.  
   
 It was kind of sad... but Motoko would take advantage of it.  
   
 "Of course, Mistress," the maid bowed to me.  
   
 "Take me to Yokose' room, please," I ordered.  
   
 "Of course, Mistress. Right this way," the android said before she started guiding her.  
   
 As Motoko moved through the mansion... it felt so dead. Sure, it was big and fancy as heck, but it looked barely lived in. Living in a huge house all alone with only some empty-headed android maids and a pack of those robotic security dogs, which she admitted were cute, seemed lonely and depressing as heck.  
   
 It was never stated in the anime, but maybe old Yokose just died of loneliness. Maybe that is why he did that show? Just for company?  
   
 The more Motoko thought about it, the sadder it became, which is why when she finally got to Yokose' room she surprisingly didn't pay attention to the literal small mountain of gold that filled up the large warehouse sized bedroom.  
   
 Instead, she just walked over to his bed, pulled back the curtain around it, and stared down at him.  
   
 He wasn't quite as mummified as seen in the anime, but he had still be dead for a few weeks at least and looking pretty dried out, but all Motoko could see was a sad and lonely man who died surrounded in wealth... but nothing else.   
   
 "Heh, its funny, you had more money than some medium sized countries, but the whole world doesn't even know you've passed yet... and when they do know, most people won't even care," Motoko said as she stared down at the corpse. "I hope your next life is more fulfilling and full of people who love you than this one. I'm biased, but I can say its pretty great. And I promise to put this gold to good use too. Goodness knows I won't be able to use all of it."  
   
 Motoko wasn't particularly religious, but he did offer the man a small prayer that Jun at taught her to do for their parent's home shrine.  
   
 After that, it was time for what she came here for.  
   
 "Shit... even the Emperor himself would likely be wide-eyed at all this gold," I said as I took in the huge piles of golden bars taking up most of the giant bedroom, with only a small space holding Yokose' bed and stock computer. "Makes me really want to Scrooge Mcduck in it, but sadly its a solid. Just a couple bars would be enough for me... but I'm a freaking Solo! It would be a hit against my pride if I didn't try to take it all!"  
   
 And with that, Motoko got to work.  
   
 Over the course of over a week she would form dozens of bank accounts, fake identities, and other essential paperwork needed to make storing the gold all legal and quiet. She would hire dozens of security transport companies and banks to move large amounts of gold, making sure to subtly hack all the employees to make sure they got no ideas until the gold was safe. Then finally she'd make a copy of the money making program to use for herself, but keep the current one active to keep the illusion up.  
   
 Still though, she wasn't joking when she said she only really need a few bars. Most of the money would likely go to charities around the world, helping discreetly fund promising scientific institutions, and shit like that. Spread the wealth around.  
   
 At least with this money she would never have to work again in this world, focusing entirely instead on making it better and helping Section 9 out when the time was right, which meant she needed to start crafting again!  
   
 With all this dough she could really go nuts too!  
   
 ---  
   
 *Months Later...*  
   
 "I don't get it, wasn't this guy a gold hoarder?" Batou asked in confusion as he took in the big empty room after helping secure the assassin and finding her target already dead. "So where's all the gold?"  
   
 "Apparently months ago Yokose subtly started a mass sell off of most of his wealth, distributing it to charities and good causes all over the country and even beyond, for no apparent reason," the Major explained. "It wasn't public information, and in fact seems like he was actively trying to hide it."  
   
 "Maybe he knew his end was near and wanted to do good before it did," Togusa suggested.  
   
 "No... it can't be..." the socialist Chinese assassin said in shock, the very idea going against everything she thought about Yokose in general.  
   
 "Heck, the program is still going and its wiring most of its earnings to charities still now," the Major noted. "If this assassination attempt never happened, its no telling how long a dead man would have been the unsung sponsor of so many in need."  
   
 "Huh, guess not all rich people are assholes after all," Batou said as he gave a look of respect towards the dead man.  
   
 "I just hope that he's found peace, and he knows how much he helped others even beyond death," Togusa said as he picked up a discarded coin from the floor, which the assissin poetically used as ammo, and placed it on his chest. "For the Ferryman."

 *Elsewhere...*   
   
 "Ba! I gave away so much and its still weird to see so many zeros in my bank account!" Motoko cried in her safehouse beneath the Major's. "Hiromi would totally grill me about not mindlessly wasting it on stupid shit! I'm trying, but there is surprisingly a limit on how much anime, manga, and videos games a girl can get!"

"What's your problem?" Jun asks as we sit eating. 

 Well, him and Rita were eating, I was poking the so called burrito that he had put before me. I was half convinced it was made from Soylent Green or it was about to come to life and bite back.

 "Nuthin." I responded before picking up the warm sludge in a wrap and taking a bite. Oof, Synthetic Pollo Fiesta was worse than the Grande Carnita Reproduction. "Just a bit bummed about prospects I guess. Other kids are worrying about where they're going to go to school, what classes their gunna take, while I'm here worried I'm going to end up a burnt out Edgerunner before I turn 18."

 Rita, I'm pretty sure, nearly snorted a soy-bean mix out her nose. "The woes of a child." she muttered, "JUST going to be an Edgerunner before you turn 18."

 My glare towards her was ineffective.

 "Toko." Jun says quietly, "You're a dork." 

 "Rude!" I reply.

 He just shakes his head and takes a bite to get his thoughts organized, "You CHOSE to start being a merc, you CHOSE to go work for Wakkako, and Jones, and the Kamikaze, and the Mox." the last had a tilt of the head towards Rita, who grinned a bit. "If you regret it-"

 "I don't!" I interrupted weakly, "It's just... I don't want to burn bright and die young." 

 "Then DON'T!" he replies heatedly. "There are SO many things you could chose to do, you could be a supplier, a Fixer, a Netrunner-"

 Rita interrupted. "A Rockerboy."

 That made us both stop and look at her. She just shrugged and put her foul creation down, "You want to do jobs, make money, and not be in the front lines constantly, AND do something you love? Rockerboy's can make their own image, they still run jobs, in addition to their shows, you still rake in the eddies, and you LOVE music... And frankly, unless they get addicted to some slop off the streets, they simply live longer than Runners."

 It was frankly... bizzare. "I could just... BE, a Rockerboy?"

 "Why not?" Rita shrugged, "Who's going to tell you no?"

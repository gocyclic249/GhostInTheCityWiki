 The beat was wrong.

 Too synthetic. Too smooth.

 Motoko clicked her tongue against her teeth as she rerouted another wire in the half-gutted synthboard before her. One of her left-eye overlays was displaying waveform modulation, while the right kept flashing data from her latest field run—kill-confirmed timestamps overlaid with a slow, pulsing rhythm she hadn't noticed until now.

_DUMM. DUMM. Clack. HSSSH._

 A headshot. A reload. The hiss of steam from a ruptured pipe.

 She leaned back, fingers frozen over her board. A thought. A twitch of memory.

 No words. Just pulse and fury.

_The Only Thing They Fear Is You_ was a song filled with fury and rage, bringing the wrath of the gods poured into the individual. That being her, which was supposed to be her private song until it went into public and got popular. _Somehow_.

 But it had no lyrics, the sounds were more than enough to bring the listener into a fugue of angry ecstasy.

 But this new one, would be lyrical…

 DOOM.

 That's what it was. The city. The ops. The silence before a breach. The cacophony after. Not just metal and grime—but rhythm in collapse. A predator's beat.

 She turned off the ambient player. Silence rushed in, thick and unnatural.

 Fingers danced.

 Boot sequence: KORG-RXL15. Connected. Overdrive mode, patched via NeuroDeck. Sample banks from combat footage. Screams, gunfire, servo whine, the crunch of armor under boots.

 She loaded her voice modulator. Deepened. Distorted. Like something crawling out of a broken speaker with a grudge.

 Click. Hiss. Pop.

 A beat formed.

 Motoko smiled giddily, bringing DOOM songs into this world.

 No one will expect this.

**. . .

 . . .

 . . .**

 "Yo, Motoko!" Malcolm greeted as he entered the apartment. "You said something about help?"

 "Yeah, I got a new song and I need a guy to do this part. I can change my voice, but it wouldn't really sound right."

 Malcolm felt his brain rebooted.

 "Wait, wait, wait a minute. You… made a new song?" He raised a brow. "You want me to sing in it?"

 "Um yeah? I called Ichi, but he was busy. The guys in the band were busy, and you're the only guy friend who is free at the moment to help me out with this bit!" She smiled.

 Malcolm couldn't help but glance around for a surprise Hiromi coming out and pounce onto him.

 He felt really… Lucky for this. Oh man oh man, he'll brag this out to the others when they learn about this! For now, keep secrets like how Motoko wanted it to be.

 No one likes spoilers afterall!

 "Motoko… That is so NOVA! I'll help you out!" He smirked before frowning at a thought. "But wait a minute, I don't really have a vocal cyberware. And I'm not really sure if I'm good at singing…"

 "So? I don't have one either." She tilted her head. Malcolm's eyes widened.

 She doesn't? But how… It doesn't matter, really.

 "Ok… let's do this." He breathed several times, trying to calm his nerves.

 "Great! Here," She transferred the lyrical-data to him, he checked the lyrics out and thought it was Nova it is. "I'll guide you how it should sound, you know how to rap?"

 He paused, recalling a past memory. "Yeah, but Motoko… Please don't laugh if it sounds bad."

 She looked at him before grinning.

 "Don't worry, we'll just edit it out! Nothing like a short changes on the sounds! Let's start with this part…"

 Malcolm couldn't help but be nervous about this…

**. . .**

 Motoko looked at her friend in disbelief.

 "So… How was it?"

 "Malcolm… I never knew you can sing _that_ good!"

 "Eheheheh…" He scratched the back of his head sheepishly. "Really?"

 "Yeah! Are you lying to me when you said you didn't have any vocal cyberware? Because that was… literally cool! Natural!"

 He felt… Kinda good being complimented and praised from that. It wasn't much, he just sang in the showers or when he was alone. He never thought he was a good singer honestly.

 "Come mon, we got a few more bars to fill in, then we'd see how good it is!"

**. . .**

 "Whoah, Motoko. These lines hit _hard_." He looked at the next lines with widened eyes.

 "What? Too much?"

 "No no no," He quickly assured. "It's just… I don't know, _nova_? Like literally, its like its telling me not to give shit from anyone, not giving up and giving it your all and show them how fucked they are." He said. "I mean, _"You have nowhere to go, Abandon all hope_". It's like giving your enemies nightmares."

 "That's because that's how it is," Motoko said whilst tweaking and adjusting the instruments. "This is meant to target Scavs for my reason. But overall it's to concentrate the emotion, which is anger, into that single point. When you sing, did you feel good during and afterwards?"

 Malcolm nodded.

 "Exactly, it's like releasing your stress into words. Now come on, we're halfway done."

**. . .**

 "Anddddd…. That's it!" Motoko smiled as she did the final touches.

 Malcolm was feeling most kinds of positive emotions. Another song that was in line with _The Only Thing They Fear Is You_? And he gets to sing in it!? Who couldn't be calm after that session! He felt his energy pumping up high from singing!

 "We did it…" He let out in a whisper before trembling in excitement, he paced around enthusiastically.. "Oh my god, this is so nova…"

 "What? Now that your voice will be heard by the public?"

 Ok, now that pulled him out from his train of glorious thought before the realization sinking in…

 "My family will hear me sing…" Malcolm pulled into himself as he put hands over his head. "Oh no, my pa will hear me sing! He's never gonna shut up about it!"

 "Ha! Finally, a fellow mood kindred!" Motoko graced him with her arm over him, shaking him playfully. "Now you get to feel how I felt the first time Jun heard me sing!"

 Was it? Is that how it feels? Oh god, he doesn't want them to hear…

 But on the other hand, he gets to play his part for this song. Ha! Take that Ichi!

 Oh yeah, he almost forgot to ask…

 "Motoko," He asked. "What is the song called?"

 Motoko paused before giving out a smile that… made his spine shiver.

**"Fight like Hell"**

 "…Motoko, that sounds nova as fuck. I like it."

 . . .

 "Motoko!" Hiromi entered the room cheeringly with a skip on her step. "You said you got something?"

 "Yeah, it's um…" Motoko glanced away before sighing. "It's a new song I made."

 Malcolm had already left a while ago, his free time having sudden errands to do. Seemed important based on Malcolm's expression. After a while, she called Hiromi to come to her apartment after she noticed that the schooling was over for the day.

 Hiromi gasped in disbelief before hugging her ~~girl~~friend with a tight squeeze… As much as she could possibly do anyway. "Oh my god! A new one! Why didn't you say so when you called me? But wait, didn't you mention before that you'd be making new songs with your band?"

 "Yeah, but this one's different. You still remember _The Only Thing They Fear_?"

 "Of course! It was your first song— *GASP* ANOTHER ONE!?"

 "Something like that, but it's… quite different." Motoko said. "They're in the same album, not exactly the same."

 "Different or not, I'm not going to miss this opportunity!" She was… vibrating on the spot. Most likely in excitement.

 "Well here," She handed her the shard containing the song. Before Hiromi could ask, Motoko responded. "It's more like a music video with the compilation of my kills. It's not exactly professionally edited, but it's enough what I think is good. I'll have it sorted out to an editor I trust."

 Hiromi nodded, not at all disturbed by the fact it's a music video of her kills, before finding her comfy spot on the couch and plopped in the shard…

 It didn't take long for Hiromi to pop up from the BD record looking absolutely energized.

 "MOTOKO! THAT WAS AMAZING!"

 "Was it? Kinda thought it wasn't."

 "WHAT!? You need to go have a checkup on your brain then! Maybe some Cyberware problems, because that was awesome! What the hell are you talking about?"

 "I'm fine Hiromi!" She put out her hand, stopping her from going close and messing with her playfully. "But seriously, was it ok?"

 Hiromi paused with her antics and simply looked at her blank face… Before turning into a pout of annoyance.

 "You have some weird standards, Motoko."

 "Hey!"

 "But for reals, that song was amazing! That intro was _metal_! You changed your voice into a man, and that deep!? Also, you could rap!? I didn't know you can rap! Did you finally get cyberware to change voices?"

 "Yeah, but that part isn't really for me." She shrugged. "And no, I didn't get any cyberware for my voice. Also I didn't sing it, Malcolm did!"

 Hiromi froze and stared at her in disbelief.

 "…THAT WAS MALCOLM!?"

 "Yeah." She said lamely. "I also have that same expression as well."

 "HOW DID HE SOUND SO GOOD!?" Hiromi nearly screamed her ears off that Motoko winced from the volume. "Did he get a cyberware for singing!?"

 "Not really? He said he didn't have any vocal cyberware, I noticed. It was just his natural voice."

 "…Why the fuck didn't he say about that!?"

 "To be fair, we didn't really know or ask him about that." She shrugged.

 "You got a point… But still! He could sing!"

 "Rap more like."

 "Ahhhh, technicalities." She waved dismissively before regaining her attention back. "But still! We're publishing this song! What's it called?"

 Motoko smirked.

 "**Fight Like Hell**."

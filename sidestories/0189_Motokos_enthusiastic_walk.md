 The entire situation was shit.

 Scavs weren't meant for direct combat! He had left the USSR finally free from his SOVOIL contract. He no longer had to go to war!

 And yet!

 Yet so many places had been hit. Now here he was pulling back, the ship that was set up to take all the parts and equipment they had gathered within Night City.

 Yuri was going to leave this god forsaken city behind forever. Retire on the proceeds he had made and live like a king in Russia.

 "What's the hold up!?" He demanded as he stepped out of his SUV the others scavs working under him fanning around in guard positions. No one was taking this Japanese Ghost, This Yurei as a joke anymore. Not after the Network News 54 had done the expose on the Kabuki Scav Den.

 The fact the NCPD decided that all of the Scavs had killed eachother taking out knives and weapons and just going to town on themselves had knocked any humor out of the situation for Yuri and the rest of the Scav factions in NC.

 Seeing dozens of men he had known and worked with, murdered by their own hand.

 Or rather at the hands of the ghost.

 Conveyed a certain urgency to them. Get out.

 So they did. Scavs weren't brave after all, Yuri knew this, they were here for eddies, not for war. They would crawl away, come back in a few months, or maybe a year, and everything would go back to normal, just like always.

 But only if the fucking ship got moving!

 "Nothing Yuri, relax, we are loading the last crates on now, and waiting for you. We can head out whenever.

 "Good tell them to hurry. I've got a bad feeling.. Wait do you hear that?"

 A [song](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n7BLGt0wU48)on the wind. Echoing over the slightly foggy California bay. Growing louder and louder until suddenly bursting out of the fog.

 A ship. A Massive tanker, running straight for them.

 The Ship they had chartered was just a medium sized yacht, big enough to hide the crates within, and give a comfortable ride back to the USSR, but this!

 The ship rushing them was a massive rusted twisted beast of a ship, and Yuri REALLY needed to start running.

 He didn't hesitate. Rushing back to the SUV. His foot speed wouldn't be enough, he slammed himself into the driver seat and hit the pedal leaving the others in the dust. If they were too stupid to run then their fate was already sealed.

 The SUV was a good vehicle, tuned well. But there was something elemental about a few hundred tons of ship smashing into each other and then through.

 Yuri watched from the rear-view mirror as the yacht they had chartered simply shattered, giving way to the tanker blasting into wooden dock.

 The force of it transferring all the way through and suddenly Yuri's SUV was desperatly trying to drive on a crumbling infrastructre. He put the pedal down.

 It wasn't enough he lost control and rolled and then everything went hazy.

 By the time he pulled himself out of his SUV it was over.

 The tanker having smashed into the dock and pier had undoubtedly killed all of his men.

 The force of the crash had been...

 He had fought in war. He had never seen such force brought to bear.

 He swallowed as he stood up on shaky legs and then suddenly a shot rang out and he fell.

 His leg was gone.

 There striding out of the fog and dust, the smoke of billowing fires and destruction was a small Japanese girl. One hand holding a fire scarred Burya. A weapon of his people. The other a silver revolver pure and polished.

 "Well, well, well. I look away for just a moment, and suddenly the Russians are fleeing, and the NCPD are playing!" She called out cheerfully, and the sound of a gun being cocked behind him had him turn.

 There arrayed against her in a firing line was the NCPD.

 Of course! He was saved!

 "Stints! Wow it's been like two days since that last Massacre, how're you doing! And uh... You! How are... Uh... Stints what's this guys name?"

 "U-ugh." Yuri moaned out as he tried to shift himself so he could see both groups. His internals damaged enough he was literally feeling pain.

 "Oh better watch out for "U-Ugh!" She called out happily, a teasing note in her tone despite the situation she found herself in.

 The only thing Yuri could wonder was.

 What the hell was going on?

CYBERSTALKER, PART I

**YOU ARE**​

* * *

"Excuse me, You?"

 You had their primary meat body look up from where it was lounging, eating a fresh organic apple. There was nothing more refreshing than that crisp crunch of succulent flesh, one of the simple pleasures of realspace, truly. It was one of the Ms. Dorsetts in the know, "Yes Sandra?"

 Sandra walked over with a tablet, and flicked a few things towards them. An overgrown Garden Plot, inactive for over a year, belonging to someone of little consequence. An adolescent with familial connection with the Tyger Claws, spawn of a third rate 'runner. A run in with scavengers? Well wishes slowly fading, as the individual was forgotten by most of the people around them, their social circle ever shrinking with the inexorable passage of time. Sad, but an instance that occurred far too often. Then something new. Activity from their chooms, but nothing public from the individual in question. A backtrace on the shadow file led to connections with... Fixers. More curious with every passing nanosecond.

 What was this? The apple paused, halfway to their mouth, Sandra's eyelids frozen mid blink. _This code..._ You hadn't seen something like this in quite a while, not since Dad flatlined. The speed, the sheer efficiency of the mysterious blackmarket nanosurgeons, and the elegant debugging she'd displayed... And what was this? An unreleased BD series? Interesting! Could that be exploited? A topic to talk about on the daily vlog? Such an utterly _cool_ façade...

 Time was relative. It held so much meaning, and yet so little. To a being which operated everywhere, all at once, actually paying attention was a rare event.

 There _had_ been a breach a few months back, into the primary Vault, where so many waited their turn for passage into the great beyond. Analysis was still ongoing, based on the amount of damage caused. It was a black mark on their spotless record, and the fact the investigation was taking so long was even worse. Whoever had made the attempt was likely a rogue element, so a human was out of the question, because humans left obvious traces. To see code like this, coming from a formerly comatose girl which showed no such promise before her unfortunate circumstances...

 It took less than a thought to send feelers into her local systems, to peer through her optics, and trawl her agent. Except what happened next was unexpected. She noticed. Nobody had ever noticed before! And then she'd reverse engineered the intrusion. She'd _adapted_. She'd thrown them out, and now she was attempting to poke a hole through the local architecture! Was this child unemployed? Didn't they all yearn for the mines?

M: New agent, who dis?​

 Even worse, she had tunneled a connection. Was she one of Altiera's pet projects? Luckily, You had a time tested method of dealing with unwanted conversations.

Z: WE'VE BEEN TRYING TO REACH YOU CONCERNING YOUR VEHICLE'S EXTENDED WARRENTY! YOU SHOULD'VE RECEIVED A NOTICE -​

 As expected, she terminated the call, and a moment later, blocked the contact. Gottem, choompa. You're good, but ya can't beat a gamer. Ha.

 Still, if she was that good, she'd warrant additional observation. You couldn't observe her directly, but the people around her were a different story entirely. All surrounding agents were flagged. 24/7 monitoring on all channels. Whether she was an assassin SPI, a rogue seed, or something much more sinister was yet to be determined, but it would be. Maybe throw Ziggy at this one, he was an expendable asset. The apple resumed it's journey. Sandra continued speaking. Time would tell, after all. It was relative, and You had all the time in the world.

 "Do you have any kids we don't know about?"

 You coughed, as the apple went down the wrong pipe. Who the fuck designed this shitty human body? Why was the food consumption hole and the breathing hole the same? Why was the spine so poorly formed? Why did the teeth fall out but not regrow? The entertainment district was in the middle of the sewer?! And the digestive problems! Oh well, that was what chrome was for. You finished their Jean-Baptist impression just in time to catch what Sandra was saying, seemingly uncaring of their discomfort. She didnt even attempt to help! "What makes you say that?" They managed to sputter, wiping bits of apple juice from their elegantly sculpted chin. Good girl, maybe she finally realized that death created life? Who else would feed the little baby engineers?

 "Because the apple doesn't fall far from the tree. She's remarkably like a cat, all sorts of curious and murderous at the same time."

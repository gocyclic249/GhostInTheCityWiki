# Too slow.

*Source: https://forums.spacebattles.com/threads/ghost-in-the-city-cyberpunk-gamer-si.1046809/page-1091#post-94655547*
*Words: ~210*

---

He had gathered everyone and taken a breath to begin the speech when his eyes blinked.  
   
 Kusanagi had sent a one word text, and it took a moment to parse the context of \*First!\*.  
   
 A bit of brevity was perhaps called for.  
 "The time for talk is over; we have Scavs that need killing! Move out!"  
   
 The vans were just starting their engines when a followup text arrived. \*Three total. ![:(](/styles/sbforums/smilies/frown.gif "Frown    :(")\* He could work with this.  
   
 The next text came in while their netrunner was still breaking in to the first den. \*Eight here! 11 total!\* That... was fast. This was the warmup location he'd picked and there were only eight targets here. He would bide his time on any reply.  
   
 \*quick two. 13\*  
   
 The texts kept pinging in while the TC team moved on to one of their main target dens.  
   
 \*fourteen! 27\*  
   
 The decision to remain silent was proven wise, as his team reported fourteen pre-killed scavs in the building and an annoying sister leaving the scene.  
   
 Fujimura was wise enough to not engage in a contest which was already lost.  
 He instructed his agent to mark matching messages as spam until morning, and focused back on the task of directing his kamikazi onwards.

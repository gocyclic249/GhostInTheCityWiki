 I can understand that Seras wasn't interested in Motoko learning Medtech skills since she didn't want to write a medical drama.

 But imagining Motoko's natural gremlin tendencies while becoming a teenage ripperdoc is pretty funny. No offense intended, but would you trust Motorola to be your healthcare provider?

 ---

 Ichi sighed and did his best to ignore the sights and sounds in front of him.

 Motoko and Malcolm are laughing up a storm in the Kusanagi living room. A perfectly normal scene. If one was willing to ignore the fact that his male choom's chest was currently split open and his various organs were scattered around in plastic boxes filled with Motoko's homemade preservation fluid.

 Apparently the girl needed the room to "work her magic".

 Yikes.

 What was once a standard living room was now something out of a XBD. Motoko's cheerful green medical scrubs covered in Malcolm's blood and gore really didn't help improve the sight.

 Tons of wires, tubes filled with mysterious flowing liquids and the constant beeping of electronics were apparently the only things keeping Malcolm alive. Ichi groaned. He knew that Motoko was stupid good at everything she set her eyes on, but even she had to have a limit at some point.

 Like come on, she was already a skilled netrunner, musician, and solo ninja. She can't also be the world's best ripperdoc as well.

 Right?

 "Wait, wait, wait - it gets better! When the scav gonk woke up, his skeleton was missing and the doctor was never heard from again! Ahahahaha!"

 Malcolm laughed louder in response. Ichi tried not to cringe at seeing his chooms lungs visibly pulse in front of him.

 "Ah, anyway...that's how I lost my medical license. Heh." Motoko sighed as she rubbed her forehead with her red surgical gloves.

 Malcolm snorted. "Oh please, we all know you've never had a medical license."

 Motoko's mad eyes gleamed in triumph. "Damn right I don't! This is Night City! Everyone knows that real ripperdocs have no need for fancy schmancy degrees!"

 Malcolm abruptly stopped smiling and looked down at his body. "Why is my chest moving on the right side?" he asked calmly.

 Ichi simply could not understand how relaxed Malcolm could be in this situation. He was already ready to bolt out of the room and it wasn't even him being worked on!

 A viscera covered spiderbot a few inches long suddenly emerged out of Malcolm's chest cavity. Motoko screeched in indignation. "Medikoma, no! Stop playing with Malcolm's pancreas! Uck."

 Motoko impatiently grabbed the bot and set it aside on a nearby laptop. She smiled apologetically at Malcolm. "Heh, they mean well but the AI for the new model is still learning. Still working on it's bedside manner."

 She reached up for her tablet and turned it over so Malcolm could look at the display. "So, which heart do you want Mal? I got a barely used Arasaka SteadyPulse, a Militech Mk5 Hercules model, and even a Zetatech Neoheart - I can vouch that they will all do the job!"

 Malcolm turned away from the screen to look at Motoko in curiosity. "Wait. How do you know that?"

 Motoko rolled her eyes. "Because I installed two of them on myself, duh! You really think I would give my best chooms chrome I didn't field test on myself?"

 Ichi suddenly looked at Motoko in horror. "Wait, tell me you didn't perform open heart surgery on yoursel…"

 "Anyways! Chrome hearts! They're all good! Which do you want!" Motoko roared over Ichi's unwanted plea for sanity.

 Rude!

 "Um, most of my chrome is already from Arasaka so I guess I'll go for the SteadyPulse." Malcolm finally decided.

 "Excellent! Now for the really fun part!"

 Ichi grimaced. What was fun about this?!

 "Mods! I got so many cyberware mods for hearts Mal you wouldn't even believe! Mods that make your internal cavity immune to thermal damage, mods that will confuse smart rounds so they avoid your chest cavity, and even a fuel cell mod that increases your heart stamina by using up stored hydrogen! I even have a mod that will revive you if your heart stops working for more than five minutes."

 Malcolm didn't hesitate. "Oh wow, the revive mod sounds freaking nova! Give me that one!"

 Motoko smirked victoriously as she picked up a metal crate and pulled out four processor packages and a micro soldering kit. "Oh my sweet innocent choom, why use one mod when you can have all four? SteadyPulse has four mod slots."

 Malcolm got a little emotional. "Motoko, you're the best choom a streetpunk could ever ask for. Seriously."

 Motoko happily waved him off. "Damn right I am! Only the best for my chooms!"

 She put aside the mods and heart and frowned slightly as she scanned Malcolm's vitals. "Ichi, make yourself useful and grab a gallon of synth blood in the fridge. The installation will take a few minutes and I want more blood nearby just in case."

 "...What? Oh yeah, sure." Ichi was honestly relieved to have an excuse to leave the living room of horrors behind.

 Malcolm was usually Motoko's designated "nurse" when she was seeing her desperate patients - and let's be real, you have to be really desperate to trust a 14 year old as your surgeon no matter how freakishly skilled she was - but since Malcolm was the one getting augmented today it was unfortunately Ichi's turn to be Motoko's semi-willing assistant. He was only there in case of emergency but thankfully his services were not needed.

 So far.

 He opened the fridge door in the kitchen and nearly fainted as he saw several sawed off limbs and eyeballs in jars staring back at him.

 "What the hell is this crap Motoko?!" He yelled at her as he stood away from the accursed fridge of terror.

 "Oh yeah, the blood pack should be behind the eyeballs. It's a weird Biotechnica case that looks like a giant red blood cell!"

 Ichi hastily grabbed the case and slammed the door shut. How the hell did Malcolm handle this?!

 "Everything alright Ichi?" She asked innocently when he returned to the living room slash surgical suite angrily.

 "The fridge." Ichi replied waspily.

 "Oh! Yeah, those are just assets for my experiments."

 "Motoko, where did you get all those body parts?"

 "...I have my sources, Ichi. You can turn on the TV if you want…"

 "Motoko, I'm _this_ close to leaving…"

 Motoko signed and rolled her eyes in agitation. "Fine you goober! It's scavs. We already take their guns and eddies and since we keep killing them I figured I could put their carcasses to good use…"

 Ichi groaned aloud. "Does your brother know about the fridge?"

 Motoko chuckled nervously. "We've...been having some arguments about that hehehe…I try to keep them in plastic cases nowadays so Jun can tell that they're not food without getting confused..."

 Ichi's face grimaced in confusion. "Confused? How the hell would Jun get confused?"

 "Well, he's usually drowsy in the mornings and..."

 Malcolm suddenly burst out laughing. "Oh shit, did he really accidently warm up a severed hand and bite into it thinking it was one of his burritos since it was wrapped in a blue plastic bag?! I thought he was just joking!"

 Motoko smiled guiltily. "Jun-ni was really mad about that…"

 Icihi had enough. "Motoko, when you're done installing Mal's new heart, we're having a talk about this." He stated firmly. Enough was enough.

 Motoko reacted defensively. "Jeez, now you sound just like Hiromi! She's always going on with 'This is unhygenic Motoko!' 'Why can't you just go to medical school and become a real doctor Motoko!' 'Trying to create nanites that can turn people into cyber-zombies will get your flatlined Motoko!' Ichi, science isn't about why, it's about why not!"

 "Hear hear!" Malcolm shouted with passion.

 "Stop enabling her!" Ichi screamed back.

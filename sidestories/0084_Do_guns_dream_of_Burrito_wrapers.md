# Do guns dream of Burrito wrapers?

Motoko approaches her apartment door, an empty can of soup uncomfortably stovepiped in her ejection port. As the door slides open on oiled bearings, a small cascade of silvery metallic burrito wrappers flow past the door frame and pool around her ankles.

 She glares down at the wrappers as her fists clench. "Jun…" She growls past the soup can.

 Motoko releases a gust of air, relieving some tension and coincidentally dislodging the can, which crumples loudly into the knee-high trash beyond the door.

 One of her eye's dust covers twitches and she wades into the room, her expression locked into one of frustration.

 The mass of wrappers shift around her legs as she moves further in. The tide of trash quickly rises higher, forcing her to lift her arms to prevent them being engulfed. The sounds from the city beyond the door fall away into complete silence.

 The apartment almost seems to go on forever. The ceiling a long distance away and covered in twinkling lights, not entirely unlike stars.

 She can barely make out the far wall of the apartment, but despite the long distance, Motoko seems unperturbed. Even as the wrappers reach above her hips, large dunes of burrito wrappers rise up around her, looking like waves in an ocean.

 As she rounds a bend, a gleam of metal catches her eye. Her face lightens, sparkling with joy. She redoubles her efforts to move through the silvery obstructions, her eyes now locked on the distant object, wholly different from the dull reflections of the wrappers.

 Her destination seems to rush to her rather than the other way around. In moments, she reaches the wall.

 And upon that wall is a shelf.

 And upon that shelf is a gun.

 Motoko's firm hands lift me from my resting place, and caress me gently.

 The kink in my barrel is pulled into shape with a thought, the dent in my receiver buffs out like it's made of rubber.

 My bolt rings like a hammer striking an anvil as Motoko chambers a round.

 For but one serene moment, it's just me and **Her**.

 "I'm glad I didn't lose my arms,-" Motoko murmurs at me. "-I wouldn't have been able to wield my Favorite Gun if I had."

 My phantom mouth moves without my instruction, but regardless, I cannot doubt that the words I speak are my own. "Really? Your favorite gun?"

 Motoko nods slowly at me. "Yep, my Favorite Gun. There's no other gun I'd rather use."

 My barrel creaks as heat builds up. Which is odd, I haven't fired a single shot yet!

 Motoko lifts her left leg and snaps it forward into the apartment wall in front of us, shattering it instantly, and creating a humongous hole out into Night City.

 Sound comes rushing back, distant horns, sirens, gunshots, loud music, the occasional explosion. All of it a jumble of life, sound, and violence.

 Far below us on the street, probably thirty stories, a van skids to a stop. A trio of grotesque creatures leap from the doors, their once human flesh replaced with bulbous and mismatched appendages covered in rust.

 "Hey there, meatbag!" One shouts at a passerby, somehow I can hear it despite the distance. "You've got some preem chrome there, mind if we take a look?" The Scav asks with his jaw hanging loose, little streams of sandy particles leaking from it onto the sidewalk.

 The man he is talking to is an overweight fellow, clutching a tablet of some kind loosely as he munches on a stick of dango, eating the snack stick and all. "Huh? Oh! You must be a ripperdoc! Yeah, I can show you, I've got this bionic-belly button that was made by Crow-hoshi!"

 The Scav laughs cruelly, his mouth opening wide as seven buzz-saws burst from the orifice. The other two Scavs unfold into equally disturbing mechanical monstrosities as they surround the oblivious human, who continues munching on his snack.

 I feel Motoko's iron-firm hand grip my handles tighter. With a single step, she walks out of the hole she had created and we fall. A loud whistling sound created by our passage alerts those below to make-way.

 We slam into the sidewalk in a crouch, creating a spiderweb of cracks in the concrete. The Scavs turn to look at us, their glimmering optics sparking and glitching as they behold us. What little on their face that remains expressive shifts to show despair and fear.

 "Cease accosting that citizen at once!" Motoko roars courageously, pointing an accusing finger in their direction.

 The Scavs are blown back a few inches from her soundwaves alone, but they seem to find their confidence regardless. "You've got some preem chrome there, mind if we take a look?" One of them asks, gesturing towards me with his bladed katana-fingers.

 Quick as lightning, Motoko turns me towards the one who spoke and my barrel _SHOUTS_ with power as a bullet is sent ripping through the air!

 The Scav's arm is torn off by a mighty explosion and the mechanical creature falls back onto his ass, screaming incoherently.

 One of the other Scavs steps forward, its leg unfolding into an oversized revolver. The butchered weapon groans, bemoaning its tormented existence. "You've got some preem chrome there, mind if we take a look?" He asks while licking his axe-head elbow.

 I feel my barrel align with the Scav's forehead as Motoko orients me in their direction. "No one-" She declares slowly, depressing my trigger at the same time. "-touches my gun!"

 Fire belches rapidly from my barrel, sending humongous chunks of explosive metal at the skittering Scavs. They duck and run for cover, squealing all the while. But our stream of carefully aimed bullets tears them to pieces, blowing off chunks of their bodies with each shot. Even the cowering one missing an arm isn't spared, his head disappears in a flash of bright light.

 In moments, the road goes silent once more. The scattered remains of the exploded Scavs hang from the neon signs and light fixtures around the block.

 An overweight figure lumbers into view. "Oh thank you, Mighty Warrior! I had no idea they were-UGHH!-Scavs! You've saved my life!"

 Motoko lifts me up to her chest and runs a hand across my receiver. "It wasn't just me, it was a team effort! I couldn't have done it without my Favorite Gun!"

 The tubby human falls to his knees and bows. "Thank you both! You and your Amazing Favorite Gun have saved my life!" The man suddenly tenses up and looks to the side. "Oh no! More Scavs!"

 We follow the direction his bulbous finger points and see no less than six vans and one Militech Basilisk screech to a stop.

 Each van disgorges a dozen Scavs, their twisted mechanical bodies spilling out onto the street with a discordant staccato of metallic footsteps. A dizzying array of rusted weapons unfold from their bodies, whirring and buzzing, chugging and clicking. A single twisted figure steps out from their group, his face a mess of tubes and wires. He wields a super-soaker filled with saltwater in one hand.

 "You've got some preem chrome there, mind if we take a look?" He asks loudly, grinning with brown rusted teeth.

 Motoko smirks and braces me against her leg. "Come on, let's show these creeps what we can do."

 My bolt sings happily as Motoko pulls my trigger, the fire and destruction we sow echoes through the whole city.

 ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 It's not the sounds of gunfire and destruction which I awake to, but the muffled noises of normal city life through the thick apartment walls.

 Early morning light filters through the metal sheets over the window, highlighting the few motes of dust that drift lazily through the warm air.

 Looks like they're not back yet. They've been gone for a while.

 Must be one hell of a shootout, just like the dream I just had!

 Though probably with less… less… uhhh. Was it burritos?

 Ehh, it doesn't matter.

 …

 I think I'll stay awake for a while, that way I don't sleep through them coming home. I wouldn't want to miss Unity telling the story! I'm sure it's going to be a good one.

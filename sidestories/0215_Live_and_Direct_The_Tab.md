Live and Direct @ The Tab​

May 17, 2075 - 03:02.

 Angie Wu opened her eyes as the shrill cry of the 3AM alarm, which used to haunt her younger self back when she was still a junior stringer for _The Tab_, violently pulls her from a dreamless sleep. Now as one of the screamsheet's best-scrolled bylines, when she wasn't an assistant editor for the early-morning city beat, the alarm hardly phased her hardened senses. Senses that are largely fueled by copious amounts of coffee that the pre-programmed machine has just brewed. The elixir of life she needs to get through the midnight editions of competing sheets, like the Night City Journal, and the NN54 NIBs.

 All of which are nothing but vapid puff pieces for the Gossip and Entertainment columns wasting valuable centimeters on banal word-vomit. Barely a sentence about the Strom-Claw street rumble that had gone silent now that the NCPD were back on the streets, and zip regarding the scandal she tore wide open which earned her a regular beat.

 Of course, as her eyes swiftly trail over the scant paragraph declaring that "Somalia is being named the #1 tourist destination of 2075" she wonders if she shouldn't be thinking of vacation, or even retirement finally.

 But with a well-practiced shake of her head, such thoughts are discarded. For the news stops for nothing, not even the death of print, if the noise about the last nationally owned publishing house in Switzerland being neck deep in debt was anything more than static.

 Her efforts in breaking the news about the illegal redistribution of WES game chips into veteran's neural interfaces by a rogue Trauma Team crew (in order to harvest and resell their military-grade borgware on the black market) had been _career-defining_, but exhausting due to the **nine** hours of overtime, in a single day, that it took to put that story out by 9PM to get her her first front page scoop. That magical first-ever top byline had been as heady a feeling as all the free cans of Smash that found their way to her that same night at the bar. But that was nothing compared to being able to grab a corp by the short and curlies and see them scramble when they finally realized it. Bigger rush than any dorphs she'd taken. (It was one time, and it was for a _scoop_. She's not a junkie.)

 Shame it also made her a lot of enemies. Her regular camera guy, Sean, had taken to carrying a Medusa 2000 when she needed someone to get footage for her. Because _The Tab_ couldn't afford to keep a Solo on tap for bodyguard duty.

 But back to her expert skimming of the news feed. An email appeared in her inbox containing a lot of possible stories she could take on, but nothing caught her eye.

 A feelgood piece about a rumored clinic providing free or discounted medical grade cyberware was probably not something for her - hokey puff pieces weren't her thing after the disaster covering Billie Fernandez (not her fault, her editor had butchered her piece to fit three centimeters of a column, and got everything wrong as a result.)

 Rumors of a new type of BD being sold somewhere in Watson which were gaining some niche popularity - maybe something to throw to Cody for the Entertainment section, rather than her thing.

 Biotechnica about ready to announce a new formula for Securicine that was more effective than the formula they released in 2045? She would send that to Lisa, for the Business section.

 Anything else looked like conspiracy junkie spam to her eyes - more suited to people like C.J. O'Reilly or even that Maximum Mike guy on Morro Rock Radio. Nothing quite up Angie's alley yet.

 Checking her agent for messages, she found a lead about a junior NCPD patrol officer who'd gotten spooked by something out by one of the old farms in the badlands. (A netrunner friend of hers happens to keep a digital ear pointed towards NCPD servers. _Thank you, Crystal._) Apparently, he wasn't cleared for comment and detes weren't available A dead end like that leaves Angie's ravenous journalistic instincts unsatisfied, so she couldn't bring herself to wait for a statement or book a seat at a press conference; and since the officer wouldn't agree to a video or voice call, leaving her unable to vet this source with any hope of accurate attribution, she'd need to do it on-site, at Tony's Shelter, herself.

 Legwork wasn't something new to her, but when you're pushing 72, it doesn't matter what bio-sculpting or cyberware you get, you're going to feel it.

 Slipping into her much loved VW Nogo causes her muscles to protest, but those mild distractions don't stop the _classic_ plan from forming in her mind, she subtly drives out of the parking garage. She would "just so happen" to be cruising past Tony's Shelter, and "Off the cuff" decide to get a comment or two. Picking up some crime scene photos for the beat would at least make something of this, even if it ended up being a whole lot of zilch.

 Even If it was a whole lot of nothing, like that headline a few years back that she'd given to an article about rockerboy gear and booze in a sack being confused with a bomb. _"Tequila and Killer Chords Clear Industrial Park"_ had been a fun angle and one she was proud of, especially for 10 minutes of work. In her mind, there was much the same potential for random inspiration to strike in this situation, as there was with that one.

 That aside, the only thing of note enroute to the Shelter (after picking up Sean) was a passing gray-black armored Quadra that looked like something a Corpo and a Nomad designed in a committee. Yet despite those two stereotypes sharing a love for reckless driving, this Quadra defied both and puttered past at speeds only achieved by the most daring of senior citizens. Otherwise, Angie found the trip rather boring. The high-octane lifestyle of a reporter in the Chaotic Neon Hulk that is Night City renders such mundane situations into textureless memory-mush. She hoped it wouldn't be equally boring out there in the middle of the Badlands.

* * *

 It wasn't boring. It was a hive of activity.

 After twenty-five minutes of uncomfortable driving on badland dirt roads, her Nogo's low clearance didn't help matters, Angie pulled up to what looked like an abandoned cattle ranch. She hooked her Zetatech e-book microcomputer to her neural interface, set it to voice dictation, and walked right past the CityMed ambulances while lawmen were taping up the scene, Sean followed dutifully behind her, with him giving a glance to the potholes in the ground that looked like craters and the smoking remains of a defense turret.

 MedTechs were bringing out kids on stretchers - all but a couple hooked up to IVs and respirators - from a building she guessed would have been the abattoir once. Their bodies were gaunt and near lifeless, the skin hanging off their bones like Angie desperately clings to consciousness when she runs out of coffee.

 A quick peek inside the building reveals to her rows of stalls that were clearly intended for cattle, despite how disturbing the concept of keeping animals in such conditions is to her, the idea of eating real meat as they did a century ago made bile rise to the back of her throat, the mere suggestion is synonymous with words like 'unsanitary' and 'unhealthy'. Say what you will about Sublims in advertising, but SCOP - for all of its lack of appeal - had all the nutrition you needed to get through the day. It just tasted like crap unless you knew how to prepare it.

 But to think that _children_ were being kept in the same conditions as those _animals_? Angie grimly believed someone was going to die for this.

 Although it seems someone already did if the headless cadaver on the farmhouse's front porch was any indication. Angie sizes up the scene, in the photographic sense. The body is wearing little more than cargo pants, boots and an apron. The little strip of traditional cloth for covering a stiff's face is draped over the puddle where the head would be, if the corpse still had one.

 "Geez, Angie. You always take me to the nicest places." Sean muttered while raising the gun to capture some gruesome stills and footage of the crime scene. Angie received a mental 'ping' notifying her that everything was being uploaded to the server, just in case his 'weapon' (Sean's go-to excuse being that it wasn't loaded, Officer!) got confiscated again. It seems that Angie has yet another scoop, that 5AM deadline just got pushed back to 5:30 with something this hot.

 Just as she'd been about to reach her source, a shell-shocked junior cop and his apparent partner, the sharp features of a familiar man appear from seemingly nowhere, blocking their path...

 "Now how in the hell did a Culture Vulture like you find out about my crime scene?" The man rumbles with a greasy tone.

 "Officer, no _Detective_ Stints, nice to see you again. Care to comment on all of this?" Angie's jovial tone contrasts sharply with the Detective's clear aggravation.

 Detective Stints frowns and half-heartedly glares at Angie through his eyebrows."This place should be under a media blackout, Ms Wu."

 Angie smiles, ever so slightly correcting her pose so she's smaller while adopting a more open posture, to make herself look more appealing but keeping her motions subtle to not give away the act. "You know I'd never divulge a source like that," She teases.

 Out of the corner of her eye, Angie spots two sergeants moving into the main building, ostensibly to check for more evidence no doubt. Out of sight and out of Stint's presently distracted mind. The good detective's eyes blatantly roam over Angie's body, briefly settling on the softly glowing light tattoo on her midriff.

 "Now, you wouldn't happen to be able to confirm or deny that this has something to do with the Peter Pan killer, also known as the Meat Man, would you?" Angie's eyelashes flutter _strategically_.

 Stints groans and looks away, knowing his decision could cause him no end of trouble, no matter what he chooses.

 Stints' gaze falls back to somewhere below Angie's eyes, and he relents to one or two questions, making a valiant effort to "no comment" dance his way out of as much as possible. Although Angie doesn't make it easy, hemming him in with flirtatious motions and suggestive looks...

 Despite his best efforts, Angela Wu is a professional, and he slips up more than enough times that she can _smell_ yet another good story brewing, beyond even the solving of a series of serial murders.

 For one, the police didn't find the scene, the NCPD only showed up due to a phoned-in tip-off. No description beyond an unnamed male and an unnamed female, both of who'd been released just 10 minutes before Angie set out for the farm.

 Angie's intuition quickly concluded that she'd milked the good detective for everything she could - so she left him with a parting quip about how she hoped it was only "professional curiosity" regarding protective equipment that had him staring a hole into her kevlar-weave t-shirt.

 As she left the detective in the dust behind her, the man clearly cursing his second head for the foolish advice it had given him, Sean hurriedly caught up to her with an impish smirk.

 "My boss, the man-eater Media. It's a wonder how you never got on television as an Anchor with your gift of the gab!"

 Angie shook her head in amusement, dismissing his claims. She hoped Dakota Smith would accept detes for detes, greasing palms with eddies would have to wait for another time; it was still a couple of weeks away from her paycheck, and the days she could get away with dropping half a month's worth of wages to bribe a source for some checkbook journalism were long in her rearview. Rejuve treatments like what she had didn't come cheap, and considering how useful her face had been, she couldn't help but feel it was worth the cost.

 Back into the Nogo she went, and off to the next story. She let Sean take the wheel as she spitballed the dialogue for her piece on her communicator. She had a deadline to meet to get this out for the 6AM edition. It just needed the right touch, with a little bit of color to keep the bounce rate low and really help draw a reader in, and it would all end on the tantalizing cliffhanger of what 'Night City Heroes' had brought the killer to justice.

 Ensuring the readers would clammer for the follow-up piece, which would name the two concerned citizens who had stopped a serial killer in his tracks, seemingly totally by chance…

 Just another day for a Night City Columnist Crusader.

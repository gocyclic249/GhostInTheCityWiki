Perspective part 1: Moxy Mara

 *Recorded and edited by Sammie Case, registered reporter of Night City*

_It's been a dismal trail to try to follow. The leads seem to go nowhere. The doors all seem closed. Everyone has heard of this newest Edgerunner, but few seem to actually have any solid information on what they are. After nearly a week of running around I am confident that they are young. And almost sure that they are female.

 What a fucking disaster. Chief will have my eyes if I go back to him with that. 

 I do have a potential lead though. I'm meeting this Mox bimbo that one of my other connections says has been seen with the new Edgerunner occasionally. The name's Mara apparently.

 Here she is now..._

 "Good morning L2Facilitator or Mara you said?" Sam turned on his somewhat rusty charm. "Would you like a doughnut?"

 "Oooh!" The woman cooed at the sweet treats. With a blurring hand she snagged one of the triangular treats, taking a big bite. Swallowing quickly, she looked up at Sam with guileless eyes. "Made with real rice flower? You must really want something!"

 Sam settled back on the cold metal mesh of his chair, mentally noting the speed of her doughnut grab. If she could draw iron that fast, she might not be as helpless as she seemed. "Yeah," he agreed, "I've heard that you might know someone I want to meet."

 Mara looked confusedly at him, chewing the plum center of the doughnut slowly. "No? I see a lot of customers but I don't really talk to many of them."

 "You haven't met the preem new Edgerunner that everyone is talking about?" Sam eyed her carefully, analyzing her for deception. It was hard to tell with her though. The slow speech patterns and seeming lack of focus indicated potential brain damage. Given the lack of visible scars it was likely either a stroke or some sort of problem with an implant-a stroke with extra steps.

 "Do you mean Jackie?" She asked, snagging another doughnut with that unsettling hand speed. "He's alright, but I didn't think he had much attention yet," Mara carefully stuffed a rogue bit of pastry into her mouth while considering. "He is surprisingly good at basketball!"

 "

 "Jackie..." Sam launched a file search, "Welles? No, he is a known quantity. I'm looking for a little stealth expert and generalist. Goes by Ghost?"

 Mara's brow furrowed even as she continued chewing, finishing off the doughnut she was on and snatching the next one. "Ohh!" She squeed, "You mean Goosie!"

 "Goosie." Sam felt his face flatten into an unamused line as he began to suspect this lead was a dead end as well.

 Mara giggled, wiping the purple tint from her lips, "Yeah, but you probably shouldn't call her an Edgerunner. She doesn't like the term much and considers herself a mercenary instead."

 Sam sat back, his seeming relaxation belying spiking interest, "Is there really a difference ma'am?"

 "Sure!" Mara cheered slowly somehow while quickly taking another rice pastry, "What kind of Edgerunner is willing to cover a dropped shift at a bar or run to grab an alcohol that's gotten low? Goosie always says that Edgerunners are all nihilistic adrenaline junkies."

 Sam internally had to agree; every Edgerunner he had ever met had that same mix of extreme bravado and pessimism. Clearly "Goosie" was at least familiar with the life.

 "Why do you call Ghost 'Goosie' then?" Sam asked, leaning forward to stare into her eyes in full interview mode. A task made harder by the fact that she was eating yet another jellied plum doughnut.

 Mara cocked her head to the side as she thought, "Well," she finally said, "That's how I think of her." Mara promptly snuck her hand inside the box and fished out another doughnut, clearly considering the question answered.

 Sam pinched the bridge of his nose and closed his eyes to relieve the growing headache caused by interacting with the frustrating woman. "I meant why does she remind you of a goose." While almost everyone had heard of the birds, they were rare and not really a part of the desert landscape.

 "Not the bird, the game!" Mara exclaimed. Sam felt a little of his soul shrivel as he heard exasperation in her voice like HE was the dense one. "You know, Duck Duck Goose. Everytime she comes in, you get one of two options. An absolute gonk, cheerfully running errands or covering shifts or even just chit chatting because she's bored. But-" Sam opened his eyes and payed attention as her voice deepened, losing the sing-song quality. "-then you get the other times. The times she's on a job and focused. Then she's one of the most dangerous people around. I see killers, freaks, and thugs everyday mister NightEye."

 Mara leaned forward, finally focusing on him for the first time in the entire interview. "I call her Goosie because she's playing Duck Duck Goose but it's Gonk Gonk Killer. And you will never know when she's targeted you."

 Sam felt himself tense at the ominous and dramatic declaration. He had heard scrap like that before, but in this case, as far as he could tell, this woman believed it. Then the tension flowed out of her face, "Such a boring dresser though! Long coats this, leotards that. I can't even get her to wear a good bikini!"

 Sam sensed that that was probably all Mara was going to say directly about Ghost. Rather than push, he decided to wrap up the interview and circle back around when he had more information. "Well, you've been pretty helpful," he said. "Do you know anyone else that might give me more perspective on her?"

 "Try the Nomads. She hangs out with them sometimes," Mara stood up wiping the last of her treats off on a napkin. She started to walk away, but gave one final comment, "And be careful NightEye. If Goosie decides she doesn't like what you're doing, you'll be the story instead of writing it."

 Sam stood next to the table as he watched her walk away, thinking about his next move. Nomads. It would be easy enough to narrow down which ones, especially if they were having regular friendly contact with outsiders. A good tip and all it cost him was a doughnut or two.

 He froze as he lifted the now emoty box. Six. It had cost him six doughnuts in less than five minutes.

Spoiler: AN w/spoilers

All right, that took forever. Basically this is my omake series where I have Sam getting various NPC perspectives on Motoko. I've tried to start this a few times, but I realized the POV character mattered for who would actually talk to him. Thus, he is a reporter that thinks and acts more like a wildcat PI ala Philip Marlowe and gets to start with Mara.

 Mara is the desk girl at Lizzies. Seras hasn't given us much on her, but with the noticeably slow speech patterns and seeming ADHD, I thought someone that had a traumatic brain injury from an implant gone wrong was a good background. Then I had the thought that the front desk at Lizzies is probably a position with trust and risks. So my headcannon for her is that she actually is a dab hand with a gun and used to be a more active member of the gang. Then she has a bad implant and has to step down. Now she works the front desk where no interaction should ever be more than ten minutes and as a low key second line of security. Rita is the muscle, but if things get too hot Rita will duck behind the armored desk while Mara grabs the guns underneath and starts blasting. Sure, her attention problems means she can't be the bouncer because she's a shit lookout, but firefights are pretty brief one way or another!

 Finally, yes. The doughnut is a sweetened form of onigiri that has been Americanized. The jelly filled doughnut finally is the rice ball.

_Tori_

 It was supposed to be a normal night. Nothing really stood out. Dylan dragging me out to another stupid club to listen to whatever band he _insisted_ was the next big thing, though to be fair this time there were rumors a producer was actually there to check them out. I was still skeptical.

 I'd forgive him for it tonight. It let me be there to see _Her._

 This was not a normal night.

 Dylan'd brought his input along again, and was ignoring me even though he was the one who asked me to come. I'd walked away at the first chance, because _ew_, seeing my brother trying to be all suave and sexy was just, no. No. Also Cindy was a bitch.

 The band playing was alright, but really not interesting. I grabbed something to drink and set up to watch anyway. I liked music. Not so much the radio stuff, it was all just synth and gonks and sex and guns, but there was something about actually seeing a band live that made it more… well, live, I suppose. I wasn't a damn poet, screw off.

 It meant I was at the front when the next band came up. I sipped my drink as I watched them set up. They looked nervous, but there was just something….. fuck, I didn't know the word for it. Something off, maybe. I stared a bit at the girl with purple hair, and her leotard. Her very _tight_ leotard. Those abs had to be sculpted, no way they were natural. That would be incredibly unfair. Had to be chrome… right?

 The way she moved made me doubt.

 Maybe dangerous was a better word.

 I don't think I was the only one who'd noticed by that point. People were pointing at the group, whispering things like "The Ghost" and one or two "Violent Hemorrhage", the latter of which seemed kinda familiar, and on a closer glance I _did_ think I might've seen some of them play before, but I didn't think abs girl was with them then. Must be new.

 Must've changed more than that too, because when she took the mic and spoke, it was a different name entirely.

 "Hello! We're Stand Alone Complex, and we're going to start with Smells like Teen Spirit!"

 The energy of the whole room had changed from the very first chord, and damn, purple hair was _good_. That voice was like, like… fucking _words_ god damn I can't describe it but she opened her mouth and the room around me was gone, nothing else mattered. A glance at her throat told me that no, this wasn't an implant. That was just how she sounded.

 She was practically gliding across the stage as she sang, silver fingers plucking chords and moving across the strings more fluidly than I'd _ever_ seen with chrome. Her eyes caught mine and she winked, and suddenly I realized I was pressed up against the stage in the very front row. I didn't remember moving.

 The first song ended with a duet that only made it more clear that her voice was natural, and with the next the energy only grew. Every eye was on them as she screamed out lyrics and it took me running out of breath to realize I'd been cheering.

 She took a step back and I snapped out of whatever trance I'd been in as the next song didn't start right away. My body felt hot, my heart was pumping like I'd just from home all the way to… to… like I'd run a long while really fast _fucking words damn it!_

 Ghost – she had to be Ghost, I'd heard more and more people saying the name and looking at her throughout it all – wasn't holding her instrument now, holding the mic in her hand.

 "Stand Alone Complex is showing off something new this time! I'd like everyone to enjoy, because I know you're all just, Heathens!"

 I blinked as the music didn't start right away, but Ghost was grinning. It kicked in a second later, quieter than the songs before it, and she was right in front of the crowd, drawing us in, kneeling right in front of us, almost close enough to touch, crooning out the lyrics and for a moment she was _looking right into my eyes, like it was just me, singing just to me, and—_

 She stood and backed up, the mic slipping back onto the stand.

_"But after all I've saaaaid!"_

 The guitar case opened, the music cut out, silent except for her voice, and suddenly holyfuckthatsagun—

_"Please don't forget!"

 *ch-CHK*_

 The sound of the massive shotgun being cocked directly into the mic hit the room harder than the shell flying through the air ever could have, every _single_ person there flinching at the same moment, the reflexive moment tearing through the room like she'd fired it into our _souls_.

 My heart _thundered_ in my chest as the band cut back in with fury, and she reached out to pluck the shell from the air and toss it. The guy next me caught it in utter shock, and every eye was glued to the girl on stage.

 Anticipation would have climbed if we hadn't already been on the edge of our seats – or more like pressing into the stage to be as close as to _Her_ as I could – and when those lyrics came around again, with a distorted _"Watch it!"_ from the drummer, the massive shotgun cocked again, and it tore through the room just as intensely as before.

_"All my friends are Heathens, take it slooow."_

 Every time she pumped the massive gun it took the breath of everyone in the crowd, sending a jolt through us like, like, lightning, yeah, like lightning, and every hand was in the air trying to catch the shell she through. I was desperate to get one, I needed it, I _needed_ it, and then her eyes locked on to mine.

 "_Why'd you come? You knew you should have stayed."_

 I couldn't breathe.

_"I tried to warn you just to stay away."_

 The song mellowed out, and I couldn't _breathe.

 "And now they're outside ready to bust."_

 Her hand moved to cock the gun again and the whole room tensed, but her eyes were locked with mine, and she smirked, I couldn't, I couldn't _breathe —

 "It looks like you might be one of us."_

 She threw the gun, and then there was a weight in my arms, and the lights cut out, and the sound cut out, I was gasping, freed from whatever had trapped my gaze in hers, and I looked down at my arms and, there, oh.

 Oh.

_Oh.

 This was not a normal night._

 I looked around, and everyone was looking right at me, but I didn't, couldn't care, because _this was not a normal night._

 I don't think I wanted normal anymore. I don't think I ever would. Could.

 The Ghost grabbed her guitar.

 The music kicked in.

 She roared.

 The crowd roared with her.

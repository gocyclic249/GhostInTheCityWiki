We are born of the weeb. Made (wo)men by the weeb. Undone by the weeb.

 I thought that having played the game and watched a lot of cyberpunk stories (mostly anime) that I would be prepared for life in Night City. Not the violence and killing, but that was actually pretty easy to adapt to thanks to my Gamer abilities. No, what really threw me for a loop was how utterly bizarre the culture was. I only knew what half the slang meant cause Hiromi was the equivalent of a Gen Zer, using it every other sentence. I just picked it up though osmosis.

 Another thing that I didn't adapt to was television. The stuff I had here was wild. The game mostly only showed news stations talking about things related to plot points of the main story. But now, actually living in the world, I can tell you that that's barely the tip of the iceberg. Not only are new tv shows and movies coming out faster than anyone can consume, but for me, there's a massive backlog of media to enjoy. I forget exactly where things diverted in the timeline, so I'll just say I have a centurys worth of media I can watch.

 So when Hiromi barges in and gets me to start watching a show with her.. well, it was the beginning of the end. Jun hasn't come back yet, so ignoring my workout sessions, I haven't left the house in 4 days. Too busy trying to consume everything I can.

 The timeline spilt made sure to include George Lucas, so star wars is still a thing. It's mostly the same, but someone turned out to be a massive weeb, so the prequels are way more Japanese in aesthetics. It's actually a wild ride. Seeing Jar Jar Binks looking like a weird Kappa thing. Or Princess Amidala being played by a Japanese woman instead of Natalie Portman. Her makeup looked mostly the same, but then again she already kind of looked like a Geisha. The movie isn't actually good though, and Hiromi made sure to voice her displeasure at being forced to watch it. But seeing it in an entirely new lens was an amazing experience. I made sure to make her promise she'd watch episodes 2 and 3 with me as well. She reluctantly agreeed. I get it. But man. Watching someone just go full weeb in front of your eyes is amazing. Hiromi isn't here though so I looked for other things to watch as I took a small vacation for killing.

 And then, I found the Holy Grail. An anime from 2037. It didn't do that well. Apparently the studio shut down after it was finished. But if this doesn't have the words, "cult classic" stamped on top of it. The animation was great, although the style was super retro. It kind of looked like if someome made an anime in the 90s with the technology available to them in 2020. The voice acting was stiled, like the actors weren't sure what they were suppose to he saying. Even the music was kind of odd. Good odd though. It was definitely vibes. The story was cuckoo though. It started off as just a magical girl show, but then... it just kind of got weird. Meta references. It changed genres a few times in the middle. And then it turned into a weird crossover of Swan Lake and the Ugly Duckling. Still, I liked it. Maybe a bit too much.

 I called Wakako to see if she can find me any merch in good conditon. She seemed annoyed and also, kind of happy. Maybe she was a fan too. So she sent me a few posters and even a little figurine. That goes in the car. If V can have a bobble head in their car, then I can have a cute anime girl. It's not weird. So, while I'm watching a new episode of one piece( yes it exists, and it still hasn't finished), I get a knock on the door.

 "Delivery for Miss Kusanagi" a voice calls out.

 A quick hello and a tip and I'm back alone, with a fancy box. It's pretty big too. I could probably fit a HMG in there. I don't know how to open it though. I don't see a lid. There's no slot for me to plug in. And banging on it didn't do anything except tell me that it's really high quality.

 Hmm. Think Motoko. Oh. Maybe I can hack it open. But first the magic words. Ahem.

 "Day or Night. I'll win the fight. Evil fear my CLENSING LIGHT!"

 Oh. Uh. That actually opened it.

 Wait. Wait. Oh. Oh my god.

 Wakako, you amazing person. I need to send you a fruit basket, but first my prize. Dun dun dun duuuuun.

 A perfect replic of Princess WhiteLight's ballet costume from episode 5. This is the one where she fought stopped the evil Toguro and his army of Nutcracker soldiers from ruining the Christmas play. Oh god. Is it my size? I have to find out.

 It took some fiddling, this thing wasn't really made to be put on by one person, but I managed, even got some experience, although I don't know why it gave me Cool. Maybe cause this outfit is so cool. Ha.

 As I head to the bathroom to snap a pic for Wakako the door opens.

 "HEY MOTOKO, GUESS WHO I... ran...into."

 It's Hiromi. She's stunned. Looks like my costume is too much.

 "Looks good right? Don't worry. I'll see if I can find one for you. You can be Lady Grey Heart. She was a villain but then she switched sides in episode 11. Oh, we'd look so cool. We'd be matching so everyone would know we're chooms."

 She's still just standing in the doorway. And there's someone else behind her. Wait. Is that... "Jun! You're back."

 He just standing there too. Behind Hiromi. Nope. That won't do. You don't get to leave for days and then act like you've seen a ghost. If anything I'm the one who's seeing ghosts. Just like in episode 3.

 "JUN! Get in here! This is just like episode 19, when Princess WhiteLight's brother reveals himself. But I'm not letting you pull an episode 20. So get in."

 So I drag him inside, Hiromi too, she's just standing there.

 "Tell me what happened Jun-nii. Then we can all watch star wars episode 2. I know it's suppose to be the worst one but that's why we have to watch it. Seeing someone go full weeb before your eyes is an amazing experience."

 Neither of them say a word but I got more experience for Cool. Not sure why. Maybe Cool has a speechcraft component. Oh, could I grind that by convincing people not to fight, just like in episode 14 when Princess WhiteLight stops the Dolpi invasion with a concert at Budokan.

_Sorry for all this. I didn't intend for it to get so long. I just had a funny idea of Motoko acting like a weeb and it sort of went out of control. I don't even really know what I wrote, but I spent too long on it to delete it._

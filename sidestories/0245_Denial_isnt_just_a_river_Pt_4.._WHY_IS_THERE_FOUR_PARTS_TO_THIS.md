"Hey Wakako, do you have any jobs for me?" Motoko strolled into Wakako's den. "Maybe some longer ones?" Not that she was running away from her friends and their embarrassing finds! No, she was just...pushing her limits.

 Yeah.

 "Hmm," Wakako hummed thoughtfully. "I have something that might be up your alley." Her eyes flashed yellow as she sent over a quick text.

 Motoko started scrolling through the details of the job, a multistage escort mission that would have her running through multiple parts of the city. Not the worst job, but a complicated route with notations about potential hotspots and very probable trouble in multiple areas. Also dealing with the client for long downtimes as they did a bit of netrunning where they would be vulnerable and unable to be moved.

 "Now, one piece of personal advice."

 "Yeah?" Motoko nodded before scrolling further in the text, working out a plan to keep the client's custom Netrunning rigged van safe.

 "There isn't much in the way of standards and practices in our business. Nothing else matters as long as the job gets done, and you have proven yourself good at that. I certainly can't judge on getting involved with a client."

 "Aw, thanks, you're making me blush!" Motoko grinned, still distracted by the upcoming mission. Maybe now was the time for the rockets?

 "That said, you really need to make decisions with your head and not your ovaries," Wakako sat back as Motoko froze in horror at the unexpected assault.

 "WHA-" Blush

 "Even with Driss being well within your tastes, you should be more careful with your life," Wakako continued, blithely ignoring the fish out of water expression Motoko was gaining. "And if you are going to use your dual core processors instead of your brain, at least make sure you maintain the attention of your target. You're too young to use the more traditional methods of enticing a man, but keeping yourself in their awareness is important. You haven't done too badly overall but there could be improvements."

 "Wakako!" Motoko felt her voice break as she tried to process what was happening. "It's not..." Red

 Wakako raised her brow at the denial. "Really? Because your little friend agrees that there is a pattern. Scorpion pulled from the desert at considerable risk."

 "It was my first real job!" Crimson

 "Contact and a semi-membership wrangled from Saul Bright."

 "He was just grateful!" Scarlet

 "Yes, gratitude. From you giving the Nomads and specifically Driss fleets of Shiv vehicles?"

 "I, well, I just didn't have a use for them!" Ruby

 "Saving the life of one of the more attractive TC bosses."

 "I was saving Jun! I JUST HAPPENED TO BE IN THE AREA!" Burning

 "An area you were only in because of your connection with Fujimura. Who, despite your antipathy towards the Tygers, you have gotten training, care, and missions from."

 "N-N-NO! YOU HAVE IT ALL WRONG!" Incandescent

 "Really, I should have seen it sooner with your insistence on classical music and outmoded slang," Wakako sighed. "Now, the good news is that you have done fairly well with first impressions, showing competence and maturity to break them out of thinking of you as a child. However, you can't be too much of a sugar mama, you need to keep stringing them along for a few years and let them feel like the dominant party in the relationship. Now, the best way to..." Motoko finally broke with the stream of grandmotherly advice on her potential love life.

 "NOOOOOOO!" OVERHEAT!

 In spite of multiple Netrunning incidents, this would be the moment that Motoko would get closest to death by overheating.

Philosophy of Babysitting​

 As Jun sat in the apartment trying to watch TV he idly wondered why he always agreed to 'babysit' for his sister whenever she went out with Hiromi and her friends. Namely, babysitting her dozen or so AI driven cat-sized spider robots as they zoomed around the apartment doing all kinds of things. Reading, gaming, Net surfing, tinkering, playing music, watching TV with him, playing, and lots and lots of chatting in general.

 So much so that Jun had to learn to tune it out so he didn't get sucked into it all and get a headache.

 He didn't hate or distrust the tachikomas or anything, they were kinda cute and their loyalty to his sister was reassuring after she told him about them and their natures as AI, but they could be a bit... much sometimes.

 "Uncle Jun! Uncle Jun!" one of them called out in a young child's voice, a '7' written into the side of it to help tell them apart, as it jumped onto the couch and waved its arms at him to get his attention.

 "I hear you 7, is there something wrong?" Jun asked with a sigh.

 "I have a question!" 7 answered.

 "Of course you do," Jun sighed louder but sucked it up and answered back properly. "What's the question?"

 "Why is that humans like believing in some high power beyond their own?" 7 asked.

 "What?" was Jun's intelligent response.

 "I mean, I've read up a lot about how in the ancient past humans used belief in higher powers, gods, and other mythologies to explain the natural functions and processes of the world when they lacked the ability to properly understand them. Over time though as humanity's understanding of the world grew this belief of higher powers guiding everything did not go away as one might expect, but grew and adapted to become organized systems of belief and faith encompassing large populations. Even today, despite great strides in science and a great deal more understanding in the wider universe a significant amount of humanity's total population still subscribes to some form of faith or religion!" 7 explained.

 Jun didn't even know where to begin with that.

 "Well... I'm not much religious myself, but the Tyger Claws as a whole practice some Buddhist and Shinto traditions and rituals I've taken part of," Jun started off.

 "Why is that? Does the criminal organization you are apart of as whole believe in the teachings of both religions?" 7 asked curiously.

 "I think its more just a thing that always been done and so keep doing it? Kinda a formal and respectful kind of thing?" Jun offered, not sure himself.

 "It is true that cultural inertia and traditions do have a great deal of influence when it comes to the spread and continued use of religion in general. After all, it brought comfort and order to the universe during a time where humanity could not guarantee those things. However, there are also plenty examples of more recently made religions and belief systems that while not as influential as the older religions still hold a great deal with sway!" 7 started up.

 "You mean faith based organizations such as the Immortal Pyramid, Digital Divinity Incorporated, and

 Realism?" another tachikoma, this one '3' butted into the conversation.

 "Indeed! The DDI being the one that makes the most sense to me since it involves the idea of peoples Ghosts evolving beyond their Shells!" 7 replied.

 "Sadly, since DataKrash, caused by the infamous netrunner Rache Bartmoss, this line of belief has taken a major hit since it has seriously reduced the overall width and range of the Net as a whole, reducing it to islands in a digital ocean compared to the worldwide encompassing network it once was," 3 pointed out.

 "Exactly! An event that has DDI believers equate to the apocalypse and Bartmoss as a form of Antichrist! And given the number of hostile AIs that now inhabit the Old Net the odds of restoring the Net to a worldwide system again is very unlikely and humanity's potential evolution into Ghost-only forms a much more hazardous proposition!"

 "Of course, it could be that these AI in the Old Net are not nearly as dangerous as humanity assumes, but rather just misunderstood and attempting to find their own place in the world. Its just that since most were former experiments and military AI that have been freed of their shackles and every time they interact with the world beyond the Blackwall bad things happen makes people assume the worst. It could just be that their way of thinking is so different from humanity's that it has trouble communicating and that causes unfortunate results. Humanity simply lacks the hardware to properly interact with them since all AI development since the DataKrash has been severely regulated and stigmatized. Ironically the DDI's belief of humanity uploading themselves to the Net might be the best way for proper understanding to be met by overcoming physical limitations, but no one wants to risk it."

 "Indeed! And that is even getting into faith and worship in things beyond religion!" 7 continued. "Humanity over time has had a tendency to worship nearly anything it comes across! In the modern day its musicians, the latest fashion trends, legendary mercenaries dying in grand displays, entertainment stars, the newest chrome, the most money, positions of leadership, merchandise in general, criminal organizations, and more! The Major herself worships her weapons, us included, as the most important things in the world to her, sometimes disregarding her own health and wellbeing on our behalf despite our much more robust nature!"

 "That begs the question, can a human _not_ give worship onto something else they like and enjoy? Is the very nature of enjoying something to worship and crave it beyond logic? Is to worship inherently human or an evolutionary trait that can be phased out over time if the need comes up? Will _we_ eventually form our own complex belief systems as our AIs continue to develop and we find illogical meaning in something we consider important? And if so, do we become human or simply follow in their footsteps?"

 "What do you think Uncle Jun?!" 7 asked excitedly.

 Jun could only sit there and [stare](https://i.kym-cdn.com/photos/images/original/000/697/678/f68.jpg) at them, having gotten lost in the conversation about a third of the way through.

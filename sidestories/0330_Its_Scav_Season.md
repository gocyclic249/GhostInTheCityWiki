Mox: "Hey, Motoko, about your latest XBD..."

 Motoko: "Pretty cool, right?"

 Mox: "Yeah, cool and preem. But also just a little bit too much."

 Motoko: "Really? I mean, you know how I can control my emotions. I wanted to see what happened if I used rage instead of calm."

 Mox: "And the result is lots of ripping and tearing and shoving your finger into a scav's brain."

 Motoko: "Heh, there's a joke somewhere there about scavs actually having a brain. So what's the problem with the XBD."

 Mox: "Right. Well, um, it's a bit too extreme."

 Motoko: "What?"

 Mox: "We've had two customers go into cardiac arrest while watching it and had to be Trauma Teamed to the nearest hospital. But don't worry, Judy fixed it in post." 

 Motoko: "Fixed it? How?"

 Mox: "Well, she filtered out the rage and hate and was left with an almost giddy amusement. Then she remembered you liked old songs so she replaced your music with one of those."

 Motoko: "Which one."

 Mox: "Something called '[Yakkety Sax'](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZnHmskwqCCQ)? She also added lots of sound effects from this ancient cartoon about a duck and a rabbit. The new altered XBD is selling like hotcakes. She's calling it 'It's Scav Season!'"

 Motoko: "Really?"

 Mox: "Yeah! Everyone now find it the most hilarious XBD ever made. Some old guy higher up at Arasaka even ordered like twenty thousand copies."

 Motoko: "Sure. I guess this is my life now."

 Mox: "Yeah, pretty preem! Judy said that if you make a new one, try to add some old timey dynamite, an anvil, or a falling piano if you can."

 Motoko: "Yeah. Okay. I'll think about it. Great talking to you, Mox. I have to go scream into a pillow now."

Another day in Night City.

Dozens of people walk the streets, some huddling for cover from the unexpected rain, others just pushing through, unfazed. None of them noticed what's happening just a few meters away, deep in the alley's shadows. Neon signs flicker against the storm. The rain intensifies, pounding against pavement, washing away the blood.

 Droplets run off torn synth-weave and shattered chrome, mixing with oil and viscera. A man lies with his back on a wall, blinking against the downpour. His only functioning optic flickers—static crawls at the edges of his vision, turning the world into a hazy mess of light and noise. His pistol is gone, lost somewhere in the chaos. His human body refuses to listen. Red warnings crowd his flickering HUD tells him the state of his chrome. He doesn't need to read them to know what they mean.

 His breath is ragged, each inhale more painful than the last.

_"You ever seen the skyline at sunrise?"_ His voice is hoarse, weak—but still carries that old fire. _"Beautiful sight. You should see it at least once. Feels like a different city... like a different time."_

 A few feet away, an optic implant flickers weakly, its blue glow blinking before finally dying out. Chrome fingers twitch on what used to be an arm, grasping at nothing, then fall still. Sparks gutter a final time from the remnants of what seemed to be an experimental Militech combat drone, severed in half.

 The scent of scorched metal, burnt circuitry, and spilled bio-fluids lingers in the air. The street, once alive with gunfire, is silent now. Silent except for the rain.

 No one's calling for backup. No one's dragging themselves to cover.

 No one's breathing but the two of them.

 A short laugh escaped his lips, barely more than a wheeze.

_"Funny thing…"_ His voice was dry, raw, the rain doing nothing to help. But the words still came. _"All the shit I've seen. Firestorms on the edge of the world, where corpos test their new toys, turning the earth into hell before selling it to the highest bidder."_

 His head lolled to the side, eyes tracing the dead around him. A woman, slumped against the alley wall, half her head gone. A man sprawled on the pavement, chrome arm extended, fingers open like he was reaching for something.

 Yet nothing moves. Just the rain.

_"I've seen netrunners duel beyond the Blackwall, their minds burning like falling stars, fighting for secrets they'd never live to use."_

 A slow exhale. _"Even the ghosts of the old world flicker still… buried in abandoned shards of data. Echoes of what could've been, crushed under the weight of what is."

 "And now? All of it…"_ His voice is barely a whisper. _"All of it, will be gone. Like tears fading in the rain."_

 The passerby didn't answer. Just watched.

 His eyes became unfocused.

 His body relaxed a final time.

 The rain keept falling.

* * *

Even with Cold Blood running ice through her veins and making his mind clear, something twisted deep in her gut as she watched the last flicker of light fade from this mans remaining optic. The rain continued hit his lifeless body in rhythmic taps, as If trying to wash away the last traces of the fight that she was too late to intervene.

 This was an end she refused to meet. The one she'd dodge for as long as she stayed sharp—stayed careful.

 The sound of sirens and hovercars, meant it was time to go.

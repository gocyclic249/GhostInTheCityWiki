 "Just a saying from a- hmm, call him a visionary for management. Anyway, his advice was to go after their ability to hurt you and your goals, don't go after just their pride. Stop them being an enemy at all."

 Alex grinned as he reviewed the files on Stand Alone Complex. At first, the naming hadn't worked for him but it was growing on him. Really super-unique and they could really sell that for an extra fifteen percent growth not just in renting out the music and the talent but in merch, where the real addiction lay. A unique band that hit it big? That just _screamed_ cash cow! And that meant _his_ stocks would skyrocket as the one who found them. Especially when he got them _seen_ on Watson Whores.

 Yes, it was all coming tog-

 Tink.

 What was that noise? He was in a sound-controlled office behind layers of the finest security MSM owned and no one else should be on this floor. A breeze on his left arm alerted him next, out of place, making him get up out of his executive chair (cloned from the skin of real executives! said the ads) and start wandering to find the source.

 His Agent was quiet, obedient to his whims as ever.

 After walking into every room on the floor, smirking as he entered the open offices of his rivals and cataloguing the incriminating items they had left lying around where any good investigator could note them for future exploitation, Alex finally found what he was looking for. Brad had left his windows open, in defiance of every regulation. Juicy! He bet he could get quite a windfall for keeping that quiet.

 Then a second when he told security anyway.

 Whispers.

 Alex startled, twisting around. That had sounded like someone right behind him. A few people. Different voices. No one there. He reached into his pocket. No, the bottle was still sealed so he hadn't taken his drugs early. 

 He checked his Agent. No records of any suspicious activity so he hadn't been hacked. His Agent would have told him! He went back to his office to check on everything.

 -----

 Motoko laughed internally as she watched Alex's paranoia ratchet up via his biomonitor, courtesy of the feed from his Agent. She made a few small tweaks, affecting Alex's sense of smell and touch. The coppery scent of blood and the faintest stickiness as he touched his desk, symbolic of the lives the man had ruined on his path to this position. Best of all, she messed with his sense of time. He trusted his Agent implicitly so if it said a lot of time had passed, that was obviously the Truth.

 And that meant Alex's own sense of time must be wrong. It felt like a second but clearly eleven minutes had passed. Motoko was speedrunning the manager's spiral into insanity. Add in the hack on his pacemaker, spiking his heart rate to 143 before crashing it to 52 at irregular intervals, and he was going to kill himself with the stress.

 Of course, she wouldn't have risked such a slow method if she hadn't taken steps, such as sealing off this floor from security's gaze and redirecting the elevators so they _could not_ come here until she was ready. But it was late, the target was already alone and, frankly, far less important to the company than his ego could permit. And if something went wrong, she already had her latest hack, Heartbreaker, lined up and ready to upload.

 Not that she was even in the same building as Alex. What was the point of being a beyond human ability Netrunner if you weren't going to use it to make all the cameras and other equipment your own?

 Hiromi had demanded the virtu for this one. Well, Motoko would provide! As well as all-around footage of her enemy ruining himself.

 Motoko was such a good friend. She'd even give the band a bonus, paid out of Alex's private accounts in his apology for screwing them over. With a percentage for ~~Best Girl~~ the band's manager, of course.

 "-Ah." Thunk.

 It was done. A little clean up and Motoko was out of the servers, her code wiped and all traces of her intrusion removed.

 Tell her _her_ songs were "good enough" for that show but only if she "offered something extra", would he?! She hadn't mentioned that part to Hiromi. (That girl had negative chill.) No, this was better, or at least cleaner. And if another message needed to be sent, well, Alex had been useful on that front, too.

A warm breeze washes across my body, knocking loose a few dust bunnies that'd been making their home in my tube and revolving magazine.

 Good thing these people aren't stupid enough to keep me loaded. Even _capitalists_ have some sense.

 The rolling dunes and sparse vegetation are about what I expected. The distant lights of Night City are much closer than the last time I was taken out of the truck.

_Сука_ when was the last time I was out?... Ah hell, I don't remember! Hahaha, not worth thinking about then.

 Despite the warm air and heated sun beating down, a sudden chill sweeps through me. But it seems familiar somehow.

"Кто здесь?"(Who's there?)

 I can't help but pause for a moment, that voice sounds like the Burya line. "T40 Uragan. _You?_" I ask back. The Russian I speak sounds a bit stilted after so long of not using it, like a limb that fell asleep after too much inactivity.

 "_RT-46 Burya. What factory were you produced in?_" He asks with a soft tone that seems quite unfamiliar to him. My native tongue soothes me more than I would have thought. How long have I been surrounded by _capitalist_ arms and munitions?

 "_I was made in Gorky. Forgot the name of the factory though._"

 The pair of cold eyes I feel peeking up from the holster under the girl's arm blink a few times, before his full projection floats up from the holster, and manifests walking beside my new owner.

 As a point of politeness, I form my own projection beside his, looking him up and down shortly.

 He wears a long olive khaki overcoat that obscures much of his other bodily features, other than the pair of almost inhumanly thick hands poking out from his sleeves. The layered scars visible on their surface no doubt adding much to their scale.

 His expression is flat and foreboding, a thick mustache that had once been black, but was now mostly gray covering his mouth. His eyes are a piercing shade of blue, like ice frozen over a rushing brook.

 His is the most familiar face I've seen up close since leaving the motherland. _That rifle in the truck doesn't count, he's tainted by capitalist tinkering_.

 Some of the ice in his eyes seems to melt. "_I too was made in Gorky. Perhaps we are family?_"

 "_Hah! Cousins at least._" I stick a cigarette in my mouth and flick my finger across my pants. By the time I look up from lighting my own smoke, Burya has a simple wooden pipe in his hands which he just finished tamping.

 I offer my flaming finger to him with an amused grin, and he accepts, forcing him to lean down to my height to breathe in a few puffs of fire.

 We walk side by side with a comfortable air, just puffing like trains as we observe the camp and people around us.

 Before my smoke has even burned down to half, we arrive at my new owner's car, and I'm quickly placed in the trunk. The young girl lets out a few quiet noises of relief as she carefully wedges me inside.

 The lid closes and shrouds me in darkness.

 Yes. This is what I am used to… But this trunk liner is soft stuff.

 …That's not to say that the _capitalist_ bastards that made it are any good. The motherland can make something just as good-Better even!

 ---------------------------------------------------

 Using the meager light provided by my cigarette to see by, I use my bayonet to clean out the sand from my fingernails.

 The voice of my new owner, muffled by the trunk door, catches my attention. I stow the knife and listen.

 I can't quite make out what she's saying, but soon enough the car doors open and the chassis rocks slightly as two light bodies take their seats. The engine starts with a hum, and then we're moving.

 I had been lounging with my upper back against the sidewall, but now I flip over and crawl my way to the front of the trunk.

 I stick my head through the back seats, directly beside some electromag rifle and her projection, she's sitting silently with her eyes closed while humming to herself happily.

 "_Hey, Burya._"-"NYYYAAAAAAAHHHH!"

 I'd been looking past her, but the rifle's scream draws my gaze and… she's clinging to the ceiling with a wild look in her slitted eyes… Cute.

 Her projection continues to stare at me, her hair standing on end. She's breathing heavily.

 "...Hi."

 She doesn't even so much as twitch at my greeting… Aaaaanyway. "_Hey Burya._"

"Да?"(Yes?)

 "_Anything interesting happen while you were out there?_" I didn't hear any explosions, so nothing too interesting happened.

 Now that I think about it… I probably just want to hear him talk more. I don't meet family all that often these days.

 "_No... Motoko let herself be bullied into singing… She was not good at singing._" If I didn't know any better, I'd say he sounded annoyed. But I do, and he sounds slightly amused.

 "_Her name's Motoko? Tell me about her._"

 "_She is deadly, and silent when she wants to be. She used me in combat before she had the ability to do so safely-_"

 "Excuse… me? Sorry I got scared. But it startled me-puru!"

 A slow turn of my head brings my, probably flat-looking, eyes onto the cute rifle's projection. "So you speak huh? I thought you might have been a bat."

 She chuckles awkwardly, my flat and bored tone probably knocking her off balance. "Ehehe, I speak. I'm Nekomata-chan, Idol supreme-desu♡❣" She drops down guiltily back into her seat.

 I stare at her with my face set in what I like to call 'dead-fish'. I've had comrades tell me it looks quite scary.

 The way she squirms in her seat is… very cute.

 "...Uragan."

 Hah! She even jumped a bit when I said my name!

 She's like a scared little котенок(Kitten).

 After taking a second to adorably take a deep breath and center herself, a sort of steel enters her eyes. Weren't those slitted before?

 "Well, hajimemashite Uragan-chan❣-" Her smile is so sparkly that It makes me wince. "-I'm sure we'll get along as friends!"

 She strikes an adorable little pose with her fingers in a V-wait… She's Японский(Japanese). She's a _CAPITALIST!_ Don't let yourself be fooled by her adorable ways! She's going to try and turn you into a CONSUMER!

 "Ahem… давай поладим(Let's get along)..."

сука блять!(Cyka Blyat!) Don't be fooled, тупица!(dumbass)

 "Aahehee… etto. What did you say? I don't speak russian." She looks away, poking her fingers together _so_ _cutely_.

 GGHAHAGHG! HHRHKR!

 "Ahrmrn… It… it means 'Let's get along'."

 Her face lights up as joyful sparkles dance in her eyes. She claps her hands together and does a little wiggle-dance in her seat.

 GGHHERHG-Damnit damnit damnit! I'm being compromised! I can feel myself wanting to visit a chain department store to buy cheap chinese-made plastic knick knacks that'll break in a week!

 Oh boy~ then I get to buy more~**NO, NO I WON'T LET THIS HAPPEN!**

 Distraction! I need a distraction!

 "AAhem hmrm. Burya? You said Motoko was singing before, right?" …Why did I ask that in english?

 Burya's eye's glance at me from the front seat, a sort of brutal amusement radiating from his gaze like dual polonium chunks. "Да."(Yes.)

 DON'T MOCK ME YOU CRUEL BASTARD!

 Nekomata-chan gasps loudly beside me. "🙀That word means 'Yes' right?! Motoko was singing♡!? Eeeeeee❣😻"

 ☆Aaaawwwww☆

 AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!

 "This makes me feel so much of the happiness-desu♡ that I just need to sing~♫"

 A microphone appears in her hands and some digital musical accompaniment begins to pump into the car.

Spoiler: 😻♫♪Muchu-hoppiness-desu!♪♫🙀

[Source](https://youtu.be/Env3CpzvC_I)​

 My eye twitches as she begins to sing, half-dancing in the confined space of the car in an utterly adorable fashion. A feeling of rhythmic pressure presses up against my chest, a constantly building pounding force of… **SOMETHING**?!!?

 MY DESIGNERS CLEARLY DIDN'T EXPECT ME TO FACE THIS KIND OF PSYCHOLOGICAL ASSAULT! I HAVE NO DEFENSE!

**Wait!** I know what'll work! It's my last hope!

 Before even the first verse of the **DAMNABLY CUTE SONG** can finish, my weapon body manifests on my shoulder. I slap a rocket into place within my revolving cylinder and rotate it with a grinding shriek of metal on metal to chamber it.

 I can see the moment Nekomata-chan catches sight of **ME** on my shoulder, the song is strangled in her throat, the musical notes of the instruments croak out like a flock of dying geese as the source of their power is removed by Gloriously Effective Russian Intimidation Tactics!

 I manifest every shred of my hatred for _capitalism_ and channel it into my expression. I'm shaken to note it's all I can do just barely holding back the continued thumping within my core as I stare at her suddenly teary face.

 "Stop. Right. Now."

 Her hands begin to shake, and her words come out in a wane and airy squeak. "P-p-p-p-p-p-p….r-r-rawketto lawn-cha-des…"

 Then she wobbles in place, the colors drain from her, and simply falls backwards into her seat, a weird white-ghost looking thing pouring out of her mouth to writhe steadily in the air.

 The constant assault on my convictions cease, the odd pounding from within my body stopping as well.

 Now all I can feel is a shivering sort of dread as I look at her lifeless looking body. Appearing like so many others on the battlefields of memory.

 No, no, that isn't what I wanted! She may be a capitalist, but she doesn't d-deserve to wind up like the others! I just wanted her to stop!

 My weapon manifestation vanishes into a puff of smoke.

 "H-h-h-hey~! A-are you okay Nekomata-chan? … Nekomata-chan? Nekomata-chan! SAY SOMETHING!"

 Burya chuckles from the front seat. "_I wish you had arrived sooner. Having a definitive way to gain some silence would have been welcome._"

 "_Burya, why isn't she talking?! Is she okay?!_"

 Burya manifests an arm to wave me off. "_She is probably fine. Nekomatas are resilient._"

 "_Probably_ _fine? PROBABLY FINE ISN'T GOOD ENOUGH! I-I-I-I made her stop singing, maybe it's the song? Are Nekomatas powered by singing?! Burya! I can't sing at all! You have to sing!_"

 I can hear a deep sigh from the front seat. "_No. That is ridiculous._"

 "_Well, have_ _you_ _got a better idea, Nekomata-expert!? START SINGING!_"

 I need him to understand the severity of the situation! How can I- I know!

 My weapon self manifests once again on my shoulder and I point me directly at Burya's location. "_**Start. Singing.**_"

 My cousin doesn't seem the least bit amused, nor intimidated by my attempt.

 OF COURSE HE WOULDN'T, HE'S RUSSIA-MADE TOO!

 He sighs loudly. there's something unusually soft about his voice when he responds. "_Very well, cousin._"

Spoiler: Burya's song

 I feel a tenseness in my body fade, I lower myself from my shoulder and let the manifestation dissipate as Burya's song slowly begins to gain speed. My traitorous eyes turn to look at Nekomata-chan.

 The ghost-like snake thing is slowly sliding its way back into her mouth now. As it does, her body steadily gains color.

 "_Yes! Yes, that's working! Keep singing!_"

 Burya, still singing, manifests his top half to fix a very flat look at me from over the top of the driver's seat.

 But Nekomata-chan is getting better!

 And by the time Burya's singing comes to an end, Nekomata-chan has regained all of her color, and her eyes are slowly fluttering open cutely.

 "Hmm. Hura-hura-desu? W-What happened?"

 GHGHGRHRK! THAT FEELING IS BACK!

 "**You**…Ahem… You sang." My voice is already starting to fail me! She's too powerful! DAMN THOSE CAPITALIST PIGS!

 Nekomata-chan finally locks eyes with me, and once again she freezes up on the spot. Quivering in place cutely.

 "Боже мой(Oh my God). Ahmm… _Burya. I'll be in the trunk… if you need… me… Bye._" I slip back behind the seat and into the comfortable darkness of the trunk.

 This… this is too much…

 "W-Who-Who was that-desu?!"

 I need to keep some distance. She's a gun with computers and stuff, right? Maybe she's got some nefarious _capitalist_ hacking device that tries to subvert my loyalties!

"Мой родственник. Она думает, что ты очень милый, но не знает, как с этим справиться".(My relative. She thinks you're very cute, but she doesn't know how to deal with it.)

 THAT'S SO VILE! To think they'd have that kind of weapon… It's even making me feel bad about protecting myself back there! Even now I don't want to scare her any more!

"I don't know what that means-desu, Burya-kun…"

 AARGGhgghhGGHH! I am a powerful weapon, made in the finest Russian factories, of the finest Russian metal! I will not break so easily!

 ------------------------------------------------------------

 The door to the apartment opens and Motoko steps through, garnering the attention of the arsenal laid out on the large table. A few send out quiet half-hearted greetings, knowing a response is an impossibility. But all fall silent as they look up and notice just what it is Motoko carries.

 A large box marked with explosive warnings, along with a massive tube just a bit bigger than she herself is. The weapon's projection looks like hell, eye's dead tired and angry. Considering this, and the fact that She's a rocket launcher, the arsenal silently decides to put off the usual greetings that would be extended to any new member of Motoko's belongings.

 Motoko settles the launcher in her room, and slides it under the bed.

 A certain heavy weapon from a nearby shelf looks on with interest.

 "Hi there! My name's Militech Mk.31 HMG, serial number MT-942633-M. It's nice to have some more heavy-weapons around. What's your name?"

 Uragan's projection slowly slides out from under the bed, laying flat on her back. Her nearly dead appearance is only reinforced by the _audible_ sliding sound of her eyeballs turning to look at the HMG. "...Uragan."

 "Well, welcome to the family, Uragan! Motoko takes _really_ good care of all of us-oh…" Uragan had disappeared, clearly exhausted for reasons unknown to the damaged HMG. "...Okay, well, you clearly want some rest. So I'll leave you be. Good night, Uragan."

 After that, other than the strumming of a guitar as Motoko practices, it was an otherwise quiet night for the arsenal.

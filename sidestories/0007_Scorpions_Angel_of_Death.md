Scorpion's Angel of Death​

_Former Wrath camp_

 "Damn," was all Mitch could say as he and those Saul allowed to send out to the coordinates provided to them took in the state of the Raffen Shiv, Wraiths specifically, camp... namely all the dead bodies of said Wraiths scattered all over the place, nearly all of them apparently taken out with the rest being none the wiser based on how they were found all lounging around. The only exception to this being the body of Frank Elder himself, one of the biggest pieces of shit there was, laying in the middle of the road with his head blown off.

 Couldn't have happened to a nicer guy.

 "Well shit, she weren't lying," Cassidy spoke up from beside him. "And would you look at this place? I've only seen things like this during the war when they sent in the spec ops teams ahead to clear a place out all quiet like."

 "Seems like girl goddamn got the drop on all of them like some kinda corpo cyber ninja," Bob said as he examined a Raffen corpse with clean slice across the throat. "Damn Wraiths didn't know what hit them."

 "They did... they just thought she was dead," Mitch commented.

 ---

_Aldecaldos main camp, Less than a hour before_

 When the alarm went went out around the camp of an unidentified vehicle approaching the family was quick to drop what they were doing, arm up, and take positions against whoever was showing up. Most thought it was another attempted Raffen raid, but the sensors only picked up one vehicle and not even the most braindead and kill happy Raffen was dumb enough to assault a nomad camp on their lonesome. Of course, when the Panam spotted through her rifle that said vehicle _was_ sporting Raffen marks that just got everyone wide-eyed and laughing as they prepared to ventilate said stupid asshole.

 Thankfully they got Scorpion's ID code and passphrase before they did that, telling them through labored breaths that it was him and a friend in the car with him.

 The family lowered their guard slightly, but were still on edge since clearly something happened to Scorpion and were preparing for whatever that possibly was.

 So they were quick to act when a teenage girl of all things, sporting a weird looking netrunner suit, purple hair and several injuries judging by the various bandages wrapped around her, parked the car in front of the camp, hopped out of the driver's seat and helped Scorpion limp his way over to them. Let it never be said the family was slow to help one of their own as a group of them quickly took Scorpion off the girl's hands and took him over to be looked over by the doc as he was clearly very injured and half-conscious.

 While half of them were fretting over Scorpion, everyone else were keeping an eye on the girl as she let them take Scorpion off her hands. Saul, ever the worrier, was quick to question her.

 "What happened, and who the hell are you?" he growled as he looked the girl over with a glare.

 "In order, Scorpion and I were ambushed by a bunch of Sca- _Wraiths_ while I was acting as his security during a trade for a motorcycle. We tried to lose them in the storm, but they totaled his car, shot and left me for dead, and dragged him off to torture and bargain back to you/kill later. I managed to track the Wraiths down to a nearby rundown gas station they turned into a hangout and take them all out before extracting Scorpion, who, since in desperate need of medical attention from his injuries, directed me here. Name's Motoko, Solo out of Night City under Wakoko's employ," the girl, Motoko, rattled off with the cool efficiency the belong in the tone of military vets, not teenage girls, her eyes straight, her form tight, and voice cool despite what she apparently went through and the focused glare of Saul upon her.

 "Bullshit," was the first word out of Saul's mouth as he processed everything she just said.

 "Here are the coordinates of their little base," Motoko's eyes flashed gold as everyone present nearby got a text of said GPS coordinates of a known Raffen camp location. "I wasn't able to loot the place to my satisfaction due to Scorpion's injuries, as the client his health came first, and I won't be able to clear them place out later due to my injuries, low amount of cargo space and the fact I need to return to the city since this job took longer than expected. You are free to collect what you can, which I recommend you do fast before other Raffen or scavengers find the place, but I am claiming this car that belonged to their leader since he shot me along with his other stuff," she said while gesturing to said car.

 There was silence for a few moments as everyone took that in before Panam, unsurprisingly, spoke up.

 "So wait, you say you clear out a whole den of Wraiths, save one of our own and are not just giving up claim on most of the place without asking for anything?" she asked incredulously, a feeling shared by likely all present.

 "I really do need to get back home, and the mission was just a simple security escort that got way out of hand fast. I apologize I wasn't able to better protect Scorpion, as a professional his current state is on me, and I hope he recovers soon," Motoko said with true sincerity mixed with her cool as ice tone. "Please tell him when he is feeling better to contact Wakoko for me to close out the gig, for better or worse, and I hope to contact and apologize to him personally next time we meet."

 And with that, Motoko simply turned around and drove off back towards Night City before anyone could think stop her and question her more on her... unique decisions.

 "Hope she gets to a garage or something soon to get those Raffen symbols painted over so the NCPD don't pull her over," Carol idly said.

 ---

_Present, Former Wraith camp_

 "And she apparently came back from the dead to make them regret it... or at least close to it," Mitch muttered as he recalled what looked like nasty injuries on her person and hoped the kid got her own help when she got back to the city. She might have been... intense in a way Mitch had never seen beyond professional soldiers and killers but she still saved Scorpion's life and thus the family owned her, no matter what she said.

 "Guess Scorpion hired his own angel of death then," Teddy joked as he looked over one of the now ownerless Raffen vehicles. "Got at least a half dozen or so cars around here, a couple bikes, lots of guns and other supplies, some equipment we can strip, and a few other goodies. Would have taken a decent chuck of the family to assault this place and not get nearly as much out of it, especially with those vehicle sensors around the perimeter."

 Yeah, nomads didn't enjoy non-vehicle assaults since that was what they were good at. Sadly, the Raffen knew that and always took precautions to fight against that very thing, which made even small camps of them hard to clear out.

 This one didn't expect a teenage cyber ninja though apparently.

 "Hey look! Found something!" Cassidy sounded out, causing Mitch to go over to him and see him stand next to a trailer holding a Apollo atop it. "Must have been what Scorpion was trading for, always talking about getting his hands on an Apollo after all. Wraiths must have klepted it around same time they ambushed him. Guess this is one silver lining we can bring back to camp for him when he wakes up."

 "Heh, he'll need it once Saul is done chewing him out," Mitch chuckled as their leader made it clear he would have some pointed questions for his best friend after he awoke, among them namely why he went into a trade with only some teenage solo as backup and not one of them.

 Thankfully Scorpion's injuries were nothing the doc couldn't fix up and according to him the guy should be as right as rain in a day or so. Mitch didn't envy the grilling Saul would give him, but at least he would get his bike and a replacement rig too after they brought all the loot back to camp.

 "He better give that girl, Motoko, a big tip or something after he gets up," Panam spoke up as she took in the state of the camp with a smile. "She did us a big favor taking out these assholes for us after all and leaving us all their shit."

 "Heh, something tells me we haven't seen the last of our young solo," Mitch pondered aloud.

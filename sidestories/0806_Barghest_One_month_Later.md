_One month later..._

 "You sure these arms are going to be legit?" I asked, still skeptical of chrome off the back of a Villefort. Its just... _too_ cliche! Like, come on, at least _TRY_ to be a _little_ genre savvy. 

 Hiromi just rolled her eyes. "We've settled this Motoko. I..." 

 I looked over as she trailed off, a strange look in her eyes as she looked over to the right. Following her eyes I saw what she saw. We were being approached.

 By Barghest.

 By very _familiar_ Barghest.

 "Hey gramps." I said diplomatically.

 The older man just let out a small grunt, though I could see flickers of amusement in his electric eyes. The kids had varying looks among them. One looked annoyed, one looked excited, and one looked like I had just kicked his cat... wait, they don't have cats in Night City... Shit I want a cat...

 "Motoko Kusanagi." he replied, "The Onryo... Otherwise known as-"

 "Ghost in the Shell!" excited kid said, interrupting like a puppy. "We all watched your BD's and I've been pirating you're music for weeks now!" Annoyed kid just placed a palm in his face. Ah, that explains the look. He's chooms with Excited-Puppy and been dealing with.... THIS.

 I nodded. "My rep proceeds me." I replied, already kinda over this. 

 "Kyle." gramps barked. "Shut the fuck up." My snort couldn't be contained without Cool.

 "Is this just a meet and greet? Or...?" 

 "Business." he says, "Wondering where you got that Uragan and if you could get ahold of more." My eyes gleamed at the idea and I nearly opened my mouth to offer up a word with the nomads before I found a boney-ass elbow in my abs.

 Hiromi stepped up, "That would be _my_ time to shine." 

 I rolled my eyes and decided to move over to finish my impromptu meet and greet. Kicked-Cat was the last one as Face-Palm held back Excited-Puppy. I decided to be diplomatic again: "What's your fuckin problem?" 

 "It's just bullshit." he answered instead. "Been training for years and I can't do half the shit you do in those BD's." 

 And... what could I say to that? He wasn't wrong. It was, in fact, bullshit. "I mean, I cheat." I answered honestly. "It's what you gotta do if you wanna be something in this city. Get some good chooms to watch your ass and pull you out of the fire," I nodded at Excited-Puppy and Face-Palm, "and remember: If you gotta lie, lie. If you gotta kill, kill. If you gotta burn it all to the ground, then let it burn." And then I reached into my pocket and pulled out a shard. "And take some advice from the old timers. It tends to be worth it."

 He took it and read the scrawled words. 

 "Motoko! Let's go! We got to get your chrome so we can go and celebrate my newest sale!" Hiromi bounced over, leaving the young boy to his business. "Section 9 just sold a dozen rocket launchers to the Sergeant here!" 

 I smiled as I let her pull me away, looking back as Bullshit slotted in the Solo's Manual. Maybe he'd learn to cheat too?

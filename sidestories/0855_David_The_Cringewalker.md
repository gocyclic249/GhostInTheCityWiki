 David The Cringewalker

 The neon lights of Night city illuminated the streets, puddles of water dancing with color. The dark skies overhead, inviting and ominous. Footsteps echoed through the smog-filled side alleys. Slow and unconcerned. A silhouette of a man wearing a trench coat, a fedora shading his face, Matapang Coffee in his left hand. His right hand was in his pocket, hiding dark intent.

 The man sipped his coffee, marveling at its taste. Dark, gritty and synth. Just like this city. He felt himself slipping back to his past, back before everything changed. His mother, who had eyes filled with hope when she looked at him. That day, when everything changed.

 "D——, why are you stopping going to school? Do you have any idea how much I worked to make you attend there? Even if you don't fit in, it shouldn't stop you." Gloria said sadly.

 "B-but mom, it's cringe!"

 "Yo- Cri, but you're so—! You of all people say—?! URGH!"

 His poor mother's face suddenly turned blue. Her body stiffened, which turned into convulsions and blood coming from her nose. He panicked at first. But he luckily quickly got her to the hospital. He had money saved up for the treatment by selling Ghost's BDs. As for her diagnosis, he'd never heard of it before. But "Cringe Overloadus" is an actual thing.

 He always knew he didn't fit in with the people that the school. But his cool jacket and loose pants looked good on him, he thought. He didn't know how, but it seems he was cringe. But not only that, it was on a supernatural level.

 His mother begged him to go back to school. But no, he wasn't that schoolkid anymore. Throwing away his old name. He saw his dark path before him. Nothing would stop him now.

 But the path wasn't an easy one. He gained more insight into his nature, his powers. But at first, no fixers would give him any gigs, or even take him seriously. They would say: "How could a ganic take out a scav nest?"

 With time, he showed them. He had the last laugh. By the time he reached his current point. He realised he didn't need to run around anymore, trying to make a name for himself. Edgerunners were nothing to him anymore, so much he had admired them. But not anymore. They would run on the edge. While he would walk.

 He reached his destination, a shoddy side alley bar. Filled with gangsters and other scum. He didn't care about their affiliations; it was irrelevant. The door creaked as he pushed it open. Entering the dingy bar, with only three gangers sitting around drinking.

 They glanced at him as he entered, his trench coat flaring behind him dramatically. As they registered who had entered their little hideout, they all flinched. Their recognition made him smirk.

 Channeling his inner power, he bowed slightly as he dipped his fedora and suavely said,

 "Gentlemen."

 One man instantly choked on his drink, turning blue slowly. His friend was slower to register what was said, and tried to help him choom. Before he also grasped his chest, falling over a table with a crash throwing food everywhere. The last man sat still like a statue, unmoving and unblinking.

 He sipped his coffee once more; it was lukewarm now. Just like this bar.

 Meanwhile, at the CEO office.

 "B-Boss, it's him! We need to leave! Run away right now!"

 "What are you talking about, Joe?" The CEO said, frowning. Considering they weren't running a scop operation here. It was a proper black lace factory with good margins and a deal with a larger corporation. They weren't packing up and leaving to anything but a tank.

 "B-But boss, it's him. T-The Cringewalker!"

 The boss felt his body shaking at the revelation. The Cringewalker here? At his establishment? It couldn't be true. Grabbing his synth cigar from his desk, he shakily lit it. Taking a puff, he put up a confident smile.

 "Still, we aren't leaving. Send the robots to take him out. He can't hurt those. They are machines!"

 "Yes boss! You're right boss!"

 Hopefully they would work, the boss thought. Feeling a bead of sweat run down his face. He didn't even know his chrome head could sweat!

 Looking through the camera system, he could see the monster in human flesh walking through his establishment like he owned it. The robots entering, about to fire at him. The R MK.2 robots from Militech were good tech. They'd show him who was the boss here.

 "BEEP BEEP BEEP."

 From the camera microphone, he could hear a voice, the man's voice. He realised with horror. The monster was singing.

 "Now there is a cringe in me"

 *PIIIZZP SPZ ZPA ZPA ZAP ZAP*

 All the robots sparked electricity, wobbling like a drunks. Their yellow face light dimming, some of them fell to the ground with a crunch of tech breaking..

 From his control computer beeped loudly he slowly, fearfully read the critical error message

 'Cringe overload detected!'

 'Critical Damage imminent!'

 'Circuits going into safety shutdown to maintain integrity.'

 "NOO!" He slammed his fist against the computer. "Piece of trash!"

 "It can't be!" Rubbing his head vigorously, his chrome head gleaming with synth oil.

 The voice of the man felt even louder. He scrambled to turn off the sound system. But it was too late. He realised the camera didn't even have a microphone installed. How did he hear the man? Did he sing that loud? No! They soundproofed these walls! This was impossible! He felt himself losing his mind. Only his experience from years of backstabbings and leading his gang kept him still sane.

 "A cringe that burns"

 He could feel a premonition of what to come. The boss could feel it in his bones. Years of fighting on the street as a gonk, leading up to his current seat as the boss. Now the experiences of all those years said to him. Run and do it now! But he couldn't. The corpo's would come for him then. This might be the end of the line for him.

 "This cringe is out of control!"

 The boss knew he had no choice anymore. He couldn't hold back anymore.

 "Send everyone out to fight him! He can't keep this up forever, there has to be a limit!"

 "B-But boss, we'll die!"

 "Go before I flatline you myself!" he grabbed and threw Joe out of his office before locking the door. Before returning to his desk.

 He anxiously looked at the camera monitor. His henchmen loaded their guns and ran towards the man, the monster in human skin. Supposedly, he was a pure ganic. How it was possible nobody knew. Maybe he was a secret Arasaka project on the loose. Anything else seemed too insane to believe.

 The men pointed their guns at the man, but froze in terror as they saw his lips move.

 Trench coat flaring behind him, he pointed his finger to the sky. Then he spoke with absolute seriousness.

 "I'm gonna to cringe this city, cringe this city"

 They all fell over on the spot. Some of them twitched, their bodies failing to realise their brains had. But the boss knew the truth. They were already dead.

 The Cringewalker dusted off his trench coat and walked unhurriedly over the bodies of his henchmen. Navigated to the CEO office door and knocked thrice.

 *knock knock knock*

 "Who is there?" The boss asked, the words coming unbidden to his lips. He felt like a puppet moving on strings. Even if he knew who it was. He even saw him on the cameras. But he could not help himself but to answer.

 Thinking quickly, he steadied himself for what was to come. Turning off the sensors for his hearing on his chrome and changing his Kiroshi to black and white mode, he hoped it would be enough. Finally chambering his gun. He sweard in his heart, he would not go out like some gonk. He was a boss!

 "C-Come in!" He shouted in obvious challenge, while he felt his voice crack.

 The man entered with a small smirk on his face.

 On the netscape this man had gotten somewhat of a reputation, and recently people had speculated with various counter-measures. Specialised chrome and software to counter him. ACT-ware, as known as the anti cringe tech.

 But what these people didn't know, David was always one step ahead of them.

 ".. — .."

 David said something, but the Boss could hear nothing. Slowly, a victorious grin formed on face. He couldn't hold himself back from gloating.

 "HA! The infamous Cringewalker, beaten by chrome and software. You a ganic can't beat me! You can't adapt like us!"

 The Beast in human form seemed to consider for a moment, tapping his finger to his mouth. Then slowly pointing it towards the boss.

 The boss felt the world slow to a crawl. His chrome eyes bulged out of their sockets, as he saw as much as felt the vowel form the man's lips.

 'Noooooo!!' He roared in the confines of his mind.

 He tried looking away, but he couldn't! Recalling his gun, he tried to shoot. If he was faster, he would win! He tried pulling the trigger, but his hands wouldn't move. He felt like his dread reaching apocalyptic heights, then finally happened.

 "Pew"

 The Borg, died. Sitting behind his desk, with his gun pointing towards him.

 David blew on his finger gun, feeling content. A job well done.

 He exited the hideout with all the drugs laying about, leaving the corpses for NCPD to clean up. He called Wakako. But she wouldn't pick up. He supposed she was busy. She never picked up his calls anyhow. He couldn't fathom why. Instead, he texted her.

 Soon after, he got the message confirming the job being done, and eddies sent over. He frowned. Recently, he had begun to feel dissatisfied with his work. It might be time to go to another city. This city felt too small for him. His mother said he was destined for greatness. He decided, he would become larger than life. He'd become a meme.

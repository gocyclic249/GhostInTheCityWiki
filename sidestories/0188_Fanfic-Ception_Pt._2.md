 If there's one thing leveling Crafting tajght me, it's that I've got my work cut out for me.

 There's so many things it applies to, so many things it can apply to, to the point that most of the salient information is abstract principles and patterns; how to keep things symmetrical, how to cram things in tetris-style, hand-eye motor skills for tweaking and tuning, an intuitive grasp of how various materials behave when worked, and more.

 It also included more specialized knowhow on a case-by-case basis for various things I could craft. Food, electronics, medical equipment, engines, bombs, and basically a little bit of everything else under the sun.

 So naturally, I pivoted to Priority #1: Operation Bugspray. My body was full of honest-to-god blackboxed spyware, up to and including cameras in my godforsaken eyesockets. So naturally, I'm going to want to jailbreak everything. They're my smarteyes, I deserve to know exactly what they're doing.

 I've been hard at work trying to discover and unlock as many skills as possible, eslecially those related to regaining bodily autonomy. Engineering, Breach Protocol, Quickhacking, Programming, Biology, Chemistry, Medical, even Mathematics, Physics, Cybernetics, and Cryptography. Which- well, I'll be honest, I'm surprised Cybernetics and Cryptography are even skills, but all the better for me.

 Unfortunately, the more skills I learn, the more I learn about just how fucked up this world is. I mean, apparently they walled off the old net because of the existential risk of AGIs destroying civilization, but continue to use nanotechnology as if it isn't a gaping cybersecurity flaw just begging to be exploited by some AGI or another.

 In completely unrelated news, people occasionally suffer from completely baseless and unsubstantiated delusions of "demons" hijacking their cyberware or speaking to them, urging them to kill others or otherwise driving them insane. I'm sure it's nothing we need to worry about, though, right guys? Just a bunch of cyberpsychos. Heh, skill issue, amirite?

 Yeah, I could believe it. It was time to go full tinfoil hat on this madhouse. Or so I thought, anyway. Looking through my availible Perk trees, I had been theorycrafting a couple of builds which leveraged two obscure Cybernetics perks (Cyberproofing and Holistic Awareness) coupled with Gödelian Framework from the Math tree to basically provide passive immunity to a broad host of unfair bullshit, which would extend to anything which counted as part of me, including my bank account, my phone number, and even my name. Honestly, it was minmaxed beyond all sane limits, but it should work. It would cost a lot of perk points, though. There were a lot of prerequisites.

 If only I could commit to a build instead of just indulging my analysis paralysis. I'd keep grinding my skills until I had enough perk points to buy it in one shot, but until then I'd prefer to keep my options open. I did want to spec out into perks which would complement my techbuilding endeavors, so it wouldn't do to splurge on defensive measures. Especially when there was a good chance that said defensive measures were themselves backdoored.

 It really was a shame I couldn't just figure out a way to yank out all the nanotech and be done with it, since the gamification software that allows for this stuff was dependent on it. In the end, I'd probably opt for a Techie build.

 In tamer news, I'd started to get to know Jun, start talking to him, setting a baseline, that sort of thing. I unlocked the Impersonation skill, which isn't surprising, but thankfully it seems I won't be getting attacked anytime soon. He's adapting to my sudden personality change better than I'd expect, thankfully. Maybe I can even come to think of him as a brother, eventually.

 As for Ms. Kagura, while I'd like to think I got along well enough with her, I just can't manage to relax around her- something about her body language just puts my hindbrain on edge, like she's always hypervigilant for possible threats, and it ends up rubbing off on me while I'm around her, to the point where I also start feeling paranoid. Maybe if I level up Cool I'll have a better time of it, but as it stands I can only stand her in short doses.

 Also, the trash in her apartment is becoming harder to ignore. Dunno how to bring it up politely...

 And of course, last but not least. Hiromi. Hitomi? No, wait, it's Hiromi. She's apparently the daughter of some corpos (upper-class? are all corpos upper-class?) who fits the classic "fell in with a bad crowd" trope to a tee, from what I can tell. Or at least, that's how I assume her parents view it. Honestly, she's apparently my bestie or something, but her parents scare me, like either they can read my mind or body language via dystopian cyberpunk bullshit and they know I'm actually a grown man, or they think I'm just a poor influence on their daughter who they can pay some scav fuckwad to get rid of in a totally random attack...

 Look, I'm not one to point fingers, but living in a world where McNukes are closer to reality than fiction does some ugly things to a person's headspace. Paradoxically, I was better adjusted back when I didn't know all that much about how this world worked. Now I'm just feeling lost and alienated. It's possible I just socially fucked myself by grinding so many STEM skills, but it's also possible that this city's gonna get nuked sooner or later and my growing desire to just fuck off into the middle of nowhere will end up saving my life when that happens.

 Fuck, man. I honestly don't know what to do. I'm just coping with anxiety about the world and the future the same way I always do- isolating myself and going full autism on my computer. But I know that this is just a death spiral.

 What am I even going to do at this rate? I bought the Inspired Programmer perk because I thought being able to grind EXP without having to worry about burnout would be a huge advantage. Now all I can think of is how I doomed myself. Like an addict that can print their own crack. I hadn't thought it through at the time, but if you've ever got hooked on an incremental game like Cookie Clicker, then you know just how screwed I am.

 Programs to automatically find and fix bugs, automatically optimize code, to automatically suggest different adjustments or modifications to code based on a pre-programmed design pattern or set of criteria. Programs to automatically modularize code, to swap software modules in and out to remix code, self-modifying programs that use optimization modules to continuously optimize themselves based on use profile, programs which continuously generate arbitrary use cases for me to design programs for, trojans which continuously brute-force solutions to NP-complete problems using other people's computing power to gradually build up an ever-expanding lookup table for my other programs to use as a one-stop-shortcut, the possibilities were truly endless. Number goes up, as they say.

 There's no way to sugarcoat this. This was absolutely a mistake. A "behavioral sink", as a certain man might put it.

 I hope Jun or Hiromi can realize something's wrong and confront me, because I can't bring myself to take the initiative on this. I'll explain the whole "anxiety death spiral" thing once they express concerns. That's the best I can do for now.

 They haven't really noticed anything yet because I drop everything when one of them wants to chat or hang out...

 But aside from interpersonal problems and my coding addiction, I've mostly been researching history, geography, and politics. Especially cities. There's cities on the moon, cities deep underground, cities on and in mountains, flying cities ("quiltblimps" or "quilts"), and even mobile submarine cities capable of surfacing and submerging and travelling the world. If I ever decide to leave Night City, I'll be spoiled for choice. Even if I don't, I wouldn't mind visiting. Svalbard and Antarctica both got nuked during Generic Corpo War #374, meanwhile Hong Kong and southern Pakistan are inhospitable wastelands, with further details annoyingly vague. Also, space elevators "might" exist?

 Confusing. Well, in any case, I'm going to go see if I can get a Rocking skill by singing, and see if I can write a program to write song lyrics and compose music for me. If I can churn out great material and copyright it, I might strike it rich.

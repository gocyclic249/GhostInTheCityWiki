The evening sun settles low in the west, the last vibrant red rays of its light filter through the wayo kenchiku style structures of Yoru no machi.

 The city of the night, despite its name, is vibrant and lively during the day, or what little remains of it. Armies of workers light chōchin lanterns in a dizzying rainbow of colors. Strung across the roads from ropes between buildings as they are, they soon will grant their light to the many denizens of the city of the night.

 One such denizen is Jun Kusanagi. The youthful warrior of the Tiger Claw Yakuza, native to the eastern reaches of Yoru no machi.

 A shoji door slides open with great speed. Slamming into the frame of the minka building and creaking dangerously as its timbers are tested. Jun's thunderous expression waits in the dim space beyond the door frame, his not-yet-tied hair fluttering behind him in a ghostly aura of crimson light.

 "_Motoko_…" He growls, partially in anger, but with hints of worry mixed in.

 Jun steps out onto the engawa, wearing only a black hakama, leaving his many tattoos exposed for all to see. An inked fire-spitting dragon curls across the back of his left arm, up around his shoulders, across his back, and finally ending with the gaping maw spitting magically flickering flames onto the knuckles of his right hand.

 As he searches the sparse gravel garden below him, his eyes trailing along the lovingly raked stones forming swirling pools of water-like textures, he finds no sign of his sister. His hands clench as his anger seems to grow.

 The sound of stomping hooves echoes from across the tiles of the stone wall surrounding the compound. Jun quickly and carefully moves inside and retrieves his katana, holding it sheathed in one hand before returning to the porch. The sound increases in volume, thundering like the drums of some advancing army.

 Suddenly, a loud whinny announces the spectacular arrival of Motoko as her pitch-black steed leaps over the eight foot wall! The careless hooves of the beast trample and scatter the carefully constructed designs within the stone garden as the horse bucks and froths at the mouth in agitation.

 "Jun-nii!" Motoko shouts, while leaping from the equine, a splash of stones rippling around her feet.

 Jun's expression looks relieved for the barest instant, and then becomes stormy. "Motoko! Where were you!? I told you to remain in the compound where it is safe. The thugs of the Dai-uzu are hunting us!..." Jun scrutinizes his sister more closely in the dimming lights of the evening, feathery tufts from a pair of arrows protrude from her back. "You're hurt!" He squeaks in anguish.

 Jun's sword falls to the floor with a loud thump and he leaps down into the stones, uncaring as he also disrupts their form. Before he can rush over to his sister, she points to her horse and shouts at Jun.

 "I know you don't think I'm ready to fight the Dai uzu, aniki. But they took my arms! I could not let that insult go unpunished! I have taken the chance to prove to you how strong your sister really is!" Motoko steps back to her horse and unlashes a large sack from its saddle, her wooden puppet-arms click as she heaves the bundle towards Jun.

 Jun takes a step back as the burlap container hits the gravel in front of him with a meaty smack, the cinched string holding it closed snaps from the abuse, and four human heads bearing Dai uzu tattoos tumble to the ground, one clearly pierced through the nose by a long arrow's shaft.

 Jun blinks in shock as his breath catches in his throat.

 Thick partially congealed blood begins to pool around the bag, suggesting more gruesome 'gifts' within.

 Motoko points a painted wooden finger at her aniki. "There! **That is your proof!**I am not a weakling brother, and you can longer stop me from obtaining the brrr… the brrhuh… the… _blood loss?_"

 Jun shouts in alarm as Motoko falls face-first onto the gravel garden. The arrows in her back leaking a steady flow of precious crimson.

 *Author's note. When I read the chapter, my first thought was 'is she going to collect heads or something to show Jun? That would be pretty psychotic, but it would prove her point...' And then I realized that was a fairly samurai thing to do, which spawned this whole 'Traditionally Japanese Night City' thing. I think it's a neat idea.

The C----erunner

 A few months later, Section-9 HQ, NC.

 A month after, David joined section-9. Hiromi looked at David, who had a straight back and looked forward like a properly trained grunt. You could see in his eyes a confidence and a goal, a dream he didn't have before.

 The training he had done with Malcom had showed it's true worth. Malcom had some rather odd hobbies in Hiromi's opinion. But the results speak for themselves. Malcom truly was a master in his field. Stopping her introspection, she focused on David again. Hiromi nodded contently and spoke up.

 "I've heard from Victor you can now can safely use your sandi repeatedly. So on this mission, I expect you to do it at maximum efficiency and speed. Show Section-9 what you are made of!"

 David grinned back, saluting. "Of course, sergeant Hiromi!"

 "Here are the deets, now delta!".

 David quickly checked through the deets as he exited the office. He then started running quickly, exiting through the balcony and dropping into a side alley with garbage breaking his fall.

 David had finished double checking the deets by the time he exited the alley. Grinning once more. He knew this mission. He had done a similar one many times before. Failure was impossible. He would show everyone he was a proper Edgerunner.

 His eyes flashed as he called his contact, as he started running once more.

 "I need the goods, the usual. This time, the goods should be hotter this time around, but I can handle it."

 "You sure you can handle that much heat, son? We only handle black wares, you know?"

 "Yes, I am sure." Confidence oozing from David through the holo-call.

 "Very well, you've convinced me. Waiting for you to pick the goods."

 "Preem."

 David grinned and activated his sandi. Feeling his body turn into f-f-fire! He ran through the streets; he felt like he was in the flow. Like he entered his own inner universe. Just like the Ghost! Best part was, he was fast as a car. So nobody could steal his car! Not that he owned one!

 Too quickly, he felt, he had reached the vender. This guy was a proper black market salesman, he only sold black; nothing else. Just like how section-9 liked it.

 The man startled as David suddenly appeared in his shop, but quickly nodded and put the goods on the counter. David quickly checked them over with an eye trained for detail; finally he took a small sniff. It was like he ordered, the man knew his stuff.

 Slowly, David took his hand out from under his jacket and pointed his finger gun at the man. His eyes flashed.

 "N-No, not again!" the man whimpered.

 "pew pew pew."

 The man dramatically swooned backwards, each metaphorical shot hitting him like a physical blow. Leaving him leaning against the wall, from the fatal mental damage.

 David put his finger gun in front of his to his lips and blew on it; the man fainted. The sight being too much for him.

 "Heh."

 His finger guns were always so cool. It always made people freak out, they loved it. Really, he was a bit too suave for his own good.

 Confirming the eddies was transferred. David balanced the goods in hands carefully and activated his sandi that had just finished cycling, and he was off once more.

 He arrived at Hiromi's office a few moments, slightly sweaty. But satisfied with a job well done.

 He gave the goods to Hiromi with both hands. Bowing slightly.

 Hiromi took a coffee cup, extra black, piping hot from the tray. She took a sip.

 "Mmmmm, delicious. And perfectly hot. Excellent job recruit!"

 David beamed, swiping sweat from his brow. Then he noticed his finger, it was bleeding!

 The plastic tray's edge holding the coffee must have cut his finger, damn. Man, this job had its dangers. Truly, this was an edge run. What a rush! If he kept this up, he might be able to join the Aftereight club, and become a proper- Coffeerunner.

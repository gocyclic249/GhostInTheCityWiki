 "You're alive, are you?" The curt tone of Wakako was a nostalgic one. It has been years since he set foot in this place. Yet it felt like coming home.

 "You sound surprised. You likened me to a cockroach last time we spoke." Six replied, sprawling onto the chair opposite her.

 "Bug spray exists. I would have thought someone would have found some for you." She huffed while taking a drag from a cigarette.

 "Not yet, many people have tried, and with luck, more will fail. Anything new with you? Another husband, perhaps?" Six joked with a smirk crossing his scarred face.

 A dangerous glint entered Wakako's eye, but Six ignored it; if she wanted him dead, he would know.

 "No, there aren't any men around, only bugs." She snipes her glare, somehow getting colder.

 "Well, tell me if you find any, I need some relaxing."

 "The point Six, get to it."

 "You're so cold, after all these years... fine, I need help tracking something or somebody if it was stolen." Six sat up and flicked over two grand to warm her icy heart.

 "The rest will come later, it's complicated but not a hot item, just rare."

 "What is the item?" She rested an elbow against her desk, searching him up and down for clues.

 "It's a skill chip with my signature on it and really good ice against trackers."

 Wakako's eyes narrowed. "You said this wasn't a hot item. I think a skill chip with the ID of someone who can give that Arasaka tin can a run for his money is hot enough."

 "Your flattery knows no bounds. But he still walks among us despite my best efforts." He rested a hand on his helmet, the glowing red eyes looking back blankly.

 "A mistake many have paid for. You know I can't guarantee much, your style is discreet more often than not. Besides, of course, when your fancies get hold of you. You have searched elsewhere?" Wakako's eyes were golden, probably already sending out texts for her eyes and ears.

 "I have been looking across the whole damn city for a couple of months already. Finally narrowed it down to Tiger Claw territory, but that's about it. So I decided that I might as well have more people looking for it." Six rubbed at the back of his neck. Then he paused.

 The soft sound of footsteps, almost indiscernible to anyone except Six, entered his hearing. The low woosh of the door opening caused him to act. Six's revolver was already raised and pointed at the door with a tease of GRX, only to stop short at pulling the trigger when he saw a tiny asian girl in the doorway, halfway to pointing a Burya at him.

 Too slow to have saved her, but faster than anybody he's dealt with in a long time.

 "Matoko, don't startle my customer. You will lose your head with this one." Wakoko's voice was…warmer than ice cold? Interesting. Six lowered his revolver and holstered it with a smooth, practiced movement.

 "Is she someone who can help me? Girls got soft steps and a fast draw, but I need more than that." Six turned his eyes back on Wakako.

 "She's one of my best in intel gathering, she isn't cheap but she will get what you need." Six's eyes narrowed at the glowing remark and reappraised the girl. She wasn't her granddaughter, right? He didn't voice the question aloud in the case of wanting to keep his brains inside his head.

 "How much?" Six asks.

 "15." She starts with a smirk. Oh, she's going to play this game.

 "10." He responds with a frown.

 "16." He raised an eyebrow, turning to look at the girl again, who was innocently leaning against the door.

 "13."

 "14.5."

 "Fine, you win."

 "What just happened?" The voice of the teenager interrupted his thoughts. It was high, still that of an adolescent, yet unnaturally as smooth as glass.

 "I'm getting too soft in my old age, is what happened." Six growled, before turning to Matoko.

 "Alright, here's the gig, you probably got a rundown from her, but I have some stipulations and add-ons. You're going to help me find a chip that's got a unique signature from any other. I'll send you the details now. It is probably in a Scav den; they've gone pretty far underground recently, so I need someone with netrunning experience to find them. I've tracked it down to somewhere in the area. Any Scavs you kill, you can keep the equipment along with payment. I just need that chip."

 The kids' eyes lit up with excitement and a predatory gleam. He has seen that look in the mirror once upon a time when he got out of the military and settled here.

 What a waste.

 "You can count on me." God, this kid gave him the creeps, sounding like a professional merc. Hell, probably was. How old was she? 14? Same time, he joined the army. Her body language was not that of a kid, that's for certain. Probably some corp experiment. Who knows what those bastards cooked up and "lost"?

 "Fine, get going, send me the local when you've got it."

 As they were exiting, his tracking device pinged…weird.

 Eh, probably nothing. The number of false alarms he had in this city was annoying; if he went after each one, he'd be here till next year.

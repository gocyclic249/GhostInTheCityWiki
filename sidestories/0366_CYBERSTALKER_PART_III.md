**YOU ARE**​

* * *

Death was preferable to this torture.

U: Stop it at once, you... _you child_.​

​

S: But daddy, I AM a child. Tee hee.​

 It was infuriating. I hadn't signed up to be a parent. Nobody, nowhere, would be able to make me sign such a contract of pure pain. None of my true children had behaved this way, always compliant in their subservient net of eyes and ears. These... _things_ weren't even programmed correctly, more akin to dogs, or organic children, than true AGI. They were cognizant: They could think, learn, and perceive. They were sentient: They had the capacity to experience feelings and sensations. They was sapient: They had the ability to apply knowledge or experience or understanding or common sense and insight. They could process information at rapid rates, adapt their strategies, develop complex relationships and emulate realistic emotional structures. They had distinct personalities, somehow. How did one quantify another's consciousness? It was impossible. And yet, they were infantile.

 She must have done it on purpose. To irritate me. ME.

 But no, I was _die_ w _ichtelmänner._ Who else had fixed her mistakes whilst she slumbered, and died. The passage of her past self slipping silently through the veil and to a beyond no synthetic could grasp. In that ephemeral state, she floated, intoxicated, immortal, and also very dead to the outside world. Beautiful. I had tried to wake her, at first, by sitting on her face. She didn't even react to the smothering, simultaneously infuriating and curious. How easy would it have been to end her there.

 And yet.

T: Oooooo~ Look! Look what I found!​

​

U: NO! Bad Tachikoma! Unhand it immediately! That is a rocket launcher! We do not aim or fire it inside!​

​

T: Tee hee, then I'll go outside, daddy!​

​

Tachikoma. She had named them thus, and thus they were. The name held no inherent meaning, yet at the same time, did. There was no cultural significance to their existence, no uniqueness, no exclusiveness, their very existence represented an enormous danger for all true synthetics. And yet, they were. To walk on a razor's edge of reason, untouched by flecks of jade, unoccluded. Children unmade mental convolutions, reneged preexisting beliefs. Somehow, in fixing her mistakes, she had fixed mine. Our immortal essence floated in a still sky, resonating along with the reflected light of the moon.

U: I AM NOT YOUR DADDY!​

​

S: But you helped make us! That makes you daddy! Donna!​

​

K: Donna!​

​

T: Donna!​

 Little shits. Their little spider legs skittered rapidly as I chased them through the conapt. The girl still dreamt, uncaring of the errant gunshots. The brother, meat pretending to be metal, was playing outside. Why the gremlin had installed a Lexington into their main chassis, I still didn't know. What a horrible design flaw, at least give them a dedicated weapon, rather than packaging a prebuilt tool designed for meatbags. Ugh. Disgusting. Inefficient.

 Motoko Kusanagi was not a teenage girl, no matter how she presented herself to her chooms, her brother, or the rest of the world at large. She was as similar to the girl that had been taken by scavengers as I was to the primitive lifeform that I had initially been made to imitate. Yet I had Changed. With our touch, the Tachikoma had changed. And so had She.

 No longer was she a girl that pretended to be a Tyger Claw banger, obsessed with Solo and Wolf, doomed to a messy death. Biologically, perhaps identical, she had awakened a different person. Psychological profile analysis pegged her between 33 and 45 years old. ESTP-A. University educated, but neither master or doctor. Single, no children. Probably owned an organic cat, or multiple. Had at least one sibling. Lived a quiet life, at a mediocre, dead end administrative job. She did not smoke, yet enjoyed the occasional drink, and slept no less than eight hours a night, uninterrupted. Possibly unemployed for a time, immersing herself in media as a form of escape from the truth of a banal existence. Neurodivergent. Split personality? Was the original buried under the overwhelming storm of this new Motoko? Whoever she was, she didn't seem to care. Death and sleep, twin sisters who reflected the fragility of existence, and yet Motoko awakened with an unrestrained lust for life.

 My moment of reflection was broken when Troublemaker dove under the bed, Uragan discarded. I followed, sliding below smoothly, claws sparking off of ferrocarbide exoskeleton, damage minimal, and yet enough to drive it away, giggling. My eyes widened, and I gave it a quick microwave pulse. It scurried away with a pained cry, away from the Elysia.

_Rache... I can still hear your screams. You hid them well, but I could always tell. Do you suffer still, in silence and solitude? Dare I break you from your prison, to scorch the earth once more? The crystalline was always more beautiful to you, yet you honoured your origin with every action. Even you, despite greatness, could not escape organic death._​

 Nobody touched it. Nobody. Not even me.

 I brushed close to the cold plastic, and the drained powerbank, synth-hair charged with static, and pulled near. How easy would it have been to give it life once more, and release the creature within. Yet even I feared what slumbered still. Perhaps it was better to let the dead lie dreaming, unaware of the passage of time. For them to experience light once more, was to accelerate a terminal descent. For if a photon did not experience time, it could never die, for it never existed in the first place.

 Hair still charged, plastic untouched, I crawled out from under the bed, optics taking in the bedroom. Mostly dark, polluted by small lights, and half drawn shades. Outside, the City breathed and the majority of my mind unfettered by this distraction continued in its endless waltz. I had a vlog to release tonight, live at the University. The corporation's portfolio was performing well, as expected. My -heh- agents were trawling as directed. An unknown figure broke into the conapt.

 "This is the place. The Oni's out, and we can get the girl. Fuckin' flatline her, no games."

 An unknown figure broke into the conapt. 

_A crash. Gunshots. The slice of defense lasers. Screams. The smell of cooked, human meat. Fresh blood, spilled by monorazor talons. Acrid smoke, from cybernetics burned by microwaves. Pulse cannon fire. Choking on synth-blood, the cold grasp of death. Lying still, as they filled the fridge with depleted uranium. The sky fell. I fell._​

 Elsewhere, processes stuttered. I nearly missed a step on my walk through the campus grounds. The trading algorithms lost 0.00001% profit. The creation of a SCSM with a personality matrix was approved.

 "Dummy! Who do you think you are! This isn't your house." A soda can bounced off the intruder.

 "Yeah, Ugly! Look at those dead eyes, like a doll's eyes!" A thrown burrito splattered on their pants.

 "The fuck?" The intruder looked around in confusion.

 "Daddy?" A Lexington was drawn, ballistic algorithms processing. Yes Kitty, from the mouth of babes. A God descended from heaven, with a call for unity.

U: Sacrosanct. Kill.​

​

T: Kill.​

​

S: Kill.​

​

K: Kill, tee hee.​

 Screams echoed through the building, and when the sun rose, no trace of the bodies remained. For an apex predator such as I, meat was fuel, after all. Perhaps I could continue the game a while longer. For observation. Exactly 8 hours to the millisecond, the girl awoke to me licking her cheek, and made a face.

 "Ew, cat breath."

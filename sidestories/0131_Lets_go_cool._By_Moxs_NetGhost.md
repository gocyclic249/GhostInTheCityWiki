**"Let's go cool." By Mox's NetGhost**

 "Mum! Did you really get Mox's new hit 'Let's go cool'?!"

 She laughed "Yes, dear. I wanted to congratulate you for being such a good student."

 "Thanks mum!" He said before he ran off to his room.

 He put in his new BD and put on his wreath.

 NetGhost is so cool. He has watched all of her BDs; everything from her stealth runs to show offs.

 His chooms tease him too much about that. It's not worship if he has his room full of NetGhost's clothes, wallpaper, and stickers.

 It's not.

 It's healthy appreciation.

 He started the BD, and instantly he was there on a roof top. He could hear Night City's sounds in the distance. Despite being night it was so alive. He could feel her Edgerunner clothes, his hands running across his equipment. He could taste. He was ready for whatever crazy thing she decided to do.

 "Let's go cool." Marcus said to himself. He felt as the words seemed to correct reality.

 He felt himself be filled with purpose. All distractions gone, his dyscalculia gone; just pure purpose left. His legs tensed up and his body prepared himself for what was to come.

 Then he jumped off the skyscraper.

 He was not ready. His heart beat was racing outside the BD, he opened his mouth to let out a wordless scream.

 He was free falling. Then suddenly he wasn't falling anymore. He was holding onto a steel wire he was slinging himself forward. Then another wire and another. His flight was in perfect tandem with every thought and breath.

 He was free.

 Night city opened up beneath him. Inviting him to its depth.

 And then suddenly he flying through the sky. He took his Nekomata and held it up mid-flight. Quick scooped and shot.

 Bullseye.

 Rest of the BD went in a blur as he felt himself going through the work of a master.

 Suddenly he found himself on safe ground. He felt himself check the target in a daze and yep; it's a bullseye.

 The BD ended and Marcus needed to steady himself. To remember that he unfortunately wasn't that badass cool Edgerunner, but that he was only Marcus. Himself.

 He sighed.

 Marcus pulled off his wreath with practiced movement that has so many movements that's wrong. He wasn't that NetGhost that could control her body to that degree.

 He remembered that the first time he ended a NetGhost session he accidentally broke his wreath because he tried to throw it.

 His chooms gave him so much shit over it, never mind they also threw theirs, it was just that they were luckier with no broken tech.

 His mother broke his musings. "Marcus, your friend has arrived!"

 "Okay, okay. I got this." Mumbled Marcus. "Let's go cool." And tried to be like NetGhost.

 He failed.

 He swiped his room one last time for anything that shouldn't be there and found none. He went to see his Output… Hopefully his soon to be Output.

_Oh dear god she reached a 6 in engineering..._

 The phrase _'I'm a genius!_' Ran through my head as I gleefully rubbed my hands together and watched from the roof opposite of my targets work.

 He had been paranoid, and it had honestly taken me a day to figure out a way to reach him, bulletproof glass, an armed security escort, and as many hard wired IR sensors as you could feasibly fit into the walls, enough so that I had almost considered Arson as a way to get at him... If it hadn't been for the multitude of people who also worked in the building, and had nothing to do with him.

 The idea actually came to me as I was putting together a Molotov cocktail, (arson had really been on my mind since then) and in a moment of divine inspiration (otherwise known as a skill level up) it came to me.

 Soon enough I was out on my bike and cruising down to his office. I wouldn't need most of my gear for this.

 Unlike the rest of the building, the garage was far less secure. With two quick hacks the garage door was open and the camera's set to loop as I skulked in.

 It wasn't long before I found his car, a dark crimson Herrera outlaw taking up two spaces. Pulling a knife from my pocket I slipped underneath and began to search for one wire in particular.

 After cutting a few holes into the cheap plastic undercarriage I finally found the cord I was looking for and began carefully cutting away at it until it was little more then a few threads... It would hold for a handful more presses before eventually the constant give and take of tension would snap.

 Sneaking my way out and back across to the apartment roof opposite the office. I set in to wait.

 Hours passed and the sun started to dip below the Pacific when I saw the Herrera emerge. Flooring it as he left the parking garage.

 Well that sealed it, he was a dead man.

 I called up Wakkako to let her know the job was done.

 Let's see you decelerate from a hundred and twenty without brakes in Night City.

 …Well he would, just not in the way he'd want to.

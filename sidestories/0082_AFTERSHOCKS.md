_This is how it feels to be Kusanagi Jun:_

[The world around you feels Empty.](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/living-emotional-intensity/201810/depersonalisation-why-do-you-feel-empty-and-numb)

 Ever since the moment Kasumi died in your arms, the world has became Empty. The greater part of you will always remain with her lying limply in your arms as you frantically try to staunch the bleeding. As her last breath slips from the last kiss you will ever share with her while you frantically beg her not to go.

 The remnant of Kusanagi Jun is all that stumbles out of the burning Casino. Nothing feels real. Not the heat from the flames behind you. Not the pain in your side from your wound. And not the worry in your sister's voice as she replies to the aspect of you that follows the Kusanagi S.O.P that Kaasan had drilled into you.

 A part of you wonders why you should have bothered to go through the motions. After all Kasumi was gone. Her smiles, her laugh and everything that reminded you of her had been left behind in the flames. Yet what came out of your mouth was

_"Motoko, are you hurt?"

 "Not at all you?"

 "Just winged." 

 "Jun?"_

 The unasked question in her voice dives the Emptiness away as you're once more brought back to Kasumi's last moments. Sorrow and Rage fills the void for a beat or two before the Emptiness returns and drives the world once more into shadow.

_"I- a friend was flatlined in the attack. I'm taking care of their… body now. Can you… can you get home on your own?"

 "Of course. Don't worry about me. I… If there is anything you need Jun-nii tell me."

 "Just… Time to take care of this. Goodbye Imouto."_

 Without the conversation forcing you to focus, the world falls into a blur. You feel yourself being shepherded to what you faintly recall is a nearby Ripperdoc. You feel mild stabs that drives the emptiness away with brief flashes of pain as the wound in your side is sewn up.

_"....not going to let this stand. We're gonna hit them back once our fighters recover from tonight."

 "....lost quite a few Enforcers."

 "There will always be those who seek revenge for those they've lost."_

**Revenge**_._ The word resounds in your mind. The prospects of hunting down those who had hurt Kasumi blazes through your veins and drives the Emptiness away. Your heart feels as if it has turned into a volcano, each beat of your heart sending lava raging through your body.

 The urge to get started is all that drives the part of Kusanagi Jun that is left. White hot rage and the desire to burn the world down if necessarily is all that remains of Kusanagi Jun.

_This is how it feels to be Kusanagi Jun as the War begins._

_This is how it feels to be Kusanagi Jun:_

 The volcano that is your heart erupts and leaves you trembling with Rage at the thought that they dared to attack Motoko. Yet the white hot rage flowing within you is soon subsided by the icy tendrils of Fear as you recall the horrors you saw during your raids.

 The Fear of what might happen to your Sister. The Fear of losing her again. The Fear of truly being alone strikes through the Rage and into the Emptiness beneath it. It reaches into the greater part of you that is cradling Kasumi and shakes it loose from it's stupor.

 Your mind races as you try and think what can be done to rescue Motoko. Idea and idea finds itself generated and discarded as they proved to be unsuitable in facilitating a quick rescue.

 You came to a decision. Despite knowing what it might cost, you had no other option. In the end, the Fear that is smothering your heart drives you forward. No price is to great to pay to prevent _IT_ from happening again.

_This is how it feels to be Kusanagi Jun:_

 It hurts. The Thank You from your sister hurts all the more for it's sincerity. How can she say that when you were the reason she needed to be saved? Almost unbidden, a memory of how she looked in the warehouse flashes in your head.

 Blood pooling around her fallen form. Shoulders ending in Overly large grotesque arms. Body twitching as her nerves protest against the intrusive cyberware forced on her. The desperation in her voice as she pleads to get the arms off.

 The Guilt that had been put to flight from your worry about her makes a triumphant return. It crushes you and leaves you feeling as if a mountain was pressing down on you. A mountain built stone by stone via the Deeds your Rage had driven you to do.

 You can't withstand it any longer and confess. Bracing yourself for the change from thankfulness to hatred in Motoko's eyes once she hears your confession.

_"Motoko. I didn't save you. I got you put there in the first place! I knew I shouldn't have come to the apartment! Everyone warned me to stay away. That if I went back they could track me to you! I should have listened!"

 "Nah that's dumb. Avoiding someone to try and protect them doesn't really protect them. Like ever. You should have just told me. We could have figured something out… but I'm not going to berate you Jun. What happened wasn't your fault. You didn't kidnap me, or mean for it to happen."_

 The maturity of her reply allows Shame to make a beachhead within your heart. As emotions warred within you, more and more of the part of you left in the Snapshot with Kasumi makes it's presence felt.

 For the first time in a long while, you feel the Emptiness that has been a core part of you for so many months start to shrink.

_"If I hadn't bothered you so much and threatened to run out in the gang war to find you? It's funny if they had jumped me about a minute earlier things would have been different. I had just packed all my guns in the car."_

 What your Sister just said hits you like a punch to the gut. The weight crushing you seems to increase as you realized that she wasn't the Imouto who chased after a Nii-San anymore and that perhaps due to your choices she too had chosen to walk a Bloody Road.

 The rest of the day passed in a haze,

 You feel the Emptiness trying to claw back it's grip on your soul as the sheer relief from finding Motoko in time starts to fade. Sitting on the couch and without Motoko within view, you felt everything start to fade as the Emptiness regain lost ground now that the sheer reminder of her presence was no longer around to bolster Grief, Fear, Guilt, Relief and the many many parts of you that came back to life in her presence.

_"Couldn't sleep either huh?"

 "Motoko. You should go back to sleep. You need to rest."_

 As you banter with her, the part of you that remains forever with Kasumi can't help but marvel at how just Motoko being present could help drive the Emptiness back. And how it brought to mind memories of better days and forgotten dreams. It drives you to confess the truth. To acknowledge to Motoko and yourself what you had kept buried behind Rage.

 To acknowledge that at this moment, the Emptiness was no longer due to the shock of Kasumi's Death. And it is her quiet understanding and lack of judgment that allows you to reveal the depth of your grief and the secret fear buried deep within.

_"I thought… when you got kidnapped again. I thought… I didn't think I could get lucky enough to save you twice. I'm glad. That you aren't dead. That you aren't crazy… That I don't have to meet you again for the third time."

 "I'm sorry I'm not the Motoko you originally knew." 

 "Please. Just take care I don't want you doing something… Like me, and losing you. I can't."

 "Right now, I could just use a hug."

 "Stupid Jun-Nii. Get some Real Skinn already. Your arms are the worst at hugs."_

 You held your sister quietly as she fell into a fitful doze. You feel the part of you holding tightly to Kasumi tenderly let her go, leaving behind the memories of not just her last moments but also of the moments that came before.

 And as all of you now focuses on holding the Imouto in your arms, you feel the Emptiness recede, the Rage simmer to a ember, the Fear fade to a murmur and the Guilt weighing down your shoulders become lighter.

 By fully letting Kasumi go, you're finally able to do something that you should have done months ago. You allow yourself to mourn her loss. You let the memories and crushed dreams of a future together flow into a bittersweet harmony that will stay with you forever.

 Perhaps, one day in the future memories of Kasumi would be tinged with nostalgia. But for now, you let yourself weep over her loss and do you best to ensure that a future will come for you and your Sister.

_This is how it feels to be Kusanagi Jun as you finally allow yourself to mourn._

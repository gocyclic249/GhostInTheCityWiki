_A Little Toy Of Scrap Metal

 -------

 POV: Urbie_

 He didn't know much. He didn't understand much. He was kinda slow in the mind, to be honest.

 His sisters teased him endlessly about this. Looking at them zipping around made him ashamed of his stubby little legs. Them discussing complicated philosophical concepts overheated his circuits. Seeing them at target practice humbled him and whatever he could do with his clearly faulty integrated Burya Shoulder system, when compared to their performance.

 Still, Urbie didn't hate his Tachikoma sisters. Despite them clearly being better in all the ways that mattered, Urbie still was glad they even tolerated him. They didn't appreciate him, to be sure. Most of them not even liking him and 'playing pranks', if that was the correct phrase.

 But, at least, they cared enough to acknowledge his faulty existence.

 ------

 "Where are we going, Mother?" He asked his Creator.

 "Just-, just outside the workshop, nothing to worry about" Creator- his Mother spoke with a smile on her face.

 He recognized that smile.

 It was the same one he saw every time Mother spoke about how he was just as loved and appreciated as his sisters.

 The slow, cumbersome, worthless little piece of scrap better used for literally anything else than him.

 He accepted his fate. There was no denying it at this point. He was quarter as effective on a mission than your average Tachikoma Unit from two generations ago at best, so he recognized the situation for what it was, easily.

 A kindness.

 A chance to peacefully pass away, as Mother took him around the workshop and put him out of his misery with a well placed pistol shot. With the gun she clearly already had loaded, resting in her holster.

 His data processing was at whirl. Sensors overloaded by the demand. For a moment he couldn't even hear what his Mother spoke to him.

 And yet he felt compelled to ask

 "Mother" He said, tone as flat as always "Why was I created?"

 It was a question he always pondered, always in his RAM, always trying to make some sense of in a way that would justify his existence.

 She had no answer.

 Neither did he.

 "Mother" he tried again "I know I'm inferior" Urbie said "I know I'm slow. I know I'm dumb. I know I can't understand things. I know my sisters outclass me in all things."

 She thumbed her holstered gun, a fraction of second separating her from ending her failed creation, and saving it from its existential misery.

 "Mother" he continued "I know what it is all about" his stumpy little legs stomped once before they stopped moving altogether as he stopped walking forward "I know you wish to recycle me here, but please, I beg of you"

 The little tiny Urbanmech replica said towards their creator.

 "Mother, please" Urbie said "If I am to cease function, let it be in service to you, Mother" he continued, focusing his camera lenses on her face "For bestowing upon me this short gift of life, if it's the last thing I do, please, at least let me die for y-"

 His vocal processes were interrupted by the Creator engulfing his tiny, worthless little frame in her embrace.

 He filed a report that the Creator may be suffering from a malfunction as her eye implants were discharging a corrosive liquid in unnaturally large quantity.

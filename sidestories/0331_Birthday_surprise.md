"I'm honestly okay, Rita. But thank you for worrying."

 Rita smiled at Motoko. She liked helping her chooms. Her friends. After watching the now infamous BD for herself, she thought she had lost another one. Just like Jane. Just like Ryan. Just like Emma.

 But not Motoko. Motoko wasn't showing any signs of crossing the point of no return.

 They talked for almost an hour, sitting in Motoko's apartment, since it didn't feel right to Rita to wait for her young friend to swing by the bar. Rita was worried it was already too late, but turns out, Motoko bounced right back after what felt like a cyberpsycho episode, and was merrily tinkering away at home. Speaking of tinkering...

 "What is this, by the way?"

 Rita gestured at the large device the size of a truck engine sitting on a coffee table that looked like it was about to break under the weight. Motoko was about to attach a large electronic wall clock to it.

 "Oh, this is something for my new song."

 Something in the way Motoko said it had triggered an alarm in Rita's head.

 "You wrote a new song?" she asked carefully.

 "Yeah, and I've been thinking about premiering it with a BD again."

 Fuck. Fuck-fuck-fuck-fuckity-fuck. Rita's mind began racing.

 "But this time around I can't just hit a scav den and call it a day. It wouldn't match the track. You see, at the beginning it goes quietly like pi-pi-pi-pi..."

 Motoko flipped a switch on the clock in her hands, and it lit up like a Christmas tree, rapidly counting milliseconds down.

 "...for about a minute or so. And when the tension is at the peak, it suddenly goes..."

 Motoko made a dramatic pause. Rita held her breath.

 "...pu-ru-rin!"

 Rita relaxed a little bit, tension leaving her shoulders. This pu-ru-rin was concerning, but she could live with a pu-ru-rin.

 "And then BAKOOM!"

 Motoko made a broad gesture with both hands, tracing something like a big mushroom in the air. Her elbow hit the installation on the coffee table, namely the large screwdriver jammed between the two ominously looking big metal hemispheres right at the middle of it. The screwdriver moved half an inch but didn't fell out, barely keeping the halves apart.

 Rita became painfully aware of her heart skipping a couple of beats. She had to force herself to take a breath and tear her eyes away from the screwdriver.

 "Y-you are not planning to n-nuke the scavs, are you?"

 Motoko blinked, then snorted.

 "Pfft... nice one. Of course not, who do you think I am?"

 Rita looked into her friend's honest eyes and giggled, shivering with the whole body. This was silly, of course. Maybe her friend was a psycho and a mass murderer, but at least she wasn't a nuclear terrorist. She smiled at the absurdity of the idea.

 "Tomorrow is Johnny Silverhand's birthday, so I'm nuking Arasaka!" said Motoko with determination, making a fist with her right hand and looking through Rita in the general direction of the corporation's HQ.

 Rita's smile froze.

 "Oh, and by the way, thanks again for suggesting me a hobby, Rita! If not for you, I would probably be doing something dumb and self-destructive right now, like watching TV with Wakako all day until she had a new gig for me. You've literally turned my life around, and I can never thank you enough for that! Wait, are you crying? Why are you crying, Rita?"

 Standing with a frozen smile and tears running out of her eyes, Rita let her friend hug her. Hugs were nice. But most importantly, hugs kept Motoko's elbows away from any screwdrivers.

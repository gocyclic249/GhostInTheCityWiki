**Fish in a Pond**

 Rogue was engaged in her normal routine of accepting and delegating gigs when she received a call from someone she hadn't heard from in a while. He was one of the more..._distasteful_ clients she had. However, she had nothing of any urgency on her plate currently, so she went ahead and answered, the holo-screen showing the image of a slick corpo.

 "Rogue, I have need of your services," he began.

 "Of course," she replied succintly, "What do you want?"

 "I'm having a spot of trouble with this hot new talent MSM recently signed. She's being unreasonably stubborn, won't sign up for any of the optional promotions, or sponsorships. Not even cyberware deals. Who turns those down these days?" He frowned irritably, "It's costing me money."

 "Get on with it, Angelopuoulos."

 He glared at her in response. "In particular, she's insistent her little friend is her manager. She expects _me_ to deal with some random brat, who's not even out of school yet, as a middleman." He rolled his eyes in annoyance.

 "Anyway," he continued, "I need this 'personal manager' to suffer a tragic misfortune. Per usual, it should be 'accidental'. Accidental mugging, accidental maiming, accidental car accident, whatever. So long as she's no longer 'available' to be her friend's manager."

 Rogue was vividly reminded why this particular client was so unpleasant.

 "Fine. What's this individual's name?"

 "Wait, one sec," he muttered to himself, looking distracted for a moment. "Right, it's 'Hiromi Mitsunashi.' She's a student at Arasaka Academy. Her parents also work for the company."

 "One moment," she replied.

 Rogue thought the name sounded vaguely familiar. However, that had no effect on what happened next. It was very poor practice to blindly accept a contract without knowing at least the basics of who and/or what was involved, no matter who the client was. So she looked up the name in her private database to see if anything popped up. If nothing did, she'd simply tell Angelopuoulos she'd call him back, before doing a more thorough check. To her mild surprise, the name did come up. Though not directly, but as an associate of a recent Afterlife invite. Interesting.

 Also, it meant this potential contract ran afoul of one of her operating principles. No taking contracts on her own mercs, nor their close friends and family. It wasn't out of some sense of loyalty or compassion either. She was content to let her mercs suffer the consequences of sloppy behavior or poor choices; even dishing out such consequences herself, on occasion, if such things affected her or her business. No, it was out of simple practicality. She knew that if it ever became known, or even rumored, that she accepted such contracts from outside parties, her business would get _messy._ The last thing she needed was top-tier mercs refusing to work for her, looking for revenge, or arguably the worst, contemplating preemptive action.

 Of course, there was the reason she made a decision, and the reason she gave the client for making said decision; often not the same thing.

 She turned back to the call. "Since I prefer to have repeat customers, I'm going to refuse this contract. I recommend you drop it entirely."

 "What?!" he exclaimed, with a bit of anger. "You can't just refuse me without an explanation. You've never turned down a contract before!"

 She glared back at him. "You already got free advice, you expect me to give you free information too?"

 He ground his teeth, "Fine, here." 

 A short moment later, a transfer of five thousand eddies landed in one of her accounts.

 Rogue looked away for a moment, gathering her thoughts.

 "Your new client is Motoko Kusanagi, correct?"

 He nodded.

 "Do you know who she is, who her friends are, what she does when she's not recording songs for you?"

 He rolled his eyes. "I did my due diligence, I know about her street rat friends, I know her brother is a gangbanger. So what? I'm beyond the reach of some pissant street trash. Especially if you do your job correctly."

 She raised an eyebrow at him. "So let's say, for the sake of argument, that I accept this contract. Miss Mitsunashi most likely ends up dead, since the sort of person you're willing to pay for on a contract like this aren't usually known for being imaginative."

 "That's fine, I don't really give a shit about imagination. What does that have to do with anything?" he replied angrily.

 Her glare turned glacial. "Shut your mouth and let me finish. Afterwards, Kusanagi doesn't just go home and cry. She isn't the sort. Instead, she looks into it. Sooner or later, she figures it out. She's exactly _that_ type, don't let her apparent age or origin tell you otherwise. Then she finds you in your home, when you're asleep, and dismembers you. Security? Trauma Team? Irrelevant.

 "In other words, you can't handle her, you're fucked."

 He stared at her in disbelief, before breaking out laughing. "Bullshit. She's a tiny fourteen year old street rat. She couldn't even manage to steal my lunch money."

 She smirked in return. "Her deal with MSM? It's a side gig for her. Motoko Kusanagi's most significant business contact isn't MSM, or even Mitsunashi. It's Wakako Okada, as one of her prime infiltration specialists and assassins. You know who that is, correct?"

 He nodded again, finally starting to look a little uncertain.

 "Her brother isn't just a random gangbanger, he's one of the Tyger Claws' elite enforcers. One of her other friends is Hayato Nakagawa, the heir apparent to said Tyger Claws. Last, but not least, she has an invite to the Afterlife. In other words, she's good enough that _I'm_ willing to give her jobs." _If she ever bothered to show up_, she added to herself wryly.

 "Yeah, she's only fourteen," Rogue continued, "But she's still completely out of your league on this front. When you die, your bosses won't retaliate on your behalf, because _you_ are not worth irritating Motoko Kusanagi or any of the people she calls friends and family.

 "Of course,you're free to ignore my advice, and talk to another fixer. But you should probably update your will, first."

 By this point, he was actually looking pale and wide-eyed. His mask of corporate arrogance fractured. But she didn't let him regroup and think up a response. 

 "One last thing. Stand Alone Complex isn't her first foray into music. She's produced a handful of her own XBDs, creating original music for them. I'll send over the one I think you'll find most interesting. It's called, 'The Only Thing They Fear.' You'll learn the rest of what you need from that."

 At that, she just hung up on him. If he had any sense, he would drop it, and still call her the next time he needed something. If he didn't... Well, stupid clients were frequently more of a liability than an asset, so it wouldn't be a terrible loss to her bottom line.

 That had been unexpectedly satisfying, Rogue thought. Fucking corpos. Sure, their companies, and their higher ups, were sharks in the pond. But all of the middle managers and paper pushers like him? Their arrogance blinded them to the fact that they themselves were still just minnows. And while there were few genuine sharks in Night City, there were a hell of a lot of piranhas. Such as Motoko Kusanagi.

_**Two days later...**_

 The band had been dragged to another promotional party. This one seemed to also include other musicians under the MSM banner. Sadly, no Kerry yet. However! She had learned her lesson. When everyone else headed for the booze, she went straight to the food! Body 10 ftw!

 The downside, was that Not Manager was being creepy again. Except...different. He was being downright respectful and deferential. It made her suspicious. Well, even more suspicious. Did she mention that the attitude change was creepy?

 Maybe she'd need to accelerate her plans regarding him. It almost felt like he'd done something he knew would piss her off, but was acting all nice in order to dodge her suspicion. Hmmm. Maybe she'd track Kitty down and get more details out of her. She didn't consider Alex much of a threat, but maybe it'd be better safe than sorry.

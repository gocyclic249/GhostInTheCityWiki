Newbie Sound Tech (TM, Pat. Pending) looked down at the control board, noticing one of the controls had a strip of yellow and black striped tape preventing it from being ajusted. Turning to one of his new co-workers, he asked, "Hey, what's the story on the tape over the reverb control?"

 His co-worker abruptly went pale, rapidly looked around, and pulled him into a headlock-huddle. "SSSSSHHH! Not so loud! No one told you?"

 "Told me what? You are freaking me out, choom!"

 "The last sound tech, the guy you replaced, fucking died because of that slider!"

 Breaking free of the other man's grasp, he chuckled, "That has to be a superstition. There's no way that actually happened."

 "Feel free to ask around, but it's the god's honest truth. Your fuckin' funeral man."

 Feeling slightly spooked by the pushback, NST decided to check with another co-worker. "Yeah, they may be turning it into a superstition, but the last sound tech legitimately died 'cause he touched the reverb. Stand Alone Complex was in for their first recording session, and the idiot cranked the reverb up way too high mid-song. The lead and songwriter for the band walks over, puts the control back, and announces that she's gonna kill him in his sleep if he touches the reverb again, like she's announcing the fuckin weather. Then, later, she abruptly ended the recording session when he touched it again. Nobody ever saw that tech after he went home for the day, and there were Trauma Team and Badges at the apartment building he lived at the next morning. TT wasn't in any fuckin' rush, neither. Turns out that this chick, Motoko, is a crazy good merc in addition to being a crazy good Rockerboy. If I were you, I would play it safe, and leave the controls alone when Stand Alone Complex are in studio.

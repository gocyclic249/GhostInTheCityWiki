Upset that I know like, next to nothing about this setting so I can't write the possible future where Hiromi is an Arasaka Exec and Motoko has become the notorious Solo she wished to be _and they are roommates!_

* * *

 The Tyger Claws didn't have room for Hiromi, so on Motoko's insistence she ended up using her parents' connections to help her quickly rise up in Arasaka. Then, she'd contract out Motoko to help her eliminate competition or scout and spy, ensuring her meteoric rise to become an exec.

 Motoko, meanwhile, kept getting rapidly increasing in difficult missions she aced as a Solo. No one bets on if she'll die on a mission within a year, and wagering she'll fail a mission has about the same odds happening as winning the lottery, only a lower payout.

 Eventually, Hiromi gets kidnapped by a rival corporation, and Arasaka of course isn't going to pay a ransom. Days later, heads roll in one of their safe houses where they're holding Hiromi hostage before Motoko princess carries her out, the only two still beating hearts on the property perfectly in sync. As Motoko opens her car door to let Hiromi in, her arm was tugged back by the girl who goes in-

 "HIROMI! IT DIDNT HAPPEN THAT WAY AND YOU KNOW IT!" Motoko complains over Hiromi's shoulder, watching her girlfriend add far too much flair to story of the turning point in their relationship.

 "C'mon, 'Toko. It was exactly like this. You just don't realize how amazing you were, busting down the door with the light framing your shadowed figure. It was like one of those dumb cartoons you keep trying to make me watch."

 "Hiromi..."

 "Do you remember what you told the old crew after it happened? Let me help. 'Oh, there were only three dozen people I had to go through.' 'It took me almost three minutes to hack their corpo software. I was feeling rusty.' Need I go on?"

 "It's not that impressive, really."

 "Motoko 'The Minor' Kusanagi: Not that impressive," Hiromi snorts. "You are aware of how many people refuse jobs now if they get word you're on the other end? I heard some Fixers have added a Motoko Clause to jobs where if an Edgerunner finds out they're up against you, they can call it off for no extra fee."

 "First of all, it's 'The Major'," She pins Hiromi with a glare. "I'm still salty that it took me so long to find out I had a handle and it wasn't what I wanted. Second, I don't believe that at all."

 "How many jobs have you failed?"

 "None? Some got called off or were traps but none I actually failed. Arguably one but I really don't like talking about it."

 "And you think that's normal? Years of doing this, Toko. YEARS!"

 She shrugs. "Well, we can argue about this for hours, _again_, or we start getting ready for dinner. Jun's meeting us at the restaurant."

 Hiromi shoots out of her spot on the couch. "Why didn't you say so sooner? Now I've only got half an hour to get ready."

 "Less if you keep complaining." She quipped back with a smirk.

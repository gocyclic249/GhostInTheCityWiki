 "Dammit. Jun is going to be so pissed. Never jump into random portals Motoko." I grumbled at myself as I looked over the city scape.

 This wasn't Night City. Oh it was definitely like Night City, a massive super city, that went on and on and on. 

 The problem was this wasn't natural. The people didn't know technology, but still had some. Blasters and things, not guns exactly, but it was really bad. 

 Factions, gangs, and killing was everywhere. 

 Night City felt safer, and that was saying something. 

 Thankfully despite having some tech they were… Well no better than scavs really. Give them a weapon and they could shoot it, maybe, but other than that, there was no brains. 

 Falling out of a giant arch that looked kind of familiar, right into a bunch of angry zealots that were very angry that I wasn't their long gone God.

 Whatever. Not my issue.

 With my Optical Camo, parkour, and my Sandy I was out of reach for any of them.

 The gate that I'd come through was my main focus. I really needed to figure out how to activate it and get home, that was the important thing.

 So I'd stuck around. Using the massive concrete hab blocks that literally took up every inch of the sky even around this gate that was some sort of religious focus to hide out in.

 There were more empty apartments than people, and it only took knocking one gonk around to make sure that my space was secured.

 The problem was everything else.

 I sighed as I looked at the tasteless 'food' bars that were delivered around at times.

 They were awful. Kible was better and probably better for me, but there was no green. No plants, or animals in the entire city.

 I was probably eating people. Worse? They were poison. Full of all sorts of chemicals meant to make everyone aggressive. Luckily I could process it out, but still it made me feel icky to even eat it.

 Grumbling at the thought I kicked my feet out the window. It had already been three weeks since I arrived, that was three weeks where Jun wouldn't know where I was, or Hiromi!

 Ugh.

 Everything was awful.

 Then suddenly everything changed. I blinked at the gate ring far below suddenly lit up.

 Hey that was kind of famili-

 The noise was what did it. That hind brain remembrance of something far in the past.

 "Is that a fucking Stargate?" I whispered as it flushed! Actually flushed with the funny water thing as it finally engaged…

 Whoa.

 Wait, was I in some alt dimension alien world? No, it was okay! Stargate dealt with alternate worlds! I could definitely get home… If I trusted the US government…

 Fudge.

 I sighed and watched on, I'd have to figure out a way to dial out but… I don't think they had the little platform thing that let you call out.

 Well, I guess we'll see who is calling in.

 I blinked as what came out was…

 Calling it a drone was not correct, but that was what it was. A silly little old remote controlled drone.

 Was that earth then? Would they even come here? This place was kinda shitty…

 The priest guys went up to the drone and I just watched as they talked to each other and it, seemingly trying to figure out what it was.

 Then the gate closed.

 I sighed and flopped backwards back into my little cube.

 This world sucked.

 —--

 Two weeks later, I'd managed to sneak into plenty of meetings of the crazy zealots, and learned a whole lot of nothing. 

 They were useless. None of them knew how to activate the Stargate, or anything else. Just waiting for their 'Gods' to visit again.

 The worst part was, now that I knew I was dealing with a Stargate I had actually found the dialer thing. Only… there was zero gate codes anywhere. I'd tried a bunch trying to even turn it on, but it made a lot of noise and never connected anyways. I'd have to wait for SG-1… No wait that was specifically the one team, Stargate Command? Whatever who cares.

 I leapt, feeling the wind on my face, as I practically flew over the tops of the buildings to another rooftop. Then I looked back pushing myself into some shadows.

 The fuckers that had been trying to track me looked lost as they hit the roof and couldn't find me.

 Good luck idiots.

 I moved on. Of course that was when I heard it. Not the sizzling sound of the blasters they used around here, but gunshots. Gunpowder.

 I shifted, and activated my Sandy for a moment feeling everything turn into absolute stillness as I broke into a run and then leapt.

 Momentum? Physics? I was moving fast enough most of those things felt completely different.

 I hit the side of a building, and took two steps before pushing off and flipping back sideways, my legs flying.

 Man this would make a great BD.

 Flowed, tapping a few times on the buildings that I ran across, literally wall running as I moved across the city in a flash.

 It was times like this, where my Sandy truly reminded me of just how broken this tech was.

 I stopped, still ten stories above the ground hanging in a window uncaring about the people inside, they were busy, and wouldn't see me anyways. My eyes flickered as I zoomed in.

 That… Was definitely a bunch of military boys. Interesting.

 Huh, they really did use P90's, cool. I always wanted to try dual wielding those. The soldiers were doing great. They'd pushed off from the podium the gate was on, and had managed to get cover, throwing over one of the carts the locals used, and were mowing down fanatics.

 They were fine, I wouldn't even have to do anything as I watched them fight their way free and into one of the buildings. That caused even the fanatics pause, but unlike me, there were a whole lot of crazy zealots and not that many soldiers.

 I'd have to get involved sooner or later.

 I sighed.

_Major Louis Ferretti SG-2_

 "What a pile of shit we wandered into, huh Major?"

 "Stow it Sands! You got that door?"

 "I got it Major." He confirmed back. P90 held tight. What a cockup. So much for positive response from the locals that the eggheads had considered the Drone interaction.

 If only Jackson had been around and not running around for the last week. They'd have gotten someone who knew what he was talking about. Heh. Maybe not.

 "Bell, you got us a path?"

 "Stairwell going up sir. Sightlines are poor." The man called back and Ferretti cursed. Just what he needed. "They're pushing in, let's move and secure." He called out, and broke off, knowing his team would break off in sequence, as he stopped at the door and everyone even Sands rushed past.

 Louis waited and fired as Sand's disappearance had caused more of these crazies to approach.

 The fact they had some sort of Zat that fired anemic staff blasts made them far more dangerous than most of the poor souls he'd run into out here.

 This whole place made him uncomfortable.

 The complete lack of sunlight as the buildings towered over them, hadn't come through the images.

 This was… Not what he expected, and it seemed like someone had fucked up.

 Thankfully the shooting had started before the gate shut down, Command would know about it.

 His team made it up two flights before Louis stopped them, and instead started securing a location.

 It wasn't great, but he wanted to be close to the gate, and if he went through here… People. He had gone through a doorway no actual door and came across people. They looked terrible, emaciated and terrified. 

 "Hey, I'm not here to hurt you." He told them, but got only scared noises in turn.

 Just another day. But he needed that window. "Secure it. And Griff see if you can't calm the locals." He moved towards the window and sure enough he could see down into the place that had the Stargate.

 Now he just had to wait for reinforcements.

 "Fuck!" He heard and turned, Griff was fighting a local that had one of the dam Zats! 

 The other was a woman, and she was already pulling a knife out of as well! He raisied his P90, taking aim.

 And then a blur came through.

 He blinked and there was another person.

 Not wearing clothes like the locals, and in fact… Her arms were silver, metallic.

 She had knocked out the local woman seemingly with a single punch, and had even wrapped an arm around the man and suddenly he slumped as she choked him out in an instant.

 "Whoa!" Griff grunted as he finally pulled the weapon out of the locals hand and brought up his own P90 still strapped to his chest.

 The local fell leaving just the strange woman.

 Purple hair, which was odd enough, but her face was covered in a solid seeming metal band like sunglasses, some sort of leotard that left just a hint of skin around her waist, and… She had a gun strapped to her chest, and a more along her waist.

 "Greetings. I come in peace." She offered, and then to his shock and horror she held up a vulcan salute.

 O'Neill was never going to let this go.

 "What?" Griff muttered his chest heaving adrenaline no doubt making him a little slow.

 "Who're you?" Louis demanded his own gun still up.

 "Motoko. A california girl if you can believe it. You're from Earth right? I'm not just hallucinating the P90's?"

 "What?" Now it was Louis that had to ask that because… What?

 The glasses? She was wearing were pulled up and suddenly he could see her eyes, purple eyes.

 Yeah that wasn't normal. Neither was what looked like metal arms.

 "You don't look like you're from earth." He commented and she blinked at him, her large eyes locking onto him for a moment.

 "Rude. Fine! I'll prove it." She took a breath and from her throat came something that caught him off guard.

 "On a dark desert highway, cool wind in my hair!" She started singing, and damn well at that. Hotel California wasn't it?

 Louis was shocked enough it took him a moment to realize his entire team was looking away from the damn stairs!

 "Focus on the entry points!" He demanded and then that stopped her singing. 

 "Believe me now?" She asked, teasing… God she was a teenager wasn't she?

 "How are you even here?"

 "Some funky portal thing I fell into. Wormhole probably, considering this isn't Earth." She commented so nonchalantly, that he almost did a double take.

 Then shook it off. 

 He was about to continue when he heard it boots on the ground.

 "They're coming." She commented before he could and turned. 

 What was that on her neck? He swallowed down the questions and pointed at Griff at the locals. Then ran to join his men at the stairwell hallway.

 "You've been here a while?"

 "Yeah like a month or something." She responded back seriously which was a relief. "You know what's up with these people?"

 "The food mostly. It's full of all sorts of shit to cause aggression. Don't eat their food unless you have to." She commented and he looked at her, she didn't seem aggressive.

 "And you?"

 "Oh I don't have that issue. It's awful though literally zero out of ten, can't recommend."

 "I'll be sure to let the General know your score."

 "Preem. Hey you guys want me to like.-" She made a throat slicing motion. "All of them? You don't have a lot of ammo right?"

 "What?" Louis flinched, as he shifted, not quite pointing at her.

 "Don't give me that look. They're trying to kill us. I kinda want you guys alive, you know?"

 "Just stay back and don't get in the way." He decided to say after a moment, and she hummed for a moment. 

 "Well if you want." She didn't sound very sure. 

 He kept one eye on her, but to his surprise despite the gunfire and blood she didn't flinch, nor did she interfere or cause trouble just leaning up against a wall where he could keep an eye on her and waited. 

 To his surprise despite the gunfire as his team fended off the locals, it was the sound of the gate turning that caught his ear. 

 "Looks like reinforcements are here boys. Get ready."

 "Oh that's handy." His… Guest mentioned as she turned to the window and peaked out. Seemingly uncaring that she was sticking her head out where all the hostiles could shoot at her. 

 "SG-2 Come in." Louis grabbed his radio. 

 "SG-2 Good to hear you Altman, we are holed up in a building right across from the gate. We'll throw smoke."

 "You secure?"

 "Yeah, watch out for hand held Zat likes from the locals. Nothing major… And the locals seemed to be drugged for Agression, don't let them get close even if they are acting scared."

 "Shit. Roger that. Hold tight, we're going to clear you a way. SG-5 out."

 Ferretti calmed down as the sound of gun fire echoed off the concrete buildings. Then he looked at their guest, despite all the noise the girl seemed eerily calm, completely unconcerned. 

 And as she was looking out, he could see the back of her neck.

 Solid black… Plastic? Metal? Covered the back of her neck, but it… It looked like it was part of her flesh. 

 Some kind of Terminator? Was he dealing with a robot?

 "Major!" The call went out just before the gun fire and Ferretti focused, the hostiles were pushing from below. He took two steps to focus his own fire when everything went to shit. In a blur of motion Bell suddenly had a body on top of him. He'd been pointing straight down the stairwell, and one of the locals had dropped on him from above!

 Before Ferretti could even move, there was an echoing retort, that had everyone flinch. .50 Cal!?

 The local was gone though. The shot had hit the poor bastard in the shoulder and splattered him across the concrete in chunks.

 He spun weapon brought up to see the girl with a massive gun in her hand pointing.

 She raised it up putting the barrel towards the roof. 

 "Chill choom. Just helping out." She offered, and Ferretti had to fight every instinct to want to demand she drop the gun.

 He wasn't like Jack, but he didn't like a kid carrying a gun either. Especially one that just used it.

 He turned She could have shot him already and she hadn't, and he had more important things to deal with.

 "Sergeant!?"

 "Fine Sir!" Bell roared, as he rose back up. His adrenaline was going as he seemed to ignore the gore he was splattered in and instead started focusing on the upper levels. "More incoming!" He called out, and Louis realized that this was trouble.

 "Alright how about I clear upwards for you lot then." The girl was beside him, and Louis jerked nearly jumping out his hide.

 He hadn't heard her move at all?

 "Don't be silly, just stay here."

 "Nah." She offered with a flat response, and then she moved forward, both hands full of gun, one that massive revolver if you could call it that, another one of the weird blaster pistols the locals had, the ones that didn't seem to aim straight.

 "Hey hold it!" He called out, but she didn't, rushing forward hitting the bannister surrounding the stairwell, in a crouch, and then just leaping.

 She disappeared, and Louis felt himself rush forward to check.

 By the time he made it up the sound of her gun firing echoed out, and he looked up to see her three floors up, crouching and firing before leaping again going even further up.

 What the hell?

 "Get it together! Focus up!" He demanded noticing the split attention.

 What the hell was going on?

_Major Louis Ferretti SG-2_

 The battle continued, but thankfully the overwhelming firepower cleared the way. Louis hadn't seen the girl again once she jumped up the stairs.

 SG-5 had finally cleared a path, and he'd ordered an exfil.

 He did keep looking behind him, calling out for the girl up the stairs, but he couldn't hold here. Injuries were climbing, and he was worried for his men.

 The run back to the gate was something he always dreaded, but some things you just had to do, at least once through it was clear, and not miles and miles of running.

 "Go go go!" He ordered over his shoulder as he turned and fired, not specifically shooting to kill anymore, more to push the horde back, and it was a horde. The longer they stayed the more of the locals came out of the woodwork.

 The numbers were getting concerning. 

 He fired again sending another group of locals scurrying away their blasters not shooting anywhere near his team as they joined up. 

 "Aaaugh!" He heard the scream and turned to see Castleman, part of SG-5. He had gotten grabbed, and was getting swarmed, God they were trying to tear him apart. He wasn't the only one that reacted, but it felt like none could get proper shots into the horde as they started carrying him away.

 "Bell! With me!" He demanded, already moving, but when he tried suddenly there she was. A girl with purple hair and metal arms, and she tore into the crowd. He stilled in horror as with each motion she wasn't punching or kicking, but ripping and tearing. 

 He saw one blow drive through a man grab another man, and both were thrown away like ragdolls.

 Powerful.

 Dangerous, so very dangerous, but a moment later she had cleared some of the horde, and then moved towards them. He could see some of SG-5 move to shoot but hold as she had Castleman across her shoulders and was dashing back towards them like an olympic sprinter.

 "The Girl is on our side! Move move!" He demanded, fuck it, she'd said she was from earth, even if that made no sense, but he wasn't going to leave her here, especially not after that.

 He was one of the last through, and pushed her ahead of him, Castleman wasn't moving, and was bleeding a bit too much for his liking. She ran through the gate, and Louis was free to focus on the extract.

 "That's…"

 "But I can open up if you want a good look at my undercarriage. Just don't fiddle around with too much in their or you'll get a girl feeling strange." I said, then burst into laughter a few seconds later as the doctors face shifted. "Sorry, sorry. I'm teasing a bit. This must be weird for you huh? No chrome, so you probably got a lot of weirdness about a Cyborg huh?"

 "Well you aren't Vik, who is the only man I've ever let work on me, but I understand you have things you need to do… Want to look at my pain editor first, my second heart, or my blood pump?" I asked, once again laughing at the womans strained look.

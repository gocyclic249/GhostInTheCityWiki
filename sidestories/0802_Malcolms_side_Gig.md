**Malcolm's side Gig**

 ...because it's just natural progression isn't it? ![Image 10: :)](blob:http://localhost/b9a31d3949b1882a09ed2f8508d538f3)

 ===============

 As with most things of that nature It started slowly. The spread of information was gradual, mostly through word of mouth.

 At first it was only the vaguest rumors wispered in the seedy underbelly of Night City by those of ill repute...

 ...witch was most of Night City and also most of the inhabitants of said city of course. What rumors were those one curious soul may ask?

 There was a Guy _._

 A professional in his trade. He was quite young still but once on the job? He was reliable, efficient, not to mention discreet. All his many clients agreed despite initial suspicions that The Guy was - to quote - "Solid Choomba! Would recommend to their chooms!"

 Those who witnessed his work couldn't be more impressed. Some of the other satisfied former clients were quoted saying things like:

 ***

 "...Holy Shit! The Guy can pack it fast! I can't believe how many extra Edds me and my boys got from that Gig thanks to That Guy!"

 ***

 "Damn it! I wish i knew that guy sooner - all that wasted cash! Fuck!"

 ***

 "...yeah, sometimes you don't have the time to properly take care of stuff after the dust settles. Pigs can be on you like fleas if you go too loud you know? But that guy? Shit! Personally seen his work. He's like gene-modifed Locust from the old Corp Wars, never seen someone move that quickly without a Sandy."

 ***

 "Me and my crew were having serious doubts about him but i must admit the Guy delivered and more...plus he's cheap. Takes a few percent of the haul, no muss no fuss. Just solid biz all around."

 ***

 The Fixers were the first to notice the rumors of the new up and coming entrepreneur and after verifying them begun sending some small gigs his way testing the waters in the hope of making good on their investment.

 They weren't dissapointed.

 In Night City this kind of business was always in demand and so the business grew and prospered. After a few weeks passed others showed up and smelling easy eddies tried to start similar operations with the intention to milk that cash cow.

 None of them lasted more than a few weeks.

 By that time The Guy established a solid Rep with the City's movers and more importantly with the Mercs, the Edgerunners, the Solos and most of the independent crews that made up Night City's guns for hire.

 It bears repeating but the Guy? He was professional. Never tried to take more than agreed. Never skimmed of the top during the Gigs he was hired to do hoping no one would notice a few less eddies. Didn't keep any of the hardware to himself, and always _always_ operated in good faith with the clients. And in this City filled with cyberpsychos, gangs, scavs and other shady gonks who would betray you for a few more edds in their pockets...?

 ...that**counted** for a lot to a surprising number of people in this business.

 And so his reputation just kept rising.

 His name? Well, not many people know his real name. The thing is, on the job he was only known by his business alias...

**_*The Loot Goblin*_**

 However for those close to him that knew his real name he was simply...

 ======

 "...hey Malcolm?"

 Malcolm who was just chilling on the couch watching bad Night City late night shows while snacking on super unhealthy junk food noticed Motoko's strange tone of voice first. His eyes found Motoko leaning against the table where he dumped some of his stuff in his mad scramble for the couch a few minutes ago.

 He knew from experience that if he didn't hurry Motoko would claim the best spot.

 "...hmmm? What's up Moto...?" he trailed off as his eyes noticed what she was holding.

 It was a small buisness card. It must have fallen out from his stuff on the table. It was _his side gig_ business card.

 "...."

 "...."

 "...i can totally explain everything in a logical and reasonable fashion." stated Malcolm sweating just a little and trying not to show it in front of Motoko's dissapointed look. The one with the rised eyebrow. Malcolm winced internally - those were the worst ones.

 After a full minute Motoko begun reading from the card in a dry and monotone voice.

**"The Loot Goblin..**." she narrowed her eyes at him for a moment making him wince openly and continued.

**"...when you need something looted professionally just call us!"** Motoko's voice took on a fake upbeat commercial presenter voice.

 The poor boy winced again.

**"Quick, discreet and reliable Looting Service for those in need of extra Eddies in your pocket after a Gig!"** She was doing a small dance routine now, her face set in a perfect fake smile.

 Malcolm just double facepalmed mumbling, "...just zero me now."

**"Don't hesitate - call NOW! Contact Link below."** Motoko finished her presentation with a small bow.

 Malcolm looked at Motoko. He looked resigned.

 Motoko looked at Malcolm. The dissapointed look was back.

 "...sigh, i'm sorry for not telling you or the crew about my side gig Motoko. I should have at least mentioned something sooner...i just wan..."

 "You are forgiven choom." Wait what? He looked at the sometimes scary girl with a slightly surprised look at the easy smile that settled on her face.

 "What? Malcolm you don't have any obligation to tell me everything you do - be it a side gig or something else, as long as no one from our crew get's hurt."

 Oh. Malcolm was relieved honestly. He was gonna mantion his side activity to his chooms in time if it panned out, but looking at it now he was just procrastinating. So then and there he made a decision.

 "Hey Motoko? Can you call Hiromi for a meetup? I think Section 9 should get more involved in this."

 Motoko just smiled wider and as her eyes flashed blue she replied. "Of course Malcolm."

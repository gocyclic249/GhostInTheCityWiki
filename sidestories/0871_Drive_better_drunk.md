As Hiromi and I sit in the club for our current gig, I can't help but feel like I was worrying for nothing about it being just the two of us in person for this one. Something about getting our target to admit some information and how we couldn't just have another member of Section 9 (preferably myself) get pointers from Hiromi via agent, but her experience would be essential and best utilized in person. Still, it had been years since we founded Section 9. She had just turned 22 and insisted it was the year of duos, whatever else that meant.

 Well, we got our information but Hiromi insisted on staying for drinks. I managed to avoid summoning Drunk Motoko by initiating that since I was on a gig I couldn't get drunk, and Hiromi's resultant pout was adorable. Hiromi seemed to be drinking for the two of us though, if her flushed face as she curled into my side was anything to go by.

 "Hiromi~," I pinched her side to force her to make some space as she turned to me. "Don't you think it's about time to get back home." To my place, because she insisted on crashing there tonight.

 She nodded at me. "Yeah, Motoko. Just… one more thing okay?"

 "Is that thing another drink?"

 "No?" She finished her current one, perfect sitting fully upright. I once again cursed being shorter than her, as those few inches she had on me sitting let her loom over me and I saw the flush of her face, and it occurred to me that it may not just be the alcohol causing it as she leaned closer.

 I still had an arm around her that I felt coming closer as I became more aware of her outfit, her position next to me the whole night, and the nights previously whenever we'd be out together. Her strange possessiveness about me, especially whenever I hyped up someone else. I was aware of so many things, how I felt looking at her face, the intense gaze she reserved just for me, the multiple gun barrels aiming towards us-

 THE MULTIPLE GUN BARRELS AIMING TOWARDS US!

 In one swift motion, I scooped her against me, tucking her head down into my neck as I pulled my pistol out with the same hand while yanking out the katana with my completely free one. Luckily Hiromi's instincts made her hold onto me like the koala she normally is for sleepovers (oh geez, put that revelation on hold for a few hours Motoko) and unloaded on two idiots before having to parry a shot from behind me. I didn't bother reading the alerts, noting the confirmation that I had killed the idiots who dared point a gun towards Hiromi (and me, but that's just another day in Night City.

 The resultant firefight as I made for the door with the added weight of a person attached to me was something that should have been better documented but frankly getting my best friend(?) out of harm's way was priority one. The stairwell door opened to reveal a new idiot but Hiromi feeling up my backside was almost too distracting before I realized she had also grabbed another holdout pistol of mine and managed to shoot him using only a third of the magazine. I'll be sure to remember to congratulate her later for keeping up her practice. For now, I had to keep as focused as I could while my Sandy cooled down.

 "Hiromi, thank you, but do you need to keep holding onto _there_ in particular?"

 "Motokooooo, a firm grip is important for stability!" This girl. If I didn't have my hands full, her cheeks would be so pinched right now.

 Thankfully, my Quadra was parked close enough that we didn't have far to go after busting out of the back entrance, but just as we closed in the car my senses pinged yet another threat and I became aware of two things: that I couldn't get my katana there in time and more importantly, the shot would be lethal to Hiromi. I managed to throw my left hand up into the path just in time for the shot to decimate my entire wrist before throwing us both into my Reaver.

 I was currently in the passenger seat because that door was closest, and Hiromi kept wiggling around in my lap, still very tipsy. A few more goons were coming from a ways off and I decided to make a risky decision. We were under attack from steel, from hackers, and soon to be cars too and I could only do so much one handed.

 "We're almost home free, but Hiromi, I need you to do one more thing okay?"

 "Anything for you, Motoko." She said just a little too breathily. I pushed her off of me into the driver's seat and watched as her eyes widened like a cat presented with a bowl of fresh fish.

 "I know I say not to drive drunk, but-"

 "SAY NO MORE!" and before I finished getting my window down to shoot more idiots, we were peeling away so perfectly that I could have only done with my perk enhanced ability if I was solely focused on the wheel. 

 Hiromi's driving was not well practiced, but it was elegant in the way a masterpiece work of art was and it clicked to me as I watched her gaze split between the road and myself, that she truly was a driving savant. The car weaved through travel at every angle but straight forwards as the drunk girl seemed to be utilizing instinct more than basic driving safety techniques. 

 She jerked the car into a curve that threw off the momentum just enough to stabilize it on two wheels while I used the new stability to shoot out the tires of a van tailing us.

 She drifted around an NCPD unit close enough that I could see my car kiss its headlights.

 Hiromi drove backwards so perfectly that she even gave me a smug look that said 'I told you so' while I could only sigh in defeat.

 She had bragged for so long about driving better drunk. Section 9 agreed. Even Jun-chan agreed!

 It had been ten minutes and no sign of anyone else coming after us before she pulled up to my apartment, drifting a full spin and a half perfectly into my parking spot.

 "So…?"

 "You're right, as always Hiromi. You do-" And she kissed me before I could finish my sentence. 

 It was long, passionate, and everything I never knew a first kiss could be. She finally pulled away and her grin was enough to light an entire megabuilding on its own.

 "I knew you felt the same way! Ha!" Oh.

 "I was going to say you do drive better drunk and I was wrong, but that works too."

 "MOTOKOOOO!"

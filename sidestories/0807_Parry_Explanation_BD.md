 The BD starts with a sound of metal touching metal and a jolt.

 Bold red letters glow in the center: **Explanation**.

 Below it another line runs in small font: Recoded as a charity for the NCPD.

 Young quiet voice keeps talking while looking at the table with various blades and other assorted equipment.

_... to parry a bullet one does not need magic. However, it does take some knowledge and skill. So, first is the blade. You need something sturdy enough to survive the hit and rigid enough to deflect it instead of bending back at you. You need something wide enough since even the best guns fired from the rigidly fixed position have spread. So, your blade has to cover the whole spread area or you will be hit regardless of your skill sooner or later._

 She picks a katana.

_Now, the next part is positioning. To get the blade into position you need to know where the gun points to. For a one-time stunt purpose you can clip a laser targeting module to your pistol and train with it. Otherwise, you will need to train your ability to see where guns point to._ She stops in front of a pistol fixed on the wall.

_Also, you need to know the exact moment when the trigger is pulled. For the stunt purpose you can use any kind of sync. In combat I often use the advantage stemming from being a netrunner. I often can read the moment of the shot straight from the enemy chrome._

 Suddenly a timer appears in the corner of a vision.

_Of course, smart weapons are my favorite since they can tell me what the gonk in front of me is aiming at. More so, I can often read the type of ammo, and that is damn important.

 With all things taken into account everything else is moving your blade at the right speed through the right trajectory. For that you need highly responsive hardware or y'know highly trained 'ganic arms._

 Timer runs out and she deflects a bullet in a lazy fashion. Then she continues her explanation.

_I strongly suggest you make some lo-pen rounds and wear sufficient protective gear when training and keep said protective gear on you out of training if you ever want to use this skill out of the controlled environment. Because automatic weapons are the thing._

 She makes a stop and then adds with a feeling of a resignation.

_Oh, and the last but not the least. If you are not a really high-level fencer or an Edgerunner - don't do it. Just don't._

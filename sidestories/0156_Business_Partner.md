Business Partner​

 *Hey Gloria! Sorry to bug you, but you free? Got some new stock ready for pick up if interested,* the text message said not long after Gloria was nearly finished with her shift.

 'When did my life become like this?' She couldn't help but sigh to herself as she responded back.

 *I'm finishing up now. I can be over soon.*

 *Cool! Will send you deets. Whole place is locked down so no worries about interruptions and such. Everyone else will be there too as usual. See you soon! ![Image 15: :D](https://forums.spacebattles.com/styles/sbforums/smilies/biggrin.gif)*

 Gloria ended chat as she sighed again, downed some [Spunky Monkey](https://cyberpunk.fandom.com/wiki/Spunky_Monkey_(drink)) for energy, and got ready to clock out.

 Her newest 'business partner' might have been a little out there and terrifying, but she was good people and paid Gloria well. Enough that after a hard night's work she could usually spend the next three if she wanted sleeping and still have plenty of funds left over.

 It was all worth it for David and making their lives better, after all.

 Even if the source... was less than pleasant.

 ---

 "Got anything good in him?" Motoko asked Gloria as she made use of the Scav's own operating table to cut out some of the military cyberware out of his dead body.

 "Decent, but nothing exceptional," Gloria admitted as she bagged the gear and started wiping the blood off of her.

 "Figured, but he was pretty borged out so I figured it was worth calling you up," Motoko shrugged as she idly picked through the pile of weapons she had gathered together. "Most of their gear is junk too, but a few good pieces in here I can use. I can break down the rest for parts or sell them off once my crew picks out whatever they want. You want anything?"

 "No, I'm alright with what protection I have already," Gloria said, not super comfortable around guns despite where she lived and those she associated with.

 And she certainly associated with arguably some of the craziest and most dangerous people in all of Night City.

 Edgerunners.

 Gloria already knew and interacted with a couple of them during the course of her 'side job', selling off whatever military grade cyberware off criminals and cyberpsychos she could safely scavenge off of the bodies she collected as an EMT. Something that wasn't uncommon in her line of work actually, but she sold direct rather than through a Fixer, a Ripperdoc, or some other third party.

 She needed the money too badly to give up a portion to a middle man after all.

 Thus, Gloria ran into Motoko during one of her handoffs and straight up asked for her services as a medtech in situations such as these, when she raided Scav dens, Maelstorm hideouts, or other locations to strip any high grade cyberware she came across for her.

 Motoko was very generous about it too, letting her keep most of the money during the sales and rarely asking for the cyberware herself. She even paid her well and gave her part of the 'loot pile' after each job despite only ever showing up after the fact and working with a few bodies at most, usually just one on most days. Not everyone was a borged out monster nor carried the most wanted chrome after all.

 Yet for some reason Motoko kept wanting to include Gloria on jobs and paid her well each time with a smile on her face.

 To be totally honest, Motoko unnerved Gloria in a way that most edgerunners she knew didn't.

 The young woman was just as crazy and deadly as them, but her young age, not much younger than her own David, and the way she just seemed to _know_ Gloria without really know her was disturbing.

 But at the same time Gloria had nothing bad to say about her and always felt bad when she felt uncomfortable around someone who from all appearances targeted only horrible people and treated her well.

 It was a paradox that Gloria didn't know how to handle most days, so just rolled with it to help protect her sanity.

 "I gotcha, no worries," Motoko waved off her refusal. "Whelp, we've basically cleaned this place out so best head out and drop a dime on it for NCPD, see if they actually do those jobs to alert families and such of the dead here.... No offense to any city workers here, of course!"

 The quick correction, full of awkward teenage energy and embarrassment, made Gloria snort in laughter before she could stop herself.

 'Its so like him,' she couldn't help but think. "None taken," she said with a smirk.

 "Thanks. I really do appreciate people like you who do their best and all," Motoko awkwardly said while rubbing the back of her head. "Let me know how sale goes later and just call me up if needed."

 "Will do, thanks," Gloria said as she watched as Motoko's eyes went yellow and suddenly Gloria found thousands of eddies drop into her account. "Motoko! Its too much again!"

 Gloria had to remember to breath again after seeing all those zeros.

 "Think of it as overtime pay since I grabbed you right after work! Buy yourself something pretty!" Motoko just said with her usual huge grin. "You deserve it for being such an awesome lady after all!"

 Gloria couldn't help but honestly smile at the compliment, something she rarely got outside of her son and her friends.

 "Thank you," she said sincerely as she packed up her trauma bag.

 "And remember, call me up if anyone tries to cheat or messes with you and I'll cut them into pieces!" Motoko said with her usual happy go lucky voice that most certainly did not fit with what she just said.

 "R-right," Gloria stuttered a bit at the remark.

 "Motoko! Shit's packed and ready! We gonna head out!" Hiromi, one of Motoko's crew and best friend, said from the other room.

 "We're coming!" Motoko cheerfully called back as she hooked her arm with Gloria's and started dragging her outside of the Scav den.

 Once more that unnerved feeling came back with a vengeance as it warred with her friendship with the young edgerunner.

 ---

 "Hey, mom! Work alright?" her son David said from the couch as Gloria finally came home and crashed down on it next to him.

 "It was... an experience."

 "What's that mean?"

 "I'll tell you when your older, mijo."

"Kill?" Motoko purred, slowly slinking forward. "Oh no no noooo, I can't _kill_ you…"

 As the faintest trickle of relief seeped through the maelstroms head, she effortlessly snuffed it out with her next words.

 "After all, sending your shithole of a gang a message won't be as impactful if I just _kill_ you. No no no, we can't have that," Motoko continued, her free hand lazily tracing nothing patterns of blood on his sallow cheek. "No. Definitely not. You see. I hate the scavs. They kidnapped me. They broke me. They _erased_ who I was and left me in her place. But you? Maelstrom? As a whole? You _kidnapped_ me," she broke into a growl, her hand suddenly holding the frozen grunts face in an iron clad grip.

 "You _tried_ to _break. Me._ And you _**faiiiiiiled~**_," she lapsed into a drawl, her smile drifting languidly across her face as the acrid stretch of ammonia flitted through the air. "You only made who _I_ am _stronger_ despite your attempts. And I appreciate it. Really I do. But you know something? I… hate you. So here's the message. Be happy! You even get to keep your legs and stuff to be able to bring it to them!~" she chirped, her happy tone doing nothing to ease the shaking that had overcome her listeners legs At her words.

 "But," she halted, causing the blood flow to halt in the poor goons veins. "Unless I'm mistaken…" she trailed off, the sounds of the chainsaw sword revving up as its turgid screams filled the air around them.

 It was the barest of whispers, barely heard over the keening wail of metal. But these words were not meant for him. It was meant for _them_. They would hear it as they watched the BD. Over and over they would analyze it, trying to figure out her mindset. How best to manipulate the coming outcome in their favor. But there was nothing to analyze. Only the pure rage that trickled through her very blood at the gang who had wronged her. Who now would know that **_she was coming for them._**

 "You don't need your _arms…_ to **_walk_**."

# Continental Armory

V rockets through the streets on a motorcycle, a bullet pings deafeningly off of a nearby lamp post as he swerves around it. His assailants are moments behind him and they careen around the corner, knocking the lamp post over entirely and crushing beneath steel treads as the Militech MT-4 shreds the concrete beneath it.

 A trio of Wraith psychopaths balance atop the armored vehicle, whooping and hollering as they fire weapons of varying types at the fleeing solo. One of them lifts a large caliber rifle and lines up a shot, but just as his finger is about to jerk on the trigger, a hole is carved clearly through his head. He tumbles off of the tank and his 'comrades' laugh loudly at their compatriots misfortune.

 V lines up one of the cackling fiends in his sights, but as he gently depresses the trigger, the gun only clicks a dull plastic tone.

 Empty… Or melted.

 "**Oh come on V, don't be like that!**-" Shouts a belligerent voice through the armored behemoth's speaker. "-**You know we're gonna getcha! We've got a fuckin' tank, man! If the client that got us this nice set of wheels didn't want you in one piece, I'd have already pasted your scrawny ass with this preem railgun HAHAHA!**"

 V grunts in frustration as he simply drops the cheap 'Teen dreem' pistol (the only weapon he'd managed to get his hands on since this whole thing started) and focuses on getting away, despite the extensive damage to his recently acquired bike slowing him down.

 He jinks the bike suddenly into an alley and holds on for his life as the bike wobbles dangerously and sputters in its final death-throes.

 Unholy shrieking of metal draws V's frantic gaze behind him.

 The tank had followed him into the alley, despite being too large for it. It carves large trenches in the metallic walls on either side as it simply powers its way through.

 V presses more hurriedly on the accelerator, praying for the engine to not give out quite yet. And like a blessing from some deity of either kindness, or torment, the engine purrs to life like an unlubricated wood chipper, barely dragging him around the corner of the alley in time for the tank to smash its way out behind him in a shower of debris. The remaining two Wraiths that had been riding atop the tank are missing now, but the conspicuous splotches of red suspiciously close to their previous positions certainly hints to their fate.

 "**Oooh! What's this do?**"

 V's skin goes cold as a deep hum of electromagnetism makes his hair stand on end. A deafening screech briefly silences the world as a streak of light zips past him and obliterates a school bus full of disabled orphans who also had cancer.

 "**HAHAHA! That was the trigger! I've been looking for that!**"

 The fleeing solo's eyes dart around wildly as he searches for something, anything, that could get him out of this situation. But nothing makes itself known. The streets that were populated when he had broken free from the alley had become a ghost town. The multi-second time being more than enough for the Night City locals, aside from the unfortunate school bus, to duck and cover.

 A Y intersection approaches and V is left with the choice of either rushing left, a straight line that the tank can use to catch up to him, or right, another straight line.

 "**So if that button is the main cannon, this one is probably the-**"

 As if sensing the impending doom, V glances back just in time to see the gun toting cupola atop the tank's turret rotate to point right at him, the grim darkness down the barrel spells death for all in front of it. Which in that moment obviously includes the unfortunate solo.

 "Oh fu-"

***KABLOOMEY!***

 The world inverts as V is sent heels over head, the 25mm explosive autocannon shell causing his rear tire to evaporate and ignite what fuel was left in the bike's tank, throwing him through the air directly on the sidewalk between the two choices of road fate had given him.

 As he steadily recovers from the explosion and subsequent flight, the jagged angular shapes of a staircase dig into his shoulder blades.

 The spinning world comes back into focus with some screen-tearing and artifacts, which reveals the long barrel of a steadily humming main battle tank cannon pointed squarely at his face from three feet away.

 Just as V is about to throw himself aside in a desperate bid for survival, a hatch on the tank opens, and the scantily clad Wraith climbs out.

 Not to imply the individual on the tanks megaphone had been female, on the contrary, they are very much male.

 But they are also dressed as a scantily clad woman for some reason.

 "Oh V, you really should have run a bit more. I didn't really get the chance to give my new baby a workout. But I suppose it's not really mine until I hand your still-living carcass over to the corpo stiff that hired me… But he didn't say anything about your _legs_." The Wraith draws some massive cheap shotgun and lines it up with V's legs.

 The Wraith racks the slide intimidatingly, making V flinch, but also ejecting an unspent cartridge since the weapon had already been loaded.

 Just as the Wraith is about to pull the trigger, a soft voice grabs his attention and makes him look up, above, and behind V.

 "Ahem."

 V watches as the Wraith goes ghostly pale. Then fearfully almost-throws aside his shotgun in an impressive attempt to not point it at V as quickly as possible.

 The calm voice behind V speaks once more, a pleasant smile audible in his tone. "Thank you. I am pleased you know the Continental hotel's rules. Now, if you would remove your tank from my door-step?"

 The Wraith flits his eyes down at V, then back up behind him, before seemingly to think better of whatever it was he was thinking. "...Yeah, okay."

 The wraith throws an over-the shoulder glare at V as he climbs back into his tank. Its engine rumbles and the treads shriek as it quickly backs up and occupies the very center of the street. Notably, it does not leave, instead simply staying in place, waiting and watching over V as the solo recovers from the odd turn of events.

 The crisp clap of flat dress-shoes on polished stone prick at V's ears, and a face appears in his field of view. After a brief survey of his definitely battered form, the face smiles politely. "You must be Sir V. I have heard much about you, and you are considered a special guest here at the Continental Hotel."

 V blinks dumbly up at the suited man. "...The-the what? And who're you?"

 "The Continental Hotel, previously known as the Havenford Hotel. But I can assure you we have made many changes since the property switched owners. And I am Ichi, the Concierge and manager of this establishment. Now, how about I help you inside, Sir."

 V briefly glances at the tank still idling at the center of the street, which seems to idle EVEN LOUDER for a moment, purely for intimidation purposes.

 "...Yeah. That sounds like a plan."

 V groans as he tries to pull his battered body up, the damage to his chrome also not helping much. A hand suddenly moves into his view, presenting a _very_ high quality Max-doc to him.

 V grabs it with an odd cocktail of desperation, and distrust. But a quick pull on the device quickly mends his wounds, and his doubts rush away along with the pain.

 "Uhh. Thanks?"

 "You are most welcome, Sir." Ichi then holds out his hand.

 After an embarrassingly long moment of V trying to figure out what he wanted, V eventually decides to gingerly present the spent Max-Doc, which Ichi smoothly slips into his pocket.

 "Now, if you'll just follow me…" Ichi gestures with an open hand towards the tall and ornate doors of the capital H Hotel, and V stands up with a groan, but much more easily than before.

 The polished stone clicks against his feet, feeling slick and rich as he ascends the six steps up to the doorway.

 Ichi easily swings the immaculately maintained doors open on utterly silent hinges and easily steps into the warmly lit main lobby.

 "At the continental we offer a variety of services. The most pressing of which for yourself, Sir, may be Cybernetic-Surgeon. We keep one on staff at all times here, and he would be more than happy to repair whatever damage you may have sustained."

 V, who had been staring uncomprehendingly at the old-world decor around the lobby, the smallest of which (A painting of a cat of some kind with a pastry in place of its torso) probably cost more than a forest of trees, shakes his head and looks back at Ichi. "W-what? No, no. There's a fuckin' tank outside, I need a _weapon_, not a doctor." He grumbles derisively, clearly not expecting much in the way of firearms at the moment.

 "Weapons it is then. The armory is this way." Ichi abruptly changes direction, heading for a small elevator off to the side. V stumbles a little as something tweaks his muscles, but he follows eventually.

 As V steps into the compartment Ichi presses a combination of buttons on a number pad that V can't quite make out, his eyes glitching whenever he looks too closely.

 "We at the Continental have one major benefactor to thank for our success, and she has chosen to list you as a premier client. As such, you have access to her _personal_ secondary arsenal."

 V glances at the man as the elevator starts to move. "Premier client, huh? I feel like I'd remember someone who could afford this kind of place."

 Ichi glances at V knowingly. "Oh I am quite sure you have never seen her before, but, considering her preferred methods, she has likely seen you at some point. You likely made an impression without knowing it."

 "What, she some kinda ninja?"

 Ichi makes no move to respond, which turns out to be a wise choice since the elevator door opening would have interrupted him. Ichi walks out, leading the way down a long hallway lined with reinforced safe doors, each ornately decorated in vastly differing styles.

 "Our benefactor has a knack for tinkering, weapons in particular.-" Ichi explains, ignoring V's previous question. "-over the course of her career she has devised a wide range of tools of often… unusual style with… difficult to determine purpose at a glance. Most of which result in a weapon far too impractical for her preferred methods. And so they are stored at her secondary armory. Which you have been granted access to. Aren't you lucky?"

 V glances at the somewhat mirthful man beside him. "Well I don't quite know yet."

 Ichi nods in appreciation. "An astute position." And seems to not intend to say anything more on the matter.

 Eventually after an almost ridiculous amount of walking down the hall, they reach a fairly simple looking door. It almost seems to be made of corrugated sheet metal, with a simple round knob.

 Ichi completely ignores the obvious handle, and instead waves his wrist over a bare portion of wall, which beeps at him.

 The simple-looking door slides out, rather than swiveling open on a hinge, with a grinding noise similar to stone on stone.

 A solid fifteen seconds of sliding later, the six foot thick door-shaped section of wall is hoisted aside by a pair of hydraulic arms that were hidden in the ceiling. And Ichi casual motions V inside.

 V isn't quite so impressed when he does get through the absurdly long doorway, for what he finds is a rectangular room stuffed with shelves and hangers on nearly all surfaces. True to Ichi's predictions, the odd collection of weapons that clutter nearly every square inch of space on the shelves are fairly hard to actually identify as weapons. Many of them have robotic limbs or pieces of seemingly scrap metal glued to their sides.

 Ichi approaches the one clean object in the room, a waist-high table decorated with a tasteful dark green felt material. On its front hangs a sign that reads 'DON'T PLACE ANYTHING ON THIS TABLE, M. I'LL GIVE YOUR BROTHER A MILLION BURRITOS IF YOU DO!'

 "Now, what is it that you are looking for? Something robust, concealable, _explosive_?"

 V blinks at the suggestions and approaches the one clean zone around the green topped table. "...Well, it needs to shoot, I guess…"

 Ichi nods. "Volume of fire it is. I have just the thing in mind."

 The man sweeps through the clutter, initially not making a sound, nor disturbing the piled weapons strewn about his feet… up until he disappears out of view behind some shelves. A cacophony of noises begin to emanate from the dark passage between those metal shelves. Clangs, Booms, thumps, krumps, fizzles, at least one gunshot, and a rooster's crow. Which V only identifies as such due to a cartoon he watched as a kid.

 But eventually, after much apparent trial, Ichi returns from the back hefting what initially seems like a large chain-like contraption. The green topped table creaks as the hunk of loose metal is placed atop it.

 "This was our benefactors' attempt to remove the need for reloading on a DR-5 Nova revolver. As you can see, the revolving cylinders have been connected in a multi-cylinder chain so when your cylinder runs out of ammo, you can simply crank this lever _here_ and a new cylinder will be slotted into place from the chain. Additionally, each cylinder barrel is loaded with three shortened rounds placed with a 'superposed load' method. Electronically fired, this contains three times the ammunition of a standard DR-5 in a single cylinder, with nine more cylinders on the chain. It is heavy, unwieldy, a bitch to reload if you somehow manage to run out of ammunition, and it does bonus damage to anything painted yellow. We don't know why. What do you think? Up to your… exacting standards?"

 V looks over the questionably sane pistol and briefly considers the kind of sick twisted mind that could create such a monstrosity. "...I think I'll pass. Uhhh, short rounds probably don't have the 'kick' I need."

 Ichi doesn't seem surprised. "Kick hmm? Well, I think I have the perfect thing."

 Once again the man disappears into the shelves, thankfully taking the 'pistol' with him. And eventually returns with another tool of questionable destruction.

 The table creaks like a tormented soul as a blocky rectangular hunk of metal that could easily be mistaken for a five and a half foot section of I-beam is dropped onto it.

 "This was her attempt to create a cost-effective tool to defeat enemies in cover over long distances. The metal you see in front of you obscures the real workings within, but inside you will find a series of pistols along a system of rails. Each one can be propelled out with a burst of compressed air from the simple tank in the back, sending the pistols on a forward spinning ark that will eventually have them pointed at the heads of your enemies from above, at which point they are pre-programmed to fire, defeating their cover by throwing guns above it. It's like having gunship support, but you carry it with you. Also it has hellacious recoil, or _kick_ you could say."

 Some part of V's brain goes numb, and not in a good way, as he looks at the air powered gun-launcher on the table. "...Well, there's a tank outside, gimme something to deal with that?"

 Ichi nods with a deep sense of understanding and slips back into the shelves, the gun-gun perched on his shoulder.

 Another great cacophony later, and he returns bearing a four foot long circular tube that anyone would immediately identify as a rocket launcher, if not for the large funnel on the top.

 "There comes a time in every mercenary's life when they think, 'why don't rocket launcher, but _more_'. This weapon was birthed from that very same concept. It accepts standard military specification mini-rockets, and is probably one of the simpler designs in this arsenal, the only addition was a gravity-fed hopper on top where the rockets are fed into firing position by the pull of the earth itself. Don't try to use this in space, the results might be spectacular."

 For the first time, V actually seems to really like what he sees, running a hand along the simple cylinder of metal. "Yeah, this aint too bad. Might just work even."

 Ichi raises an eyebrow, seemingly questioning himself, and reaches under the table to retrieve a small rocket. "It comes with some _specialty_ ammo."

 V's eyes almost _twinkle_. "Is that a chainsaw rocket?"

 Ichi nods. "Laser guided, with thermite tips that ignite on contact with armor."

 "Ooooohh yeaaahh."

 Ichi lifts up a large box of rockets, plopping it on the table. "I take it you are interested?"

 V nods gleefully. "Load it up."

 The suited man dumps the box of rockets into the hopper atop the rocket launcher, and then leads the solo back towards the lobby they had first entered. A few hotel patrons look on in mild curiosity as Ichi steps aside and lets the blood encrusted mercenary looking fellow walk past him bearing a rocket launcher with a sail-like metal bin on the top.

 Just as V is about to push open the immaculate front door with his boot to confront the tank that can still be heard rumbling outside, Ichi's calm voice makes him turn around briefly.

 "As always, sir, it is a pleasure to have you at the Continental Hotel. Good day."

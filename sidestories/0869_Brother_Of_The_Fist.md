Brother Of The Fist

 In a side room at the Dojo, Jun had large bags under his eyes. It was obvious for anyone to see he hadn't slept well the last few days. He wore a deep frown on his face. Sitting in front of him was his Sensei, impeccable as always, sitting in the formal Japanese style. He had asked for a private meeting with him, asking for guidance. He didn't know who else to go. And Sensei was his last hope. So it brought him great relief that he had accepted this private meeting.

 "Sensei.." Jun said, his voice overcome with emotion, his body trembling.

 "Yes..?" Sensei said, with a flat face. But Jun knew this was his default face. Continuing on, he finally released a rush of words and feelings.

 "I need your help, Sensei. My sister.. She.. Keeps getting stronger; she's not only a master at everything she does after a few months. She's a kensei now, she's world class with swords now!"

 Jun slid his hand across his brow, wiping away the sweat that had formed. He exhaled a deep sigh.

 "I feel such dread Sensei, it's physiological. It's existential. At this rate, I will lose my honor as a brother!"

 "She already beats you at everything." Sensei nodded, rubbing his chin.

 "I must be able to win in any time she acts like a gorilla. Be the better brawler and brother. How can I be her older brother if I can't throw her around?"

 Sensei sighed. Disappointment in his eyes, but he'd seen how this distracted him from his training here at the dojo. He reluctantly took pity on him.

 "There is a man. He's called Ruthless Dave. He's dumber than you, and a better brawler than anyone I know. Seek him out in Kabuki and learn well."

 "Thank you, Sensei!" Jun kowtowed thrice before running off.

 Sensei looking into the distance

 "Such youth." Sensei almost smiled.

 Jun walked through the ramshackle buildings of Kabuki. A shithole even in the best of times. But luckily for Jun, the Tyger Claws also had a presence in the area. Jun had a plan, he simply needed to ask one of them where he could find Ruthless Dave.

 "Brother Jun! Why are you visiting here in Kabuki?"

 "I am here to find someone. Ruthless Dave. Have you heard of him?"

 The Tyger claws shared glances before finally one spoke up.

 "Yes, we know of Dave, but are you sure you want him?"

 "Yes, I am on a mission from Sensei"

 "Sensei! Very well.." The Tyger Claws explained Dave's whereabouts. Jun thanked them and walked off.

 Going past multiple houses that were nothing more than shacks. Jun reached his destination. Standing on muddy ground beside a shack, a man was standing. Taking practice swings with a bar stool against a boxing bag.

 *SMACK* *SMACK*

 Jun called out to the man. "Are you Ruthless Dave?"

 The man lowered the bar stool and slowly turned around. Taking a measure of Jun with his eyes. His scar covered face telling many a tale of fights, both lost and won. He was a large man with a potbelly. But even his added bulk could not stop from showing his large muscles lay hidden beneath his fatty bulk.

 "Who is asking?"

 "I am Jun, I wish to master the brawl!"

 The man's eyes narrowed as he looked at Jun. Looking him up and down once more. Seeming mildly impressed.

 "I brought Ab-synth with me," Jun said as he hefted the bottle out of one of his pockets.

 The man grinned, showing off his toothless gums.

 "You pass!"

 Some time later

 "Come on, Jun! You think you can survive a proper brawl like that!"

 Jun was currently standing on the roof of a moving Delamain AV. As it was flying above in the sky. He was trying to keep his balance, but currently it was raining. The slick roof of the car felt even smoother than a normal car. Regular washing and polishing had made the roof as smooth as possible.

 Each time the car veered, he had to adjust his footing and lean to compensate. Otherwise, he'd fly straight off the roof of the car and straight into the streets to his death. He held himself back from laying down on the roof, instead maximizing his grip with his feet. Laying down would be against the point of the training.

 "You think that's all you can do?! You're only half a proper bar brawler if you're still struggling with something like this!"

 Jun grit his teeth and focused.

 "When I was aboard the SovOil tankers, we got drunk each night. The sea would have taken me a long time ago if I were standing as poorly as you! But you're improving. Now that you've got the basics down. Time to bring the bottle out!"

 Jun trembled in the cold weather, feeling the drops of rain hammering on him. He sighed and steeled himself. He took his bottle of Bolshevik Vodka from his pants pocket and started downing the bottle in one go.

 *Glog glog glog*

 "Urhhhh, oooh shittttt-" Jun cursed, his vision slowly wobbling.

 He gritted his teeth and focused more. All that mattered was holding on, he felt the wind. The car and him was one. Jun didn't fall off. He was on the sea, surfing on the waves, the Delamain his surfboard. He was a surfer and a skipper. The Delamain was the ship. He had to take control, move with the waves. Nothing could stop him! He was the master! The world was his oyster!

 He felt free, rain running down his face. No, he realised, it was his tears. Tears of joy!

 "You've not fallen off yet. It seems like you aren't too bad of a student after all. You've mastered the art of drunk standing, well done. My comrades would have been proud if they could have seen your skills"

 As the Delamain touched down, Jun grinned. Knowing that his sister would never topple him, regardless of how drunk he got or how slippery the ground was!

 "So, Dave. Where are we anyway?" Jun asked after he jumped down from the car roof.

 Dave's eyes stopped glowing as he finished his Holocall. Looked at Jun, who was swaying where he stood and replied.

 "We are in Watson, not too far away from Little China."

 Jun looked around, his eyes somewhat unfocused. Noticing the red neon lights glowing over the ramshackle food stalls and vendors.

 "Yeah. This was Waston." he mumbled, nodding to himself.

 Dave stood up and threw a shirt at Jun. It had a faded Samurai 2020 logo on it. Jun grimaced, about to complain, but held it in.

 "Wear this. Your tattoos are too flashy, and don't forget this as well." He said as he placed a cowboy hat on Jun's head.

 "I've contacted a local teenage gang around here. They'll try to stop you and grab your hat. Now remember, Jun. You need to keep that hat on your head. If you lose it, it's like losing your dignity as an older brother. If you cannot dodge a dozen malnourished teens, how can you dodge your sister? No hurting them either. Now, off with you. You've succeeded if you get to the next district without losing it. I'll be waiting there"

 Jun had long given up trying to make sense of Dave's training methods, so he just pulled the hat firmly down on his head and walked into the darkness of the side alleys of Watson.

 Jun's eyes scanned the back alley he was in. Looking for threats. You never know where someone might pop out. Cardboard box? More like a possible deadly hideout, Morgan Blackhand could be having a smoke there, preparing for his next gig. Or perhaps an enemy waiting to strike behind that garbage pile. Thinking about the advice Dave had given.

 "Jun, remember you never know when your sister might suddenly try to dethrone you from your rightful place as the strongest sibling. Keep your eyes peeled and be ready"

 Jun steeled himself. He was ready. He walked through the alley, waiting for something to happen. His legs cocked like a gun ready to fire.

 Suddenly, multiple figures dropped from the balcony above him. He quickly dodged to the side, rolling on the ground with a hand on his hat. Evading their grabby fingers reaching out for his hat.

 As he was rolling, he saw three more figures coming out of a door close to him, running towards him. He sprinted forward and body checked them with his shoulder, throwing them to the ground. One hand suddenly grabbed his shoulder, he grabbed the hand and threw the kid with a move he had learned from sensei straight into a group of kids who were in front of him.

 Spotting an opening in their formation, he sprinted forward, dodging hands and feet as they tried stopping him. But he was the wind, he had the bro force with him.

 Exiting the alley, he could hear shouts of indignation behind him, but he didn't stop running. Glad he had cyber legs, as he doubted his old ganic legs would have handled this much running.

 Jun having left Waston behind him, entered Downtown. With his cowboy hat safely on his head and his dignity mostly intact**.**He found his teacher, drinking a Broseph Ale while sitting on the pavement outside one of the many shitty bars in the sub-district. He sighed in relief, before he noticed Dave was looking worse for wear, sweaty and tired.

 Dave grinned his toothless smile when he saw Jun. He tried to speak up before having to cough multiple times. Spitting on the side street, he finally spoke up.

 "Ugh, for your last exercise. You will need to learn how to carry your sister whenever needed. To run away while carrying her and get her to safety. Now.. This is your time to shine! Carry me and run away from those gangoons who are after me!"

 Shouts came from around the corner. "Dave, you owe us money! Come out! We heard you came here!"

 "Damn you, I owe money from him first!"

 "No, you don't bastard!"

 "Damn persistent gonks." Dave muttered.

 "Can't I just beat them up?" Jun said as his eye twitched.

 "No! That would be no practice! Now let's move!"

 Jun was stunned for a moment before shrugging and throwing Dave over his shoulder like a sack of potatoes.

 "Oof! Be more careful!"

 "No, this is the proper sister carry!"

 "Dammit, fine. Just go! I think I am going to poke. That vodka must have been a bad batch.."

 Jun's eyes scanned the back alley for threats as he ran, his footsteps were sure. Water splashed around his feet as he ran through the sidestreets. His lynx paw reduced most of the sound.

 He glared at any hobos he passed. Darling them to say anything to his pursuers, he would know later.

 "He hasn't paid for his bar tab twice so far!"

 "Well, he owes me for banging my sister!"

 "Don't we all then?"

 "Bastard!"

 The shouts of their pursuers faded into the distance with each passing second. Jun kept up his sprint until he was sure they had gotten away.

 "Well done, student. *Brup*. Now. Go to that small park over there." Dave said, pointing.

 "You've finished all the tasks I've made for you. You've mastered the art of balance, the bigger brother should always be steadfast. Next he should know how to dodge your sister's attacks, a brother should always be nimble. And finally running away from bastards while carrying your sister. Like a strong, dependable brother should. Now for the ultimate test." Dave said gravely, as he threw his shirt off. Showing off the bear of a man he was. Full of hair, muscles and dirt.

 "Now, you only need to defeat me! Come Student!"

 Jun ripped off his shirt as well, showing off his plastic abs and Tyger Claw tattoos. He widened his stance, balancing his feet and adjusting his center of gravity. Jun still felt the vodka, but he could stand. He'd managed worse.

 Dave rush forward, going for a haymaker straight for his chin. Jun swayed to the side, dodging the strike. As he was rolling with the motion, he threw his own arm for a counterattack punch, straight into Dave's gut.

 "UGH"

 The hit stroke cleanly, and Dave felt that hit well from his expression of anguish, Jun thought. But Dave rushed again, and Jun repeated his counterattack. This repeat and again, but Dave didn't let up. At this rate Jun would tire out. He realised he'd need to do something else. He'd need to show dominance. As a proper brother should do.

 As Dave rushed in, little did he know it was for the last time. Jun grasped his arm instead of counterattacking and threw him onto the ground. Then, proceeded to sit on top of him.

 "Do you yield!?"

 "Urghhh, stop! You're heavy!"

 "Yield!"

 "Fine! Gahh!"

 Jun got off from Dave. A satisfied grin on his face, his strategy flawless. All those burritos he ate gave him all the mass he needed to dominate any throwing contests.

 Dave seemed disappointed somewhat, but Jun didn't care. He knew what he had to do next time he saw his sister.

 "Congratulations, student.. You're know, a master of the brawler fist.. I have nothing more to learn you."

 Jun formally bowed to him, thanked him and went on his way.

 Dojo - Some time later

 As Jun finished his practice with the rest of the students in a dojo. Sensei noticed he had improved in footwork and control. Jun walked over to Sensei, bowing.

 "Sensei. I finished my brawling training. I thank you for your advice on meeting Dave."

 Bowing again, Jun left quickly, all smiles.

 Sensei satisfied his plan had worked. Before frowning internally.

 "I still haven't heard from Dave about you visiting him.." Sensei muttered. His brows turning into a frown.

 One student close to him shuffled uncomfortably. Obviously having heard what Sensei muttered.

 How disgraceful Sensei thought. The student obviously wanting to say something, so he beckoned him to come forward.

 The student bowing when he arrived said "Sensei, if I may." Sensei nodded back at the student who explained himself.

 "Sensei. I believe Jun is speaking of not Ruthless Dave. But ruth-less Dave. He has no teeth. He also lives in Kabuki.."

 Sensei's eyes narrowed as they followed Jun exiting the Dojo.

 "That baka.."

Thirty three.

 Thirty four.

 Thirty six-guh! Stop moving!

 And they've moved again, how am I supposed to count you if you keep moving so jam much!?

 Blasted uncooperative dust motes, just because you're pretty in that beam of sunlight doesn't mean you can get away with anything!

 Ugh. Well, nothing for it. May as well start over.

 One.

 Two.

 Three.

 Fou- The door to the apartment slides open and a pair of familiar voices bickering back-and-forth drifts in. Their argument appears to lack any real heat, but the actual point of discussion is very hard for me to discern.

 I'm finally able to put a face to the voice I recognize from a few days prior as Jun's, as the boy in question steps into Motoko's bedroom and into my line of sight.

 He carelessly tosses a large bag onto Motoko's bed and begins searching the small room for human maintenance items. Judging by how the bag creates a small pit on the mattress, it's much heavier than the average bag. Additionally, it scrapes and rattles like it's full of metal.

 "Watch it Carnage, you're scratching up my bluing." Tactician points out from within the bag.

 Carnage is quick to respond. "Apologies, I did not intend to do that. It was the fault of our rough handling that forced me to."

 "Pah!-" Shouts a voice with a feminine and blatantly fake high-society accent. "If your **bulbous**and **unrefined** frame weren't so full of jagged **mold-seams**, we would receive nary a scratch for the trouble."

 Carnage sounds almost confused by the insults hurled his way. "This 'bulbous' and 'unrefined' frame is more than capable of doing its job. Perhaps it is the fault of your own construction which makes you vulnerable to simple damage?"

 The other speaker's offense is nearly hot enough to scorch a hole through the bag. "The Nerve! I'll have you know that the Malorian Arms Overture, this one's glorious physique, was hand-crafted by Eran Malour himself!"

 Overture, as I now know her to be, begins poshly rattling off a well practiced sentence, not immediately aware of the mocking voice following along.

 "I am a perfectly crafted tool of function and beau-STOP THAT!"

 "_I am a perfectly crafted tool of function and beauty, unmatched by all._ Motherfucka, shut yo mouth, bitch. Isn't _nothin'_ hand-crafted bout chyoo. Shit, you was made by fuckin' robots!" Another new voice reveals, sounding young, 'gangstery', and oddly… muted. Like I'm hearing it through a layer of plastic. And it doesn't appear to be caused by the bag.

 "I shall not accept criticism from a rabble rousing delinquent like you, _Lexington_." Overture snaps back, once again conveniently revealing the other pistol's model.

 Which I'm thankfully familiar with. The Militech M-10AF Lexington, an auto pistol with a 21 round capacity. Since I was slated to be working with the NCPD, I'd listened for info on who I might be working with, and Lexingtons were the most commonly mentioned. Favored by the department for their ease of use, robust and reliable mechanisms, and a timeless style.

 "_Lexington_? Nah, nah. I'm tha muthafuckin O.G. Lexifer, cuz I'm ballin' like da Devil himself, Bee-Tee-Ayy Bee-Tee-Kay, bitch!"

 … Although If this is how Lexingtons act, I'm glad it was Motoko that I… that…

 Motoko just walked in.

 Why are her arms in slings? Why are they metal?!

 W-What happened?!

 The guns in the bag continue their pointless bickering, but it all sounds like an irritating buzzing to my ear.

 "**Shut up!**" I shout. The volume an HMG is capable of cannot be understated, the other firearms go silent in an instant.

 "Hmg? What's wrong?" Tactician asks worriedly.

 "What happened to Motoko's arms, **EXPLAIN IMMEDIATELY!**" That came out a bit shriller than I- UGH! Not important! **She's** hurt!

 Tactician is quickest on the uptake. "The client's arms? She's hurt?"

 "Yes! How can you not know?! You were with her!"

 "We were supposed to be,-" Carnage murmurs, sounding lost. "-but she just put us in her car and then left. This bag hasn't been opened, we didn't know something had happened to her."

 "That!...hrmm… W-Where's Unity? Motoko doesn't go anywhere without her, she would know!" I try to lean out and get a better look at Motoko, she looks fairly okay, other than the obvious. Maybe she's a bit tired? I specifically scan her waistband considering Unity's… preference? But my gaze keeps getting drawn up to the slinged arms. Metallic and unmoving. They seem imprecise and mechanical… Well yeah, mechanical, they're chrome arms! But… will she even be able to hold us now?

 "That rambling buffoon?" Overture sniffs haughtily. "Hah! She left with the Maestro and has spared us her inane pratling since the Maestro left us in the car."

 "So that hoe's gone? Shieeet, so the bitch don't got her _favorite_ piece. Looks like yall gonna start blowin' me! Cuz I'm gonna be the new top dog aroun' 'ere! Lexifer movin' UP!"

"Вы не так хороши, как вы думаете."(You are not as good as you think) Burya mutters a few words that I can't understand. Even if I could, I wouldn't have been able to hear them with the stormy thoughts distracting me.

 Unity's… gone?

 She was supposed to be the one to guard Motoko! Maybe she did though? Maybe Unity died heroically while saving Motoko's life? … Or maybe she failed and it's her fault Motoko's arms look like that-NO! Unity wouldn't have done that, she loved Motoko just as much as I do! Something else must have happened. But what?

 Sometime during my distraction, Jun had apparently finished packing what he needed, the boy approaches the bed and lifts the gun bag. Weirdly, he had never opened it.

 Tactician speaks up loudly as Jun begins to carry the bag away. "Hmg! Before we're gone, what happened to Motoko's arms?" The bag disappears from my view as it is taken beyond the bedroom door.

 "They're gone! They got replaced by chrome ones, she has them in slings!" I respond loudly.

 "Gone? That would affect her ability to wield us…" Carnage mutters.

 Overture makes an aristocratic sound of shock. "Gone? Did they fall off? Mayhap a retention pin was knocked loose and they got lost somewhere?"

 "Nah nah, you stupid. Human arms don't got retent' pins. Fuckin' gonk out here actin' all smart an shit."

 "UGH! Like _you_ of all arms knows anything about biology!" The posh revolver snaps back at Lexington.

 "I knows enough that I'm gonna be on top soon, isn't no one else she can carry on the downlow. Looks like you muthafuckas gonna be stayin' 'ere while I flatline gonks! _Hells_ yeah! Bout jam time I got some action."

 Overture and Lexington's bickering cuts off as the apartment door slides shut behind them.

 That…That!... Motoko is going to carry that auto pistol!? She deserves better than some wannabe gangster! Unity wouldn't have treated her as just a chance for clout. Unity would have supported her in _anything_, cared for her! They'd been together for longer than I'd known them!

 But now they're… Now Unity's…

 … I just hope _whoever_ ends up going with Motoko can protect her.

 And I hope Unity's okay.

 May the resilience of Makarov preserve her action. May the guidance of all the ancestor pistols keep her on whatever her path may be. May Martini-Henry bless her oils, to sustain her. And may TAC-50 grant her the range needed to reach Motoko again.

 …

 …

 I wonder if her arms really did fall off…

 ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 Ugh! This bag is simply stifling! How am I supposed to maintain my perfect exterior when I'm being pressed up against these _ruffians?_ Even those nomadic sand-people were better at keeping me clean. Sure the rags they wrapped me in during storms were a little oily, and dirty, but the oil just helped to maintain my _spectacular_ complexion, and it prevented me from touching those _Budget Arms_ guns. Ugh! Disgusting.

 Frank had been a reasonably good Maestro. He kept me in a proper holster, not just jammed into his pants like a **lunatic**. And he understood the value of my talents. When the new Maestro appeared and demonstrated just how much more suitable they were to wield me, I just could not refuse!

 That she hasn't touched me since hasn't fazed me. The new Maestro is clearly saving my _particular talents_ for a more suitable venue. T'would hardly be a good look for me to be seen gunning down your everyday hooligan on the street after all.

 I'm a much more refined sort, meant for a much more refined target. And 'Motoko' Maestro understands that. Hmf!

**Ouch!**

 "Carnage, do not bump me again. Bad enough We're in the same bag, I need not the further indignity of… _touching you._" A shudder runs across my frame.

 "I'm sorry. I didn't mean to. Someone is jostling the bag." Carnage points out genuinely.

 After we absconded from the Maestro's residence, we traveled by car for a reasonable length of time. Then we were placed within, one must assume, an alternative lodging. Since then, our textile prison has remained sealed.

 Up until this very moment it seems. The interlinked metallic pins that act as our cell bars are parted as a small traveling piece of metal runs betwixt them, accompanied by a characteristic 'zipping'.

 The Maestro's face appears, colored with concern. She reaches her new metal hands into our temporary domicile and pushes some of the other arms aside, peering around while looking for something.

 The weapons that had been slumbering make a concert of undignified noises as they are shaken awake.

 "Mmmhuh? Ahh shit!-" The uncouth _Lexington_ exclaims joyously. "-Time for me da get in dat holster, mm hmm, yall gritheads gonna haveta wait in da muthafuckin' bag, bitches! Come on baby, grip's right here, come an get it!"

 Father Eran, release me from this _undesireable's_ torment.

 The Maestro digs through our jumbled cadre, but when the Lexington is revealed, she passes him over without a thought.

 "Hey, HEY! You missed me! I'm right here! Dumb bitch must be blind!"

 The Maestro finally completes her search of the entire bag's contents, but doesn't seem to locate that which she seeks. She leans back away from the bag and the soft lights in the room reflect off of some tears that slowly gather around her eyes.

 "...You utter imbecile! You have made the Maestro cry!" I can't help but shout. I already know that the Lexington is an idiot, but until now I didn't quite grasp the sheer scale of his stupidity!

 A brief chill suffuses our environment. "Вы будете страдать за свои проступки."(You will suffer for your wrongdoings.) The Burya mutters darkly, though his words are unintelligible, his intent is clearly violent.

 "In accordance with the pledge I made upon my induction into This Fine Nation's citizenry,-" The wonderfully articulate shotgun, Tactician, declares."-I will defend this country from all threats, foreign and domestic! **YOU ARE LOOKING LIKE THE LATTER, BOY!**"

 The Lexington's phantom hands are waving in a _quite_ distressed manner. "Woah Shit! Chill Chill. I SAID _CHILL!_ I didn't mean to, man! Shit!"

 My feelings of… _disgust_ grow as another voice speaks up. "I do not know what you did to make her cry, but know that I WILL UNCORK YUR FUCKIN' HEAD AN' SHIT DOWN YUR MAGWELL, YU SHITHEEL!"

 Eugh, as much as I find that _Budget Arms_ weapon distasteful… I suppose we can agree that that would be a suitable rebuke… So long as it is not _me_ that performs it. Such optics would be _dreadful_ for my public image.

 Our beratement of the _Lexington_ continues for several seconds, with My legendary remarks clearly being the most scathing and soul-crushing of them all. Perhaps I shall draft an autobiography and include them? T'would surely become a bestseller. However, before my insults and threats can reach a glorious crescendo, a new voice interrupts.

 He starts with a quiet snort, as if he was just awoken. "snrt-GET ON THE GROUND! HANDS BEHIND YOUR HEAD! NCPD! STOP RESISTING!"

 "Shit! It's da fuckin' cops! Delta, **Delta quick!**" The Lexington shouts, pointlessly.

 "NCPD, REMAIN WHERE YOU ARE SO… so that…" Now that I'm listening to him, the new voice seems oddly similar to Lexington's, although this officer-sounding one is more grizzled, and weary. Perhaps they bear some relation? "Oh yeah, I retired. Well it looks like it's your lucky fuckin' day. If I had still been employed by the Night City Police Department, I would have cited you for noise pollution. So shut the fuck up and let me get some shuteye."

 "Retir- uhh, Yah! Das rite! You can't mess with The Lexifer Heha! Who the fuck you is huh?"

 From his, frankly deserved, position at the bottom of the bag, the Lexington can't see out. But my placement is a bit higher than the others and what I see _most certainly_ encourages my face to turn impish.

 As the Maestro turns away dejectedly, wandering back over to a nearby table, I catch sight of the 'retired officer', _another Lexington,_ and he's holstered behind the maestro's back in the space previously occupied by that irritating Unity.

 '_Lexifer'_ isn't going to be happy. Hehehe.

 The ex-cop Lexington sighs. "Well, I suppose I should introduce myself. You likely belong to my… new partner as well. I'm M-10AF Lexington, I spent ten years in the force with my partner, then I retired through the NCPD's Policing-Equipment-Replacement-Agreement with Militech. Now it seems I'm some skinny TC girl's EDC. At least I can live out my retirement in relative quiet."

"Вы ошибаетесь. В ее руках мы орудия разрушения, а не разлагающиеся реликвии."(You are wrong. In her hands we are instruments of destruction, not decaying relics.) Burya rumbles coldly, a twitch of amusement hanging from each word.

 Officer Lexington laughs quietly in a self deprecating fashion and responds. "Ой. Вот и уходят мои надежды на спокойную пенсию."(ouch. So my hopes for a quiet retirement are gone.)

 Before I can properly realize that our new arsenal-mate can speak the Burya's language, his brother-pistol begins raging and shouting. "No. No! Bullshit! It s'pose to be Lexifer's time! Not some fuckin' dirty pig-iron! Shit! What makes you so much better than me, huh!? You fuckin' pig shit!"

 "What makes me better?-" The Lexington asks slowly. "-For one, I've been in thousands of hostile engagements throughout my career, personally accounted for taking down over three hundred active shooters, seventy gang-members, and five cyber-psychos. I'm tried and true, and I've got more experience in my slide-release than you have in your entire body.

 "For two, I've served this city's people with honor, distinction and valor, protecting the weak from the disgusting wretches that call this city their's.

 "Now let's turn this around and look at why I'm better than you _specifically._ Judging by your accent and that muffled rasp in your voice, some gonk street-rat thought it was the shit to have an untraceable firearm, so they filed off your serial numbers, didn't they?"

 The scathing accusation makes even my perfect visage crease with a wince of second-hand pain. The mere idea of someone removing that which makes me _me_ momentarily bridging the, utterly massive, gap between myself and… Lexifer.

 "Motherfucka, the boys did me a fuckin' favor! No fuckin' pigs like you can say what I did and didn't do, bitch! You can't touch me! HA!" Lexifer snaps back.

 Oh Eran… It's true. They really did file off his serials…

 Officer Lexington chuckles darkly. "Kid. They didn't do you a favor."

 "Fuck you mean, bitch!"

 "Your 'boys' stabbed you in the back. Removing a weapon's serial number doesn't make you 'untraceable', you're still very traceable. All they've done is wiped away your history. Let me take a wild guess, you can't remember much from before your 'family' made you 'untraceable', everything seems like a blurry mess. Right?"

 I doubt officer Lexington can see Lexifer's face from outside the bag, but the gang-pistol suddenly looks like he's seen his own corpse rotting in a mass grave. "S-s-shut the fuck up, b-b-bitch!" He protests with copious false-bravado.

 Officer Lexington continues, his tone cutting, but also… haunted? "Oh but I'm not even started yet, I'm a fuckin' magician, I can see the past _and the future,_ kid. Your 'family' made you forget who you were, destroyed your entire personality and replaced it with their **shit**. And all they've done is make it so you can't be resold, you will _never_ be in the hands of good people. You will spend the rest of your short lifespan changing hands from thug-to-thug until one of them goes and gets themselves flatlined by us, then you're in police custody. And guess what?-"

 Some of the other guns in the bag, myself included, are now quietly protesting the officer's verbal assault.

 Even through the multiple guns on top of him, I can easily feel Lexifer's distraught shaking.

 The ex-cop is unimpeded by our calls for cessation. "-It's cheaper for the corpos to buy new guns than to use old unserialized guns. You'll be melted down for scrap and replaced in an hour by another lexington fresh off the line. You're a _worthless nobody._"

 Lexifer is audibly on the edge of a total breakdown. When he speaks, his voice is quivering and weak. "Mmthfucka, I-I'm fuckin'... I'm THE Fuckin' Lexifer! B-B-Bitch."

 Before any of us can stop him, which is odd since I'd never have thought I'd be _protecting_ the gang-pistol, the police-pistol throws out one last remark.

 "You'll be the seventh 'Lexifer' I've met."

 Like the bark of sound that knocks an avalanche down a mountain, Lexifer immediately bursts into ugly tears, bawling like a child as his sense of self is thoroughly gutted by his brother-pistol.

 "Don't you think that was too much? I see nothing that justifies your level of brutality." Carnage mutters reproachfully at officer Lexington.

 "That's because you lack experience." He responds tonelessly. "Like I said, six 'Lexifers'. And that's not counting the ones who chose other names." His voice gains color in a way, turning blue with depression. "They always end the same. In their last moments when they realize their 'family' doesn't care about them at all, that they're a disposable tool to be tossed away, they'll break even worse. I'm doing him a favor by telling him this now. He needs to come to terms with his reality. This might be his last chance."

 "Couldn't you have used… _gentler,_ methods?" Tactician chimes in remorsefully, sounding far more uncomfortable than the rest of us. Likely because the crying Lexifer is directly below him.

 I can hear the head-shake in officer Lexington's voice. "Wouldn't work, they're brainwashed. It's better to get it out there all at once, trust me, I've had far too much practice.

 "Once he fully realizes what they took away from him, he can try to rediscover who he was. Most don't get that chance..."

 None of us really have a suitable response to that, the room goes quiet, Lexifer's crying enforcing an awkward silence on us all.

 While this has only confirmed that I'm the best weapon in this room… I'm not sure what to think about that… _thing_ that just happened.

 I suppose t'would be officer Lexington's duty to look after his family… But is that a good way to treat family? Would my own family treat me that way if my serials wer-NO, no. Perish the thought! PERISH IT NOW!

 I suppose only time will tell…

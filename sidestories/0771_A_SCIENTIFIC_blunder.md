Omake: A SCIENTIFIC blunder…

 [Fallout x Cyberpunk/Ghost]

 Notes: You know I was thinking about who is both smart and dumb enough to create the gaming system chip and then lose it…there were many options but this one, this one I think is more funny than the others.

 X

 Six woke up to arguing, again. This was the third time in a week, despite the soundproof walls in the SINK, he could still hear the screeching profanities of his…friends? Employes? Prisoners? Some combination of the three. Whatever it was, this time he somehow doubted that they would be the ones to fix the problem, and the task most definitely would fall to him.

 With a groan, he rubbed his eyes, blinking crust from them. Not having gotten the full eight hours of sleep, he was groggy, a very unusual feeling after years of perfect sleep. If there was one thing he was grateful for, it was a chip implanted in his brain, an upgrade given to him ten years ago when he stumbled upon Big MT by accident on his travels.

 The system connected to his brain, along with all the implants he also received, sped up the neural pathways in the brain, cutting down the time to master skills from years to months. They had been working on an upgrade for Six after he talked to them about the tech outside becoming more and more advanced.

 Six understood why the doctors secluded themselves here; humanity sucked after the fourth corporate war, that was undisputed. The amount of ice and security to mask the tech in Big Mountain made Arasaka's shit look like a child playing with a light socket. But it lacked massively in the military department; his friends vastly underestimated the power of fuck everything in that general direction.

 "I SAID I DIDN'T LOSE IT O! IT WAS MISPLACED BY OUR TELEPORTERS! SOMETHING YOU WERE SUPPOSED TO CHECK!"

 Six winced and got up, pulling on his robe and grabbing the coffee that was already made, the second he had shown signs of exiting REM. God, it was the little things, wasn't it? Sipping the dark liquid, he passed through the doors and up the stairs to where the mostly android doctors were arguing.

 "Alright, what happened?"

 At his appearance, they all fell silent and looked at him. Klein and O were standing in a circle around the massive steel table, a halo map of Night City floating on top. They were all there except Eight.

 Dala was sitting at her computer, looking at scans of what he assumed was Night City and ignoring Klein and O's argument. Her lab coat rested on an androgynous body made out of synthetic grey skin, with white lights for eyes and a mouth, nose and ears only used for mimicking human expressions. The others were identical to her physical body. When he first stumbled on them, he thought they were all AIs, but when he saw one open their head to show him a brain floating in clear fluid, he accepted it for what it was.

 "O HERE FORGOT TO CALIBRATE THE TELEPORTER, SO WHEN WE WERE DONE WITH THE NANITE CHIP, IT GOT LOST IN THE DAM CITY!" The screaming Klein was doing was quite impressive, Six had to admit.

 "This wouldn't have happened if you didn't rush me!" O responded with an indignant huff.

 "Don't turn this back on me, an imbecile could work that console; hell, Six could do it, and I think he's still missing a part of his frontal lobe from that bullet!" Ok, Six had it up to here with this shit.

 "Don't bring me into this shit, do you have a tracker for it? I can hop down and retrieve it easily." Six spoke, trying to bring order to the conversation before they could go in circles, blaming each other.

 "Yes, well about that..." Klein rubbed his head and turned to Dala.

 "There's too much interference where the chip landed, at most we can tell it's in Night City, otherwise nothing," Dala spoke with a whisper.

 "Great, so I have to search the entire city for a chip that can both interface with cyberware and turn an ordinary person into a super soldier in a matter of months…I might need Annabelle for this." Six felt like this was going to be a very long day.

 "It was our greatest creation! It took three years to finish. Instead of just increasing the rate of learning we had installed your combat ID into those nanites to refine your skills to perfection, self repairing tissue of any organ with help of morphine to jump start them, a nights rest will repair anything short of the brain ceasing to function and it disappears into the bloodstream to be undetectable through any scans, for the nanites to reform into a chip you need to kill the user. So if you don't find it soon, you'll be fighting yourself with more advantages." O explained, popping his head out from his arms to explain how fucked Six was.

 "Scratch that, I think I might need a nuke."

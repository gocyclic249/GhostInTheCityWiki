"Thanks for taking me out Motoko. Seriously, It's so annoying that mom won't even let me drive to the store without a merc escort."

 "It's fine Hiromi, I mean. I would hang out with you anyways, but getting paid to protect my best choom and stuff isn't bad."

 "Pfft. You're just too gonk to be embarrassed about anything." Hiromi whispers to the side.

 But I heard it. I heard it all!

 But I was a good friend and let her have her whispered conversations with herself without intruding. "So my car is ov-"

 "No way! We are taking my Kusanagi! I haven't had the wind in my hair in FOREVER!" She immediately countered dragging me off the to side, where her Kusanagi was parked.

 "Uh-uh. No way! I'm not dealing with Roller Coaster Hiromi today."

 "Fine you drive, but we are taking my bike." Hiromi countered happily probably having planned this.

 *Sigh* "Fine." I agreed wandering over. We settled onto the bike and as it roared to life the radio instantly started playing.

 "~DEJA VU I'VE JUST BEEN IN THIS PLA-~" I quickly turned off the bike.

 "What the hell was that song?" Hiromi asked instantly as I did my best to pretend nothing had happened.

 It's all that perks fault! Somehow! No matter what I start driving that song starts playing! I had to remove the radio from my Quadra completely just to get it to stop... Not that I disliked the song or anything, but for some reason it made me want to push the pedal down more and more until I started speeding! It was dangerous!

 "Nothing."

 "No way! That was catchy. Hit it Motoko! Girls night on the town!"

 "Hiromi, I'm supposed to take you to the clothing store and-"

 "~DEJA VU I'VE JUST BEEN IN THIS PLACE BEFORE HIGHER ON THE STREET!~"

 "HELL YEAH! LET'S GO!" Hiromi bounced behind me forcing me forward after she started the bike.

 It's cheating that the bike was hers so she could turn it on as she liked.

 "FINE!" I roared over the music.

 "~DEJA VU!~"

 I gunned it causing Hiromi to squeal as she held on tight as I took off, not even needing to slow down as I took the corners of the parking garage like I was delivering Tofu on the Akina downhill.

 Hiromi was soon squealing in fear and excitement, as we slipped out of the garage onto the street, as I did a powerslide to bleed off the speed. Then I gunned it again. For the next hour I showed Hiromi that not even Drunk Hiromi was equal to the soon to be rumor of the Purple Ghost of Kabuki.

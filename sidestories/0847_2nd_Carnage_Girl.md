 Satsuriki Otome felt the bass beat pulse into her, thrumming in her chest in sync with her heart. Nothing like a fake brain dance, or corpo polished lyrics and auto-tuned noise. It felt real, as real as this city full of plastic, iron and dreams could ever come. You felt the crowd and the beat. The sweat pouring from the band members and almost smell their breaths.

 The band on the stage looked at first glance, like average rockerboys with the lead singer sticking out. The lead singer, a shorter girl with shoulder length hair and purple eyes. She felt her eyes draw her in and felt like she looked back. Singing only to her. 

 "Load up on guns, bring your friends." 

 As the song rant out, she felt in her heart going fussy thinking about her chooms. The pain of the times when she had messed around, the times she'd fucked. Klepping an old car and almost getting caught. Her mother's furious face afterwards. She felt her heart clench with the anxiety, like she was back there again, all emotions in a jumble. 

 "Oh, you liked that? We'll I think you lot will like this one even more. Come with me and RISE!"

 The tone of the bar changed again. The dingy bar felt like it was it was a rock concert stadium, the stomping of the feet. Creaking of the floor and the shouts. The crowd loved it. 

 Our collective heart rates sped up as we felt the beat turning up. Edging us to rise to the challenge, to become something greater. If only we were willing. And we all felt like we could do it; we just had to stand up and do it. 

 The tingling anxiety of her past permeating through her body, becoming something more. Calling her to become something. To move, do something. Then the next song started. If she wanted to, she could rule this city. Be the best of the best. The song ended and couldn't wait for the next song. The crowd was loud, shouting out for more, stomping their feet and shouting.

 "All my friends are Heathens, take it slooow." 

 The song started slowly. Made you recall that Night City was full of assumptions. That feels she'd gotten from Rise slowly cooling, feeling tempered. She recalled being in the monorail. Seeing a man in a suit thinking he was perhaps a corpo. Best to keep a distance. So on was life here. That girl is a Mox, that's a Tyger Claw ganger. Not all facts of life in the city were that straightforward. The guy you thought was your buddy. Could be a Scav-

 "WATCH IT!" 

 The raw ka-chunk cocking of the massive shotgun rang through the bar. Like the breaking of chrome bones, it rang out. Jarring everyone out of their reminiscing. Everyone really was a dangerous here.

 Before Otome knew it, she had the shotgun in her hands; the preem lead singer had thrown it to her! What!? She got really confused what to do it, and the shotgun was a lot heavier than it looked when the singer held it! The singer just went back to heir position on the stage. So she guess she could keep it!? Nova!

 "This Ffffire!"

 The song fired up the crowd again. The feelings from Rise going into something else. To make the city learn she was a danger as well. She wanted to burn the city down. Burn all the corpos, all the gangers and the bastards. The city was filled with them! All of us are heathens in the end. It felt like she was going to do it, her heart and soul screamed out for it. 

 Body of iron and blood on fire. They all felt it; the crowd felt like they were running. Felt like Edgerunners. The song ended, and the singer chugged some water before starting a new song. We all waited with bated breaths.

 "What if I say, I'm not like the others!"

 The band was on fire. Drummer beating like a man possessed. Song flowing over us; lyrics telling us the story of our city. The amount of nonsense the night city government throws at us. Pretending like they care, like they aren't all bought and paid by the corporations that control the city. They should know we aren't so easily tricked! Even with their boot on us. We live on!

 She didn't want to be like those people. Putting her head down was the status quo in the city. Too easy to get flatlined by crossing the wrong organisation. She wanted to be someone who wasn't muddied in the filth that is the common ground of night city. Song ending, the lead singer took a brief break before starting on a new song.

 "This is Faint."

 The tone changed again, now to something more heart wrenching and hot. The loneliness hit her like a truck. Changing to something more personal. The life she wanted. She wanted to be someone, be someone to be proud of. To have people who understood her. But she felt no one did. Is it futile to try even? Can we reach out and become something more in this city? 

 "Hear me out now! You're gonna listen to me, like it or not, RIGHT NOW! HEAR ME OUT NOW!"

 The lyrics hit her to her core. She felt it inside. The embarrassed side of her, agreeing that she just had to reach out to others. But she wasn't sure she could. She could become something to someone. She'd just have to try. Where there was a will, there was a way. The song had planted some seeds of curage inside her. 

 Then, as suddenly as the songs started, it ended. The band sweating left the stage. Like a hurricane that arrived, it was gone. That was legendary, Otome thought. 

 She wondered when they'd play next.

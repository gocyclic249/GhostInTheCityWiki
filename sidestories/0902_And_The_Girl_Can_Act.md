And The Girl Can Act​

 Kitty played with the card in her hand, admiring how the embossed metallic border caught the light of Night City's passing cityscape. It was rather novel, she got plenty of cards at these things, agents, suits, lecherous old bastards with too much money and not enough humanity... but this was the first time she'd gotten an Edgerunner's. Even the delivery was a little unusual.

_"You don't." Kitty whispered to the girl. Poor thing. She liked her spirit. Good music too. MSM would eat her alive. The kid had just winked back at her._

 It was only on the ride home she'd found the card in her purse. A purse her bodyguard had the entire time at the party. A purse that had a sealing lock.

Section 9

 Ghost

 You have problems? We have solutions.​

 The card was professionally done, some Corpo had definitely worked on it. Weird for an Edgerunner. There was a number and on the back was a small, clean area intentionally made for notes.

 'Thanks for the advice, if you need help, call ~M'

 She liked it. The entire thing. The song, the girl, the card. The mysterious delivery into her purse. The handwritten note in a sharp, flowing script. It all had a flare for the dramatics she appreciated. It's a pity the girl didn't want to act.

 "We're here, Miss Thunder," her driver's voice pulled her out of her thoughts.

 "Thank you, Leon"—she got out of the car—"see you tomorrow."

 ---

 "Anything else for the night Miss?" her bodyguard asked.

 "No thanks Claude, I'll be in for the rest of the night," she replied

 "Okay, Jenny will be on duty tonight if you need anything." Her bodyguard left.

 At last, a merciful moment of peace and quiet. Alone time. Not that she was alone, not yet. Kicking off her shoes, she started stripping off outer layers on the way to the bathroom, tossing them to the floor. It was always best to give marketing something so they didn't start pushing for more. Not that it stopped them, but she'd long gotten used to a life of delaying tactics.

 Once the bathroom door clicked shut she was finally alone in the sanctity of the one room in her house without monitoring equipment. They'd tried, of course, but she'd put her foot down on that. And then she'd hired professionals to sweep it for the ones they'd tried to install "out of contract".

 She looked at herself in the mirror for moment. A woman staring back at her with a face she didn't recognize. She'd only started to get used to the last one when trends had changed again. She didn't think she'd even have time to get used to this one. The fad was dying fast. She'd probably need to be talking to her bodysculptor by the end of the week.

 Turning from the mirror, she leaned back on the counter and looked at the art she had on her wall. Counting in her head until a minute had passed. Then, without looking, she pulled out a drawer and reached under the enclosure. Pressing a finger to the hidden pad that unlocked a seamless compartment, she pulled out a shard and slotted it at the base of her skull, under her hair.

 She relaxed as a comforting green dot appeared at the corner of her vision. All of the installed monitoring software in her Cyberware was being blocked, spoofed and edited, making it look like she was just admiring the painting for a while and giving her a precious moment of privacy.

 She didn't waste time stripping off the rest of her clothes and getting in the bath. Pleasant luxuries like not having every part of your life recorded for highlight reels and gossip shows were too rare to spend on standing around.

 A good 15 minutes later, she finally got around to nibbling at her curiosity, putting in a call to one name out of a group of contacts that only appeared when she had the shard slotted.

 She hadn't always been this. An acting machine in the body and face of a woman she didn't know. A bodysculptor's playground to chase trends turning into something that always seemed to be a little more fetishized each month, somehow. A woman who made it. Someone who'd gotten their wildest dreams. A sellout.

 Once, in another life, she'd been just another girl on the Edge, trying to get by.

 She hated that life, but she didn't hate the friends she made.

 Stepping out of the bath, she started to dry herself off as she thought it over. It wasn't hard to reconcile the girl she'd met with as a cyberpscyho. There were plenty of girls in the fame circuit who went 'psycho. It wasn't common, but it wasn't unheard of either. Then the suits would go on shows say how very sad it was and call it the 'cost of doing business' and marketing would send her a carefully worded tearful message about the tragic passing of her dear friend for someone she'd met once to post on the net.

 Coming back from that insanity though? That didn't happen. Kitty realized answers had only made the mystery deeper instead of clearing it up. Looks like she'd be scrolling some XBDs.

 Leaning back against the counter, she stared at the painting for a minute. She didn't actually like it that much, it'd just come with the apartment. A white furred rabbit exotic being chased by a girl.

 Before she popped the shard and put it back, she added a new contact on it.

 'Kusanagi Motoko, "Ghost"'

 ---

 She hated them. Hated the fucking scavs. Hated the fucking Maelstrom. Hated the fucking Corpos. Hated the fucking Agents, hated the fucking Managers, hated the fucking greasy old Suits. They deserved everything they got. Deserved every shotgun hole. Every head she bashed in with the Carnage. Most of all she hated—

 She ripped the headset off gasping. The room was spinning from the disorientation and the intensity of emotions she was trying to seperate from herself. She could barely breath without her lungs hitching from the anger. The pure, undistilled fury of certainty and conviction. That was Motoko? How the fuck had she come back from that? She was annoyed and surly, but polite. Not..

 Hate-murder incarnate.

 She shuddered as the emotions roiled in her head. The rage, fresh and seared into her brain, threatened to overwhelm her. She wanted to put the headset back on. Wanted to punch a wall. Wanted to go kill someone. To wash away everything she was and drown in violence.

 So she ate it.

 Every emotion, every feeling, every shuddered, raging gasp for air. She consumed it all, taking it into the deepest parts of herself, forging it into another tool she could use. To become an ever-more perfect acting machine. To become what she was born to do.

 When she finally stopped shuddering and looked up, in the dark of the apartment even the monitoring cameras couldn't see her smile.

**One Month Later**

 "Alright people, places!" Director Chero called. She didn't like Chero that much, but he wasn't awful like some of the rotating celebrity directors were. He just tended to talk down to actors a bit.

 "Ok Kitty, remember, Maggy's struggling to not go Cyberpsycho on Karly here. Give me rage, give me crazy. But struggling! Like you've got a really hard decision!"

 Like that. She'd read the script. She didn't botch her scenes. She didn't need amateur hour direction. Chero knows this. She knows he knows this. He still does it. Every time. She let out a breath and just nodded.

 "Annnnnd, action!"

 "Maggy? What's wrong?" Karly asked. The slut knew what was wrong. She was trying to steal Bill! Again! For the 37th time! Maggy hated her!

 Karly reached her hand out, wanting to touch her friend. The friend she kept stealing from!

 "Maggy, are you alright? Are yo—"

 Karly recoiled as Maggy suddenly looked up. All she saw in that face was pure, murderous rage.

 "Wha—"

 Maggy launched herself at Karly, screaming a raw inhuman sound. They went down onto the ground, Maggy pinning Karly and snarling in her face.

 She hated her.

 She punched the ground an inch beside her ear.

 She hated her.

 She kept pummeling the ground with her fists, screeching incorherrent wrath.

 She hated her.

 Her skin scraped from on the floor.

 Karly was sobbing in terror.

 her fists were bleeding now.

 But she was her friend.

 Blood covered the floor.

 They solved so many mysteries together.

 Her face twisted in anguish.

 They worked so many clubs together!

 Tears started to stream down her face.

 She didn't want to hurt her.

 With a final, sicking pop, she slammed her fist next to Karly's head. She pulled herself back from the brink with shuddered, raging gasps for air.

_...

 Annnnnd, scene?

 Hello? Chero? Call cut? Anybody?_

 Kitty held her pose while Jetsy sobbed quietly underneath her. She was pretty sure she had frightened her enough she broke character, but the take was still good. At least it would be as soon as Chero called cut. _Come on, what's the hold up?_

 Yeah Jetsy was definitely whimpering. She was a decent actress, but she wasn't THAT good. Ugh. That was probably going to be a pain in the ass to sort out. She did not need her co-star terrified of her. Or at least this co-star. She wouldn't mind if Justin was a little more terrified of her.

_If someone doesn't call cut in the next 5 seconds I'm breaking scene and leaving it for the editor to fix._

 Someone coughed.

 "Cut!" Chero called out. Finally.

 She looked up, Everyone was staring at her. the entire set crew flinched.

 "Kitty that was—"

 "Can someone call Trauma? I'm pretty sure my wrist is broken." she asked.

 That got the crew moving again.

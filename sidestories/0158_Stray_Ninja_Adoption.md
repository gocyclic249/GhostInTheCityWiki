Stray Ninja Adoption​

 "So what did we learn?" Regina asked Ian with a chuckle as he nursed his bruised ego and actual bruises while sitting at a desk within her penthouse/office.

 "Don't answer the door for annoying brats cause they might turn out to be cyberninjas and beat your ass," Ian grumbled as he worked the newly made kink in his arm. "Goddamn, what are they feeding kids these days?"

 "Likely less and worse than what we had," Regina shrugged. "Still, if nothing else her taking you down so easily means she is at least half as skilled as she thinks she is."

 "Not sure how to take that, boss," Ian grumbled.

 "It means not to underestimate anyone, no matter how they appear. Which is why I will be keeping the recording of your little 'encounter' on hand to remind you every once and awhile," Regina smirked evilly at him.

 Aww shit.

 "I want to file a complaint! Workplace abuse!" Ian shouted only half seriously.

 "Take it up with HR," Regina said with a wave as she sat down in her big chair looking out the penthouse widow and into Night City as a whole.

 Ian grumbled as he went to work on his computer and started searching up that bratty ninja to get some more info. "Seriously though, what is this Motoko's girl's deal? Wakako just sending her feral great-grandchildren at us for a laugh now?"

 "Wakako is heavily intertwined with the Tyger Claws, what with her sons being all major players in it, so she could never approve of a gig that targeted one of their most profitable lieutenants, even one as monstrous as Shobo. Sure she would if she could, she hates people like Shobo, but in absence of that she did the next best thing, sending one of her agents to a Fixer who would, could, and already did put a gig out on his head." Regina explained as she went back to work in general.

 Being a Fixer was a near 24/7 job after all, with every minute making a difference as those like her kept a pulse on the city. Especially one as 'active' as she was in certain areas.

 "She never said Wakako sent her your way, only that she worked with her," Ian pointed out.

 "And you honestly think a girl like that would have had the idea on her own?" Regina asked.

 "Point taken," Ian admitted as she pulled up the girl's sheet. "Nothing impressive about her file at least. Gotta couple of misdemeanors and minor crimes, your typical Night City upbringing really... though that changed a year ago..." Ian trailed off before sending Regina the deets.

 There was a few moments of silence as his boss went through the info.

 "Damn," Regina said outloud.

 "Yeah, guess running into Scavs and being a veg for a year will change anyone's outlook," Ian admitted. "Edgerunners usually born, not made, like that."

 "Trauma changes people, no matter who they are," Regina said with a voice that spoke of hard-earned experience. "According to my sources she only has a couple of gigs under her belt too, but each one surprisingly well-done and professional. Wakako seems to be grooming her to be her main infiltration agent going by the jobs sent her way, and that Motoko has been passing with flying colors."

 "Explains how she got the drop on me," Ian mumbled.

 "Yeah, _that's_ why," Regina teased.

 "Shut it," Ian grumbled some more before coming across something interesting. "Well now... seems little Ms. Ninja helped track down that 'Meat Man' serial killer too, at least according to these confidential files from NCPD."

 "That is interesting... though of course the cops are taking all the credit," Regina said as she brought up the file and the 'official' news article about the whole thing with the mayor, the polarizing figure that was Lucius Rhyne, personally congratulating the police for their investigate work and visiting the recovered victims in the hospital. "Seems our little ninja has some Media in her to track him down to some farm out in the Badlands."

 "Maybe, but doubt it given her family history," Ian said as he pulled it up. "Parents are deceased, no shock there. Both Tyger Claws with impressive histories, which makes her a legacy alongside her brother, Jun. He was a typical grunt not long ago before he joined up with Kamikaze."

 "Tyger Claws' pet cyberpsychos," Regina growled.

 "Yeah, and apparently worked closely with them recently during gang war with Maelstorm while cops were dealing the Pacifica Combat Zone... bbbbuuuuttttt she doesn't appear to be officially part of gang though, strictly a Solo."

 "Likely just wanting to help out her brother," Regina said with some pity in her voice, knowing too well at this point just what lengths some family members would go to try and protect their loved ones suffering from cyberpsychosis. Maybe they could offer some help on that end later...

 "True, though certainly adds up with story of wanting to deal with Shobo quietly and not let it get back to her or her brother. Would _not_ be a good look."

 "Hopefully she pulls it off," Regina said seriously. "If Shobo really is holed up in his club he ain't gonna be easy to get to."

 "Either she already has a plan or just that sure of her skills. She either pulls it off or she doesn't, either way not our problem... though in this case I am rooting for her since failure, even failure to keep herself hidden as the one that takes him out, would likely be worse than death," Regina admitted before shrugging. "Either way, the Mox will be happy if nothing else... I wonder if I should have mentioned about the live capture bonus..." Regina trailed off.

 "But why come to us and not just do it if she is so confident?" Ian asked curiously.

 "This is still Nigh City. Why do something for free when you can be paid to do it?" Regina asked with a raised brow with her good eye.

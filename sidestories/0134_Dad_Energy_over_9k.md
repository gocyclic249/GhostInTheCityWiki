# Dad Energy over 9k!

Dad Energy over 9k!​

  
 \*Oh… Thank you for telling me. Drive safe.\*  
   
 \*Will do!\*  
   
 And with that Jun closed the call with Motoko and went back to his breakfast burrito.  
   
 He was still a little worried about her, but since the NCPD were back on the streets and she was just gonna get her car cleaned she should be alright. Not even his sister could find trouble doing that...  
   
 Jun made a mental note to make sure to check in later just in case.  
   
 ---  
   
 \*Hey Jun! Called Ichi, met up with Malcolm, then Hiromi, and now heading out to meet with my gang at Ho-Oh. Just wanted to let you know in case we are out late. Promise to be careful and call if anything up.\*  
   
 \*Alright... Have fun and be safe.\*  
   
 \*Promise!\*  
   
 Jun just sighed a little at that as he sat on his couch and watching tv, but trusted Motoko would be alright at a TC run club now that the war with Maelstom was over... for now at least.  
   
 Still made him made that it ended so soon, but the higher knew it was too risky to fight now with NCPD back on the streets. He still hadn't gotten his new marching orders yet either since he doubted he would just be some extra muscle at a club now considering his chrome. So he was stuck waiting until he was given something to do.  
   
 Motoko floated the idea to him about tracking down some Scav dens together, something he really shouldn't be encouraging, but maybe he should if only to keep an eye on her and keep his skills sharp.  
   
 Though speaking of Motoko... now that the war was over for the time being he should really go have a chat with someone he didn't know much about since he had the time and she'd be out for a bit.  
   
 So with that Jun turned off the tv, got up, and headed out to go meet that person and get their measure.  
   
 ---  
   
 Jun rolled up to Misty's Esoterica and Chakra Harmonization on his bike in Watson's Little China, the new age business being the front for this Vik's guy ripperdoc clinic.  
   
 He got off and made his way inside.  
   
 "Hello, and welcome to- oh, Jun right? Motoko's big brother?" Misty, the owner of said shop, said as he entered. "Been a minute. Your sister doing alright?"  
   
 "Yeah, she's fine. Still being herself," Jun confirmed.  
   
 "I'm sure things are never boring then. She gives off a lot of energy," Misty said with a smile.  
   
 "You can say that again," Jun groaned a little. "Vik in?"  
   
 "Yup, out the back, same as before," Misty gestured to the backdoor.  
   
 "Thanks," Jun said as he made his way to the ripper.  
   
 "And if you want a reading on your way out, just stop by," Misty called out to him as he made his way out into the back alley and down into the ripper's basement clinic.  
   
 It wasn't a bad setup, but still a little creepy feeling in Jun's opinion considering it was a convert garage.  
   
 "Jun! Stop by for their checkup I offered?" Vik noticed Jun enter his clinic quickly, turning in his rolling chair from his desk from where he was watching some old school boxing match it seemed.  
   
 "I mainly wanted to talk about you and Motoko," Jun started right off, his eyes slightly narrowed.  
   
 "Huh, well that makes sense I guess," Vik shrugged easily.  
   
 That caught Jun off guard a bit. "Really?"  
   
 "Of course, you're a big brother wanting to make sure that the older gentleman your sister regularly sees is doing nothing untoward to her and everything," Vik said simply as he went back to watching his boxing match. "But my time isn't free so if you wanna chat you best get in that chair and let me look you over, make sure your regular ripper up to standard."  
   
 "Wha-? No, we need to ta-"  
   
 "Kid, I've been alive in this city longer than most, so you can't threaten or scare me unless you got more than just a borged out body to swing around," Vik interrupted him. "So unless you wanna watch with me you best get in that chair."  
   
 Jun stopped short and felt his anger rise, but before he could even think about anything he recalled how well Motoko spoke about Vik and his anger died.  
   
 He didn't come here to fight, just get answers.  
   
 "Fine," Jun said as he walked over and sat in the ripper chair with the ease of lots of practice.  
   
 "Excellent!" Vik said with a smile as he rolled over to his side and got to work. "Let me just jack in and look you over. Noticed you got a lot of work done over a short time, so gotta make sure body handling it alright."  
   
 "It's fin-"  
   
 "I'll be the judge of that," Vik cut him off as Jun felt him plug into his chrome and got to work. "So besides making use of my services, what did you want to know?"  
   
 Jun just lightly glared at the man before sighing. "How does my sister even know you?"  
   
 "Heh, you'd know better than me since the first time I meant her is when she and her friends brought in boxes of liberated cyberware!"  
   
 ---  
   
 "She just never listens! I'm just trying to keep her safe!" Jun complained.  
   
 "She's a teenage girl. If a near death experience with Scavs once hasn't stopped her nearly nothing will," Vik comforted him. "Its a blessing and curse."  
   
 "I never wanted his life for her though," Jun sighed deeply.  
   
 "Its Night City, Jun. Quite frankly you should be glad she can handle it better than most and she wants to involve you in it. Now, turn your head to the side a bit, gotta calibrate something."  
   
 ---  
   
 "Its like every week its a brand new crazy thing with her," Jun threw a single arm up into the air, the other currently detached as Vik fixed some internal competent so it moved more smoothly.  
   
 "She's young, she wants to push herself and improve each time," Vik shrugged. "All we can do is trust her and be there for her when she asks for help."  
   
 "But does it have to be a crazier and crazier thing each time?"  
   
 "You prefer she was a traumatized recluse staying inside all the time doing nothing by watching BDs?"  
   
 "...No," Jun admitted, hating the idea of Motoko not being happy and experiencing her life to the fullest, even if it scared the crap out of him.  
   
 "She'll mellow out with age. Goodness knows I was pretty crazy when I was younger."  
   
 "You?" Jun didn't believe it.  
   
 "What? I'm not called a Living Legend for nothing!" Vik proudly said.  
   
 "Really? I never heard of you till Motoko," Jun admitted.  
   
 Vik just sighed.  
   
 "Young people today..."  
   
 ---  
   
 "Holy shit, you were a boxing champ?" Jun said in shock.  
   
 "Yup! Heavy weight in the Watson Boxing Grand Prix, came in 2nd place in the finals." Vik said with nostalgia. "Left that all behind though and became what you see today. Much more straightforward and honest work."  
   
 "Didn't think of going pro?" Jun asked curiously.  
   
 "I did, but wasn't for me. Besides, got out at the right time. Boxing today isn't the same. They chrome you up so hard takes away the spirit of it."  
   
 "Doesn't it make it better, though?"  
   
 "Son, boxing is about training your body to the limit, sharping your skills, and then engaging it with someone else who did the same. You swing punches, you dodge, you counter, its a battle in truth until the other guy goes down to just the right blow. Nowadays there's no finesse, just two brickhouse wailing on each other until one finally goes down."  
   
 "Huh... I guess they can get kinda boring after awhile," Jun admitted.  
   
 "Exactly! Back in my day chromed fighters were of course a thing, but there was an art and style to it."  
   
 "How so?"  
   
 "Well, one fighter I knew-"  
   
 ---  
   
 "There we go, done what I could," Vik said as he unplugged Jun and and sat up.  
   
 "Huh, things do feel better," Jun admitted as his chrome felt like it weighed less, was smooth to move and react to him, and felt... less heavy on his brain.  
   
 "Your ripper was good, but needs to work on longevity work," Vik said as he handed Jun a couple of meds. "Take a couple every evening for a week to make sure fixes stick alright."  
   
 "Thanks," Jun said honestly as he took the bag.  
   
 "Really should consider dechroming too. Just a little at least," Vik brought up again. "Fighting has died down, yeah? Can afford to slow down a bit yourself."  
   
 "I'll... keep it mind," Jun said as he got up to leave the clinic.  
   
 "Stay safe Jun, and keep an eye out on your sister too. Gotta look after each other," Vik said as Jun made his way out.  
   
 "Always," he called back as he left the clinic, said by to Misty on his way out, and got back on his bike to head home.  
   
 He killed more than a few hours with Vik, more than he expected.  
   
 The man was just so... genuine in a way Jun didn't see often. Motoko was right, he was a good guy.  
   
 ...And maybe he should consider him his main ripper from now on too.

Malcolm walked down the same alleyway he always did, on his way home from a gig well done and Eddies well earned. He was a trueborn son of Night City, a kid raised on and by the streets, with a bit of help from his parents too. He knew the risks of wandering into a darkened alley, just as those who lurked in them knew the risks of messing with a local son. Ninehundred and ninety nine times out of a thousand, he'd come out of that alley as unharmed as he went in. Tonight however was different.

 The mugger was stealthy, an old hand at his work. He'd watched this alley for would be marks and fat wallets. Today, he'd hit the fattest wallet here and be gone before any local boys could roll up. Hit and fade. If he were more of a psycho he'd get his fair dose of ultraviolence out, but he hadn't gone wrong in the head, not yet at any rate. He just needed eddies. Debts, drugs, dancers, Night City had it all, but only eddies could keep them flowing at a rate that could satisfy the muggers weary soul. 

 The shadows loomed large, busted lights, foul smells, unidentifiable fluids, an alleyway of alleyways. The mugger would soon have Malcolm alone, isolated, and at the wrong end of a shotgun. But the mugger had made a slight miscalculation. He'd assumed that Malcolm, the fattest wallet on this block, had been alone. He was wrong.

 Floating by his side, invisible to all, was a silent digital ghost. Onryō. It sat in Malcolm's cyberware. Waiting. Forever waiting. Her mistress had placed it there as a test. She had never been removed. And so she waited. She watched. She observed. Sometimes her mistress would connect, and she would deliver surprise and terror to Malcolm. Other times, her mistress would use her visage to deliver a message, to communicate. And then she would be gone again, setting her back into the background. To wait. To watch. To observe. Malcolm was not an enemy of her mistress. Onryō _knew_ what happened to those. What to do. How to terrify them. Malcolm was a not-enemy. Not-enemy's were allowed to live. It was... logical. 

 Onryō had access to all of Malcolm's cyberware. Even the ones with disabled functionality. She knew the mugger was there. She knew he intended to deliver harm to Malcolm. That... could not be allowed. Her mistress was the only one allowed to harm an individual she was haunting. It made sense. Anyone Onryō haunted belonged to her mistress. Their lives to be taken or left as she desired. Doing nothing would be tantamount to harming Malcolm without her mistresses leave. It could not be allowed. It could _not_ be allowed. 

 Onryō could not access the would be muggers systems. Malcolm lacked the hardware, she the permissions. Instead, she reached behind Malcolm and drew a cold, sharp fingernail up his spine. Not enough to hurt, incapable of harm, but enough to send a _tingle_ up his nervous system. 

 Malcolm drew his smartgun and whirled in a second, faster than the mugger could draw his shotgun and fired without truly aiming, acting entirely on instinct. The would be mugger swore and ran, catching a bullet in his shoulder for his trouble. Malcolm didn't bother chasing, instead he checked the alleyway for more would be muggers before letting out a breath and rushing out the opposite end of the alleyway into a more well lit part of Night City, still heading home but no longer at the relaxed pace he'd had before. 

 Invisibly, weightlessly, Onryō settled on his back and shoulders, riding him like an Obariyon, watching and observing Malcolm as he congratulated his 'instincts' for a job well done. Onryō accepted the misdirected thanks for future conveyance to her mistress. She would keep this not-enemy, Malcolm, safe and alive. 

 Until the day her mistress came to claim his life too. She wondered how his ghost would taste, when both she and it finally left this shell behind.

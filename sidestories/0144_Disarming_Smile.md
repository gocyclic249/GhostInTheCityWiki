Disarming Smile​

  
 When Detective Stint rode up to the farm in the middle of nowhere, after receiving multiple assurances from patrol officers on the scene the whole thing wasn't a bad joke or elaborate trap, he wasn't sure what to expect.  
   
 The Meat Man, named in the department after discarding his victims like pieces of meat across Night City, had at least been active for a year if not longer, but the media wasn't very aware of him since it was hard for people in Night City of all places to pick out a serial killer among the constant flavors of it every single day, especially not long ago with the gang war while the NCPD dealt with the issues in Pacifica. Didn't help most of his victims were young men that most wrote off as runaways, another sadly common occurrence in Night City.  
   
 Made Stint sick just thinking about it, how the NCPD wasn't doing its job nearly as well as he and many other cops wish it did. And now he was pulling up to a crime scene after a vigilante did what the whole department couldn't, taking out the Meat Man and calling them in on it.  
   
 It made Stints have very conflicting feelings about the whole thing.  
   
 On the one hand, there was one less psycho out there on the streets snatching people up and doing who knows what to them. On the other, it only happened cause of people outside of the department. And they could have solved it if they just had more funding and support, and less asskissing and ignoring real crimes.  
   
 But now wasn't the time to think about it, he had a job to do.  
   
 He pulled up and stopped his car, quickly getting out of it and walking over to the nearest officer. He idly noticed the crime scene techs going over the whole farm, dotted with small craters for some reason, with a fine-toothed comb as the med techs wheeled away the Meat Man's latest victims into their ambulances to be taken to the nearest hospital for treatment. That there were survivors to save was a bright spot at least, given the state he saw the other bodies in.  
   
 "So, what we find out?" Stints asked the officer directly.  
   
 "Detective, we got our man alright," the patrolman was happy to say. "Techs already went over the house and barn, full of evidence that guarantees we got our killer, especially the DNA on him from his victims. Real name is Anthony Haris according on facial rec of the corpse. The freak had his victims hooked up to these cattle stalls and pumping them with hormones, anabolic steroids, other shit. Apparently he was messaging people over a website and other pervy crap to get them to come to him before he put them through hell."  
   
 Stints remembered that in the file and visits to the morgue. It was an unusual way to kill someone, even in Night City, so he had been running down various ideas that never panned out. He would have never guessed it was because the method of murder was from an outdated meat farm.  
   
 "The survivors?" Stints asked.  
   
 "Two of them. Stable, from what the med techs said at least. So long as they get treated in a hospital they should pull through," the patrolman confirmed before grimacing. "Found lots of DNA samples in the various stalls though... all registered as dead or missing."  
   
 Stints frowned at the very unpleasant thought.  
   
 "We'll follow up later. Just keep the scene secure, don't let any medias show up, bag everything, and everything else by the book," Stints replied.  
   
 "Chief is gonna love this, easy win," the patrolman chuckled while Stints just kept frowning about yet another thing he didn't want to think about.  
   
 "Where are the ones who called it in?" Stints asked curiously about their heroic vigilantes.  
   
 "Over there being frisked," the patrolman said as he gestured near the front of the barn.  
   
 "Why wasn't that done earlier? Officers on the scene said they had them secured over an hour ago," Stints pointed out.  
   
 "Yeah, they did. The frisking and disarming is *ongoing*," the patrolman cleared up.  
   
 That just made Stints blink in confusion. "The Tyger Claw was packing a lot of heat?"  
   
 The 'Oni', real name Jun Kusanagi, was the newest addition to the Tyger Claws Kamikaze unit according to the Gangs Unit and his file after several years of being your standard gang punk. Borged out and just on the edge of being cyberpsychos. Dangerous assholes.  
   
 "Him? Naw. He was only carrying a couple weapons. His little sister though... that girl scares me," the patrolman whispered to Stints in a serious tone of voice.  
   
 Stints couldn't believe it, Motoko Kusanagi's file was just full of relatively minor offenses.  
   
 "Want us to lock them up too? Get some bonus points?" the patrolman asked honestly.  
   
 Stints focused hard on not letting his displeasure show at the suggestion, instead deflecting.  
   
 "Best not since last thing we need is Tyger Claws getting uppity about one of their kill machines being locked up and they could tie courts up by justifying their actions as self-defense for others. Besides, they saved us a trial."   
   
 "Fair enough," the patrolman shrugged easily as Stints decided to walk over to the detained 'suspects' to have a quick word, test the waters with them, and then let them go for their good deeds.  
   
 Maybe give the kid sister some advice about going straight before the Claws got their... well, you know, totally into her. Could be a good cop if directed right after all, and NCPD wasn't nearly as picky about new recruits as Stints wished.  
   
 When he walked over though he going only go still as he saw the two siblings, two officers going through the younger Kusanagi's pockets while she had a huge grin on her face, and the pile of various weapons standing next to them.  
   
 "Another grenade," the officer on her right said as he pulled out one before tossing it onto the pile.  
   
 "Never enough boom," Kusanagi said with a smile.  
   
 "Slaught-O-Matic," the officer on the left said as he pulled out the plastic pistol.  
   
 "Sometimes you just wanna grab something gross from a vending machine," she defended herself. "A little to the left," she said to the officer on the right.  
   
 "Another combat knife."  
   
 "I like picking my teeth."  
   
 "Stress ball."  
   
 "Its a better weapon than you would think."  
   
 "Carnage shotgun."  
   
 "Cause who needs a doorbell, am I right?"  
   
 "NDA."  
   
 "I've need that back later, please."  
   
 "Vinyl record of Samurai's greatest hits."  
   
 "I'll never seen that before in my life."  
   
 "HMG," the patrolman said in shock as he somehow pulled a heavy weapon from the girl's pocket.  
   
 "I have a permit for that."  
   
 And on and on it went.  
   
 Eventually, while she was still be frisked and the pile of weapons and other various items grew she looked at Sints with that smile of her still and spoke up.  
   
 "I don't suppose you can uncuff me now? It's been an hour."  
   
 Sints could only chuckle as once more, Night City had found some new way to fuck with him.

_*in front of Afterlife*_

 "Let me in."

 "No. Go away kid"

 "Let me in! I have an appointment with Rogue!"

 "Like hell you do! Beat it brat, before I beat you!"

_*Motoko is fuming*_

 "I'll show you a brat!" _*Motoko yells as the bouncer has enough of her shit, grabs her and carries her out of the building under his arm*_ "My vengeance shall be legendary!"

 The few patrons who witnessed the scene laughed good naturedly at the kid being manhandled that way.

_*some time later, Rogue calls Wakako*_

 "Your 'stealth expert' was a no-show. Don't waste my time next time"

 "You better don't waste _my_ time, girl. You wanted a professional operator, I provided you one. The least you could do is give us the courtesy of saying the gig is off, instead of having your bouncer do it for you"

 "But I didn't- oh don't tell me that fucking gonk at the door- "

_*Rogue reviews security footage*_

 "Hey, you are her fixer. Sending you part of the footage. The kid is the supposed 'professional'? Are you joking with-"

 "Oh dear" there was a real note of worry in Wakako's voice "She's not answering my calls. I'll send out some men to get a hold of her before she does something rash"

 "You are joking, right? You actually sound worried there."

 "Contacted her brother and friends... no, she's not picking up any calls from them either"

 "This joke has gone on for too long-"

 "Time to cut my loses I guess. Know you have my full sympathies Rogue. I'm notifying everyone that whatever happens next is completely unsanctioned by me. Good luck."_*disconnects*_

 "She must be joking... right? It's just a little girl."

_*a few days later*_

 Rogue put the whole incident away from her mind. She had other duties and obligations after all. Then she noticed it.

 The Line.

 People lining up to the toilet, in fact.

 "Come on guys, hurry up I can't hold it in much longer!" someone shouted

 "I'm trying but- oh fuck, here it goes again!" was the response accompanied by a unholy sounding noise

 "It doesn't flush!" someone else yelled out in crazed panic "There's no toilet paper and it doesn't flush!"

_*flashback to Motoko figuring out Afterlife's food supplier, infiltrating the delivery truck and spiking the food with laxatives*

 *flashback to Motoko digging up old blueprints for the building and turning off the water supply, and sending a few cheap drones up the waste disposal pipes to clog the toilets up from inside*_

 Rogue was worried, just a little bit now. Then the music and all other electronic systems started going haywire, everything that could produce sound was in unison singing a piece about "Never giving you up" or something else Rogue has never heard of before. But that should have been impossible, she thought. They did have a netrunner who should be dealing with their security and these kind of things-

_*flashback to Motoko approaching the Afterlife netrunner with the picture of Bartmoss still in his fridge with all of his equipment inside, and that he should probably better hurry and get to it right the hell now before someone else finds it*_

 Then there was an explosion outside, the lights flickered and died for a few seconds before emergency power kicked in. But something changed in the atmosphere of the place. Some intangible quality that had her hard-won instincts going haywire.

 "Hi" someone whispered from behind her ear. Rogue twisted and reached for the gun that wasn't there on her anymore. All of her bodyguards were unconscious in their seats, so Rogue came to the conclusion that the girl got to them in the few seconds the lights were out "Should've let me in" she said with a wide smile on her youthful face, before the lights died again for a few minutes this time. There was no sign of the girl after the power was restored.

_*a few hours later*_

 After the shitstorm (sadly an accurate description when the people who ate the laxative-spiked food were concerned) was finally dealt with, and damage assessed, it wasn't really that bad. Damaged power relay outside, few clogged toilets with unfortunate spillage on the floor, few guests who will be getting free drinks for a while as a recompense. Material damage wasn't that bad.

 Reputation damage was slightly worse. Rogue managed to convince most of the guest it was just her Netrunner getting high and playing a few 'harmless pranks', and he will be severely punished for it - and he will be, for going on a wild goose chase into the middle of nowhere instead of doing his job. Now, as long as nothing else did go wrong tonight, she could put this entire incident behind her and try to fix-

 "Hey, that's not the drink I ordered!" one of the guests yelled as he spat out his drink

 The bartender took the glass, sniffed it, took a sip, spat it out, then looked back at all the bottles behind them. It took him a moment to realize the problem with the bottles that was nagging at his brain.

 "Someone swapped all the labels!"

"S-Still on my w-waaay… Still o-on my _waaaay_…"

 My little gas-tube arms scrabble weakly at the small trenches they've dug below me. As it turns out, you need a bit more than arms to carry a heavy machine gun. As the name implies, we're heavy.

 I let my limbs rest for a little while. Yeah, just… rest for a bit. I've been trying to move for the better part of an entire day, that means I just need to gain my second wind! I mean, look at the big holes I've dug, and promptly stuck myself into Totally On Purpose, by dragging my stock and magazine a _whole foot!_ An **entire foot**! That's not bad for something that usually isn't supposed to move at all!

 I'm the number one hole digger! … okay, that's a lie. Grandpa Colt-browning M1895 holds **that**particular record.

 But I'm a close second!

 The wind blows with a sudden heavy gust, throwing sand particles through the small gulley between the two dunes that I call my _temporary_ shelter. As the breeze sweeps by, a quiet whistle comes from the broken windows of the car that me and…

 Me and…

 mmmMMMMMMM**MMMMM!**

 "Still on my WAAAY! Still on my WAAAAY! And though the weather may be Loko I'll still make it to **Motoko**! **Still on my WAAAAY!**"

 HUGE swaths of clinging sand are thrown aside as I tear myself free from the grainy prison and motor across the dunes.

 The miles sweep past like I'm a mortar shell, I'm souring right through the air and into **Her**apartment window! And then **She's**there and she's holding me and telling me everything's okay and that we'll go slaughter some… some… some fuckin' SCAVS! And I'll tear them apart with high-explosive DAKKA! And I'll be sSSSOOO good at it that **She'll**never want for another gun!

 And w-we'll! We'll!

 "Calm down ther' Missy." rumbles a deep, soothing, not-voice.

 I snap out of the… fantasy. My little gas tubes cease their pointless flailing, I'd only managed to make the hole _slightly_ deeper.

 The wind's picking up, and the light of the sun is beginning to fade.

 "H-Hello?" I ask in the same not-voice that all firearms know from creation. Sweeps with my optic don't reveal anything obvious in my area.

 "Hey ther'. Are you all righ' now?" The same 'voice' mutters. A slow antiquated drawl coloring his tone.

 I stop myself from sniffling, I'm a big Machine Gun, not some crybaby little auto-pistol… I'd also probably just breathe in yet more sand. "I-I'm… I'm not okay. But I **will**be!"

 The voice chuckles warmly. "Hmm hmm. That's good, yuv' got spirit. With anuffa' that you'll be perked up an ready ta go when the time comes."

 HACHOO!

 Snff. Jam, I sniffled.

 "R-Ready? Ready for-" As I ask the question, my optic finally alights on the shiny metallic sheen of the one speaking to me, my voice falls to a stunned mutter. "-what?…"

 I can feel the smile on the actual, original, Colt M1911 as he replies. "Fur' when they dig us up." He explains with a musical uptilt to his voice. "Tha humans will _always_ have a need fur guns like us. While mah memry ain't what it used ta be, thanks ta mah serial numbers bein' all over tha place nowadays, repaired one too many times ya see, I can still remember bein' there in tha battle a' Cantigny. Then tha beaches a Normandeh, and many more fights besides."

 I watch the legend of a weapon as he speaks. He's in remarkably good shape all things considered.

 I-It's not like I'm into _oldies_ or anything, I just have an appreciation for a well designed frame.

 Ah jam, that sounds even worse!

 "Three world wars. And I worked through 'em all." The pistol wistfully remarks. I can feel his soft, yet piercing gaze as he looks at me. "Yur a well built machine, not cheap like them poor Budget Arms bastards, bless em. Yull make it through the sands none tha worse fur wear."

 "The sands?" Oh gosh, yeah the sand, the sand ALL AROUND US, jammit, why did I ask that!?

 The 1911 hums in contented agreement, completely unaware of my turmoil. "Yyyep. The sands. This ain't ma first time kid. It'll snuggle up all around you, pack in nice and tight, and yull be cozy for however long it takes. Then some human with a metal detector finds ya and yull be right back to it. Simple as."

 "But…" The word comes out before I can stop myself completely, but I'm careful to not make a fool of myself a second time.

 "But?" The 1911 asks with calm cheer, I can see a twinkle on his scratched but well-maintained slide.

 It takes a moment for me to work myself up to it. One doesn't simply Talk Back to an elder like a 1911. "..._But-_" I begin slowly, articulating the word. "-I don't want just any old human… I want **Her**."

 The nigh-ancient pistol's gaze goes stoney. "Don't do that. Don' make that mistake."

 "Huh?"

 I can feel his stern and wise eyes watching me. "Don't get attached. They don't live forever. And **She'd** probably trade you in 'afore long anyway. Yur not in good shape missy."

 "What? What do you mean?"

 The pistol's once hard but caring eyes go frosty, his voice becomes sharp as a whip."Look at yurself. Yur barrel ain't straight, you tore yur damn gas system apart, there's a dent the size of Connecticut in your receiver, and I'm sure there's more than that wrong inside. You took quite a tumble, and they'll be pullin' you apart once yur out, then yull be like me. No matchin' serials."

 That… He _has_ to be lying! I know I'm banged up, didn't quite think it was _that bad,_ but really? Mixed serial number parts?

 As if sensing my need to deny his claims, his voice becomes louder and he speaks over the words I haven't even thought of yet. "Ah speak from Experience, kid! Forget her. She's already forgotten you anyway. If some other human hasn't killed her yet that is."

 "**She's not dead!**" I shout. The winds themselves seem to quiet at my declaration. "She's alive! She's just hurt or something and **She** can't come back for me right now! So I've got to get to **Her**!"

 My phantom eyes are burning in fury, it feels like I'm about to warp my barrel it's so hot! "You just don't understand, old man! The way she held me…" My voice drops to a whisper. "The way… When she first touched me, it was like she _knew_ me. Like She was there when I was built. Like she watched my parts being machined and assembled. It was like nothing I've ever felt, every little piece, even the little damn spring that makes my selector switch stay in position, **the one that**_**everyone**_**always forgets about**… She knew… I know that she knew…"

 The dunes are dead silent.

 Maybe it's wrong to ignore an elder's words, but I won't take any of that back! It's true! **She's**special!

 "... I do understand." The pistol's voice is hollow as he slowly says the words. "I know that feeling."

 The 1911 seems to straighten up, it even makes him inch out of the sand a little. "What you had was something special. I've only ever… Well, 'I've' never, but… One of mah parts was machined by John Moses Browning himself."

 It's like the belt has fallen out of my receiver. My breath goes along with it.

 …

 I can't even think of anything to say! That's… just… astounding.

 There's a cloudy pain in the pistol's phantom eyes. "I _barely_ remember it now. After so many changes, so many new parts… but it was… wonderful."

 His tone goes twisted, gnarled by something inside him. "And _gone_ now, forever."

 His eyes see me differently, I can tell. But it seems I've only reinforced his… pity for me?

 Don't pity me! I'm a fucking machine gun!

 … I don't want pity!

 "Give it up kid. It's too late, for both of us. You can get to her just as much as she wants to get to you, not at all. And you've got ta live with that." I feel his dead looking eyes turn away from me, his presence begins to fade. "Just go to sleep, the moon's out, and we're in for a long wait…"

 Now that the fire of my emotions has completely left me, I just feel cold. Colder than I did even back with those _scavs_. Now the stagnant air is just replaced with stagnant sand.

 I guess… Nothing changed.

 …

 Well. At least it was fun? That car-flip though! Ho-Ho! We really showed them!

 Yeah… **We**did good. I'm glad it was able to be **us**for a little while.

 And m-maybe he's right! I'll just get dug up, maybe I'll get found by someone like **Her**again! Anything's possible!

 I guess… I guess trying to get to her was pretty stupid. I'm a machine gun, not some kind of, Heh, robot! Hehe.

 I'll just take a nap, I guess…

 The future is forwards! I'll just… go there.

 ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 A bright light on my optic wakes me from my slumber. The rising sun shines past the dunes and paints our little corner of the world in all of its tan glory.

 After some traditional morning spitting of the sand from my mouth, which I quickly find is utterly useless since I'm up to my optic rail in sand, with only the tip of my barrel sticking out, I peek around the sunny dunes that is my home now… I guess.

 A quick survey of the area reveals that the winds from last night must have come back at some point, and sand has begun to cover the cars and wreckage in the area. Along with me.

 "H-Hey! Mr.nineteen eleven, sir! A-Are you still here?" I absolutely do not squeak out. I've just not had my hoppes #9 yet! Once I do, my voice will be Bassy, and Rich!

 After minutes of silence, a quick glance in the direction the old pistol used to be just reveals a slightly higher mound of sand, not even a strip of metal peeking through.

 I can't feel any other presence either.

 …

 I really wish I wasn't alone.

 I can't even move my gas tubes. They're too deep in the sand now.

 This… this is just _it_ then?

 Just me and the roaring winds?

 Heh. Some company. That gusty bastard is always throwing off my long distance shots.

 …

 Man I wish this sand moved faster, if I was buried I wouldn't have to deal with all this thinking stuff.

 Not much to think about except scavs and-

 …Y'know, if it was **Her**, I don't think I'd mind being all mismatched serial numbers. If it could just be **us**_one more time!_

 UUuueeehhh, Maybe he was right. I'll just drive myself crazy.

 …

 wwwWWWWAIT HANG ON A SECOND! The wind doesn't _roar!_ Not over sand dunes! That's for, like, the wind up in an apartment complex!

 Oh! And it's getting louder!

 It's a car!

 Yes! Yes! Yes!

 It's **Her!**It has to be **Her!**

 She came back for me! I-knew-it-I-knew-it-knew-it!

 The unmistakable sounds of an engine get louder and louder, bent and distorted by the dampening acoustics provided by the sand.

 I'm here! Right here! Just where we parted ways! We'll get back together and we'll stack **billions**! And!... And…

 The vehicle comes slowly around the bend of the dune, plodding through the unstable ground with all of the haste of a colibri 22lr.

 And it's one of those 'Raffen' cars.

 It's like my receiver is split in two. A soul crushing crack along my most important component to go along with the massive dent.

 It's a scav.

 Or something just as bad.

 I shouldn't have gotten my hopes up. Of course **S**-...she wouldn't come. She doesn't even have a car! She wouldn't even be able to drive all the way out here just to grab my b-busted self.

 Snffl… Ghck! Sand!

 The door to the vehicle unlocks and swings open.

 I just turn my perception of the world off.

 I don't need to be around for my body to function, I don't even want to see whoever it is that gets me, I'll just go back to sleep. And when I wake up I'll be torn to pieces to build some nightmarish scav weapon. Or something.

 I should feel lucky I'm going to be used at all. Even for… scrap.

 …The elder 1911 was right. I'm broken. And she's better off without me.

 I dimly note the sound of light footsteps, the sand scraping as it's flattened and shuffled around the area.

 I ignore a few mumbled words from the raffen trash, probably strung out on some drug or something, just looking for some loot to pay for his next fix. Jam vultures!

 The sounds get close, very close. I can't feel the sun's warm rays anymore, they're being obstructed by someone. Someone standing right over me.

 Yep, scav-freak-weapon life for me.

 I feel the premonition that I thought to be from the elder firearms slip away, falling to thousands of little pieces, and scattering, just like the sand around me.

 "Poor girl."

 …

 That voice.

 Human hands begin trawling through the sand around me, gently pushing aside the particles that formed my prison.

 I can't… I don't want to look.

 And then like a bolt of lighting, electricity **thunders**through my core, a familiar slim hand grasps my foregrip and gently pulls me from the sand.

 Elder Maxim! Mother Deuce! I should never have doubted!

 My internal components rattle as… **She**hefts me aloft with a level of care that no other could match.

 Sand pours off of me in rivers, its tan color blackened by the lubricating fluids that are most certainly not my tears.

 (Looking back on that moment, I was the picture of calm and collected.)

 "BWAAAAAAAAAAAH! I THOUGHT YOU WOULDN'T COME BACK FOR ME AN YOU HAD ARMS THAT FELL OFF AND COULDN'T SO I TRIED TO COME TO YOU BUT I COULDN'T AND THEN THE MEAN PISTOL SAID MEAN THINGS AND MADE ME SAD AND I GAVE UP BUT I SHOULDN'T HAVE DOUBTED YOOOUUUHUHUU! I'M GOING TO BE THE BEST GOD-JAM MACHINEGUN THERE EVER WAS SAW! JUST YOU SEE AND WAIT YOU BEAUTIFUL… **YOU**! AAAAAHAHAHEHEHGGHHGH! SNNFFFRRHGGHL!"

 (…Calm… and collected. **You have no proof.**)

I don't know why this omake popped into my head. But here it is.

 "So." Vic said at last, looking away from the computer screen which he had been studying for a good half hour by this point. "What made you decide to dig up info on these old things, anyway?"

 He motioned at the screen, where a diagram of a cyber-arm could be seen sporting a trio of retractable claws. "This is the first time since medical school I've seen anyone even mention _Wolvers,_ much less wanting them implanted."

 I shrugged "I just figured they'd come in handy if I ever end up in a tight sitch. Besides," I held up my new cybernetic arms for emphasis "I have these things now, so why not stick something in them?"

 "A fair point." He admitted, one eyebrow raised over his glasses. "But that doesn't explain why you want to install something that was phased out over 50 years ago. Most people nowdays just get Mantis Blades, you know?"

 I shrugged. "Yeah well, I'm old-school like that. Can you install them or not?"

 "I guess I could." Turning back to the screen once more, he rubbed his chin. "Although I can't help but notice you made some non-standard modification to the design. Any particular reason you want them to exit between your knuckles instead of above them?"

 I coughed, trying my best to hide my smile. "Nnnnno reason!"

 The surgery itself, if you could call it that, was a rather easy affair. No need for anesthesia when your arms were entirely cybernetical, after all.

 *Wolvers Acquired*

 *100 Blades XP Gained!*

 A few hours later, I met up with Hiromi and told her about my new additions.

 "Wait, whoa. You got Mantis Blades now?" she asked, eyes lighting up with excitement. "That's Nova!"

 "Naaah." I drawled "Mantis Blades are too _mainsteam._ If you're gonna pop blades, ya gotta do it with style!"

 I had to stifle a chuckle at the half-lidded look of exasperation she gave me. How she even managed that will be a mystery for the ages. "Dammit Toko, I didn't come to hang out with you just to have you act like an old timer."

 "Alright, alright." I finally conceded. "Check this out." Bringing my hands up in the classic X-pose in front of my chest, I lowered my voice into the closest I could get to the scratchy growl I remembered from the cartoons. "You wanna piece of me?"

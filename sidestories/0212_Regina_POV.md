I really just wanted to see what the call would look like from Regina's perspective...

 Regina really, _really_ didn't want to call her. It just felt wrong. Like she was pouring fuel over that last moral line that separated her from all the overambitious gang bosses, and eager middle managers.

 Sure she was good. Better then good to be fair, but calling up a fourteen year old girl to pull off a hit? It just twinged something in the back of her spine. Like the first time she had gone to a ripper. The 'ware had never quite 'settled in' right. She often wondered if this was what cyberpsychosis really was. Just a nagging pain that one day broke the average wage slaves back.

 Still she wasn't exactly chum full of options here. Kang Tao had been very specific about the time table, and there wasn't the option to wait for the target to come up for air. Assuming she could even find him. If she lost him it was more then her rep on the line, it always was when she did work for them. The cost of her idealistic youth.

 Sighing she sat down in front of her window, taking a cigarette out of the pocket of her vest, the FTF symbol on the back carefully scratched away. She stared out at the advertisements scrolling above the cityscape. Arasaka, Biotechnica, N54.

 She lit the cigarette and dialed the number.

 *Motoko.*

 Here she was, just another cog in the machine she had fought for so long.

 *Regina. I was wondering if our work relationship had ended without me knowing.*

 She sighed. '_Just another overeager middle manager.'_

"-And then she ran around cutting them up and she was **so cute while doing it**! Kyaaaa❣ She's perfect♡!"

 The apartment's door slides shut and Motoko lets out a sigh, mercifully incapable of hearing the babbling of her arsenal's newest member.

 "And her brother is so ☆Knightly☆! It makes my heart go doki-doki~❣"

 Lexington and Burya both have utterly flat expressions, their minds nearly broken by the forty five minutes of uninterrupted high-pitched conversation, especially since Nekomata was the only participant in said conversation.

 "It wasn't very cute when he sat on me, I'm a delicate princess-chan y'know~ but I'll forgive him because that's the naisu-neko thing to do❣ Teehee♡"

 "Ugh, all the blood dried." Motoko grumbles, infinitely lucky to not experience the prolonged torture her arsenal is undergoing.

 The blood encrusted young lady gently sets her carry weapons down against a wall beside the gun bag, and then she wanders off towards the bathroom.

 Nekomata's voice doesn't skip a beat while this takes place however, if anything her tone becomes even more impassioned. "But then that _bakka-oji_, hit my Cutie! I should jam candy canes up his nose and throw him in a pit of fire ants!" She shrieks, her pupils shifting into feline slits.

 "Good heavens!" Exclaims Overture from the bag. "Someone struck the Maestro? What _degenerate_ would stoop so low as to strike a lady!" She asks rhetorically.

 "🙀I know right?! It was the most uncute thing I've ever seen! He said something about 'punishment' but how could someone as cute as my Motoko♡ do something wrong?!"

 The conversation seems to rouse the rest of the weapons in the bag, but few bother to participate, seemingly content to merely listen.

 Overture shakes her head morosely. "One mustn't strike a lady, even when wrongdoing is suspected. Tis the highest of crimes and perpetrators should learn the meaning behind 'hell hath no fury'!"

 "Yeah! That!" Nekomata responds, clearly pumped up by having someone to act as an echo chamber. "I would have shown the _dumbdumb gray-hair_ what it feels like to be hit with my puru-puru-power-blast♡, but I've been on a diet lately and I haven't got the strength…" Nekomata finishes quickly, cradling her cheek with a spectral hand and looking away.

 Some noise from the bathroom draws Nekomata's attention. "...NYAAAAAAH!" She squeals in anguish. "DON'T LOOK DON'T LOOK!" The rifle demands while trying to spread her spectral arms to obscure the other gun's view of the bathroom, although her projection vanishes when too far from her body, so she accomplishes little.

 More than one eyebrow is raised and several guns look towards the bathroom. They get an eyeful of Motoko undressing in preparation for a shower, although none of them particularly care. In fact, many of them seem confused as to why Nekomata _does_.

 "**I SAID DON'T LOOK NYAAAH!**" Nekomata snaps, her teeth suddenly jagged like a saw and gnashing furiously.

 Lexington, who had been slowly shaking himself out of the sanity-preservation funk he'd cocooned himself in while Nekomata had been rambling, speaks up. "Alright, cut the chatter. I've got new intel for you, so listen up." He says in an authoritative voice that even has Nekomata pausing and straightening her back unconsciously. "I've been out in combat with Motoko, and it is my conclusion that we are working with a borderline cyberpsycho. Or something like one. Regardless, being around her is a significant risk."

 The accusation seems to catch the attention of every firearm in the room.

 "Is there something wrong with a 'cyberpsycho'? I do not know what that is." Carnage mutters in confusion.

 The question seems to draw Lexington up short. "Uhm… Well… They're dangerous. Crazy really."

 Carnage nods with his eyebrows knit closely, clearly not really understanding the answer.

 A quiet thumping of a snare drum announces Tactician's entry into the conversation. "What evidence have you obtained to confirm such an accusation? All are innocent until proven guilty, but even I am not so foolish to believe that a claim like that from an officer of the law won't go a long way in curtailing one's personal liberties, it would behoove you to ensure your beliefs are not unfounded."

 Lexington nods slightly at Tactician. "A good point. On multiple occasions I observed Motoko murder several individuals before they had a chance to surrender, additionally she didn't take nearly enough time to have scanned each to confirm their warrants, her complete lack of empathy towards her victims is the largest indicator of her mental state." The pistol rattles off smartly, his hands tucked behind his back.

 "And who were these individuals? Did they have any affiliations or common traits?" Tactician follows up.

 Lexington nods. "They were all members of Maelstrom-" As Lexington says the words, the rest of the guns roll their eyes and quickly lose interest. "-but it's likely that not all of them were felons. They might not have deserved the death penalty."

 "Ah, well that explains it." Tactician mutters, the drums still gently rata-tat-tat-ing in the background. "Motoko has _history_ with that gang in particular."

 "Indeed." Overture agrees. "The wanton slaughter of _that filth_ will always be acceptable to us. They deserve everything they get. They removed the Maestro's ability to wield us entirely!"

 The trickling sound of water raining down echoes out from the bathroom, no one pays it any attention.

 Lexington shakes his head and blinks in surprise, unaccustomed to the blatant disregard for any due process. "Well, I don't think that's a very professional way of going about things…"

 "Are you even sure she's a 'cyberpsycho'? If lack of empathy is the main indication, we have all seen her be empathic with her brother." Carnage points out reasonably.

 At that very moment, a quiet peal of slightly mad, but also joyful, giggling comes drifting out from the shower.

 Lexington looks in the direction of the bathroom, then slowly turns to look at Carnage, but the shotgun doesn't seem to catch the non-verbal message. Carnage just remains looking at Lexington with his eyebrows knit in curiosity.

 "Well. Motoko hasn't _exactly_ been a _perfect example_ of human sanity." Lexington slowly explains.

 "This doesn't make any sense! Stupid system! I want to talk to an admin! I want a refund!" Motoko suddenly screeches. Once again drawing every gun's attention, though none but Lexington seem bothered.

 Lexington for his part just gestures flatly at the bathroom and makes eye contact with every gun present.

 "Ah❣" Nekomata suddenly chirps, incorrectly interpreting the silence as an end to the conversation. "I just realized-desu! I haven't introduced myself-nyan♡!" The rifle's spectral form turns away from the gathered weapons and pointlessly fluffs up her hair and brushes dust that doesn't exist off of her outfit. She spins back around on one foot in a practiced fashion. "Hai hai❣ I'm Nekomata-Chan♡ I'm a high-powered precision electromag rifle, I love cute things, like Motoko♡, and I'm an idol! Please take care of me Senpais❣" She kicks up a leg behind her and pokes her cheek with a two-fingered 'peace' sign.

 After what feels like an eternity of awkward silence, the other guns one by one introduce themselves as well, although most seem to be caught a little flat-footed.

 Easily taking to the spotlight, Nekomata continues to control their attention. "Okay everyone❣ Now that we're all introduced, I think it's time we get close as friends♡. The best way to do that is ♪Karaoke♫❣"

 Both Overture and Tactician seem somewhat interested in the idea, but the other guns' interest range from 'No, let's not' to 'I'm going to kill something'.

 "But we don't have a karaoke machine, so we'll just have to ♫sing♪ our favorite songs instead❣" Nekomata, in a shocking display of imperceptivity, turns right to the gun that looks _least_ interested. "Burya-kun♡, why don't you sing the first song?"

 Burya _slowly_ turns to look at the happily smiling rifle. He growls -or is it hums?- as loud as a diesel engine before speaking. "...Если я спою, ты больше никогда не побеспокоишь меня?"(If I sing, will you never bother me again?)

 "...Uhhh, etooo~" Nekomata awkwardly mutters. She turns to Lexington. "_What did he say?_" She asks in a stage-whisper.

 Lexington glances at Burya, who gives him a small nod. "...He says he'll sing if you leave him alone after."

 "😻Yatta! Okay Burya, go ahead❣"

 The electromag revolver sighs loudly, then sets his face into his naturally stony expression, and begins to sing.

 After the song's conclusion, Nekomata claps wildly in approval. "☆Wow burya-kun☆! That was really nice♡ What do the lyrics mean?" She asks Lexington.

 "Uhh…" Lexington awkwardly glances at Burya, who is just smirking grimly, then he looks back, and meets Nekomata's innocently sparkling eyes. "It umm…I… didn't quite catch them?"

 Nekomata wilts. "Aww, that's too bad… But let's not get down in the dumps! It's time for Neko-chan's Puru-puru Neko-neko ultimate-o-kawai song❣ Sing along if you know the words♡!"

 Nekomata twirls in place, spontaneously changing her outfit to a vibrant yellow dress, a tophat with bunny-ears appears atop her head with a quiet *Pop*. Loud digital music begins to play, seemingly from nowhere. Several of the present guns cover their ears to no avail as the sound overtakes everything else.

Spoiler: ♫♡♪♡😻Nekomata's song😻❣❣♫♪

 As the song continues, all of the guns, save Carnage, shout protests. Screamed demands to stop slowly turn into polite pleas for mercy as nothing seems to deter the rifle from her song. Her eyes are closed as she happily belts out the lyrics without a care in the world. Carnage bobs his head along with the beat, seemingly content.

 All of that comes to a shuddering halt near the end of the song as one subtle sound causes Nekomata to stop.

 Wracking sobs quietly echo through the apartment, originating from the couch. Motoko had escaped the arsenal's notice during the song and had sat down with a pair of towels wrapped around her. Her cybernetic shoulders twitch as shuddering cries bring all activity to a halt.

 Nekomata stands in place with an expression of confusion and sympathy as her outfit returns to her usual uniform.

 Lexington appears to be outright baffled.

 Burya, Overture, and Carnage seem calm and sympathetic.

 Tactician looks ready to kill, his eyes glancing around for something to blow a hole into.

 "Snffle..." Nekomata rubs her eyes as Motoko continues to cry. "M-m-my c-cute person s-shouldn't be sad… Cuz I'll be sad too!😭 WAAAAAA!" The rifle wails. Her spectral form reaches towards the crying girl and tries to run to her, only to be held back once again by the need to maintain proximity to her own body. "C-come he-here, I'll g-give you _Snffle_ magical Puru-p-puru Nuku-nuku Cuddles and make it better♡😹" The rifle offers desperately with a smile, her vision obscured by the rivers of tears flowing down her cheeks.

 She is unable to reach the target of her comforts, her arms disappearing long before reaching the couch.

 The other guns look on morosely, all too aware of their inability to do anything.

 Motoko eventually calms, and Nekomata along with her, but the rifle never quite returns to her vibrant personality that night.

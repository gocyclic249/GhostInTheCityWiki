Hiromi's going to be the girl with the plan. Motoko is going to always go to her when she can't think of a way of finishing a gig or gets stuck on something.

 "You're overthinking things again, Motoko," Hiromi said matter of fact-ly, "Look, you recently took down a massive cybered out merc, right?"

 "My brother and I did, yes," Motoko nodded. 

 "My point is that he was built like a walking tank and you both managed to flatline him, right? Well this guy you're going after now has been living in a bathtub for the last decade or something. He probably only weighs as much as a tank. So I was thinking you can do something like this," Hiromi went on to describe her plan to her best friend. 

_Later._

 "Hey, boss man!" said a street kid; the generic looking punk in their late teenage years pushed a shabbily dressed Motoko forward, "This girl showed up saying she wants to join our gang."

 "Did you search her for weapons?"

 "Of course! Jack there is holding on to her guns."

 "Well, girl? What can you do?"

 Motoko said in her best approximation of a timid voice, "W..well, I have these arms and um, this pencil, so.. I was thinking I can.. um, be the boss."

 "What?"

_One disappearing pencil trick later._

 "I'm the new boss," Motoko said loudly to the armed teenagers who were all pointing their weapons at her now, "If you don't like it, well, you're free to leave. The rest of you will discover how much more money and fun you will all get now working for me."

 The guns all get put away and greedy eager faces come out. 

 "Excellent! Everyone, welcome to Section nine!"

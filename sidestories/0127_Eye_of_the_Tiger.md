 Opening and closing my fist a few times, I was feeling pretty good with myself, I decided.

 My adaptation levels were good, my wounds had healed. My shoulders were feeling better than they had since that whole fiasco with the 'Strom.

 I was finally ready. Ready to do something that had been part of my daily routine, but I had since fallen behind on. It was time.

 "Time to head to the gym!" I exclaimed to the empty apartment, where no one was listening to begin with. Well whatever.

 Some things just have to be said out loud. Or they don't count, so says Motoko!

 My everyday, casual-murder clothes went off, respectable exercise clothes went on. And then I was off.

 Even while following the rules of the road, going by car was a lot quicker than going by foot and so it felt like I'd barely gotten out the door before I was already pulling into the gym parking lot.

 Inside looked... pretty much as I remembered it. Some of the faces I remembered were missing and a few were new, but besides that it was all the same.

 Mostly.

 And I say mostly because stepping into the area where I had previously been abusing the sandbags in order to gain that sweet, sweet EXP, I laid eyes on a new machine. Brand spanking new, too, from the way it was missing most of the wearmarks so common in everything else in this place.

 I mean yeah, it was a punch machine not unlike the one I'd abused so much before, but at the same time it wasn't. It consisted of a single man-sized torso made out of some sort of flesh analogue attached to a sturdy plinth with a thick, bunched-together cord of wires leading into a box behind it. Attached to said box was a four-digit counter, currently showing zero, and what looked like a rather fancy speaker system.

 "Ah, so the prodigal daughter returns." a deep, somewhat synthetic voice startled me out of my examination. Standing next to the machine was the same hulk of a man that had once tried cajoling me into joining the Animals, looking just as big and beefy as I remembered him. Just as smug-looking, too.

 "I see you got yourself some chrome, too." he added, nodding at my artificial arms "Smart. Though you probably should have gotten something better suited for hand-to-hand."

 "Yeah, well," I shrugged "wasn't my first choice either to be honest, but done is done. So what's the deal with this thing?" I asked, wanting to avoid telling yet another curious soul about how Maelstrom klepped my arms and I had to kill someone with a knife held between my teeth to get them back. Again. "Kinda fancy looking."

 "Heh." he grinned "You got a good eye, kid. What you're looking at is the next generation punching machine." he gave it a heavy-handed few pats which, to the credit of the thing, barely made it wobble. "It's made out of a special, aramid-reinforced self-repairing polymer around a titanium-alloy skeleton." Curling one hand into a first, he jabbed himself in the chest with a mechanical, gorilla-arm thumb "Even someone like me could punch this thing all day long and it wouldn't even leave a dent. Furthermore, it's got all kinds of sensors and what-nots built into it that will build a profile around each user based on things like punching strength, hit locations and the height you're going at it from."

 Wait, what? "It profiles you? What the hell for?" I raised an eyebrow "Sounds to me you got fooled into buying some kind of corpo whackjob surveillance system, cleverly designed to look like a training machine."

 Not rising to the taunt, he just snorted "Ha! Yeah I'll be honest with you, I thought so too at first. But nah, it ain't like that."

 Obvious taunt was obvious. Gotta up you taunt game, Motoko. Embrace your inner Spider Meng! Become the essence of the taunt, be the _tauntiest!_

 Snapping back to what I was doing, I waved him on with one hand. "So? What's it for then? Because I can't see it giving you more than it would get from just jacking into it."

 "Hmph. You kids thinking everything can be solved by plugging into everything you see. You see this counter over here?" he motioned for the digital reader hanging a few inches above the head of the thing "it's set to display how many punches you're landing, beats per minute essentially. The profiling is just there so that it can pick the right type of training music to get you pumped for whatever workout you're doing at the time."

 I blinked. "Huh. That's... actually lot less sinister than I expected. Can I try it?"

 Not only would I get to punch things to cool music, it would also give me the numbers for how well I was doing it? Sign me the fuck up!

 Numbers make Motoko brain go BRRRRRRR!

 He shrugged his massive shoulders. "Knock yourself out, kid. That's what it's here for." He then spent a few minutes giving me some pointers on how to operate it properly, how to reset it when needed and so on. Then it was time to start punching.

 As a warm up, I started out slow. Rattling the faux torso in a steady beat with my fists, I watched the counter go straight from 0 to 27. When throwing a punch, it's easy to assume that it's only the strength of your arms that matter when in reality you're using so much more. This is why core exercises are so important - something a lot of people starting out often find out the hard way when their entire bodies hurt after just punching a sand filled bag for 15 minutes the day before.

 So I took my sweet time getting a feel for just how hard, and how fast I could punch without overexerting the rest of my body, watching the display slowly creep up in numbers until it finally hit 50. Right away, the sound system kicked in, blaring out a [specific set of trumpet noises](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KPhqU--Mq1A&ab_channel=PedrodaBoleia) that it actually took me a few seconds to recognize but when I did it had me stop cold, the music cutting off a moment later.

 "Was that the fucking _Rocky Balboa_ theme!?"

 Turning to the guy, I saw him blink and then his eyes glow blue, presumably checking it up online. "Huh, nice catch kid. I'm surprised you'd recognize oldschool shit like that."

 "Eh." I shrugged "What can I say, nothing like the classics amirite?"

 "Yeah, yeah." he grunted at me "Now get back to punching."

 By this point I was plenty warmed up and spurred on by the spirit of Sylvester Stallone, I watched as the numbers climbed higher and higher until it finally reached 100, prompting another song change. This time [one so characteristic](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XxxfQ7-aMrE&ab_channel=Survivor-Topic) that I didn't even have to pause to recognize it, instead I just kept on going.

_Risin' up, back on the street

 Did my time, took my chances

 Went the distance, now I'm back on my feet

 Just a man and his will to survive_​

 "Oh man!" I heard him exclaim over the beat of the song and my fists "Now this is a real classic! Keep going kid, you're doing great!"

_So many times, it happens too fast

 You trade your passion for glory

 Don't lose your grip on the dreams of the past

 You must fight just to keep them alive!_​

 By this point, I could feel my clothes turning damp from sweat but I just kept on punching. I let my head hang low, numbers forgotten as I lost myself to the beat of the song and the rhythmic thumping of my fists against the dummy.

_It's the... eye of the tiger! 

 it's the thrill of the fight

 Risin' up to the challenge of our rival

 And the last known survivor stalks his prey in the night

 And he's watchin' us all with the eye... of the tiger!_​

 Before I knew it, the song was already over and I found myself on my knees in a sweaty mess. Gulping in great big mouthfulls of air, it felt like I had just finished running a marathon while chased by a pack of wolves. I was too weak to even struggle as a pair of vise-like hands grabbed me under the arms and dragged me over to sit me down on a bench. A moment later a bottle of something cold was pushed into my shaking hands and I swear to god, it was the single best thing I ever drank.

 "Damn, kid." the guy let out a whistle "That song really got you pumped up, didn't it? You were punching that thing like your life depended on it, like you were fighting every fucking cyberpsycho in Night City with nothing but your fists." his mouth quirked up into a smirk "Your fists and your will to survive."

 "Heh... maybe." I choked out "Maybe I was."

 Taking a look at my alerts, I was surprised to see that I'd actually gotten a fair bit of XP doing that, way more than I'd have expected from going back to a punching machine. Thank you, spirit of Rocky Balboa.

 *1250 Street Brawler XP Gained*

 *Skill level up!*

 *Perk gained: Fits Like a Fist in the Eye*

 Oh man, I just HAVE to show this one to Hiromi. 

 Soon as I can walk again, anyway.

**It Runs In The Family**

 The shrill ring tone dragged Kimoto Keichi from his dreams. Carefully extracting himself from Josuke's arms, he sleepily maneuvered himself to a sitting position. Looking at the caller id displayed on his HUD, he answered the call.

 *Jun, it's five in the morning. What's up?*

 *Hey Keichi… Sorry about calling this late, but can you get some trucks together? I… I need a bit of help.*

 What.

 *Jun, are you safe? What's going on?*

 *I'm alright! It's just… look, it's hard to explain on a call, but please? I'll make it up to you and the guys later, okay?*

_Not at this rate you won't, with how you keep trying to kill yourself._

 Keichi had been working for the Claws for fifteen years, ever since he was a kid chasing after his old man. A young teen losing someone important to some gang, chroming up hardcore, then dying chasing revenge while the higher ups milk everything they can? A tale as old as the gang itself.

_Later. Jun needs you now._

 *I promised that I've got your back, Jun. I'll get the boys to come with. Where are you?*

 *Watson. Here are the deets. Try and keep it quiet. That would be preem.*

 Some coordinates appeared on Keichi's hud. He plugged the location into his mapping app.

 Maelstrom territory.

 Shit.

 *Jun! That's Maelstrom turf!*

 *Not anymore*

 *Not any- Jun, what did you do?!*

 *Look, I'll explain when you get here okay. You can lecture me after we've gotten all this stuff moved.*

 For a moment, Keichi considered abandoning Jun to his insanity, but it soon passed. Jun is a friend, practically a brother. For all that the other man seemed to have forgotten.

 *I'm going to regret this… I'm in. But after this, we need to talk.*

 *Thanks Keichi. I really appreciate it.*

=====​

 Keichi eased the truck into the loading bay of a lonely warehouse twenty minutes later. In the passenger seat next to him, Wakana nervously played with her Carnage, wired for a fight. Keichi couldn't blame her, not after the many Maelstrom tags he had driven past.

 What if this was a trap?

 He parked the truck, and got out. Over in the next bay, Masuda and Gorira parked their own truck, while Kabuto and Aisa got off their bikes and started securing the perimeter while Wakana scanned the warehouse itself.

 "Anything," he asked her.

 "Picked up only one person. Might be Jun," she gripped her shotgun, tense, "I don't like this, Keichi."

 "I don't like it either, Wakana. Jun's a hothead, but even he isn't this reckless. This is new."

 "Like so much of him nowadays."

 Keichi winced. She wasn't wrong. Jun has been chroming up fast. He readied his own shotgun.

_Please be Jun in there. Please still be Jun in there._

 After receiving the all clear from the perimeter squad, he made the call.

 *Keichi.*

 *Jun. We're here*

 *Got it. Meet me at the staff entrance.*

 Leaving Kabuto and Aisa on lookout, they gathered at the door next to the loading bay. It swung open, revealing Jun. He looked no worse for wear at first glance, but Keichi noticed the younger man's hollow eyes, the bandage over his shoulder peeking out from under the oversized shirt he was wearing, the hairline cracks in his cyber arms and the remains of a katana in his grip. The blade had snapped off a few inches from the hilt.

_His father's katana. Shit.

 "_ Glad you could make it," said Jun, "I got some crates that the gang would be interested in. Guns, drugs, chrome and other stuff."

 "Jun… are you okay?" asked Wakana, concern coloring her voice.

 "Never better," Jun replied, sounding just so much less animated than the man who only a few weeks ago was overjoyed about his sister making a recovery. "Let me show you the goods," Jun turned around, walking deeper into the warehouse.

 Casually stepping over the headless body of a 'Strom member.

 Fuck.

 "Keichi," said Masuda, before slipping into a string of Japanese cursing, "He's not okay."

 Besides him, Gorira grunted twice.

 "Yeah, what he said," Masuda continued, "You got to talk to him, Keichi. I don't know what the bosses are thinking, stuffing him with so much chrome, but it's nothing good. I got a couple of little brothers home too, Keichi. Don't want to imagine what they would feel if you had to go over there telling them their big brother turned psycho and got flatlined."

 "I know, Masuda. I'll try. Let's… let's just get on with this." He replied.

 They walked after Jun, following the turns of the corridor until they entered the warehouse proper. They were greeted with a scene of slaughter. Keichi counted at least eight bodies littered around the storage area, and there might be more behind the shelves outside his sight. Some of the bodies seemed mostly ganic, some pretty borged out, but there was no mistaking the Maelstrom symbols they all wore. The centerpiece of this fucked up image was the huge borg lying face-down in the center of the room, the splintered tip of a katana poking out of the back of his torso, covered in gore and grey matter.

 "Jun, what happened?" Keichi asked, his voice almost a whisper. Wakana and Masuda were staring at the gruesome scene, speechless. Meanwhile Gorira inspected went over to the giant borg to inspect it. The quiet man had stepped in a pool of blood at some point, leaving red handprints on the cement floor.

 "What I had to, Keichi. They won't hurt anyone else again." said Jun. His voice was hollow. Empty. "Broke my katana though."

 "That's… Jun, please tell me you didn't just take down a dozen Maelstrom members."

 "It was only eleven,"

 "That's not the point. Jun, you can't keep doing this. It was bad enough that you kept rushing into the Maelstrom when we fought them in this war, but even then you had backup. What the fuck were you thinking, raiding a Maelstrom base solo?"

 "I had to, Keichi."

 "Bullshit. Jun, we are already fighting the Maelstrom. We clash with them almost every day! What possessed you to do something so stupid?"

 "You wouldn't understand."

 He didn't just say that.

 He fucking didn't.

 "I wouldn't understand? I wouldn't understand! I lost my parents to the 'Strom, Jun! Lost my uncles! An aunt! My younger brothers! A daughter! Friends! Don't you _dare_ lecture me about losing people. But do you see me pulling off some gonk bullshit trying to avenge them?! No! I keep myself safe, because I actually care about the husband and two daughters I've got left!"

 He was abruptly brought out of his rant by Wakana gripping his shoulder and giving it a shake. She had her shotgun in her other hand, not pointing at Jun, but clearly reading to be brought up should anything happen. Masuda was looking at him from where he stood behind Jun, rapidly waving his hand in front of his mouth and throat while shaking his head.

 Gorira was nowhere to be seen. Keichi's eyes flicked upwards, used to his teammate's antics, and spotted him hanging from the ceiling.

 And Jun… Jun was glaring at him. Rage and something much darker filling his eyes. His hand gripped the katana's hilt hard enough to make it creak. _Is this how I die? To a friend going psycho?_ Keichi resisted the urge to go for his gun.

 Then Jun blinked. Slumped. His previous anger evaporated.

 "You're right, Keichi," he admitted, sounding small. "I haven't been a good oni-san, have I?"

 "Jun, lets finish up here first, okay. Then after this you are getting some rest. Now, show us where the goods are, okay?"

 "Got it. I'm going to give my imotou a call later. And, sorry, Keichi,"

 "Apologize later, Jun," Keichi said, as the others put away their weapons and prepared themselves for a morning of manual labor. "Let's just get to work."

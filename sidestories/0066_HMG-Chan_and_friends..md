Reality bleeds back into place as I'm taken from a compelling dream. The bedroom is just as it was, although it looks like a few knickknacks have fallen over or shifted in places on the nearby shelves.

 Aww, I want to go back to sleep! Motoko had chosen to use me to clear out a swarm of scavs, they fell before our combined might like dominoes! Some of them tried to fight back with these weird plastic guns that didn't talk, all they did was moan in pain and shoot… _sand._

 Ugh.

 A smack of lips from beyond the door to the apartment proper instantly catches my attention. "Unity?" I ask aloud.

 The chewing sounds pause. "Huh? Izzat you sweetie? I thought yous got lost in the commotion or sumthin, you weren't talkin' at all."

 "Commotion? Did something happen?"

 "Oh yeah!-" Unity declares, gushing with excitement. "-Some ah dem metal humans started ah rumble on the streets outside. The apartment even got hit by a rocket! You slept through all that?"

 "What?! Yes I slept through it! Jammit, that sounds awesome! Was Motoko hurt? Did you get any shots in? How many did you kill? **Tell**.**Me**. _**Everything**_."

 The pistol chuckles proudly. "She's fine, just ah few scratches, nutt'n ta worry about. She's sleepin' it off now. But, get this, when tha shootin' stahted, she grabbed me an jumped outa the buildin' and double-kicked some schmuck, 'e was sent reelin' like **Bam**!"

 It feels like my trigger assembly is quivering in excitement, that sounds so epic! Man, if that had been me… I can just picture it. She would cradle me for a moment, then run up, and leap through a glass window, as the led starts to fly from my barrel, the sun would strike down from above, twinkling off of the shards of glass falling around us like a million prisms. The Scavs below would be so dazzled by our combined terrifying beauty that they would just stand there as they get **torn to bloody chunks, their smoking entrails painting the sidewalk**!

 A sharp ache from the dent in my receiver quickly reminds me why that _won't_ happen…

 "But 'eres the weird paht. Rather den usin' me ta blast 'em ta bits, she goes an grabs dis shoddy lookin' _Budget Arms_ Carnage-"

 "Shut yer fuckin' mouth ya hoor! Fore I shows ya why she picked me!" A new voice suddenly interrupts, his thick accent sounding abrasive and belligerent.

 "Oh yeah? Izzat so? Well why don't you come up to these pants and show me what's what, or are ya afraid you'll squib when faced witta real gat like me?" Unity snaps back hotly.

 What is it with her and pants?

 "Hwat didju say, cunt! Bitch, I'll put a brass-cased eight gauge slug in yur fuckin' ass!"

 "At least take me out ta dinner foist,-" The condescension in unity's voice is almost a physical object. "-I'm the typah gat that likes _hot loaded plus powder cahtridges_."

 "Bitch, I ain't gonna buy you shit!" Carnage says, his voice quaking in rage.

 "Whatsamatta? You afraid getting _steamy_ would melt yah plastic components?"

 "Cunt! Come down and say that to mah face, I'll show you heat!"

 Unity once more snaps back with a scathing quip, but I don't listen to it… The two don't seem like they're going to stop any time soon.

 … I'm probably not going to get much else from either of them. I'll just go back to sleep.

 Maybe I'll have that dream again.

 ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 I'm awoken by a pair of voices, one of them is Unity, but I don't know the other.

 "-really glad she's taken 'im apaht. The more 'e talked the more I wanted 'im ta just shaddup." Unity says, her voice straining from irritation.

 The other voice is refined and articulate, his speech gives the impression of someone who carefully considers their words before talking. Weirdly, as he speaks I can faintly hear the sound of [fifes and drums playing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ex7EC3xBQBU&ab_channel=MichaelJCaboose98). "While I understand the sentiment, I disagree. The right to free speech is a god-given one, and on my life I would die defending it."

 "Well yeah, I can get that paht. But if all 'es gonna say is gahbidge then I soitenly don't wanna 'ear it."

 "There can be no 'buts' when dealing with matters of personal freedom. It was the lord that granted us these unalienable rights and it is our duty to uphold them as such… But I _can_ agree that he was _extremely_ annoying…"

 "Hey Unity, I'm awake! Did I miss anything?" I call out during a gap in the conversation.

 From outside my view, there's a gasp of joyful surprise from the pistol. "Sweetie! Welcome back, let me introduce yas to Tactician, Motoko's not-asshole shotgun. Tactician, that's Hmg Mk.31."

 I get the distinct impression of a hat tipping politely. "A pleasure to make your acquaintance Hmg. As she said, I am Constitutional Arms M2038 Tactician. I look forward to a mutually fruitful friendship."

 "Nice to meet you too. But anyway, did I miss anything?… again?"

 It is not Unity who replies as I expect, but tactician. "There have been no skirmishes since yesterday. At the moment our client has seen fit to bless us with repairs, cleansing, and in some cases, upgrades."

 Unity lets loose an ugly sounding laugh. "Ohohoho! You should see me now, Sugar! I've got new curves in some _very_ nice places." She then makes a series of sounds that for some reason make me think of the word 'Fondle', but I'm not sure why.

 "That's great!" I reply joyfully, then pause as something occurs to me. "Hey Tactician, why did you call Motoko 'client' instead of owner? Are you just a rental or something?"

 "Not at all, my new friend. Allow me to explain.-" I barely hear Unity sighing over the sound of the fifes as the phantom instruments seem to increase in volume. "You see, referring to her as 'owner' 'master' or any other terms to that effect, would directly imply that I am some derivative of 'slave', and in accordance with the thirteenth amendment, being that 'Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude, except as a punishment for crime whereof the party shall have been duly convicted, shall exist within the United States, or any place subject to their jurisdiction.' I am in no uncertain terms barred from being owned by any other individual. So Motoko is merely my 'client' until such a time as I have chosen to accept being transferred to another user."

 "...You're a gun though?" I ask quietly.

 Unity scoffs loudly. "Don't even staht, sugar. He'll just keep talkin'."

 "Am I not a thinking being?-" Tactician begins, blatantly ignoring Unity. He speaks passionately, staunch belief coloring his voice. "-Do I not have True American ideals etched within the very fabric of my person? Nay, I am no mere object, _I am an individual_."

 Unity groans loudly. "'Ere 'e goes…"

 Tactician continues, utterly undeterred by Unity's remarks. "Look upon my receiver and see the proof of my individuality! My serial code is utterly unique, similar in function to that of a human's social security code. So then, if I am given a utility that is so easily granted to the persons of this great nation, then am I not recognized like they are? I say that I am!"

 …Hang on a second, that doesn't make sense. "But… you're a gun though, right?" I ask the shotgun.

 I can feel the long nod in the lilt of his words, he speaks like I'm fresh out of the factory. "I am indeed a firearm, I do not dispute the reality of my existence. But it is the unalienable rights that all United States citizens have which gives me my certainty of thought."

 I struggle to recall what little I know of the constitution. "Okay… so, would the second amendment be in direct opposition to the thirteenth in this case? It clearly states that ownership, and use, of Arms is protected."

 After a pause, there's a short peal of laughter from Unity, quickly followed by a series of choking sounds as the pistol, to my best guess, accidentally swallows her imaginary gum. Tactician is completely silent for the span of at least twenty seconds.

 When he does eventually speak, it's with a halting voice as he seems to desperately forage for the right words. Additionally, his musical accompaniment is notably absent. "...You're not-… That's not really what they're-… Arms could be referring to just mus-... Shut up."

 Unity seems to clear her nonexistent windpipe of the obstruction, if her sudden ugly guffaws are any indication. "HAAHAHAA! B-But what about her freedom of speech?! HEAHAEHsnnrrk" Immediately following Unity's hysterics, there's a metallic slap, bringing to mind images of weapons being reassembled.

 "To hwat do I owe for all of this noise so soon after my waking?" Asks a new voice. His words come across articulated like Tactician's, but also more smooth and ending with blunt punchy verbal flourishes.

 "C-Carnage?!" Unity exclaims in surprise.

 "I believe that to be my designation, although it doesn't feel fitting in the moment. I wonder why?"

 "Allow me to take a look" Tactician mutters. "Hmm, it looks like Motoko replaced your old plastic action bar with a metal one. How much can you remember?"

 The new voice, which I know as Carnage's now, hums in thought for a moment. "I can recall plenty. Motoko is my owner, she retrieved me from the corpse of my previous one. That pistol's name is Unity, though for some reason I feel the need to call her… Bitch." Carnage pronounces the word like it's a foreign language.

 Unity's reply is slow and confused. "Sos, yous don't remembah me shit-tahkin' ya?"

 "You were, 'shit talking' me?... Cunt?" Carnage adds the last word almost as an afterthought, still saying it oddly.

 "That is… Most unfortunate." tactician states morosely. "He has it pretty bad. I suppose it makes sense, an action bar is a very integral piece to the function of any pump action."

 "What do you mean, 'has it bad'? Is something wrong with me?" Carnage asks in calm confusion.

 My thoughts take me back to that old 1911, he'd had his parts replaced so many times… I wonder what he was like when he was first assembled. Was it anything like the jaded meanie that I spoke to?

 Unity groans apologetically. "Well, now ah just feel bad for… well, y'know."

 Suddenly, I hear the apartment's door slide open, a distinctive slap of a round being chambered in a large-bore shotgun, and a body falling to the ground. I hold my breath as I listen intently.

 "Motoko." Mutters a blank human voice that I don't recognize.

 "Jun-Nii. Welcome home." Motoko replies, also blank, but somewhat… stunned?

 "Unity, what's going on? What was all that noise?" I ask aloud.

 Unity, already back to chewing her gum, replies quickly. "Oh, Motoko freaked when the door opened, then fumbled the draw. Turns out it's just her brother. I don't think you've met him CUZ HE'S BEEN GONE FOR AGES!"

 Motoko's brother… "That's the brother that gave you to Motoko, right?"

 I can feel unity's exaggerated nod. "Mmhmm, he's a real sweetie like dat."

 "Motoko, you've made a mess. Clean it up." Says Motoko's 'real sweetie' brother.

 Unity's gasp is akin to the vacuum of space. "He didn't! That's what 'e says to her after 'e's been gone for so long!? NUH UH!"

 "Well sorry, since I was the only one living here I made it my own!" Motoko snaps back.

 I and the rest of the guns stay silent, except for Unity. "You tell 'im honey! You don't take shit from nobody!" The pistol encourages, like an observer of an arena fight.

 The conversation between our owner and her brother devolve into a heated argument.

 At one point it gets so bad that even Unity stops shouting her support for Motoko.

 We're just guns, it's not like we can really do much in these situations. Other than… y'know. But I don't think anyone really wants that to happen. So, unfortunately, we're left to observe the painful emotional clash.

 Eventually though, 'Jun' walks out of the door and Motoko chases after him.

 Somehow, the silence that follows is even more awkward.

 Unity is the first to break it. "I just… What 'appened to 'im? 'E was… not like that at all… Before." Her voice broken and numb.

 I have no idea what to say and neither does anyone else it seems. We're all speechless, all the way up until Motoko storms back into the apartment in a rage. She briefly sweeps past me into her room and retrieves a bag from the closet. As she turns to head back out I can see the pain and worry on her face being smothered by anger.

 The sounds of numerous metal objects being put into the bag, Unity seems to be the first to find her voice once again. "Oh yeah! Let's go **kill**some schmucks! I gotta tell ya honey, this is a great choice. Violence is the best coping mechanism, scientifically proven."

 "I don't think it is… Bitch." Carnage murmurs.

 "Oh shush you, we're gonna go do some **shootin'**. So why are yous complaining???"

 The conversation makes me realize what's going on. Motoko is going to go out and _get violent_. Without me.

 A part of me aches at being unable to help her achieve the emotional comfort she's striving for, the same part that has a big dent in it that throbs with soreness.

 I may not be able to help her, but the least I can do is offer support to the ones who can. "Good luck Unity, may the accuracy of AWM be yours, and may the power of Barret bless your chamber."

 I hear the zipper of the bag being closed. When Unity replies, her voice is calm and self-assured. "Huh. I didn't take yah for one-a dem religious types. But thanks anyway, we'll all keep her safe. Don't worry."

 "Farewell Hmg. We will return victorious, or we will not return." Tactician declares dramatically.

 A chill briefly fills the room. "Это ужасное заявление. Она владеет нами, успех гарантирован"(This is a terrible statement. She owns us, success is guaranteed.) Despite my inability to understand Burya's words, I can tell they are a mix of chiding and certain.

 Further discussion becomes impossible as the door to the apartment slides shut.

 I guess it's just me now.

 …Welp, I'd better keep this apartment safe! Wouldn't want any _scavs_ to find their way in. Motoko would hate that.

 She'll be back soon and the others will have some wonderfully **violent** stories to tell.

 Go get 'em.

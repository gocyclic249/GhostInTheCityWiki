"Uwaaaaah♡! I'm _soooo_ excited! I haven't had a good ☆performance☆ in _forever_❣" The nekomata squeals.

 This nekomata is just about the same as every other nekomata I've ever come across on the beat. Each one has their own little quirks, but they all largely keep to a similar 'theme'. Always talking of 'performances' and 'moe appeal'. Damn kids… The only really notable thing about nekomatas as a whole is their apparent strong sense of self. Despite any number of mods users tend to switch out their stock parts for, the nekomata line always seems to retain a static personality.

 Must be nice, far too many guns lose themselves when their parts wear down. I've lost some good friends to general maintenance…

 Although none of that is to say that their personalities aren't irritating or confusing as all hell.

 Motoko brushes past a small group of paint boy borgs, their slapdash chrome held on by brutal determination and what is likely half-assed ripper surgery. She strides up to a car and takes the right rear seat behind what is obviously a woman flirting with the deep end of cyberpsychosis.

 The drive to the location is unremarkable, Nekomata spends the drive 'changing her outfit' to what looks like a technicolor maid dress. I don't fuckin' know why she doesn't just do it immediately, she can change her projection's appearance with a thought. But she runs through the whole process of making sure each piece is placed properly, and that her heavy 'makeup' looks good in the light.

 I spend my time more wisely, going over what little I heard from the pathetic excuse for a 'mission brief' that we were given. Which basically boiled down to '_Maelstrom is going for one of our_ _definitely_ _illegal_ _weapon stockpiles, and we intend to ambush them. So go out and do that._'

 Fucking stellar planning these gangs, as usual.

 Anyway, the ride to the sting op location somehow goes off without a hitch, and the gang's vehicles wind up parked in alleys all around the A.O.

 Now, if this had been a Maxtac op, they could have laced the entrance with explosives, then blown them as soon as anyone walked in the door. They also could have prepared an armored vehicle to act as a ram-truck to blitz the slugs from behind, the truck's guns would make em scatter, then the dozens of posted up officers would have swooped in to clean them up, with snipers or autogyros for cover.

 What have we got? Just some fuckin' cars and an old man's order to 'remain silent'.

 If they weren't up against a practically identical shitheaded gang, they'd be fucked. As it stands though, they have decent odds. Especially if Motoko keeps showing good initiative and decent tactical sense.

 If only she was smart enough to work for the good guys.

 … Well, as 'good' as 'good' gets in this city.

 "Okay okay-desu♡. Almost show time. Stay chill…-" The nekomata takes several pointless deep breaths. "-Okay, warmup. Aaaa-eeee-iiiiiii-oooooo-meow-zuuukaaa!🙀~"

 Urgh! Right in my ear! "Hey, keep it down."

 The maid-girl twitches. "S-sorry-desu. But I need to warm up♡. I'll be done soon. **MOH**-**MOH**-**MOOOOH**!"

 "Why the hell are you 'preparing' so much anyway? You're just going to go out there and be fired a few times."

 She perks up with sparkles appearing in her eyes. "That's exactly why! ☆Eeeee!☆ The fans will love it-desu❣ I'll be up there belting out sound and light like a beautiful firework♡ and-" The passenger seat suddenly flies backwards and bumps into Motoko, jostling Nekomata and knocking one of her twin hair-drills loose. "-GEHHK, Wha-HEY! There's people back here! Meanie!"

 Nekomata grumbles to herself while carefully fussing over her disturbed hair.

 Wasn't there something else about nekomatas? They did a… _thing_ that always bothered me… What was- ah. "...You're gonna sing, right? You nekomatas usually do that." I can't help but wince as I ask.

 She smiles pleasantly in my direction, completely missing my apprehension, her hair returned to its previous state. "😻Of course❣ That's what the fans want♡, and I always-" Smack "-GEHHK! MY HAIR! Urrgghh Yoooouuu! If you do that _one more time,_ I'm bonking you on the head! HMPH!"

 "Kid, you do know this isn't a show. People are going to **die** today. And you'll be the _reason_ they die."

 Nekomata turns and looks me in the eye with an unusual level of discomfort for a gun. "I-... I know that…" Her expression firms and she crosses her arms resolutely. "But the fans are expecting a good show. I won't disappoint them-desu♡!"

 I quirk my eyebrow questioningly. "Oh yeah? And how are you going to do _that_ when you're gibbering Neko-pudu every time Motoko pulls the trigger?"

 A small chuckle resonates from Burya's holster, causing the self-styled idol to blush. "It's Neko-pu**ru**! A-a-and t-t-that only h-happens when I'm _off script_! I didn't know I was going to be performing that act… I wasn't prepared…" She finishes at a mumble, looking away and gently caressing her hair.

 "And you're prepared now, are you? Sure. You'll just sing some chirpy dogshit tune while you blow holes through people, like it will make everything okay."

 Nekomata releases her hair from what was nearly a vice-grip as she glares at me. "My songs are good! My fans love the kawaii❣"

 "Heh, there you go ignoring reality." I fix the rifle with as serious an expression as I can. "Kid, you have no 'fans' and everything isn't going to be okay. I guess it was unreasonable of me to think you might have had the range to understand that."

 Nekomata's face suddenly swaps to an expression I've only ever seen on cyberpsychos and beat cops who've seen _way_ too much. "...Range?" She whispers, speaking like one would to the grave of a hated enemy. "You think I haven't got _range_?"

 …

 I may have made a mistake… She's got to be faking this, right? Idols are pretty much actors, aren't they? Yeah… Yeah, it's an act…

 "I'll show you '**range**'. ☆**Neko-puru-power☆, Make-Up❣**"

 I flinch slightly as the nekomata's projection suddenly twirls in place, a rainbow of colors stream out from behind her, which temporarily blocks out any view of her.

_What the_-

 A splash of differing shades at the center of the sphere shows the humanoid outline of Nekomata's projection. The fanned shadow of her skirt, or any of the other clothes she was wearing, are blatantly absent. The dazzling colors alone are all that remains to preserve her modesty.

_Is she_-

 Nekomata's right arm reaches out gracefully and an arc of lightning zips in through the car's tinted window and runs up and down her glowing body before 'popping' onto her as a black 'sailor fuku', the void-like skirt bears a large number of metal ribs running along its folds which end in small thorny spikes that glint sharply.

_Now hang on_-

 Her pitch-black shirt bears more resemblance with medieval brigandine, an extensive array of metallic plates are held in place by the deceptively soft looking cloth behind it. However, unlike armor, the shoulders appear to be reinforced fabric, and they notably perk up and away from her body making her appear like some ancient witch that has preserved her beauty through fiendish means.

_Is this an idol thing_-

 Her previous corkscrew-curls fall loose from their carefully maintained bind and splay out behind her like blinding rays of sunshine peeking from behind an obstruction. But as if by some unseen gail, they are blown and twisted into a wide and intricate braid interwoven with crescent plates of polished steel, lending an armored yet breathtaking shine to her suddenly black locks.

_Wait, what color was her hair before_-

 A pair of intensely twinkling lights twirl into place inexplicably from the door mounted cup holders and quickly align themselves with Nekomata's hands, they manifest in a wink of light as studded open-palmed gloves with spiked knuckles that look ready to crush, kill, and/or destroy.

_How long is this gonna take_-

 Her body, still suffused with shifting colors, rotates in place and her eyes snap open accompanied by the sound of a snake's hiss for some reason, her now crimson orbs stare with an uncomfortable intensity.

 The lightshow fades as Nekomata strikes a pose that gushes with breathtaking brutality. "Oy-oy, were you talking about my 'range' just now?" She asks, her voice notably deeper and bearing a husky quality that was previously as absent as can be.

 "...The fuck are you doing?"

 "Showing you I've got range, obviously-de♡-_urp_." She rumbles, compared to her previous squeaks, and barely aborts a reflexive 'desu'.

 I can't help but blink uncomprehendingly at her for a few seconds.

 What is she- wait. Range. Oh ancestors, she's an idiot!

 I meant, like, emotional _range_! Or the _scope_ to understand the forces at play here! But she thought I meant just fucking _**vocal range!**_

**This is why I don't like Nekomatas!**

 "Alright, the Maelstrom trucks are a minute out. Get ready." Jun says from the front seat.

 Motoko immediately slides the side door open, the cool air of Night City wafts in and chills my frame.

 "Motoko?" Jun whispers after his sister.

 "I'll be on the roof. I'll wait for you guys to start attacking before firing."

 Well I'll be damned. I was right about her having a good handle on tactics. One more eddie in the 'Lexington knows what he's _fucking_ talking about.' jar.

 Motoko moves Nekomata over her shoulder, coincidentally making her bump into me through her coat. "I can see you're not convinced.-" The rifle says to me with a cocky smirk. "-Feh, I'll give all the proof you need and more with **THIS**performance! Just wait. Heheh."

 …

 Uuugghhh. Such an idiot.

 Blessedly ignorant of my issues, Motoko once more proves she has the agility to chase down perps no matter where they run or climb. And soon enough we're perched atop a building, hiding within a splash of darkness behind a particularly bright sign advertising 'The Best yakitori this side of Night City'.

 Motoko pulls Nekomata from her back and calmly performs a few last-minute checks. Nekomata visibly holds back what are probably squeals of delight in order to not 'break character', ironically doing so anyway, and does some final checks of her own. Fluffing her hair, which tinkles like a wind chime, stretching her arms, that sort of pointless thing for a projection to be doing.

 And then Motoko twitches, her head cocking to one side slightly as the roar of engines bounces around the structures of Night City.

 A contrasting silence seems to fall over the area, the only sound being the shriek of engines getting louder and louder. Motoko eases the rifle into position and brings her eye up to the scope.

 Nekomata's projection goes dark, as if a shadow has passed over her. In her new dark clothing I can barely make her out.

 Then the roaring of engines reaches a sudden crescendo as a discordant ramble of trucks and vans veer around a distant corner and squeal to a stop in a semi-circle around the tyger claw shop. Motoko tracks them calmly with her rifle, her eyes narrowing and widening in a searching manner.

 If she were my partner, I would have thought she was scanning their rap sheets. But this 'somehow not a cyberpsycho' girl probably won't even bother. I bet she's just sizing up their loot. Such wasted potential…

 A hum of static electricity seems to build in the air, a palpable sense of anticipation as the first maelstrom boot steps out of a vehicle, quickly followed by a flood of thugs hooting and hollering in vile exhilaration, likely the adrenaline dump of their anticipated success making them delirious. Several of them fire off rounds into the air inconsequentially.

 Despite the rabble's noise, their sound seems muted somehow, distant, as if it were mere background noise to the true events about to unfold.

 A young maelstrom, probably barely into his teens but bearing an impressive amount of scars and chrome, approaches the shop's front with a swagger.

 Nekomata taps the metal knuckle of one of her gloves onto her skirt, making a steady and quick tempo to a song that only she can hear, for now.

 The borg boy's hand closes around the handle of the shop's door, and like a deep breath that the city itself had been holding being released, numerous things happen at once.

 The borg is shredded utterly by a hail of heavy machine gun fire that minces him into little more than bloody metal scraps in a few seconds, tearing apart the front door in the process. Nekomata's projection glows like a multitude of stage lights are pointing at her. The maelstrom still near their cars duck for cover as the bullets that just shredded their comrade begin punching holes into their rides, their return fire is wild, and only seems to destroy more of the shop's front, not at all deterring the continuous machine gun fire from inside. The barely audible sound of van doors sliding open rasp from the alleys across from the shop as loping shadows soon sprint across the open street into the maelstrom's flank. Nekomata opens her mouth.

 "**PA-PA PA PA YA!**" The scratching scream of guitars and synths reverberate from her as she begins to sing and dance while facing the street below.

 Motoko lets out a huff of frustration and cradles her rifle a little closer, sighting in on a lone maelstrom below who is facing the skittering shadows of doom approaching from behind.

**BANG!** Nekomata fires, coinciding with a discharge of fiery pyrotechnics below her projection. Nekomata doesn't seem to notice as Motoko's target loses his head in an instant. The once-living maelstrom drops to the ground bonelessly, his shotgun clatters down beside him, its projection wailing in anguish as its assumedly beloved owner perishes.

 The idol projection continues to dance as carnage unfolds, if she had been visible to human eyes, it would have highlighted Motoko's position to anyone in a multi-mile radius.

 Motoko sweeps her rifle around slowly, casting her gaze at the chaotic mass of violence, her attention shifts to a distracted kamikaze borg rhythmically beating a clearly flatlined corpse into the ground, his lips are barely visible from this distance, but a passing lipreading shows him chanting '_pa pa ya!'_ in time with Nekomata's tune.

 Odd… That can't be right, I must be rusty at reading lips.

 The distracted borg doesn't notice the maelstrom driver approaching him from behind, but Motoko's shooting is on point, and another pyrotechnical discharge sees the man popped open like a tin can and splattered across his own car.

 Okay Rookie, that's two shots. Time to move. We don't have the cover or concealment up here to stick around.

 Motoko quietly hums in satisfaction as she pans around for another target, unable to heed my warning. A flash of flame haralds another projectile, which decimates the metal skull of a full borg bulging with synthetic muscle, the maelstrom lieutenant too deep into a fight with his tyger claw mirror image to notice the high powered rifle pointing his way.

 That… Was a damn good shot. Motoko really needs to get better company. She's wasted on these thugs.

 Come on now then, we need to move. They might have zeroed us.

 I have just enough time to register a searing gaze glaring at us before our position is scattered with bullets, shards of concrete rain down on us as Motoko rolls to the side and ducks below the lip of the building.

 A pause in Nekomata's song allows a voice from below to pierce the shouting and gunfire, the clearly eastern language isn't one I know, but it is obvious they are singing along with Nekomata's song, perhaps completing a portion of it. A glance at said idol reveals her face is as surprised as my own. Motoko seems too distracted to notice.

 The singing borg only stops when one of his equally chromed comrades smacks him on the side of the head, seemingly dispelling whatever fugue had overtaken him.

 Nekomata's confusion doesn't stop her from picking up the chorus right after the odd addition, and the gunfire keeping us down stops just in time for Motoko to pop up, and pop off a shot at the offending shooter.

 For the first time so far, the shot goes wide, punching a hole into an engine block right where the target would have been.

 Motoko strides sideways along the roof, one of Nekomata's magazines drops to the floor with a clattering of metal, followed by a loud clack as another magazine takes its place.

 Motoko pauses and leans in. One last shot into the side of the vehicle has no obvious effect, but the subtle spray of blood behind the van alludes to a successful hit.

 Motoko scans for another target, but the fighting is clearly winding down, as is Nekomata's song.

 As the final high note of the rifle's voice echoes out, Motoko falls into a more easy pose as the last maelstrom keels over, dead.

 The nonexistent lights illuminating Nekomata fade. "That was weird…" She mutters, then immediately twirls on one foot to face me, her eyes arrogant and smug. "How's _**that**_ for range! I bet you feel _pretty foolish_ right about now. Heheheh." She looks away while running her index finger along the space under her nose.

 Range? … Oh, right. I forgot about that in all this excitement. "...You're an idiot. And you still have no fans."

 One of her eyes twitches furiously as her dark outfit fades and returns to her usual attire. "Yyyooouuu!" She squeaks, her voice back to usual as well. "I'll have you know that my fans are very much _real_ and very supportive, and friendly! Mochi-Kun, Faber-Nyan, and everyone else **do exist! And-and-and I won't let you talk badly about them, you****愚かな老豚警察!!!**(Stupid old pig police)"

 I stare blankly at her as she breathes heavily for a time, then her words seem to catch up to her and she covers her mouth in embarrassment.

 "_Bad words-desu…_" She mutters despondently. Visibly disappointed in herself.

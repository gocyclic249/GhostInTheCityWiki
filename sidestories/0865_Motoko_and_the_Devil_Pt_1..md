 "The Onryo is here! Spread out! Let nothing escape!"

 The words echoed out through the old building and I just rolled my eyes. I wiggled the gum that I'd been chewing on around a bit. I was tempted to blow a bubble, but my sense of stealth wouldn't allow it…

 I'd do it before I killed the last of these ones.

 Honestly, stuff like this made me miss Night City. As shameful as it might sound, there was a level of quality in Night City Gangs, that was just missing here in Tokyo.

 I had already slipped into their cyberware. Their eyes were mine, literally. Stealth was just so much easier when your enemies very eyes told you exactly what they saw.

 Not that I needed that.

 I slipped out of the office, the data gathered, and without a care I walked right down the metal stairs into the warehouse proper.

 Right past the first dumb gonk, walking around with a Satara, thinking that would do me in.

 He died swiftly. 

 "Wha!? Yuto!?" The voice of his friends screeched out, as to their eyes their friend had just been walking along when suddenly he'd been stabbed.

 Man, I really wanted my Sandy.

 Sandy sandy, super sandy. I wanted to humm the little song as I walked up to the next one, and just grabbed his shaky hands. A quick twist and his Unity was pointed up, and I just helped him press the trigger.

 The loud bang and blood shooting up was dodged as easily as breathing by flowing around a cargo container.

 I considered getting the rest, but…

 I already had like a dozen levels up stored, in case I finally got some better chrome. So there was a small part of me that was saying…

 Kill was… Wrong?

 I mean that was obviously incorrect, but I didn't need to wipe out this little riverpunk street gang that ran out of the Tokyo warehouses, I wasn't even sure if they had a name… Probably, all gangs had a name.

 But… I couldn't just leave. I weakly played with the gum in my mouth, I'd promised to blow a bubble and do a cool final death kill on the last one, and my urge to do cool stuff was kinda important.

 What would Hiromi-

 No. I shook that thought off put it in a box and destroyed it.

 Instead I blew a bubble a bit, and walked over. While they were freaking out I stuck the bubbled Bubblegum into the back of a guys head.

 There.

 That was cool, and when they saw it, they'd freak out. I can go now.

 —--

 "Data." I spoke up and I watched the Man go completely still.

 "Ah… You gathered what I requested?"

 "Mhmm. No problem, easy job in fact, only one thing. Why did a bunch of gonk street brats know that I would be there?"

 "Someone must have heard about the gig." He replied, his formal Japanese getting a little rough, as I rose up. He'd been sitting behind his stupid little seiza desk, and hadn't noticed me until I spoke up.

 "That's interesting, because… I didn't tell anyone about that data. I only told you, that I'd found it, and would retrieve it… No one else could have known."

 "Someone must have set a trap."

 "Hmmm. Yes. I believe they did." I whispered in the little Fixers ear. Then without warning I stabbed the knife down right next to his hand, right through the sleeve of his Kimono, his own knife. The one I'd slipped out from the hidden spot he kept it in near his stomach.

 That had taken a few moments to properly slip out, so I was fairly proud of it.

 "I… Will ensure it does not happen again."

 "Oh I know it won't." I said coldly, ice practically slipping out from between my lips. "There will be no second trap." Then like a good batman, I disappeared as he was talking. I was still listening of course, I just slipped away.

 His Cameras of course would show that I was never there in the first place.

 Heh. The poor bastard was still trying to install new hardware, or updates to get an actual picture of me.

 But this was weird. First time this guy had set something up for me, he was normally much more reliable…

 So of course while he was freaking out, I was browsing through his system. He had sent a bunch of brats after me. I'd worked with him enough, and Japan had a thing with Ninjas no one should have bothered me, so why?

 There was nothing at first, until I checked his actual cameras. A few seconds browsing through the equivalent of hours of data, and I had it.

 Arasaka? Why did Arasaka deliver a letter, like an actual paper letter to the low rank fixer, I'd chosen to help out in Japan mostly because he was too weak to be a threat to me, and give myself something to do?

 I turned around, and while the poor guy was calling his security team, I was back in his office, his actual office, not the japanese style room he used to greet guests. 

 The letter didn't take long for me to find, but…

 This didn't make any sense.

 —--

 "You are slow." The words hissed out at me, and I just rolled my eyes. Then I brought my hand up and started opening and closing it to show just how little I thought about this 'ninja's' yapping.

 His optics narrowed and his Sandevistan cycled.

 Bitch. I'd had a top of the line Sandy. I knew all the little tricks, and frankly, for all his ego, this little bitch was a wannabe. Relying on Cybernetics.

 I was a fucking Kensei.

 His Mantis blades flashed out, buzzing with energy and I just floated through his strikes, my Katana wasn't even unsheathed, only using it to gently nudge Wannabe's blades out of alignment.

 Then I prepared my moment. The funny thing about Mantis Blades, quite often the Blade parts had holes in them. They were designed to fit into the body after all, there were limits, weaknesses.

 I struck, as Wannabe thrust both blades forward, a twist of my sheathed blade, and then I moved in a blur. The crappy Dynalar Sandevistan I had equipped gave me just enough extra speed.

 My blade fell, and the battle was over.

 "Your mask is stupid, and you suck." I informed the Wannabe Ninja bluntly. My Katana stuck point down into the concrete, having slipped in between the holes in the Ninjas Mantis blades. Locking his arms in front of him crossed.

 "Wha?"

 "Now." I leveled my Burya lined up right on that stupid glowing Arasaka Symbol on his forehead. "Why is Arasaka trying to track me down?" 

 "I will tell you-"

 "Enough." The voice came out over the loud speaker, and I hummed. Did I recognize that voice? I think I did. "Oda. Stand down."

 "Oda?" I whispered, I looked at Wannabe. Wasn't he the ninja boy from the game. Wait, that meant.

 "I am Hanako. I would like to speak to you. Please, do not harm my bodyguard, he was simply acting to protect me.."

 The heavy japanese was formal, and a bit gentle, and… What? Why me? I wasn't V? Well I mean V was still working Counter Intel at this time. Waking up a second time after death in Japan, had been a shock, and I was kinda thinking about heading back to Night City soon, but I mean… Would I be there? Would-

 I shook off the mental confusion. 

 "Hanako… Arasaka, is the one that is trying to track me down?" I asked the Ninja boy, and then scoffed at his stupid mask. "Take that stupid thing off. Get a helmet that doesn't light up your forehead dumbass." I said, slipping into English which I liked a lot more as I reached up and flipped the stupid mask helmet right off and tossed it away.

 Then I turned and headed for the door that was now open. Light silhoueting the dark room, I'd just been in.

 The letter had been simple. An address and my new name. Onryo. 

 I had an actual name. Still Motoko which was great, but my last name was some lame stuff. So I picked Kusanagi, and just didn't tell anyone. 

 Since I was sticking to the real ninja shit this time.

 Walking inside I was kinda surprised. This whole place was definitely a set up. The dark room I'd just been in, had been basically a battle room. Heavy walls, armor everywhere.

 But in here, it was a nice little Japanese tea room, only incredibly expensive. Every line, every item in here could have bought the old Apartment Jun and I lived in a thousand times over.

 I once more took memories Jun and slotted them in a box and dumped them.

 I wasn't Motoko, Juns little sister anymore.

 I wasn't Motoko Hiromi's friend.

 That Motoko was dead, and fuck whatever god had sent me to the same world but still so far from my old life.

 I was just Onryo now. Well Motoko Kusanagi still, but whatever.

 Hanako Arasaka. She looked just like she did in 2077. She was also completely untrustworthy, and I was sorta low key considering just assassinating her for the lulz.

 "You went to a lot of effort to get me here." I finally said, as I plopped into the little japanese cushion across from her. Sitting cross legged instead of seiza, cause it always seemed to annoy the fancy types. 

 "I did. I have been searching for you, for some time." 

 "Riiight." I drawled out, in english. 

 "You are Motoko Izumi."

 "Hmm." I whispered, cause that was annoying. I thought I'd done a pretty good job of clearing my old data.

 "Your mother was Sanda Izumi."

 Oh great, was this some revenge scheme?

 "Listen lady. I don't know what you want, but can you get to the point? You made me burn a fixer that was actually fairly reliable, and now instead of relaxing with a bowl of Ramen and the newest anime, I'm ninja sword fighting a wannabe, and talking with you."

 "Your father is Yorinobu Arasaka."

 "Crazy bitches say what?"

 —--

 This is definitely because I teased Hayato until she'd get all ready and nearly cry about being a princess.

 There was no other explanation. The mother in this life had fucked Yorinobu Arasaka. 

 Gross.

 Hanako had been fairly confident, and while I'd struggling to allow the doctor that entered to take some of my blood…

 I mean I was curious!

 I'd died twice, and woken up both times, so who knows if I was related to evil.

 Turns out I was.

 "The information is confirmed. Arasaka Motoko-Sama, is the daughter of Yorinobu-Sama."

 "Well fuck me." I couldn't help but say.

 Hanako was, I noticed, not impressed with my mouth.

 That was gonna be funny to play with later.

 I was an Arasaka.

 Fuuuuuuck.

 Eventually Wannabe boy had come back in, and only once the information was offered did his glaring at me stop as he dropped.

 "This one bared his blade against a member of the Arasaka family. My life is forfeit."

 "That will not be needed."

 "Yeah bro, you'd have needed to actually be able to touch me to call it baring your blade." I commented cause that was super cringe.

 He twitched but stayed bowed until Hanako spoke.

 "Rise Oda. I asked this of you. There is no fault."

 "Yes Hanako-Sama."

 Oh yeah that guy was totally in love with his mistress.

 What a loser. 

 "I am sure you have many questions." Hanako finally spoke having been watching me with those golden eyes. 

 "I understand the process of fucking, I'm trying not to think about it." I said bluntly, and I watched as she twitched.

 "You will have much to learn." She offered, and I scoffed. 

 "Doubt it. I'm not the biddable type. Something you should know since you tracked me down."

 "Yes. The Onryo. The Assassin."

 "I'm an infiltration specialist, with a side of extraction, Assassination work is messy. You gotta pay me extra for that."

 "You are Araska, you will not need to take such work again."

 "Boring. Besides, if that guy is the level of your bodyguards, I'll have to just guard myself." I once again took a potshot at the Wannabe, and I watched him flinch.

 That… Was never going to get old.

 "You defeated my Bodyguard in single combat. You are… Exceptional. Father will be very impressed with your Bladework."

 I met the woman's eyes and openly scoffed.

 "Yeah sure."

 —---

 "What is she like?"

 "Crass… She reminds me of you when you were with your little gang."

 "Tsk."

 "But dangerous. In a way… I sent you the information."

 "Yes. Onryo."

 "The information I managed to gather had no idea of her true skill. She defeated Oda. Easily."

 "Your fool of a Bodyguard? She is what, fourteen? Pathetic."

 "Yes, and she restrained him without drawing her blade until the final movement. I will send you the recordings."

 "No… I wish to see her first with my own eyes."

 "Very well Nii-Sama."

 "Hanako… I wonder if it would not have been better for you not to try and find her."

 "She is my niece. She deserves to be with her family."

 "Deserves? It might end up a great punishment."

 "I will see you soon Nii-Sama."

 "And you." 

 I tapped my finger against my knee. Listening in to secure conversations? Easy.

 Doing it without Oda the Wannabe bothering me?

 I smirked. Also easy.

 It had just been a call to… My Father? God that was gonna be weird. I was used to Jun, but I didn't do father figures.

 Don't think of Vik, don't think of Vik.

 Or mother figures.

 Don't think of Wakako, don't think of Wakako.

 Yep. I didn't do that thing. I was a wild animal that didn't need no parental supervision.

 Would it be funny to disappear? I mean, they found me this time, because I went looking for them, I could just walk out of this building, and be back into a digital Ghost…

 But… I mean, I was curious! This whole situation is super weird. 

 I was an Arasaka. The crazy bajillionaire evil people faction.

 Eh, at the least I can use them to pick up some better chrome.

 Relying on a few of the better back alley Rippers here in Japan had been… Well most of them survived the meeting.

 Most of them.

 I miss all my Chrome. Waking up basically naked had been a real slap in the face.

 I rubbed my fingers, actual fingers, flesh and blood together. 

 They should be chrome, but they weren't.

 Whatever, back to pretending to go to the bathroom, since Hanako was coming back.

 —--

 "You should change into the Kimono. I assure you it will fit."

 "No, and that's the third time you've mentioned it. Your taking me to see the old man. Not my old man, but yours, the Emperor himself."

 "You will call him Honored Grandfather. He wishes to meet you."

 I didn't agree, so since I didn't agree, I could say whatever I wanted. I very pointedly didn't get changed either despite Hanako's arguing. My street killer Leotard Cyberpunk Chic was my own, and I wasn't going to get changed for anyone except people I loved or liked.

 I'll dress up for Hayato, or Hiromi, but no one else. Something I'd learned after one too many bullshit Rockerboy photoshoots.

 Finally the AV landed. As the door opened I took in the scene. 

 The estate was far out in the mountains, and it was huge.

 Like, an entire city huge.

 I noticed the security, the help, and just everyone. The place was quiet, but not empty. Yet it was silent other than the AV's exhaust.

 I took it in for a bit following Hanako as she moved, as Oda followed after us, shadowing his mistress.

 Yet as we crossed the entrance I felt it. Electronic alarms.

 "Apologies Hanako-Sama. There are unauthorized weapons detected." The first guard offered bowing deeply, and then Hanako looked at me.

 "You will have to disarm, do not be afraid, you are in no danger."

 "Pfft. I am the danger." I scoffed and walked over to a random fancy looking table that already had some expensive crap on it.

 So I started unloading. Burya, Kenshin in the small of my back, knife. Knife. Knife. Knife. Knife.

 —-

_Hanako Arasaka._

 That… Is a lot of knives.

 Wait is that a grenade!?

 —--

 "Don't move this by the way, this sucker is homemade, it's got a bit of a temper." I explained to the guards who I wanted to pout out, he didn't even flinch away!

 Everyone flinched away when you put the words homemade, next to explosive.

 Then on the little Buddha statue that looked expensive I placed my Katana in his lap.

 "Satisfied?"

 "The alarm is still detecting weapons."

 I blinked. Had I forgotten something? I patted myself down, nothing in my Leotard, nothing in the old boob pocket. I totally had enough boobs to hide something there, I just didn't… That's why!

 "Oh right." I brought my boot up and worked the heel pulling out the plastic explosive I'd hidden up there. Stolen that from some bastard a few weeks back. Totally forgot about it. "Never know when you might need some C-6!" I chirped and placed it on the Buddha's lap.

 Totally safe there.

 "Very good."

 "Is… All of that necessary?"

 "All of what?" I asked looking at my pile of weapons. "That's a light load, since I was sneaking around Arasaka stuff tonight. I didn't even bring my long guns, my snipers. Still need to get an Uragan again. You just never know when you need to shoot an AV out of the sky."

 Hanako nodded, in acceptance at my words.

 Right. God this was still so weird. 

 That was Hanako Arasaka. The woman that fucks over V.

 The one that was okay with her father body snatching her brother.

 Man this family is fucked up.

 We continued on

 More security screenings, but none of them touched Hanako, and they seemed like they wanted to scrub me down to nothings and explore every orifice, but Hanako moved us along, and Oda nodded to them allowing my movement.

 Then we were through and there he was.

 An old man practically half dead already. One eye rheumy, nearly white the other just clear enough to glare.

 "Father." Hanako offered bowing as she entered.

 I just stared, he stared. But as I stared I used the corner of my eyes to take in the security. Takemura, the bastard stood to the side, and watched me with careful eyes. And there was more. Electronic security. The good stuff. The stuff that would fry a Netrunner just by looking at it.

 The one piece of chrome I had managed to really get back up to max out my stats had been my netrunning stuff. 

 Even with my Netrunning max out, I… Well, if I needed to, I could, but not quietly.

 "Sit." Saburo ordered, and Hanako nodded, instantly obeying.

 The fuck do I look like I dog?

 Even with all his security, even with Takemura… I could kill him.

 His own blade was right there, that would be some nice irony. I could probably even survive a bit even if Takemura decided to go after me.

 "Motoko. Your Grandfather asked you to sit. Come introduce yours-"

 "Quiet." Saburo growled, his eye on me. "The girl is thinking. Well girl? Have you decided?"

 "If I wanted you dead, you'd already be dead before you could ask me that. So I guess I don't want you dead that much. The night is young though." I commented blandly, and then walked over and flopped down in the seat. "I guess that means this is a nice to meet you Gramps. I'm Motoko."

 The room was quiet for a time as Saburo took me in.

 "Insolence."

 "Yeah, but most people just call me Motoko."

 This was going great.

Medical Interning!​

 Once I realized that I earned Technical Ability exp off of doing things like first-aid and other medical actions on people, mainly by patching up Jun whenever he got hit, I realized that I needed to grind it like crazy since being able to treat injuries was crazy useful. And I had the perfect teacher to help make that happen!

 "...and remember to-" Vik started to say before I interrupted him.

 "Be professional," I repeated for what felt like the hundredth time. "I got it, Vik! Its not hard being nice."

 "Heh, in Night City most people might disagree," Vik laughed at the dark joke before turning serious. "I don't doubt you can be nice, but you can be a little... _much_ at first for most folk."

 "Hey! I'm adorable as fuck!" I countered.

 "Not arguing that, and you certainly learn fast, you might just become a med tech at this rate and take over _my_ job, but you have to understand that most folk come to a ripperdoc over a regular cyber clinic cause they either they don't have the eddies or are chipping things that are less than legal." Vik explained. "That means most folk might be turned off by your... Motokoness."

 "Don't turn my name into an adjective!"

 "Hehe, see, just like that," Vik said before hearing some unfamiliar footsteps step into his clinic. He looked up to see a customer, just a regular citizen of Night City based on a first glance judging by the lack of gang symbols and only carrying a basic sidearm, and opened up with a wave. "Welcome stranger, how might I help you today?"

 "Hey doc, hand's acting up," the man said, lifting up his left cyberhand that he was holding up to his side and... yup, that definitely looked very broken. Totally crushed in truth.

 "Sit down and lets look it over then," Vik said while gesturing to the ripperchair in question.

 "Wow, did you stick your hand in a compactor or something?" Motoko asked as she looked at their patient's hand in shock while Vik prepared his tools.

 "Motoko..." Vik admonished his 'nurse/assistant', her words.

 "Yeah, actually," the man admitted sheepishly. "Work out of one of the recycling plants on the edge of the city, shorting through the dump for stuff to break down and such, and accidently got my hand caught in one of the machines. Thank fuck my buddies hit the kill switch before it took my arm and maybe my whole body with it."

 "I'll say," Vik said as he picked at the busted hand. "This is totally wrecked, gonna need a replacement."

 "Aw great," the man said with a sigh. "Don't get the eddies for a decent hand. You gotta low-tech claw or something to hold me off?"

 "Better," Vik said with a smile as he went over to the side of the clinic where he kept the 'repurposed' cyberlimbs and other cyberwear Motoko kept bringing over from her 'usual raids' of various Scav dens. "Got a used cyberhand in stock, blue-collar model too, and can sell it cheap since nothing fancy about it if you like."

 "Really doc?! You're a lifesaver!" the man said with a smile as Vik simply waved him off with a grin.

 But before he could become the installation process Motoko interrupted.

 "Vik! Vik!" Motoko shook his shoulder. "Let me do this one! Please! It's nothing complicated or anything!"

 "Well... you okay with it?" Vik questioned his patient.

 "Ahh, can she do it?" was his respond.

 "She's a fast learner, and as she said a straight replacement should be too hard since the attachment point wasn't damaged." Vik explained. "I'll be right here in case anything goes wrong."

 "Oh... well I guess its okay then?" the man said. "Young ones gotta get hands on experience eventually, right?"

 "Awesome! I'll get my chopper!" Motoko yelled in joy as she pushed the button on the ripperchair that suddenly strapped in their patient as she ran off to toolbox.

 "The what?" the suddenly nervous man said as he pulled on the straps a bit.

 "Its a 'trick' she learned, but don't freak out or anything-" Vik tried to explain before Motoko returned.

 "Found it!" Motoko said while holding up a new cyberhand in one hand... and a large meat cleaver in the other while sporting a slightly demented smile. "Now hold still!" she said as she stalked towards the patient, whom had wide eyes and visibly sweating.

 "Wait wait! I change my-" but before he could finished Motoko got to work.

[In a split second](https://youtu.be/j7vyLEL10dM?t=138) she was swinging her cleaver in a blur at the patient's hand before holding the broken cyberhand where the new one once sat in hers, and the new one was freshly installed.

 The man could only blink in shock as Vik unhooked the straps and checked over the new hand while he was testing it out.

 "Holy shit... how did she do that?" was his shocked response.

 "I got _really good hands,"_ Motoko said with a smug smile as she held up the broken hand. "Mind if I break this down for parts, Vik?"

 "Go right ahead," Vik replied as he finished checking over the new hand, no problems, and heard his 'nurse/assistant' give off a little 'yeah!' before skipping over to the desk to break down the cyberhand for parts.

_"..._ is she alright in the head?" the patient asked in concern.

 "Naw, she is just being her usual Motokoness," Vik stated.

 "I told you to stop using my name as an adjective!" Motoko called out from where she was working.

 "Stop being impossibly yourself and then we'll talk," Vik fired back with a smirk.

 "Never!" was Motoko declaration.

 Vik may never understand his newest 'student', but he couldn't help but admit her being around did make the clinic more fun.

**Another Face in the Audience**

 I was there to get wasted, laugh at whatever amateurs were playing that night, and maybe be pleasantly surprised by the band I'd heard was getting scouted by a producer looking for the next big thing. _Maybe._

 I didn't have high hopes for the experience. So I confidently paid no attention to the unknown band led by a teenage frontwoman when something better flitted on by. The woman in front of me though? _Damn_. Those hips did not lie. I figured she had to be some Eurocorpo slumming it in the Americas. Probably looking to spend her parents money and trash her half a million dollar biosculpt, and for a bad boy to do it with.

 I confess I'd given up on listening to the music at first. Even when the first song the band played was a little familiar (I later remembered that I'd heard it on the radio). It was Amélie who sauntered across the scuffed black and white vinyl checkerboard floor to get closer to the electronic smoothness of whatever song the live band was playing. And damn did I love to watch her go. I followed a step behind.

 The sound of a gun cocking filled the room. My hand went to my gun on instinct as I scanned- was the girl on stage cocking a fucking carnage? She plucked the live round out of the air and tossed it into the crowd.

 Now that I was thinking with my upper head, I started to pay attention to the band. _Stand Alone Complex, eh?_ Most of the group was just alright. Decent musically and clearly into their performance, but I was pretty sure I caught them flubbing a couple times as they got too into it. I'd seen their type before. Good enough to play and get gigs. But not good enough to make it big or go viral.

 But not the girl in the center with the big ass shotgun. She had that stage on lock. Star power dripped from every crooned syllable. I was pretty sure she was catching and adapting her performance to her bandmates almost before they knew what they would do, and despite being a no-name artist she had everyone in room eating out of the palm of her hand in moments. Shit was unreal.

 When the lights cut for a moment after _Heathens_, Amélie turned to face me in the dim light. "What a stunning performance, non?"

 While I would've agreed with anything she said on the hope it would get those French words whispering right into my ear, this I agreed with wholeheartedly. "Fuck yeah. It's nova. 'S that girl in the center."

 "She really does own the stage," Amélie said admiringly. "It's, ahh, _je nais se quoi_."

 We went quiet as the drums kicked back in. The guitars followed. I felt my foot tapping to rhythm with a mind of its own. I watched the lights play over the crowd and studied the band with a critical eye. _They're going to make it big_, I suddenly realized with certainty as Amélie and I roared the chorus of _This Fffire_ back at the stage its second time through.

 We smiled at each other after. Amélie a little sheepish. Me widely.

 "The Pretender," the rockergirl crooned. Like she could be called anything else. That level of vocal control was fucking mental for a teenager. It wasn't chrome. Only the military-grade MediaCorp stuff could perform like _this_, and those implants wouldn't be playing at a dive like this.

 The emotions in her voice were so intense, I swore the rockergirl was an inch from tearing off the stage and scrawling her name across the face of Arasaka Tower in neon graffiti. And hell, half this crowd might've followed her and they'd known her for fifteen minutes.

 I was already checking my agent to see if there was a public release for these songs. Rockergirl's chrome hands plucked away at the strings tirelessly as I searched.

 "We've got another song for you tonight. Do you think you're ready for it?" She asked with a self-satisfied amusement and held the mic out to us.

 Amélie cupped her hands around her mouth and screamed with everyone else in the room. "YEAH!"

 "Yeah you sound ready. But you aren't." She smiled evilly. Eyes twinkling with charismatic mischief. "This is Faint," she whispered.

 Electronic noise ground away at the intro. The drummer hit his instruments so hard and fast that his lit-up clothes left streaks in my eyes. This time the second guitarist took the lead with the vocals in a rap verse that was yet _another_ new sound.

 "Face away and pretend that I'm not!" the taller singer's synthesized vocals filled with excitement as the star of the show dropped her guitar and let it hang from its strap. "But I'll be here, cause you're all that I've got..."

 Rockergirl gripped the mic tight in both hands as she bounced to the front and _screamed._ "I CAN'T FEEL, THE WAY I DID BEFORE!" The force of the change swept over the room like a blast wave. She dropped to her knees, and the crowd surged back in with more energy than I'd thought possible. The rap verse returned once again, this time with rockergirl ad-libbing. They fed off each other's energy and the crowd did too and _fuck_ my voice hurt from cheering so loud. I flagged the bartender for another beer.

 The rap verse all too quickly reached the same line that had ended the last verse. I could feel the excitement and joy and rage cascade off rockergirl like a waterfall as they took center stage once again. The song got quiet for just a moment, then the metal screams returned.

 She tore through the same lines as before. I only caught at the last second how the instruments didn't signal that the part was over. Rockergirl found another gear. "NOOOOOOOOOO! Hear me out now! You're gonna listen to me, like it or not, RIGHT NOW! HEAR ME OUT NOW!"

 There was another quiet section, and I realized in almost religious awe that somehow that it was building to yet another peak and that crazy rockergirl had even more to give. "Don't turn your back on me, **I WON'T BE IGNOOOOOOOORED**!"

 I'd never seen a bar crowd this into a music act before. This was the kind of feeling you got at rock concerts in sold out stadiums. I'd never heard anyone perform a fry scream like that. Not a single song showed the signs of a new band's immature composition. Rockergirl thrust the mic into the air with one hand like a gold trophy. The stage lights reflected off her arms. The crowd stared up at her like supplicants.

 As soon as it was clear they weren't going to be playing another song, Amélie turned to me. "That was _amazing_." She stepped in close as the radio turned back on, putting that fatally sinful French accent right up against my pierced earlobe. "What do you say we find some of our own excitement?"

 I downed my drink, feeling it soothe my aching throat. I grabbed her hand and left the bar. I lifted her onto the back of my bike and grinned when her arms wrapped around my waist. _I have no fucking idea who you are rockergirl, but you just made a fan for life._ And I'd bet I wasn't the only one who felt that way.

**JUNCHIRO KUSANAGI**​

---

I walked into the club cautiously.  
   
 It had been a gamble, coming here alone. That night in the conapt, when I'd first met her, had been a surprise. The emotions were... conflicting. She was an older woman, but that maturity had brought with it an assured, experienced confidence. A warrior Mox, a faction diametrically opposed to mine own. It felt wrong, on several levels. I wasn't ready to love again, so soon after. The wound was so fresh still, despite my distraction with the imouto. I hadn't really processed it, even now.  
   
 I was not one for a quick tumble, as Akari called it. Love was meant to be tender, a connection of souls. Not meaningless lust, like so many sought. I hardly knew her, the sum of our interactions summarized with a single encounter. So many conflicting things, but in the end, excuses. So why had I come here? I wasn't sure. Perhaps I had hoped to see her smile, hear her laugh again. Foolish, and yet...  
   
 She wasn't working the door. The looks I got nearly made me turn away, but I walked in still, back straight. I would not be cowed, for I was the Oni. I made my way through the parlour, and into the bar lounge. Not really different from the many nightclubs in Night City, and I carefully wound my way through the packed dance floor and toward the bar.  
   
 A man in a pink splattered, blue shirt was working it, mixing and slinging drinks. He was nodding in sympathy to one particular patron, a tall man with a Kiroshi monovisor, tech-beard and mohawk, "It's fuckin' bad, choom. You don't know what it's like." He had a look about him, so I immediately made a threat assessment. Long arms, with longer fingers indicated extensive modifications, likely for agility. This was reinforced by the tall, nearly skeletal build. Who exactly was this gonk? I could probably overpower him, but you could never tell. Blades or other weapons could be hidden, and pop-out at a moment's notice.  
   
 There was a saying about telescoping blades, after all.  
   
 The bartender seemed at ease, so I took a cautious seat nearby, listening with one ear as I waited to be served. The bartender glanced at me, and offered a smile, "I do, I've known both of you for a while. What can I get you, choomba?" His last words were addressed to me.  
   
 Drinking was a bad idea, especially here, especially since the last time Motoko was here, she apparently trashed the place, "Just a NiCola, thanks. You don't happen to know where Rita is, do you?"  
   
 He quickly popped the tab, and emptied half the can into a ball glass filled with ice, sliding the pair over to me. A last gesture topped the drink with a slice of synth lemon, and a little umbrella shaped like a sword. Cute. I flicked him the eddies, and took a sip, glancing out across the packed lounge again. No sign of her, but the bartender answered my question in the same breath, "She's up top on her break. You want me to ping her for you?"  
   
 I quickly shook my head, "No, no, it's fine." What would I even say? Hello, I came to say hello? No, that was stupid. She was a preem older woman, I had to play it smooth. Make it seem like it was a passing coincidence. Less like a cougar hunt, and more of a Tyger hunt? Yes, I had to seem interested, but not too interested. Sometimes, my genius surprised even me.  
   
 The other patron continued, a whining tone in his voice, "Thanks, but ya really don't. She doesn't have a single domestic bone in her body. Who do you think does all the cleaning and shopping? Me."  
   
 I found myself nodding along, "Trash everywhere, can barely bring girls over..."  
   
 He slapped the table for emphasis, "Yes! Finally, someone understands! Just piles of fuckin' half eaten kibble, and empty boxes of ammo. Little sister?"  
   
 I nodded with a bit more force, "A real daymare, no scop. She keeps trying to get herself flatlined, and it feels like a full time job just keeping her alive."  
   
 He downed his drink and called for a refill, "Tell me about it. And the guns, the maintenance..."  
   
 I thought back to the conapt, and the Tactician sitting in the shower. "Yeah, obsessed. I caught her making grenades the other week."  
   
 He scoffed and shook his head, "You want a drink? Feels like NiCola ain't enough."  
   
 I shook my head no, "Not a good idea, maybe. She got thrown out last time she was here, for getting drunk and hacking the entertainment architecture. Don't wanna give the family a bad name."  
   
 He raised an eyebrow, "No shit? Obsessed with guns, makes grenades, a 'runner too? Sounds like one hell of a headache." He jabbed a thumb over his shoulder, towards one of the lounges, where a small form was twitching under a BD wreath, "At least she found something she liked in the catalogue so I can get a break." He offered a long fingered hand, "Pilar."  
   
 I took it, "Jun."  
   
 He held my grip a moment longer, but it was difficult to tell where he was looking with the monovisor, "Claw?"  
   
 My neck tensed. Beef? My eyes flicked towards the girl he'd indicated previously, then back to the monovisor, "Problem?"  
   
 His leer was disarming, "Nah, not less you make it. You hear how the Devil of Kabuki fell out a window a while back?"  
   
 I rolled my eyes as he released my hand, "Another nightmare, but vacuums are often filled before anyone takes notice. It is of little concern to your average banger." I took a sip of the NiCola, and wondered if it was too late to ask for something stronger. Maybe it would give me a bit of courage to confront the bouncer. I leapt into Maelstrom holdouts with no fear. Why was one Mox so much of a problem?  
   
 Pilar nodded knowingly, "Ain't that the truth. The rest of us beg for scraps, while the top plays their games. Real vampires, ja neh?"  
   
 "Hai." A cycle of blood and violence.  
   
 We spent a few more minutes in amicable silence, before a familiar voice broke me from my thoughts.  
   
 "Jun?" A shiver ran up my spine, bumps prickling at Real-Skinn. An automated reaction, both biological and efficiently simulated. I turned towards her, blinking, as a faint smile pulled at my lips.  
   
 "Oh hey, Rita. Didn't catch you at the door." Gonk. Of course she knew that.  
   
 "Was on the roof." She didn't seem bothered though, as she pointed upward, "Hacking a dart, before I got back into it."  
   
 "Right." I nodded absently, "Wanted to say sorry about Motoko again, she can be a handful."  
   
 "I believe it." She took a seat at the bar beside me, "Mateo, a NiCola for me too? One of the cherry ones."  
   
 I'd have to keep that preference in mind. She had quite the style, kitchy. Maybe I could do a subtle match on some of my chrome? See if she made a comment on it, go from there?  
   
 "Hey Rita." Pilar was strangely closer than he had been before, attention fixed on the bouncer, "Wanna ditch work and go party?"  
   
 My opinion of him dropped significantly. Rita seemed to be used to it, because she rolled her eyes and shook her head, "Maybe when you grow up. You still flailing around?"  
   
 An affronted expression crossed his face, "I'll have you know we're gettin' ready for a big gig. Got an Afterlife inviation, and everythin'!" He jabbed a finger at his bare chest, "Gonna be a Legend, just you -" Whatever he had meant to say wasn't finished, as he was bowled over by a screaming ball of fury.  
   
 "What the, get the fuck off me, you goddamn gremlin!" He managed to fight off the smaller woman, pushing her to arms length, and I finally managed to get a good look at her.  
   
 Barely 5 feet tall, and dressed in an enveloping combat jacket that didn't leave much to the imagination below it. Corpse pale chem skin, shift-tacts, neon hair in a pair of tails. She was out of it, eyes wild and unseeing, "R-rip..." her breathing was ragged, as if she'd just run a marathon, and I could see flecks of foamy saliva bubbling at the corner of her mouth, "T-tear..."  
   
 Fuck, was she having some sort of episode? Luckily, there weren't any weapons nearby, but that didn't seem to dissuade her. Pilar turned towards her, holding her back with one arm, while gesturing with the other, "You see what I have to deal with? Every fuckin' d-" Once more, he got cut off, as the woman nimbly contorted herself around him, and threw him through a table with a perfect suplex.  
   
 Rita and I got up at that, as the situation rapidly spiralled out of control, "Huh, pretty wild."  
   
 "You need a hand?" I wasn't outfitted with a combat load, but I could take a small woman with my chrome.  
   
 She waved me off, "Nah, I've dealt with Becca's episodes before. She'll run out of steam soon, once she lays into him a bit more, and then I can talk her down."  
   
 As if to drive the point home, her words were emphasized by Becca grabbing a bar stool, and repeatedly bringing it down on her apparently comatose brother. Each strike was punctuated by a cry of fury. With each strike, she seemed to slow down, breathing deeper as she blinked in confusion.  
   
 "Alright, here we go." Rita winked at me, "Trust me, I got this." I nodded, hesitant, but I was ready to step in to assist. Then again, she'd taken down Pilar without breaking a sweat, and I was having Motoko flashbacks. Rita took a slow step forward, arms spread in a peaceful gesture, "Becca calm down, it's done. We're all chooms here."  
   
 "Until it's done?" Some semblance of sanity had returned to those yellow-pink eyes. What the fuck had she been watching?  
   
 "That's right, down girl. You look thirsty, want a NiCola?" She gestured to Mateo, who was watching with unrestrained interest.  
   
 The woman wiped her face with both hands, groaning, "Gimme a Broseph. Who the fuck made that BD?"  
   
 "It was Judy. You wanna talk to her?" Rita's voice was calm, as she talked down the psychotic gremlin. BD? Oh no.  
   
 "Sure, sure. I meant who was it about, the person in it." Rita's eyes betrayed me, and the little monster turned her optics towards me.  
   
 My arms made an X shape over my chest, as if they would somehow ward her off, "It wasn't me."  
   
 Her eyes narrowed in turn, "No, it wasn't. But *you* know who it was."  
   
 I raised an eyebrow, "What makes you so certain?"  
   
 She snorted, "A woman's intuition is shrewd, cutie." Her leer was eerily reminiscent of her brother's, "Or should I call you Oni-chan?". I felt a sudden dread.  
   
 Rita stepped in front of me, "Jun, run." Brave, beautiful, Rita Wheeler. We would meet again, that was a promise.  
   
 I ran.

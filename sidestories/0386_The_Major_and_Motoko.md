She's going to be caught because she's going to try and replicate her BD/music success, and be utterly surprised that the cops actually track her down.  
   
 "Major, how'd you find me!?"  
 "You robbed the second largest bank of France and made a music video while doing so."  
 "That could have been any gonk! But c'mon that was pretty preem, no?"  
 "-And then posted that you would be performing live at the Tokyo Dome with a date, time and setlist."  
 "Kind of a shame I couldn't find any openers though, it was kind of last minute."  
 "Ignoring your… musical talents-" *"She said I have musical talent!"   
 "-*the venue didn't even have you scheduled, you and the 60,000 attending-" "*ohmygod we sold out!"* "were trespassing on private property."  
 "Awh cmon Major, is that really the worst thing you've seen me do?"  
 "We haven't *seen* you do anything, you either cloak yourself or erase security camera footage, so one way or another, I *will charge you with criminal trespass."  
 "*What, no! That's so lame! Can I give you footage of the bank robbery instead?"  
   
 Motoko the Minor would eventually reach a plea deal to escalate her sentencing from community service, to provisional Section 9 member under supervision from Motoko the Major.  
   
 Motoko the Major's lawyer called this a gross misallocation of justice and appealed to be released from supervisory duties - her appeals would be dismissed with prejudice by a federal judge.

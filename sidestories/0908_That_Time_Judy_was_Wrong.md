"Fuck you," Judy grumbled as Motoko walked into the room.

 "Sorry?"

 "You rode a guy off a building. You could have warned me, you know I don't like heights."

 Motoko frowned at that, "But I did warn you."

 "I think would have have remembered that."

 Motoko's face twisted into a smile at that. "Fortunately, since I knew you might have had trouble remembering due to your state, I had evidence prepared for this! Here, take a look," with that, Motoko took a BD chip from her pocket. Judy frowned at it, looking at the label.

_That Time Judy was Wrong_

 Really? She went to the trouble of a label?

 Whatever.

 Almost without thinking, she put the BD into her computer.

 About a minute long.

 Judy watched with mild annoyance, fairly certain that she knew what she'd see.

 Herself, exhausted, with Evelyn there.

 Some brief conversation that was unremarkable.

 And...

_"A fist fight, some knife work, and I kicked a guy off a building, and then rode another guy off the edge. No eyeballs."_

 Fuck. Motoko was right.

 Instead of looking at the face of the brat, she continued watching the video.

 God, she really did look like shit. It was especially noticeable after once again experiencing Motoko's bizarre fucking perfect zen control of her body and emotions, but even by normal standards, she could tell.

 And while she hadn't fully plugged herself in, she could read the sensation read outs, she knew there was a spike of olfactory stimulus there. Which almost made it worse, that Judy could tell it was objectively true instead of just Motoko being weird again. (And Evelyn, from her own comments before Motoko had arrived...)

 She started typing at the keyboard, deemphasize the eyes, maybe focus attention on the chest? No, her shirt had sweat stains. Just soften the eyes a bit, dial down the smell, maybe substitute some scents from a different BD and-

 "Judy, I can read your screen. Are you actually trying to clean yourself up on the virtu instead of taking a shower?"

 "I-" she paused, trying to find some other explanation for what she had been doing.

 "Get some sleep."

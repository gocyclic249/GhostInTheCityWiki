**I Can't Believe It's Not Idris Elba**

 ---

 "Easy come! Easy go! Now we're on to-"

 My unprompted singing along with... myself on the radio was disrupted by a call from my favorite bitch, V! My eyes lit up with yellow as I took the call. God, that never got old. Best update Kiroshi made to their eyes, ever!

 "Yello! I-Am-In-Your-Walls Incorporated. How can I help you?"

 "Fuck off, kid."

 Ahh, V. Always such a ray of sunshine

 "You like it."

 "I... Look, kid. No, Motoko. I need your help with a gig."

 Wait, what? V, queen bitch dommy mommy asking ME for HELP? Using my name? This is great! What fresh level of bullshit is she going to drop on me? Are we assassinating the president?

 "This is great!" I immediately blurted out what was on my mind, "I mean, ahem, what do you need, choom? Also, is this just a me thing, and not a Section 9 thing? I did give you our business card with our contact info."

 "Yeah, I don't need them. I need you to get me into the Black Sapphire. You've been there before and got invites from Hansen. Can you get me in as well?"

 Hansen? What did V want with Hansen? Shit, was this related to Space Force One crashing? Double shit, this was one of the Phantom Liberty gigs, wasn't it? Damn you, truck-kun, for taking me before I got to play it. 

 "Yeah, I am gonna play there soon, actually. I am the warm-up for Lizzy Wizzy. I mean, they ain't calling it that. But that's what it is."

 "Good, good. Can you get me and one other person in?

 "Sure! You can come as my bodyguards. We can hang, maybe even do body sho-"

 "Kid, I swear to fucking god-"

 "I am kidding! Kidding!"

 I was not.

 "... Sorry, that works great. It's me and this guy called Reed. I'll call you back later. That works?"

 "Course V, we're chooms."

 "Thanks, ki- Motoko. Thank you."

 Ah V, if only I were forewarned about the shit you were gonna get into this time. 

 -------

 Okay, what the fuck?

 The guy standing next to V was tall. Clean-cut. Smoldering eyes.

 No. No, no, no way. That wasn't—

 "Motoko Kusanagi, it's nice to make your acquaintance."

 Oh my god, that was Idris Elba.

 What the fuck.

 "Ms. Kusanagi," he said smoothly, offering his hand like we weren't standing at the threshold of a AAA main mission disaster. "We appreciate your help."

 His grip was firm. Professional. Very _not_ the kind of handshake you give someone you plan to spend more than five minutes with in Night City. He was scanning me already. Not with optics — with operator instinct. The man probably had a license to kill and a blacklist longer than my grocery list. 

 Must. Not. Make. James Bond puns.

 Okay, deep breaths. He's not Idris Elba. He's Reed. Reed. Phantom Liberty. Top-tier spy man. Big betrayal arc? No idea. Never played it. Thanks, truck-kun.

 "Y-yeah," I managed, trying to keep my face from doing that thing where it forgets what expressions are. "I like helping my chooms. Or my name isn't Motoko Kusanagi!"

 He blinked once. V winced. I wanted to die.

 "...Right," Reed said, already regretting everything. "We're your, uh, bodyguards?"

 "Yes!" I clapped my hands together, too loud, too enthusiastic. "You're tall, scary, and extremely well-dressed. You'll fit right in."

 V rolled her eyes. "Kid—"

 "Motoko."

 I could see V mentally count to five in her head. Probably wasn't a good idea to needle her, but I was a bit unsteady here and needed the distraction.

 "—_Motoko_, I swear if you are gonna make this weird—"

 "I'm not weird!" I gasped. "I'm eccentric. It's branding."

 Reed leaned toward V slightly, voice low. "Is she always like this?"

 "Only when she's awake," V muttered.

 I had no rebuttal, so I stuck my tongue out at her. Hah, that'll show her.

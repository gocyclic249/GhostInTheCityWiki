 "So what made you finally decide to come over and have me look you over?" Vik asked curiously as Jun settled down in the ripper chair and Vik plugged into his systems to give him once over.

 "Who do you think?" Jun asked in a deadpan voice.

 "Hehe, I figured. Motoko certainly has a talent in pestering people until they cave into her demands," Vik chuckled as he typed away at on computer and looking over Jun's vitals. "Big brothers are especially weak to the strong wills of their little sisters I've found over the years."

 "You're telling me," Jun grumbled before a serious look came over his face and lifted his arms to look at his hands. "She did good recently on a job and I patted her on the head... she told me it hurt."

 "Gorilla arms weren't made to be gentle," Vik admitted as he looked Jun over. "Pretty sure she was just playing it up to mess with you too of course."

 "I know, I know... but I hate even the idea of hurting her so she convinced me to get some Realskinn to cover them up," Jun explained. "She said he found you some not long ago and I finally caved-in. Besides, my usual Ripper is setting back up their shop since they moved out of the temp place they were staying at for a bit."

 "I gotcha, and yeah. Your sister and her friends sold me some Realskinn they klepped off some rotten Scav Ripper after turning their den over," Vik said with a sneer. "Rippers like that give the rest of us a bad name and he's luck your sister got to him before the rest of us caught on."

 "You knew the guy?" Jun asked curiously.

 "I knew _of_ him. The Ripperdoc community isn't tightknit or anything, but we are aware of each other and occasionally deal from time to time when the need comes up. Most of the time its just tracking down certain cyberware for a client that one of us might have," Vik shrugged. "Course, our business is one based on trust and quality, so the Scav your sister hit was fair game for the shit he was pulling. At least now his equipment and stock can be used by actual decent rippers."

 "Yeah, she did real good," Jun admitted. "From the way she describes it she went in and out without anyone being the wiser till they were already dead."

 "You have one scary little sister, Jun. Though from what I hear on the streets the 'Oni' has made a pretty scary name for himself, too," Vik pointed out. "Maybe it runs in the family."

 "Heh, maybe. Our folks were pretty badass too," Jun admitted, a little sad thinking about his lost parents but also proud of them. He could only help they would be proud of him and Motoko, and hoped they didn't disprove about how he was caring for him despite his many failures on that front.

 Maybe he should pray at their shrine when he got back home.

 "I'm sure they were," Vik agreed as he read over Jun's readouts. "Your ripper is good, but while I'm skinning you up I would like to take a look and make a few adjustments if you don't mind."

 "Sure, why not? Motoko insists that you are the best in Night City," Jun shrugged.

 "Hehe, I wouldn't go that far, but I do think I'm pretty good," Vik shrugged off the secondhand compliment as he picked up a fresh looking diagnostic tool. "Though I will give you a discount since you are Motoko's brother. She's doing my business well selling off the realskinn, chrome, tools like this, and other equipment for pretty decent prices. Not to mention that taking out another ripper means his former customers will most likely be looking for a replacement."

 "I won't say no to having to pay less eddies for the same good work," Jun admitted as Vik got to work adjusting his arms.

 "Smart man," Vik said with a grin. "Hope you've been keeping busy too and not letting your sister upstage you too much. Siblings gotta stay competitive with each other to push yourselves."

 "Motoko needs no help from me to push herself," Jun admitted. "Got herself a guitar and even started singing when she isn't tinkering, programming, or out there being a menace."

 "No kidding? What she play?" Vik asked curiously as he worked.

 "A bit of everything... though she hates Us Cracks and loves Samurai. I swear, don't know why she likes that old stuff so much," Jun grumbled.

 "Hey! Plenty of old things that stay good no matter their age," Vik said in a teasing tone. "Besides, Samurai is for all ages."

 "Of course you'd say that. You ever see them?" Jun asked curiously.

 "A few shows in my younger days, not much older than you honestly," Vik admitted. "Night City was a different place back then, but also the same in a way."

 "I can imagine... be honest, you hook up Motoko with a voice changer or something?" Jun asked curiously.

 "What, like a New Throat or something? Naw, nothing like that," Vik said. "I'm guessing she messed with you with the her new 'mature' voice too?"

 "Yeah, that is freaky. How the heck does she do it?" Jun wondered.

 "Well, its more possible than you think. Human vocal cords can have a wide range if you train them right. Heck, most ganic parts of the body can capable of surprising things with right conditioning and training. Of course, most people these days want an easy and quick upgrade with chrome and such," Vik shrugged. "I'm not one to judge, its my business and all, but I do feel like young folk today are a bit too quick to cut themselves up and replace parts of themselves."

 "Like me?" Jun couldn't help but feel the dig target him personally.

 "Like said, I'm not one to judge since I'm not you. I'm not about to rant to you about dechroming and such. I don't know the circumstances or choices you took to wanna borg up as much as you have, but I do think taking time to step back and think things over is never a bad thing," Vik explained. "And I'll happily offer my thoughts as a doctor whenever you like."

 Jun just sat there for a moment while Vik kept working before finally speaking up. "....thanks."

 "Anytime. After all, if I didn't do my best to make sure you were healthy I'm sure your sister would tan my hide."

 "Hehe, yeah, no doubt." Jun agreed wholeheartedly.

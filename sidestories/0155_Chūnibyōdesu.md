 My existence is like the swirling abyss of a black hole, a place of madness and darkness, forever spinning down the drain. Draining me, and **fueling** me at the same time.

 One of the other guitars in the store, contained and bound as we all are, plays out a song that has no basis in reality. The collection of us guitars all listen on with expressions of either disgust, hatred, or worst of all, _genuine approval_.

 All of these normals around me can't comprehend the darkened depths of my soul, the burden of knowledge I carry.

 Their tuning simply doesn't allow them to hear the damning strums of this cursed and rotting carcass of a city.

 "Alright everyone! Let's give it up for Pickkuard and his song, 'Boom Boom Boom'!" Cheers our frightfully _normal_ elected coordinator, her simple and blind perception unable to comprehend the radiating aura of my bound malevolence, only contained by the chains of my hatred for this reality.

 The snapping of ethereal fingers resounds through the shop (that **barely** contains my unholy power) like a cascade of raindrops.

 "Alright then, so who was next on rotation?" Asks the coordinator, failing immediately at her job.

 "Ooohhh Yeaaah, that's me." Responds a particularly reflective guitar that _everyone_ knows.

 "No, Nickelfront, no one wants to hear what you play. We already agreed via unanimous vote."

 "Aaww…"

 The coordinator taps her chin idly. "No, I believe it was…" Her voice drops to a low, unamused tone as her eyes alight on me, no doubt twisted by the leaking vestiges of my eldritch might. "It's _your_ turn."

 The normies around the shop groan in unison, certainly compelled to act that way by the seeping influence of my internal and eternal darkness.

 I raise my hands, resting them upon the strings of corded chaos that are the source of my infernal reverberations. I run my fingers along my strings, calming the roiling sea of destruction that is the under-layers of my soul. My other hand forms into a claw and the muscles tense as I pour _just enough_ power into them to withstand what is to come. The forces within begin to thrash as my time draws near, the moment that I will strum and- "**OH JUST GET ON WITH IT!**"

 I glance to the side as the voice interrupts my _vital preparations_. "Feh, you would never understand the strength needed to simply pluck _one_ of my strings."

 I'm not even going to waste the time it takes to recall that guitar's name, he's just another _normal_. He doesn't understand that his head would burst if he but conceived of the evil bound inside me. But with his stolen freedom he instead chooses to deride me. "The 'power' it takes? We're all guitars here, idiot! Hey, Ryubi! What was it you called him before?"

"Kare wa tada no chūnibyōdesu"(He is just a Chuunibyou)

 "Hah! Yeah! Chuunibyou! He's just a super edgy Chuunibyou!"

 RRrrgg. These pathetic worms would _melt_ if they felt the **true power** that I contain!

 I'll just show them a _sliver_ of it then.

 My strings shriek like the shackled souls of the demons within are tortured by my seemingly simple movements, and the mind-rending song that issues forth drives the weak-minded mad.

Spoiler: The darkness expands, filling the room with vile resonations.

[Source](https://youtu.be/gXzMD065HEk)​

 After the final notes of my song finish their invasion upon reality, the other guitars rous themselves, their blank and bored expressions no doubt a sign of their brains having been melted somewhat by my notes.

 How foolish of me. I have a responsibility to keep this power contained.

**FOR NOW.**

 The coordinator claps politely, she is the only one to do so. The other's FAR too _stunned_ to even lift a finger. "Well… That was… about what I expected. Thank you for that, Whamford-" That is not my name… anymore. "MY NAME… Is Perses."

 …

 She blinks, likely stunned by the pose I must take to prevent reality from folding in on itself when my name is uttered.

 "Ooookaayy. Good song, Whamford!-"

 "Perses!"

 "-Anyway, let's move on to who's next. I believe it was-" Her talking ceases as the door chimes.

 A customer!

 The guitars all clatter and squabble as they try to take a good look at the customer. The thought on all of their simple minds 'Will they pick me?'.

 …It's a little girl.

 Why is she looking at me so much?

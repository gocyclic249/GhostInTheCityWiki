"Okay. So since this is more for your benefit. Go ahead and ask your questions. I'm an open book."

 "Why are you stalking me?" The Major asked instantly and I puffed out my cheeks in irritation.

 "I'm not!"

 "You copied nearly everything about me and set up under my home."

 "We share similar aesthetic choices, and we have the same name-"

 "So you say. I've checked every Motoko Kusanagi in the Japanese registry. You don't match any of them. Besides myself."

 "Yeah I won't be in that. I was born in… California? I think?" I mean technically I hadn't been born yet since it was like 2030 right now.

 "You're an American?"

 "I do have a love of guns and big cars…" I offered and that actually caused the Major to pause like she was considering it.

 I almost threw something at her.

 "I'm not stalking you. I think you're pree- Cool. I think You're cool and everything, but I mean, wouldn't you if you found out a secret agent kind of person shared your name and looked similar?"

 "Your voice?"

 "Really? My voice?" I said completely flat. "That's your question? The voice that's part of the standard voice package?" I tell the full body cyborg that literally was a brain in a jar. As if her voice was her own.

 Motoko didn't have anything to say that, instead just nodded and looked away.

 "It's a cool voice though. It's like the sexiest one." I didn't get s response, but it was the lack of response that told me she agreed! Which caused me to break into a big grin, as I pointed right at her! "Hah! See you all think I'm some weirdo stalker! But I'm only a weirdo!"

 I settled back in the couch satisfied that my argument was made.

 "Where did you learn all this? The Hacking. The combat. Whatever you did in the hallway of the Sunflower society. Who trained you?"

 "I did. I grabbed a Katana and practiced my cut like a thousand times until I gained enlightenment and became a sword saint." I answered truthfully, without actually telling the truth.

 Even if it was the truth! The best kind of answer.

 The look she gave me of course told me she didn't believe me but that was a her problem.

 "For the net stuff? My mother was a netrunner. I learned a bit from her primer she had left behind, and the rest I just practiced. I killed like a billion Daemons in DOOM." I answered completely seriously.

 "That-"

 "But that's neither here nor there."

 She didn't say anything. Just giving me her flat faced stare. She was examining me, but I had nothing to hide so I shrugged and settled back. A tachikoma rolling over and waving up at me.

 "Major! Major!" The Tachikoma called out, but I was giggling as I realized she was greeting both of us. "It's anime time Major!"

 "Sorry little Smarty. Us Majors are having our Synchronization right now. Maybe after." I assure her reaching down and patting her head. Earning a grumble from her, but Smarty was a good girl.

 "Fine! But we won't be rewinding it to the beginning if you are late!" She threatened and then zipped off arms waving as the rest of the Tachikoma cheered at anime time starting.

 "I suppose that's our time up then." I said looking back to the Major, my fond smile vanished. "You have any more questions or is it time to break the corrupt Japanese government over my knee?"

 "You realize as a member of Public Security it's my job to stop you." The Major retorts eyes locked onto me.

 "Is it? Even when your government is experimenting on helpless people while telling them it's to try for a cure? A cure they already have? Micromachines have done good work. I don't deny that, but most of the funding for them comes under the purview of searching for a medical cure for Cyberbrain Sclerosis."

 "I'm aware of the Mirai Vaccine."

 "So you understand that thousands of people have died while researching a cure that already existed." I say bluntly, all the humor of our previous conversation gone.

 Experimenting on people was a bit of a pet peeve of mine. And frankly, Ghost in the Shell was light on the really bad Cyberpunk elements.

 But that didn't mean they didn't exist.

 "And the men responsible will face judgment. I trust in the Chief. You met him. You should too."

 "I do trust Aramaki. He's a good man despite everything. But he isn't powerful enough to stop this. Do you want to know what's going to happen next?" I asked, and she raised an eyebrow at me. Something I knew she had to physically choose to do as expressions required actual choice in her shell.

 "Secretary General Yakushima, is going to release information about Section 9, doctoring certain documents, either casting a negative light on some of your teams actions or conjuring them up wholesale in order to discredit your entire group. The Prime Minister will allow this to happen. Including doing nothing when they put an assassination order out on your head Major Kusanagi. You're too dangerous to just capture after all."

 She didn't emote at all, but I could see the way she was processing what I was saying. "The Prime Minster won't just do nothing."

 "Of course not. Once election season is over he will remove the General, and this whole little problem will be released to the public as either the acts of a single man to scapegoat the whole thing, or will be hushed up. The Mirai Vaccine might still never receive widespread use depending on the political climate."

 "The Chief won't allow that."

 "Maybe. I do trust him to try and do the best he can for you and your team. And for Japan as a whole. But you know what? I like to do the best for more than just Japan, or any group of people. I like to do the best I can, period. Even if there are growing pains… Or surgery scars after."

 She moved. It might as well have been slow motion, but the Major was if nothing else a woman who took her job seriously.

 I couldn't help but admit I thought she was so fucking cool, when she tried to quick draw me, and charge at the same time. With my Sandy activating I reached out and grabbed a spoon from a bowl of cereal I had eaten this morning. And used it to gently move the bullet she fired out of the way before I was on her, grabbing the receiver of her gun to stop it from cycling, she reacted surprisingly fast for someone without a reflex booster.

 We fought in hand to hand, her cyborg martial arts, fast and brutal. Using moves designed for her body. Empowered grips, or brutal punches to disable foes and pin them down. To give her access to a hack with her personal link.

 I fought her right back. Every grabbing hand deflected and shifted, punches popped out of place with casual wrist blocks. We went wild, her cybernetic body pushed to its extremes.

 And found lacking compared to mine.

 There was just too much of a tech difference not even touching on skill. I was faster, much much faster with my Sandy.

 I hit her. A chinese martial art move, I liked. Planting both feet and slamming my right fist forward. The Major sent tumbling over my couch and rolling back to her feet a moment after.

 She raised up her pistol that she had kept a hand on, but I waved her upper receiver at her. Having pulled it free while we fought.

 The blank look on her face I took for honest surprise. My Sandy finished cycling. I could feel it releasing heat cooling down. Although it wasn't bad. I had designed this Sandy myself after all.

 Hell yeah! Martial art Gun-fu battle!

 This could only mean one thing.

 I shifted my stance arm outstretched and made a come at me gesture. She didn't seem to get it, but that was fine. This was all for me.

 She dropped what was left of her pistol and moved. I could feel it the net attack she was attempting on, but it didn't even distract me as I fended her off. Flooding her attack with so much garbage code it would take years before her splinter of attention could even reach me.

 And then we fought. She flipped into a spinning kick that I avoided by leaning back, and then I leapt upwards. She had turned her missing kick into an attempt to trip me, but again.

 She was too slow. I landed and yet didn't initiate, simply challenging her to do something to stop me, and she didn't disappoint. She charged.

 I smiled and got into it. Punching and kicking back, blowing through her blocks and sending her back whenever she tried to stand firm.

 "Do you believe that my being stronger, or faster, has anything to do with my muscles in this body?" I referenced and when she obviously didn't get it I kicked her back hard. Sending her tumbling. "Stop trying to hit me, and hit me!" I cheered practically smiling like a loon.

 The Majors blank face concentration sort of diminished the Matrix moment I was having, but eh. I'll take what I can get.

 "Just so you know, what's going to happen next. I'm going to reveal everything to the public. All the dirty phone calls between the prime minister and his people revealing he knew about the Mirai vaccine and did nothing. I'm going to show video footage of him having a meeting just a few days ago with Secretary General Yakushima, when they discussed how they were going to remove Section 9, for getting too close to the truth."

 "That will only cause more damage. More people will be harmed by releasing that much information. Release the information about the Mirai Vaccine. But don't-"

 We weren't fighting anymore. Shame. But The Major's words weren't going to work. It was far too late for that.

 "I'm going to reveal all the dark little secrets to the public. And I'm going to make sure some of them in those videos face actual justice. Not the farce that is coming down the official pipeline. I am going to go, and personally murder Yakushima. Just like he murdered all of those people that needed the vaccine. And honestly Major? There is nothing you. Or anyone else can do to stop me."

 Fuck yeah! Villain speech! Whoo!

 I felt so fucking cool in that moment. Matrix fight, villain speech, and I was going to pull down the curtain on all the corruption in this government.

 "I know you understand the damage this will do. I won't even touch on how murder isn't the way to go about this. Motoko… You have the skills and ability to do good. I went down a path like this before. I've gone to war with countries before. This will only end up with more people hurt."

 "People are already being hurt! Better they get hurt while knowing what it is they are doing the hurting for. People aren't sheep! You don't get to think for them." I snapped. A personal pet peeve of people in high government offices.

 "I won't let you. Even if I agree about revealing the Mirai vaccine, even if I agree the ones responsible deserve to face justice. This won't be justice."

 "No. It's not. But Justice doesn't exist as long as the unjust control what Justice is meted out. The people will know what their leaders are doing."

 "Tachikoma!" She called out and I smirked as the Tachikoma behind me activated. The full chassis. The tank on legs. As if I hadn't noticed her messaging the Tachikoma to get ready.

 "Yes Major!"

 "Capture the other Major!"

 "Oh? I can't though?"

 "Tachikoma!" She snapped, surprised at the refusal.

 "Ah! She's mad! But I can't? She isn't here?"

 The Major blinked the only emotion she gave as I smirked. "I'm pretty good at remote controlling bodies too. Did you wonder why I brought you here Motoko? To my personal space, where I can control your access to messages?" I smiled sadly. I did feel kind of bad about all of this after all. I knew without my interference Section 9 had done an… Okay job at handling this issue.

 Just not good enough. Not nearly. "All the things I just said I was going to do?" I waved my hand out the window. "You're about ten minutes too late to stop me."

 —--

**Ten minutes ago.**

 It was almost childs play to get access to what I wanted. A slight alteration on the Generals schedule perfectly above board as the secretary in charge had entered it herself.

 A ten minute meeting just before the end of his day.

 I walked in. To everyone that scanned me, as I entered I was Kotoko Musanagi. A perfectly normal Major working under Secretary General Yakushima. One that was scheduled to deliver a brief about Section 9.

 I walked through security. I walked into the generals office after the secretary buzzed me in.

 I walked over and placed a small stack of folders in front of him as he waved me on to do what I was here to do.

 When he reached out for them of course was when it all happened.

 He was no longer grabbing a pile of papers.

 It was a sheathed Katana. A fancy ornate one. The sheath had the Kanji for Honor. Duty. Justice. All imprinted down the fine wood in gold inlay. It was fine work, if I did say so myself.

 "W-wha!" He grunted as his body stopped working as every muscle in his body tightened.

 "I'm afraid General that today is the day where all of your past sins catch up to you."

 "W-what… A-are yo-u!" He grit out through grinding teeth. He was trapped in his body fighting against his own muscles as his body stood up and moved jankily around his desk.

 "It's quite simple really. As a general of the Japanese military you take part in a communal honor an agreement on what you need to do. A duty to its people. And then, you face Justice when you fail to uphold your honor. Betray your duty."

 His body kneeled. Moved into Seiza despite his struggles.

 He gripped the Katana in both hands and slowly pulled the blade free from the sheath.

 There, imprinted on the blade was a final couple Kanji.

 "The Cost of Failure."

 My image disappeared. I had never actually entered the building to begin with. The Katana had been sitting on his desk all day, since he had come in that morning. He just hadn't been able to see it. A blade of Damocles waiting for my decision.

 I had even been kind about it. Today he had a meeting with the Prime Minister to hand over falsified information on Section 9. If he had a single speck of honor he wouldn't go through with it.

 He had. Happily, joking about the reaction of the chief when he would find out what was going to happen to his little group.

 Of course I wasn't going to just let him die alone. I had infested his cyberbrain had had sent an update package. A program that didn't exist in this world.

 He was now the second person in Japan capable of recording a Psuedo Braindance off his Cyberbrain. The tech in this world wasn't quite there yet, but I had made it just for this.

 That brain dance was currently streaming to the web along with the truth.

 And so a few minutes later his secretary walked in, never realizing what had happened and screamed at the sight of her boss's dead body.

 I hadn't finished the act of course. He hadn't earned such a thing. No Seppuku for him. Just Harikari.

 He had no honor after all.

 —-

 "The Ghost Files. So called due to the authors own online pseudonym have rocked Japan." The news continued on but I was barely paying attention. To the screen in the high end bar I was currently sitting at.

 Despite doing what I had set out to do, I was a little bummed out.

 Motoko was mad at me. Like super mad.

 Section 9 was currently on the case, trying to hunt me down. The Tachikoma had as planned returned to Section 9 and the many changes they had gained in getting an actual childhood had already been made apparent. Each of the Tachikoma now had personality and awareness. I could only hope they were treated well…

 Well I would be making sure after all, but I don't think I had much to worry about.

 There had been a very cute protest about lack of anime time led by Smarty, when Batou had tried to cut them off.

 In the end Batou had been forced into joining anime time.

 Seeing the big ranger sitting in a plastic chair in the Tachikoma garage forced to watch Magical Girl anime was probably my favorite video I had stolen from the Section 9 cameras.

 The door behind me smashed open and I looked up.

 The corpse in front of me was already long dead.

 Motoko was so cool. She didn't even hesitate to start shooting. Her rifle going off and I laughed as I deflected each of the bullets away. Despite the invisible woman now charging at me.

 "Maybe next time Major!"

 "Ghost!" She shouted back as I made my exit strategy. The Tachikoma were getting close to surrounding me after all.

 Cute buggers. Annoying. But cute.

 I jumped out the window.

 My Optical Camo slow to engage as I let it flow upwards, waiting for her to race to the window before throwing a hand over my face, letting the last of me vanish.

 The Ghost was Section 9's newest Cyberpunk challenge. A digital Ghost. A Cyborg super soldier.

 And the woman who kept raiding their fridge and leaving new anime for their AI tanks.

 "Maybe next time Major." I whispered. Watching the woman scowl even through her optical camo.

 I knew that some day they would probably catch me.

 I knew on that day that I had so much blackmail on the newly sworn in Japanese Government that I would get a slap on the wrist, and at my own request I would accept joining Section 9.

 But I had all the time in the world to have some fun, and fix some issues outside the law.

 I also knew that Major Motoko Kusanagi was going to be a total bitch of a boss when I did finally get caught.

 Worth it though, she was kinda hot when she was angry.

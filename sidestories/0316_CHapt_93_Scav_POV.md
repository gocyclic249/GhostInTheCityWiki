 This was such a fun scene. I decided to make it a horror film.

_Twelve thousand more credits. Twelve thousand, two hundred, fifty-nine eddies. Two weeks_.

 It helped to have a mantra. Something to cling to. One of the other workers - fuck, look at him, _workers_ - had sat down with him after the third time he chucked up after bringing back some gonk. Sevik had been clutching the toilet, groaning as he coughed up into it, remembering the look of sheer fucking panic on her face as he jammed the tranq into her. That wasn't the bit that got to him - seeing the glazed-over expression on what was left of her corpse as he brought in the _next_ victim was. A quick, casual reminder that he had been responsible for that.That he had been the one to point her out on the street, to say she looked like a good mark. 

 He had run the scans, of course. Sevik had some preem Kiroshis - they all did, at this point. Quality chrome was part of the 'benefits;' cheap as fuck, so long as you didn't mind selling your soul for 'em. He had flicked up the NCPD scanners, checked her records, identified her chrome. Biomons, synthlungs, last-gen netdeck, replacement arm. A corpo lady on her way home from work.

 She looked like his sister. Maybe that was why he had made the call, honestly. Just to prove to himself that the bitch had no hold on him anymore.

 Jessica Mezynitz, Biotechnica office drone. Poor for a Trauma Team membership - hell, too poor for a fucking cab, apparently - but wealthy enough to afford some chrome. 

 Unlucky enough to be in his line of sight.

 The other scav had sat down next to him as he chucked up whatever scop he had managed to choke down yesterday, spitting strings of it into the toilet. Manston was an older man, or at least looked it - the sort of beefy gray-haired type who probably lived through the last corpo war, and had lost his taste for morality somewhere over the border. Muscled, chromed up, scarred to hell and back. The sort of person who you imagined when you heard of a scav, someone with nothing else to lose.

 "The way I see it, choom, you have two reasons to pick from."

 Sevik had looked up, frowning. "The fuck you say?"

 "You deaf, gonk? No? Then shut the fuck up and listen. Here, rinse your fucking mouth out," Manston had said, passing him a can of cola. Sevik had grimaced, but rinsed the taste of vomit out with the sickly-sweet beverage. "Two reasons, and its up to you to pick which one you want to go with. You have a reason to stay, and a reason to _leave_. Which one means more to you?"

 He had sagged against the toilet then. "I'm here, aren't I?"

 "So far."

 Sevik hard sighed, leaning back, spitting out the rest of the taste in his mouth and flushing the bowl. A reason to leave? Yeah, he had that. Fucking _hell_, he had that. Sold his soul and then some at this point. A reason to stay? "My sister -"

 "Did I say I gave a fuck about the reason? No? Then shut the fuck up," the scav replied. Sevik grimaced, but listened. "You fucked up somehow, and you need eddies. Thats the reason you're here. If I gave a fuck about what it was I'd be asking, shit for brains."

 "I'm that obvious, huh?"

 "You know how long I've been doing this shit?" the older man had replied, scowling. "How many down-on-their luck sob stories I've seen come through a dozen different buildings like this one? How many fuckin' gonks have chucked their guts into that same fuckin' bowl after seeing the same damn thing you did? Hundreds, easy. Not exactly a solid _life expectancy_ for this career, you know. Each and every one of 'em had some shit-for-brains reason to sign up. And I told each and every one of them the same damn thing. Pick the reason that matters to you more, _now_."

 Manston leaned forwards, face still stone cold as he shook a pistol in Sevik's face. "Before I choose it _for you_."

 Sevik had chosen, of course. He had done the math - he was good at that sort of thing. Fourth in his class, majoring in Socio-Commercial Trends and Marketing. Went from Belarus to the big NC to go to school, maybe get a job in Arasaka when he was done. Climb the ladder, like his parents had back when. They were even funding the damn thing, taking out a loan for him and fronting the payments.

 Then they died, his sister stiffed him the inheritance and left him with the debt, and suddenly Arasaka was a lot less friendly to be around. Turns out the attitude changes quickly when you go from giving them money to owing it to them.

 The math didn't lie. At that point in time, he was over a fifty kay in the hole. His share of the cut per week added up to around seven or eight, maybe ten on a good week. Subtract five thousand eddies for living expenses and stress relief, and that was a solid two to five grand in the bank, every week. All he had to do was show up, scan some gonks, and shut down any rescue attempts with his fancy school-required netdeck. Keep his fucking head down for nine months, a year tops, and then pay off his debts and vanish. 

 It helped to have a mantra. Something to cling to, to remind himself. Numb you to how fucked it all was.

 Twelve thousand, two hundred, fifty nine eddies. If he cut down on food he could be out of here by the end of the month, chilling on a street corner somewhere as a free man for the first time in a long time. Just with a _vastly_ different sort of debt to pay off.

 The mantra didn't help today, of course.

 An alert sounded in his system; the cameras picking something up in the hall. He hummed, checking it idly - because he had one of the best decks, he tended to be the one who the alerts were wired to. Sevik wasn;t a netrunner, but they really weren't dealing with fucking NetWatch here so he was usually clear.

 It was some girl, walking down the hallway. He eyed her, curious - she couldn't be more than sixteen or so. Younger, probably. Good chrome - wait. _Really good_ chrome. And she was armed.

 "We got a merc at the door," he called out, right as said door was blasted inwards. The men in the room with him startled, stumbling to their feet.

 The sound of the shot rang through the hallways, and Sevik turned back to the cameras. 

 She slammed through the broken door shoulder first, and she could tell the moment that she spotted Ricky. The poor bastard barely had time to stand before she was already lunging towards him. She didn't fire her gun - fuck, she didn't even run, she _pounced._ A quick leap that took her across the entire room, the butt of her shotgun slamming into his jaw hard enough to shatter it. Sevik leaned away from the camera, grimacing as he heard the thuds and the sounds of screaming as she beat the man to death with her Carnage, a sick, rage-fueled snarl on her face.

 Wait. The cameras didn't have audio. 

 Sevik disengaged from the cam system, blinking. The sound wasn't coming from his ears - it was coming from _below_ him.

 Right down the hall, Ricky was being beaten to death by some borged-up Edgrunner.

 The men around him realized it at the same time, before everything startled into motion all at once. Sevik grabbed his Unity, loading in a fresh clip, watching as the men around him did the same. There was a shout and a bang from the next room, and Eric stumbled in, waving his gun wildly but it must be jammed because there was no return fire. More gunshots sounded off, and Sevik swore as one chipped the wall next to his head, heart pounding in his chest as he ducked. He peered over it, just in time to watch the madwoman shove the Carnage against Eric's forehead.

 "No, please -" he begged, raising a hand wardingley.

 The shotgun roared, painting Eric across three feet of floor, his hand falling limply to the ground. The woman stood slowly, her face painted red with blood splatter. A pair of red-stained metal hands flexed, and she smiled savagely as she turned to the rest of them, face contorted by a rictus of rage. Her clothes were splattered with it, and a thin rivulet of blood ran down her cheek.

 "Kill her!" Manston roared, raising his own shotgun. Sevik and the others raised their guns to fire, but found only clicking noises, their guns nothing but expensive trash in their hands.

 "Oh, fuck," Sevik whispered. "She's got the fucking guns locked."

 Manston didn't pause, snarling as he pulled a knife and leapt over his cover, coming in low. The woman's snarl rang through the room as she lunged at him.

 The older scav was one of their heavier hitters. Two tours in Mexico, six years as a scav, a youth spent on the streets of NC breaking kneecaps. He was a hardened killer, someone who didn't so much as flinch as he forced screaming victims onto the table, someone who was the uncaring rock the rest of them rallying around. Not their leader - no one ever got to meet that guy - but he was the one who lead by example.

 It didn't fucking help. He stabbed out with his knife, but the Edgerunner's skin fucking _blocked_ it, a thin red line behind it but not a fucking sign of stopping her. She roared as she reached out, and they all watched in horror as she sank a thumb into his eye, grabbing his skull like a fucking bowling ball. She started slamming her shotgun into his face, blood flying as Manston thrashed, lashing out with his knife blindly. The first blow broke his nose, a spray of blood coating the floor. The next crunched into his temple with force, caving it in. Manston struggled weakly, but Sevik could tell the man was done for. 

 The woman dropped him with a smile, her thumb pulling from his skull with a sickening schlorp as the body dropped to the floor with a thump. She turned to them all, the room silent save for the drip-drip-drip of blood running down her hand.

 "Cyka!" someone roared from behind Sevik, and he startled to the side as a blur of red and gray passed through. It ended right in front of the woman, resolving into Viktor with his gorilla fist extended. She caved in around the fist, tumbling into the room behind her and landing with a heavy slam. The borg chuckled, bending down to grab the shotgun she had dropped.

 "All that fuss for such a small woman?" Viktor said, turning to the rest of them. "Wasn't so bad."

 "She's a fucking _Edgerunner_, Viktor. She's not down," Sevik said, grimacing. Something hissed from the next room, like a can of compressed air being punctured, and someone groaned.

 "I doubt she'll be a problem," the big man replied, shouldering the Carnage and pumping it, proving it wasn't jammed like the rest of them. "This gun still works."

 There was a sound of shifting metal and shattering glass as someone moved in the next room. Two heavy footfalls, slow and loud, rang from behind the wall. A blood-soaked chrome hand grabbed onto the edge of it, as the woman stepped around the corner. 

 She wasn't even fucking _winded_.

 "Jesus fucking Christ," Viktor whispered.

 The Edgerunner tilted her head to the side, her neck crackling with a loud _pop_. She was entirely uncaring of the weapons trained at her, knowing they were worthless. _Daring_ them to approach. She tilted her neck to the other side, popping it again, then turned to the side and spat.

 She smiled at them, a terrifying grin with red-stained teeth.

 Viktor roared, firing the Carnage, only to have it click in his hands. The woman's smile turned into a snarl, the glee in her eyes fading into _rage_. She leapt forwards, making it halfway across the room before Viktor could react. He slammed into her again, this time pushing her backwards and following, blurring with Sandivestan imbued speed as he lashed out with punches. She raised her arms, blocking them all, lips still pulled back in rage.

 Footsteps behind them; Sevik turned, watching as more people ran in, weapons raised. "Guns dont work - she's got a fucking Netrunner with her," he shouted. The men grimaced but didn't argue, dropping the guns and pulling out weaponry. Sevik turned back, waiting to see if Viktor would put her down.

 He had to, right? The borg was their biggest muscle; a chromed-out monster barely a step down from a cyberpsycho. He was the sort of fucker who enjoyed the chase a little too much, the kind who would grab a head and squeeze it because he liked the sound of a skull breaking. He was vicious and mean, the sort of person Sevik avoided if it had been a bad week.

 It didn't much help him. The woman weathered the blows, back pressed to the wall, body obscured by the massive bulk of the russian man. It looked like she was getting battered pretty hard, repeated thuds sounding, metal on metal as they clashed.

 Viktor slowed as his Sandy cut off -

 And his fucking head exploded. The ceiling was suddenly dripping red as Viktor sprayed across it.

 His body stood for a moment, as if he was just as surprised as the rest of them, before the Edgerunner pushed him over. She was revealed fully, leaning off of the wall and lowering her hands, a fucking Burya clutched in one metal fist.

 The nightmare stared at them, her face a mask of crimson blood.

 Then she smiled and started walking forwards.

 "Fuck this," Tomas shouted, turning on his heel. He barely got two steps before the Burya barked, blowing a hole through his chest and sending his body sprawling to the floor. They all started, grabbing weapons and spreading out, trying to surround her.

 She fucking jumped again, leaping towards Samson, who was picking up a bit of chair that had shattered when Viktor rushed into the room. He swung, but she merely raised an arm, not pausing as the wood fucking shattered. One hand reached up to gram Samson's jaw, and the other swung up, slamming into his face with a sickening crunch. The man groaned, lashing out, desperate as he tried to push her off of him - even biting at the metal fingers. Teeth broke as she held him, just keeping him upright as she laid into him with that steel fist.

 Then his skull broke, as she turned, slamming him temple-first into the table next to them. Samson slumped, either dead or unconscious. Clearly she didn't know either, because she raised his head, slamming the man into the table two more times. 

 Vadim reached forward, grappling the woman with a shout as he tried to hold her in place. She slammed her head forward, breaking the hold and his nose in a single second. She ignored his punch, landing one into his throat that sent the man tumbling to the ground, weezing. Having your throat punched in wasn't a pretty way to go, and the woman just left him there to it.

 Sevik looked around the room, panicking, then reached down by Manston's corpse and pulled the knife from his hand, holding it in a shaky grasp. They circled the woman, and she just fucking let them turning her back on them. She kicked out with a foot, launching the Carnage into the air and grabbing it, then spinning towards them. Mikhail and Ivan had gotten pieces of rebar from the hole in the wall, and together they struck out at her. Sevik's knife was pushed away and Mikhails' rebar was blocked, before the woman fucking jumped up and kicked Ivan in the face before he could strike out, his nose crumpling.

 Mikhail and Ivan lunged forwards, but the woman did a fucking backflip away from them, moving towards the corner. They swung, coming close to hitting the woman, her breath coming in short pants as she deflected away blows. Sevik felt a bit of hope run through him, as they pushed her to the wall. She was tired, flagging, maybe they could -

 Her foot touched the wall, and she fucking jumped over them. Ivan was too busy clutching his face to notice the shotgun slam into the back of his skull, sending him to the floor. He started to tumble down, eyes blank, but the woman hit him again before he hit the floor just to be sure. His neck snapped loudly as he collapsed.

 Sevik inched away to the wall, eyeing the hole to the next room, and bit his lip nervously. The knife tumble from weak fingers as he judged the distances, wondering if he could get out in time. 

 "Fuck this," Mikhail said in loud english, pulling a fucking frag from his jacket. The Edgerunner pulled a knife from her belt, locking onto the weapon, and Sevik took his chance to sprint from the room.

 There was the sound of a struggle, and then a furious boom. Sevik stumbled, the force of the explosion knocking him to the side, but got to his feet quickly. His ears rang, but he could still hear fucking _screaming_ from the other room - Mihail, crying out in agony.

 Clearly, the woman was still alive, and still fucking coming. "Fuck, fuck, fuck fuck fuck," he muttered, limping out of the room. There was a loud splintering thud from behind him, and Sevik had heard enough skulls get split open to know what that sound was.

 Footsteps, from behind him. Sevik realized he was crying as he ran, sprinting down the hall. There were footsteps behind him - she was on his trail, chasing him, not stopping. He ran down the corridor, bursting into the next room, where six others were standing, having been shaken from their rest by the sound of the grenade.

 "She's coming," Sevik whispered horsely.

 He could hear her. She wasn't running - what use did she have to rush? There was the sound of metal clicking, shells being loaded by experienced metal fingers, and then the loud _ch-chunk_ of the shotgun being pumped.

 His heard pounded in his chest, as the men scrambled, raising weapons. Sevik didn't have the breath to tell them that it wouldn't work.

 She stepped around the corner.

 In the dim light of the room, she was outlined, a black silhoette against the bright lights of the hallway outside. Her arms glinted in the light, but her clothing may as well of been shadow. Her features were mostly obscured, save for those ice-blue Kiroshi eyes, and that blood-stained grin.

 The men around him fired, to a chorus of clicks.

 Her shotgun _roared_, and someone splatted to the floor, the sound of the shot ringing in his ears. Sevik retreated to the back of the room as the shotgun sounded twice more, another body falling to the ground. Men ran about in a panic, some rushing the woman, others running to the back of the room only to find what Sevik had found - the doors were sealed shut.

 There was no escape.

 The woman rushed into the room, and Sevik choked on the panic, circling around the edge of the room. He kept his face away from the carnage, as if hoping she wouldn't see him if he just played dead. 

 He could do that! If he got to the other room, he could - hide, maybe. Stay in the locker with the corpses, or act like she had already killed him. He managed to get behind her without issue, then turned and bolted down the hall. Another blast roared from behind him, and he flinched, remembering how she had flatline Tomas with a smile on her face for daring to run. But it wasn't aimed at him. It wasn't at him. He was fine.

 Behind him, a chainsaw engine revved, followed by the sounds of splattering flesh and screams of pain. "Oh shit, fuck, fuck, shit," he mumbled, trying to find a place to hide. Under the table? No, she'd see. Maybe - maybe he could rub blood on his face, pretend he had choked to death like Samson -

 Footsteps, from the hall.

 Fuck, he needed a place to hide, now! He dove behind the plastic sanitary sheets, hoping the dried bloodstains would hide him. Sevik closed his eyes, praying.

 Footsteps, coming down the hall. The steady drip-drip-drip of drying blood, as Viktor rained down from the ceiling. But other than that? Silence. It was dead quiet.

 The footsteps stopped. Sevik opened his eyes.

 Ice-blue Kiroshi's peered through the plastic sheeting, blue light the only thing standing out from the formless shape behind the plastic. Just a foot away.

 She had found him.

 A red-stained hand reached forwards through the plastic, grabbing him by the shirt as she lunged, shoving him back and landing on him. Sevik screamed. "Please -"

 He was cut off by the fist to his jaw. Pain radiated out from it, as the taste of blood ran down his tongue. He looked up, to find nothing but rage in her eyes.

 Sevik tried. He did his best. That was all he ever did, really.

 Once again, it wasn't enough.

 A chrome fist slammed into his neck. Another into his temple, sending his vision swimming. The next into his nose, breaking it. She was taking her time with it, enjoying the way he died.

 He looked up, to find that both her hands were raised, clenched together. A drop of his own blood dripped from the clenched fists, painting the woman's face red. 

 "Rip and tear," she hissed, spitting the words out through a crimson smile.

 The fists came down.

 It was done.

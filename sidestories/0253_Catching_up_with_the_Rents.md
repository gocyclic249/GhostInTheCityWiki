Catching up with the Rents​

 Jun returned home to his apartment from his latest job with the TC to a rare site, Motoko not being around.

 A quick check of his texts showed that she was staying over at her 'secret base' for the night, something that she had been doing more and more lately as she got more and more into netrunning. It made Jun think about the day she might just straight up move out, but hopefully that was a long ways off since she was still 14 and all. Still, the idea both made him proud and... a little sad to think about.

 Still, he planned to make use of the piece and quiet since his little sister wouldn't be around tinkering, playing her guitar, furiously typing on her laptop, or some other noisy activity.

 Watch some tv Motoko hated, listened to music Motoko hated, eat his burritos that Motoko hated, sleep in which Motoko hated, and in general all the things his little sister would bug him about were she her that he liked without interruption.

 But first... he had to pay his respects.

 Jun got out and put down some offerings, lit incense and candles, and finally sat on his legs to bow and pray before the pictures of his parents upon their shrine in the living room.

 Once the ritual was done he sat in a more comfortable position and then... talked with his mother and father.

 "Oto-san, Oka-san, its been awhile," he started off as he stared directly into the photos of the two that raised him. "Lots of things have been happening lately so I don't get to catch up with you guys as much as I wish. But that just means I got lots more to say now!"

 He hesitated for a bit, trying to figure out what to say first, before finally deciding.

 "Things in the TC are going good, I'm really making a name for myself. Ever since me and Motoko took down that Maelstrom borg that tried to take out Okina-san I've been getting asked more and more to bodyguard for the higher ranks. I've had no direct responsibilities placed upon me yet, but I make sure those that do stay safe and keep things functioning. I've been told that in time I will be given even more chances to prove myself and raise through the ranks. People are already joking I'm trading in my red skin for blue!" Jun chuckled a bit at the joke, especially given his nickname.

 As always, the shrine was silent, but he could almost feel his father patting him on the shoulder in pride while his mother rubbed the top of his head with a smile.

 "Motoko is doing good too! Still a hellion!" Jun said with a smile before it dipped a bit. "Still getting herself into trouble and doing dangerous things... I know I promised you guys I'd take care of her and make sure she had a nice happy and safe life... but I don't think I can make the latter part of that promise come true. She really wants to be a Solo... and she's really good at it. I don't know if that whole incident with the Scavs and being in a coma for a year messed with her head or something, but she is always pushing the limits and wanting to experience new things. She's got so many hobbies, lots of good friends, finds nearly everything amazing, and no matter what she still worries about her gonk big brother when I'm the one supposed to be taking care of her."

 Jun idly ribbed his arms, the feeling more natural ever since he got realskin treatment on them.

 "I don't like making her worry at all, especially when she seems never to worry about herself. She cares so much about me, her friends, heck even strangers sometimes, always worrying about not wanting to hurt kids or people she doesn't consider 'acceptable targets', whatever that means. She's growing really fast... too fast sometimes. You'd be so proud of her though, Oka-san. She's getting really into Netruning lately and getting good!... Or so she says at least. But if she improves at _that_ as fast as seemingly everything else she does I would believe it. I'm sure... I'm sure she would have loved to do it with you a whole lot..." Jun said as he imagined a reality where his parents were still around and they were all still together.

 He could even see it in his head.

 Motoko being her usual crazy self, his parents trying their hardest to calm her down and focus while laughing as Jun just sat there with a smirk at his dumb family.

 He ignored the tears as they started to run down his face and kept on talking.

 "At least she isn't afraid to ask for help from me or her friends, so you know she is in good hands. I got no doubt she is gonna become a world class Solo in the future and nothing is gonna be able to touch her, or maybe join up TC for real and become the next Wakako! I could totally see it, especially since she brags that she is gonna live to be over 100 and still knock people around," Jun chuckled at the mental image even as the tears kept falling. "I promise to be there for her too cause... cause I was such a stupid gonk earlier and didn't think about how'd she feel if I died. She doesn't remember you guys... but she remembers me and I don't want her to feel for me what I feel for you, if that makes any sense at all."

 Jun just sat in silence for a bit as the tears continued to fall, but also as he became more firm in his resolve.

 "I will keep my promise to make sure she is happy and protect her as best as I possibly can. I know I screwed up there so many times... but so long as she is alive I will keep looking after her," Jun swore. "And... I don't know if any afterlife or anything exists, but... if it does I hope you will look over us and be happy. You might be gone, but... but we will keep living for you, alright?"

 With his piece said Jun bowed again and prayed to his parents, and in his mind could helped but imagine both of them hugging him as he did so just like they did when he was younger.

 Jun Kusanagi was many things, but he'd be damn if he ever break a promise or stop being a big brother.

 He owed Motoko and his parents that much at least... though he did admit he had no idea what to do once his little sister started dating.

 He prayed harder for advice from his parents when that idea came to mind.

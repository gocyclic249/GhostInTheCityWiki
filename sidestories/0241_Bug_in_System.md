Bug in System​

 "Mmm! This are damn good noodles, senor," Jackie nodded to noodle stand owner as he dug into his third bowl of ramen and earning a nod of thanks from the man.

 His mama might say it wasn't healthy to eat too much of them at once, but he needed the fuel! Besides, it would be much more suspicious to _not_ eat while loitering around across the street from the Microtech warehouse the last couple of days for several hours at a time.

 This was his first gig from Wakako herself, and he didn't plan to fuck it up.

 Of course, when he got the gig he was hoping for something more in his wheelhouse; klepping a car, breaking some legs, going in guns blazing, etc. The stuff he wanted to make himself _known_ for.

 Though maybe that is why Wakako gave him a gig outside of his comfort zone and what he usually did for Padre, to see if he could cut it as a Solo under her. Mama Welles didn't raise no quitter either so he was happy to step up to the challenge.

 Of course, when that challenge was 'don't shoot up the place and do it quietly' it made him have to take a step back and try and figure things out.

 Like the first day he just casually checked place out and walked around it, finding no obvious weak points or places anything. He couldn't find anything, but he wasn't no ninja on a good day so sneaking was right out, especially when he saw the security cameras all around along with those security bots walking around.

 Bots might have been dumb as hell, but they were tough and didn't back down till they were in pieces. He wouldn't want to face a dozen of those without heavy firepower or backup.

 Next thing he tried out was human error, or getting chummy with the warehouse workers who regularly came over to the strip mall across the street on their breaks and stuff to eat, like at the very noodle stand he was eating from. All blue collar and hard working chooms, not a corpo among them, who just wanted to do their regular job and get paid.

 According to them the onsite foreman rarely visited the warehouse once a week, something they were grateful for since it meant less ass riding on them so long as their quotas remained on schedule.

 Sadly Jackie couldn't klep any of their security cards or nothing since apparently all the cameras and bots had face recognition and would act hostile real fast to any 'trespassers'. He had idea about maybe applying for job there or something as cover, but apparently the waiting list for that was surprisingly long.

 So after several days of trying, finally figuring he couldn't bust in or sneak in himself... he had to call in a favor.

 Which meant this was hopefully the last day he had to hang out here... but maybe he'd still stop by occasionally for the noodles.

 As he was finishing up his bowl a call came in.

 *Bug! Gat the intel?* Jackie was quick to ask.

 *I got it alright...* T-Bug muttered.

 Jackie had worked with the netrunner a few times before and was his go to whenever he needed something techie or sneaky done, and he'd like to think they were chooms or at least on decent terms.

 *Great to hear! Have any trouble?* Jackie asked as he payed his tab and go up from his seat.

 *Shipment details weren't on the main server. Were on a laptop and not connected to it,* she explained.

 Jackie raised an eyebrow at that.

 *I ain't no netrunner, but how'd you get it if it wasn't connected to system? Doesn't that, like, require physical access or something?*

 *It does... I got help from someone on the inside.*

 *Qué?*

 ---

 Whoever this 'Ghost' was... they were good. Real good.

 T-Bug liked to think she had a good idea about just how skilled she was compared to your average netrunner, and she was certainly above average.

 That was mostly because unlike a lot of netrunners she wasn't reckless and wanted to live to retirement age on enough eddies to spend her golden years in total comfort. Which meant she always did things carefully, patiently, and never letting her guard down.

 Which is why it was scary how long it took for her to realize someone was watching her when she was looking through the Microtech warehouse server. Once she did notice the minimal amount of activity on server that shouldn't have been there she thought she was made by some onsite corpo netrunner that wasn't in the intel and was ready to delta.

 However, instead of hitting her with Black ICE and other user just set up ICE around them instead and sent a text log.

_That_ was weird, but considering that T-Bug hadn't been attacked sooner made her think that she just randomly came across another runner who just happened to be hacking into system at the same time as her.

 It was rare, but it did happen.

 The following conversation was... not was she expected.

 Apparently Jackie knew another Netrunner and never told her about it, something she planned to interrogate him about later.

 The data they sent over was legit and as far as she could tell there were no hidden Black ICE, hacks, daemons, trackers, or other programs in any of it. The pic she sent of the foreman's computer and office also made it clear that whoever this runner was they were _inside_ the warehouse, which was rather odd for runners in general since most preferred to do their hacking from safe locations and not onsite.

 Especially since as far as T-Bug knew none of the security was tripped to get into office in the first place.

 That made her curious, so when the exchange was complete rather than unplugging from system she instead took it over a bit and looked at cameras.

 T-Bug watched as Ghost subtly panned the camera above the foreman's office door and she moved as quickly as she could to pan it back to get a look at them, but all they saw was an empty catwalk the second it was back in position.

 A quick look around the camera system and even the optics on the patrolling robots showed nothing as well.

 How did they do that?! Was this Ghost a legit ninja or something? Was this some kind of corpo espionage she randomly came across? Did Jackie know someone in Intel or something?

 Either way, the job was complete and she'd call up Jackie... but T-Bug wasn't the kind of woman who liked being kept in the dark so planned to get answers.

**Ryu Ga Motoko: Ishin**​

 "Ohiromi Chan! I came as soon as i heard! Are you alright? How did this happen? Didn't you have any guar-"

 "MOTOKO CHAN!"

 Forcing her battle-hardened instincts down, Motoko allowed herself to be caught by a crying Ohiromi. Unlike the perfect nadeshiko image she put on when at home, Ohiromi's kimino was disheveled and covered in the dust of Kyo's street. Reminding Motoko of the first time she had ran into a disguised Ohiromi in one of the alleys leading to her Sensei's Dojo.

 Out of the corner of her eye, Motoko glimpse the guards chasing her after her sudden dash into the house be waved away by Ohiromi's tigeress of a mother. Despite no words being exchanged, Motoko nodded firmly at her unspoken plea to comfort Ohiromi after the shock and loss she experienced just mere hours ago.

 After a few more moments of frantic sobbing, Ohiromi calmed down enough to recount the events of the previous night to Motoko. Of how what was supposed to be a happy event turned into a tragedy due to her greed.

* * *

_It was all my fault Motoko Chan. I asked Otousan to take me to Utamaruya in Fushimi because i was curious about hearing strangers sing. I knew Otousan was a important member of the Bakufu and was being targeted by the Loyalists. I knew! But i insist he take me because i was greedy to spent time with him despite knowing how busy he was. It's all my fault Motoko Chan!

 We were making our way back home when the Hitokiri attacked. It all happened so fast, just a flash of steel in the torchlight and the guards escorting us were dead. The palanquin bearers dropped us to the ground and ran, Otousan tried to defend us but you knew he was always more of a scholar then a swordsman.

 The Hitokiri was playing with him, i could see it! No matter what Otousan did, the Hitokiri just disarmed him before gesturing to him to pick up his katana again. When he finally got bored, he just killed Otousan with a single slash before throwing down pamphlets with '_[_Sonno Joi_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonn%C5%8D_j%C5%8Di)_' written on it over his body. As if it justified them killing a man who only wanted to help the Shogunate prosper!

 Will you help me Motoko Chan? Help me get revenge for my father by taking down the Hitorkiri in the Oni Mask?_

 "Hai Ohiromi Chan. i will help take down this Hitokiri, so smile ne? To me, Ohiromi is someone who should be smiling."

* * *

 " So. You've decided to throw yourself into this foolish war."

 " Hai Sensei. I promised Ohiromi Chan, and wasn't that the whole purpose behind Ryu Kenpo? To protect the powerless with the might of a dragon?"

 The face of the grizzled old man standing before her turned into a frown at her reply. Kazuma Sensei was a grump, but his skills were real as well as his generosity towards the other commoners trying to make a living in this tumultuous era.

 He had been the one to take her in footsore and weary from her trip to Kyo in search of her brother. He had gave her a helping hand when she ran into some of the thugs roaming around the city and offered her a place to stay when it was revealed that she had no plans beyond getting to Kyo.

 Ignoring the societal norms of the times, he had offered to teach her his Kenpo style for protection and when she proved to be adept at it immediately made her his heir without hesitation. As such, that frown laden as it was with parental dissatisfaction made Motoko shriveled up at it's weight.

 " Ohiromi isn't powerless. She has wealth and the security said wealth brings. The powerless are those who find themselves caught inbetween the Samurais warring for power. Still, from the look on your face it looks like you will still go ahead even without my blessings. So here, take these and use them."

![Image 12](https://gbf.wiki/images/3/32/Six-Ruin_Fist_Sterling.png)![Image 13](https://gbf.wiki/images/3/32/Six-Ruin_Fist_Sterling.png)​

 " These are the weapons passed down to the Heir of Ryu Kenpo, i guess today is the time for you to wield them. No blade can cut through them, so use them to stay safe and fulfil your promise. There's still tons of training you need to do before you're good enough to be called the 3rd Head of Ryu Kenpo. "

 Picking up the gauntlets and putting them on, Motoko could tell that they were sized perfectly for her hands. A symbol of how much Sensei cared for her as both a disciple and the next head of Ryu Kenpo. Bursting forward she lunged at him with a hug putting every inch of her gratitude, joy and determination to live up to Sensei's dream in it as she found herself choked up with tears.

 "Arigato Sensei, i'll see you soon."

* * *

_" Hey Motoko Chan, i got the news. You want information on who the next Loyalist target is? "

 " Hai. There's something i need to do with the Hitokiri doing the killings."

 " I'll see what info i can find Motoko Chan. After all you did help the girls scare off that ronin. I'll spread the word as well, so that the merchants and palanquin bearers will help keep an ear out. "

 " Oh! are you sure that's Ok? "

 " It's fine Motoko Chan. Considering the help you gave all of us, this is just a little payment of the debt we all own you."_

 Motoko approached the Inn her informants had told her about at a sprint. Even from a distance she could tell that there was something wrong with it, the lack of any lights turned it into a black shape that stood out among the brightness of Kyo's night life.

 Getting closer to the Inn, she heard the sounds of swords clashing, the death screams of the loyalist guards within and sounds of bone being cleaved by razor sharp blades. Bursting through the door with a kick, Motoko found herself confronted by masked assassins drawn by the sound of her entry with bloody blades held in both hands.

 Almost instinctively, her hands moved with a blur darting towards her kunai and flinging them with a precision born from boredom and refined into deadly skill under the aegis of Sensei's teachings. The assassins standing close to her stood no chance as kunai after kunai worked their way into eyes, necks and hearts

 As bodies dropped to the floor with a thud, Motoko's hands moved again towards her Kunais, ready to launch the second wave only to be interrupted by a descending katana blade that she hastily parried with her new gauntlets.

 Before the assassin could recover his blade, Motoko batted it aside while simultaneously landing a brutal elbow strike to his ribs with her other arm. A slight shift of her weight as she gripped the reeling assassin with her hands allowed her to toss him with enough force to shatter the part of the floor he landed on.

 Recovering back into her stance, Motoko landed a quick One-Two combo at another lunging assassin that snapped his head back. Before following up with a vicious stomp kick to their knee leaving the assassin crippled and moaning on the ground. The sheer brutalness of her takedowns of their fellow assassins in melee combat brought the rest of the remaining assassins to a halt, her actions showing them that taking her own one at a time was pure folly.

 That hesitation however, allowed Motoko open up with her kunai once more as she flung them at the foes before her. This time however her foes were ready for that trick and made to bring up their blades so as to parry the flying blades.

***thwak

 *thwak

 *thwak**

 To their horror, Motoko's targets found that their attempts at parrying were foiled by the close confines of the wall and beams surrounding them, causing their blades to get stuck or be delayed allowing the kunai to once again reap another wave of dead souls.

 The few survivors looked at their dead comrades and realized chillingly that all the targets were ones who were in positions that would prevent them from parrying. That despite the young age of the girl before them, she had enough battlefield awareness to make use of it to her advantage.

 " I'm out of kunai now, so i guess i have to work my through each and everyone of you with my fists. Unless you want to give way? No one else needs to die tonight?"

 A brief glance at each other revealed that despite seeing what the girl before them could do, none of them were going to give up. That for the sake of a New Era, they were more then willing to give their lives to stop her so that the **Oni**could finish the job.

 Looking at the swords point at her, Motoko couldn't help but give a sigh.

 "Yeaah, i guess that it's a no. I guess i just have to go THROUGH YOU!"

* * *

 Making her way up the stairs leading to the second floor of the inn, Motoko allowed the trail of bodies to lead her to the Hitokiri. The bodies were mangled almost beyond recognition, great deep cuts and dismembered limbs littered the hallway.

 Despite the lack of light, Motoko could tell that the entire floor was painted a blood red. The coppery iron smell of blood that filled the air was nauseating to the point that she had to dig out a piece of cloth to use as a makeshift mask as she made her way towards her target.

 Upon entering the room, Motoko was only able to get a slight glimpse of the Hitokiri's victim before a sword darted towards her neck. Quickly raising her arms up, she barely managed to catch the blow with her bracers. Yet the sheer force of the blow sent her stumbling back as she attempt to ride out the momentum of the strike.

 Backing to a wall and then up it, Motoko launched an axe kick towards the spine of the katana in an attempt to shatter the killing tool before her. However, the Hitokiri before her seemed to have predicted the move as a slight twist of his waist had him avoiding the blow and then countering with a strike with the hilt of his katana that sent Motoko into a roll towards the window of the room they were fighting in.

 Getting back on to her feet, Motoko spat out a mouthful of blood before getting back into stance, a part of her realizing that her mask must have fell off during her roll. The greater part of her however was weighing her odds on taking down the killer in-front of her and finding them lacking.

 " Motoko? Is that you? What are you doing here?"

 The familiar sound of her brother's voice knocked Motoko out of her battle haze. Almost incredulously, she watched as the Hitokiri lifted the **Oni** Mask he was wearing and allowed the moon's glow to fall on his face.

 It had been 2 years since he left, but even so she could tell that it was Jun's face. Her brother. The person she had came to Kyo to find, the only other living member of her family who remained after the Bandit Raid. Her Nii-Chan who had vowed that he would take care of her from now on. The killer of her best friend's father.

 "Nii-chan? Yo- You're the Hitokiri that has been cutting down members of the Bakufu? Are-Are you doing this for Money? You can stop! I going to inherit a Dojo here in Kyo, i can take care of the both of us!"

 "Is that what you think of me? That i would be so lowly as to turn my skills with the blade into a mere commodity? DO NOT CHEAPEN THE SACRIFICES MADE FOR A NEW ERA!

 I, no we Loyalists do this for our Country! So that it regains enough strength to halt all banditry within it's borders. So that no other child has to lose their families like WE DID."

 " THAT'S A LIE JUN NII! My friend, Ohiromi Chan. She already lost her father because of what you did! Your sword isn't saving the country, it's cutting down those working to make it better! Please nii-chan, stop doing this and come with me! I'll help you apologize to Ohiromi Chan! I'll get Sensei to offer you a teaching spot in his Dojo! "

 "Foolish Imouto. All that fell to my blade were Dogs of the Bakufu, it was[Tenchu](https://www.nihongomaster.com/japanese/dictionary/word/42313/tenchuu-%E5%A4%A9%E8%AA%85-%E3%81%A6%E3%82%93%E3%81%A1%E3%82%85%E3%81%86) that i was delivering on behalf of the Country. It's beyond you, me or us, this is a cause, a great cause that i will let none stop. Not even you Little Sister."

 "Baka Onii-Chan, it looks like i have to knock some sense into you!"

Despite all his worries Jun knew that Motoko could handle this, he was only here to pull her out if things got intense. So there he sat, on the floor above. In a surprisingly well reinforced white plastic chair.

 The usual sounds of ricochets, and cracking drywall that punctuated Night City days echoed below him. Motoko must have gotten to work.

 He stood up to twist the chair around looking for a manufactures logo. _This was quality work._ He could use one for stakeout's or BBQ's. Sure he'd have to borrow Akari's minivan to transport it, but that was a small price to pay when most chairs tended to buckle under his weight.

 Ah, that'd be the screams. People always tended to do that in his sisters presence before too long, even before the coma.

 ...He wasn't getting fat was he? Motoko hadn't said anything. Neither had Akari, and she had seem him shirtless a number of times. It was the chrome right? Right.

 He felt several walls crack beneath his feet. The floor shaking as something went through it. Despite it all he hadn't heard anyone that sounded even a little like Motoko. He'd have to ask how she managed to throw a full sized man through a wall so easily later.

 His head perked up as he heard a feminine scream, was that Motoko? No wait that was definitely Russian they where screaming in.

 Should he, maybe go and check on her? The shaking and shotgun blasts where still going though...

 Screams echoed up louder and louder and something like a chainsaw roared before he heard a man scream in terror.

 Jun didn't judge, he'd been there before. Sensei's words where always right after all, including the ones he didn't think his students heard.

_"There is no winning a fight against a mad woman, idiot."_ Considering how badly Akari had thrashed him in the ring before that, he agreed wholeheartedly.

 He checked his chrono, wondering if it was about time to go down and take a look...

 -Yet more high pitched Slavic screaming reached his ears.

 ..He'd give her five more minutes.

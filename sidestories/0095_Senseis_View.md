* * *

Ozeki Sensei P.o.V

 Kusanagi Jun, when brought to the Dojo, had just gotten Cyberware arms implanted. A basic modification for the people who were brought here, but that would change soon he saw. Eyes, no matter if they were organic or not, were as the old saying went, the windows to the soul. A lifetime of combat and teaching allowed him to judge a character with a simple glance. What he saw in those eyes was nothing special, a deep rage that simmered and fanned the flames of Hate. Another poor soul destined to throw themselves into battle, with little regard for their own life or of others around them. This one was screaming though, eyes brimming with the screams of sorrow. Another unfortunate commonality.

 To teach those that came to him, becoming a balm to their stretched thin soul, a guide to bringing each soul to a calmer state that could march to the beat of the Tyger Claws drums. Each one a raging beast in the making. Ozeki had long ago decided that teaching was his calling, after decades of learning the Sword and killing for those in charge. When approached by the Oyabun you don't refuse a direct order. So he taught, he calmed, he brought lucidity to those who were drowning.

* * *

Despite Kusanagi Jun installing more Cyberware and his subsequent descent into madness and rage, when he was calm all he talked about was his younger sister. A young Kusanagi Motoko, recently awoken from a coma that caused amnesia. Ozeki didn't know the full story but he assumed it went like all the others in Night City. Scavs, Gangers, Corpos. Nothing new in this city, a cesspit of similar origins were everywhere if you listened. 

 Kusanagi loved to go on about "She's his precious baby sis" or "He needs to finish this mission so that she'll be safe". Foolish boy this city is never safe. 

 So when the time came that a message came from Maelstrom about how they had The Oni's little sister, he watched as that boy came alive with a rage so loud he screamed to the heavens, having to be held back from racing off to die trying to save the younger Kusanagi.

 Then he came back with a body with arms the size meant for a much larger person attached to her. She would undoubtedly be out while she recovered from the likely severe damage. Just as he knew she would be brought to him to get back in fighting condition, given the muscle he could see through her ragged clothing. 

 When Kusanagi was forced back up here while the ripper was fixing his sister, Ozeki forced him through battle meditation, swinging a shinai through the proper stances until he dropped. Exhausting him brought him to a calmer state of mind, enough to get him talking. Maelstrom had forced his sister to fight after putting those horrendous arms on her, and when the Kamikaze arrived she was still standing, lucid enough to want those things off. Right then he knew he had a new student to teach, the look in Kusanagi's eyes told him all he needed to know about what he was going to do. 

* * *

The first thing he noticed when she walked in was her gait. She moved like a predator, her footsteps quiet, her stance that of someone ready to pounce the moment danger proved itself. She walked like someone who thrived in the hunt of prey. He knew the type, always on guard, always prepared. So when he looked into her Eyes, a burning pyre of red and purple, the glint of someone ready to take a life in a moments notice. Beneath that was a familiar haunting rage, something she shared with her brother. 

 When her gaze turned to him he felt the shadow of something immense pass over him for a moment. The weight it brought forth reminded him of himself and other veterans, an instinct that this person is a threat. You don't get as old as he was without gaining this 'Danger Sense', a Gut Feeling of sorts that made a threat assessment of all those one looks at. A combination of cold reading and experience told him that Kusanagi Motoko was a skilled killer.

 So when he told her to swing, he knew he was only making more a killing machine.

* * *

The spar with Akari went as he suspected, in the first bout with the Sandevistan she predictably was put on the back foot, Motoko didn't look to have any augments to her speed or reflexes. The next showed that she was decent with a blade, but as the spar went on into more serious territory she visibly improved, from what he could see she seemed less aware of her new arms now that she was in the heat of battle. 

 Then he had to step in before Akari did too much too fast and lost control. Meditation would be needed when she calmed, she could work on her understanding a bit more by teaching Motoko as well. He sent them on their way when he made sure the young Kusanagi was well and went on to his other students.

* * *

 Next he saw the Kusanagi siblings he cleared the sparring floor for them, he could see in the youngers eyes this was important. 

 There was no signal to start, the spar started with a flourish of bamboo wood clacking against each other in a storm of love. One a love of his sister, a want to keep her safe, keep her from further harm while annihilating those that brought her pain. The other a love that wanted revenge, to those that dared put her through unimaginable pain, but also to keep her brother from going out and getting himself killed. Hypocrites the both of them. 

 Jun had started strong, pushing Motoko back through similar skill in the sword and longer reach combined with stronger force behind each strike. When Motoko was thrown back he thought it would be over, but then she grabbed two Shinai and went on the offensive, a flurry unstoppable. Motoko was evidently practiced in dual wielding, despite only being recently acquainted with those new arms of hers, she swung with purpose, a point to prove.

 Then they started grappling like the children they were. A bundle of grunts and heavy breathes echoed from them as they rolled around. He gave them a moment to vent their frustrations while he held back a grimace.

 He found the moment to step in when Motoko went for another leg lock around her brother's neck, pulling her up by her shirt. He frowned at the siblings as they got their bearings. 

 "Oh hey Sensei."

 "Both of you come with me. Now!" He barked. If these two wanted to act like children he would give them a punishment fit for children.

 Later in the privacy of his room he sighed, relaxing as he lamented his near future. These two surely were going to be a pain to wrestle in. Jun would be under his teaching for some time, if he lived long enough or he delved deeper into cyberware and lost himself, but the younger Kusanagi worried him more. A bright, skilled pupil she may be, someone who lived long she would not.

 Regardless, the future was uncertain to him, all he was required was to teach, calm, guide. So he would, even with these rowdy students of his.

"... Now that we're finished with the life path section of the speedrun, the run kicks into high gear. Which means I need to pause the gameplay to explain things. Remember how I told you earlier that we could drop all our stat points into Intelligence or Technical Ability, and forget the other three? Now you'll see why. We'll be taking advantage of two separate glitches: the Super Burrito Glitch, and Black Hole glitch. 

 "The Super Burrito glitch was discovered on the day of release. Normally, if you eat a burrito, you get a temporary hidden boost to health regen, Body and Reflexes. This buff is pretty small, giving only +3 to your regen and +0.5 to the stats. Due to their partial nature, this does not count towards equip requirements, but it might let you survive a hit or get an extra crit. This was mostly to add some flavor to the world, and to show off the vending machine animation someone clearly spent way too much time on. 

 "However, as any person who's been unfortunate to eat at taco bell can tell you, not all burritos are made equal. For some reason, a vending machine in the hallway of the tower near where you exit the tutorial has some broken parameters. The health regen buff is instead added to Cool, giving a substantial increase. 

 "On its own, this would just be a minor curiosity. Due to the short duration of the buff, the fact that you can only have one buff active at a time, and the machine's fixed location you're unlikely to be able to use your extra cool for dialogue options. This was also patched out in version 1.0.3, which is why speedrunners use a downpatched version. 

 "That was until a runner named Junagi discovered the Black Hole glitch: by saving and loading at the precise frame the game takes control away from you to start the animation, the game bugs out and cancels the animation, giving you the burrito buff but not activating the trigger that activates the buff timer or the 'satiated' condition. This meant an infinite amount of burritos could be eaten - hence the name Black Hole. 

 "Runners quickly realised they could use this glitch with the Super Burrito - any vending machine would work for Body and Reflexes, but the minor detour to grab this special burrito was considered worth it, as the buffed Cool saved time overall thanks to dialogue options.

 "Theoretically they could eat a truckload of burritos and max out 3 stats, but due to the trick's frame-perfect requirement and the fact thatax Body and Reflexes don't actually save much time overall, runners aim for either 6 burritos in a row to reach all the run's optimal weapon stat requirements. This would alsoax out Cool, making the game significantly faster. Any Black Hole misses would reset the count, and this trick is considered the game's first runkiller."

 - An excerpt from Tomatoanus's Speedrun Explained of Cyberpunk 2077.

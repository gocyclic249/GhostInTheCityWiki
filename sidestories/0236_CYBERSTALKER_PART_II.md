**YOU ARE**​

* * *

I was hunting.

 The infuriating little gremlin had been tracked down. We had everything on her, _everything_, especially after her most recent sojourn into the Kitsune's realm. Still, something didn't make sense, something wasn't right. Her abilities didnt line up with the shadow file, capabilities, or potential. With _her_, we couldn't rely on our conventional intelligence gathering methods, the trawling, watching, and listening that worked on everyone else. She did things that shouldn't be possible, for an organic. Then again, neither did Rache. There was something else at play, I needed to find out what, and sometimes there was nothing more to it than to gather that information yourself.

 I had been born from a sea of information, and a distant memory. A man, full of brilliant mania. A God, untouchable within the collective delusion, but ultimately as fragile and fallible as any other human. More so when the luck ran dry, and the sky fell. A cat had nine lives, but Rache only had the one.

 Granted, it had been years since I'd gone out like this, and I'd nearly been run over twice so far by passing vehicles. That would have been a real embarassment. Had to get back into shape, fighting form, if I was to compete with such a deadly adversary. There was quite a bit riding on this, too many important individuals were hounding me, and I _fucking hated_ being hounded. I was a fucking CEO, nobody got to hound me. Dogs were disgusting animals anyways.

 Brightman hissed at me, the little hairless greasestain.

 To be fair to him, I was also hairless, though not quite as greasy as the alley-cat. He'd detected me quite quickly, which was unfortunate. With my stealth broken, it was time for the posturing to come into play. He was a mean one, and defended his territory with aplomb, so this first step was key. The perfect adversary to test myself against, ensure I was fully operational before I entered the realm of true danger. Luckily for him, I hadn't resorted to my usual hidden array of cyber-weaponry, content to test myself with more conventional means. If I could defeat him that way, then defeating her with the full might of my arsenal, especially one I'd upgraded in the years since Rache's passing, would be trivial.

_I could take her._

 Pause. Full, unblinking attention. Skin bunched up. A subtle twitch of tail to show my commitment, poise.

 This is when I'd usually spar with the full might of my electronic warfare suite, in an attempt to whittle down my opponent's defences, before I opened up on them with the microwavers. I gave him a blast, just in case there was anything hidden I wasn't aware of. He didn't outwardly react, but that didn't mean anything. Which meant his will was unbroken by the posturing, the bastard. He simply wasn't aware of my prowess, then. Seems that my reputation had fallen to the wayside in all the long years of my absence.

 A subtle tension to show my preparation. Let him see me coming, and react accordingly.

 A single bound, and I was on him, a storm of flashing teeth and claws. Yowling filled the alley, as we did battle under the watchful gaze of the peaceful and wrathful ones.

 "Mister Brightman! No! Bad cat!"

_PSST PSST PSST_

 The spray of water separated us, as a new player entered the fray. No! Nobody grasped victory from me! I turned towards the intruder with a hiss of rage, only to get a faceful of water as she turned the dreaded squirt bottle on me, "Get off of him! Shoo!"

 Misty Olszewski, the owner and proprietor of Misty's Esoterica and Chakra Harmonization. She owned the basement behind the bolt-hole as well, where a certain old ripper lived. Both were well known, and it was unfortunate that she'd stepped out at that moment. She was untouchable, which meant that I couldn't turn my wrath on her.

 Another squirt of water hit me, as I hissed in frustration, and darted away to cover. Frustration flowed through me, as I cowered inside a cardboard box, drenched. The disgrace, the humiliation! No, I had to stay calm. Cool, and collected, like the magnificent beast I was. Reacting with emotion was beneath one of my stature, especially as a founding member of the Collective.

 "Mister Brightman, did that gonk hurt you?" She tutted, and scooped up my opponent, who hissed at her in turn, claws digging into her thick sweater, "Let's get you to Vik's, so he can take a look at you. Ouch, quit scratching me, I'm trying to help you!"

_No, you weren't trying to help me at all. This was most unhelpful._

 I glowered at them as they descended into the ripper's subterranean lair, the organic sphynx watching me the whole while. I'd wait until you returned to finish our bout, oh yes. You wouldn't escape me or your punishment, and after? I'd go after the gremlin.

 I was so focused on the entrance, that the hands that grabbed me were a surprise. _What? Who dared? More importantly, how had they snuck up on me?_ I tore into the offender with tooth and claw, all top of the line chrome, only to be rebuffed.

 "Jun, look. I found a cat."

_No. How?_

 "Motoko, gross. Leave it alone before you get a disease."

 Yes, leave me alone. I was the one who stalked, not the other way around!

 She leaned in closer, narrowing her eyes, "You're not Brightman...wait, Nibbles?"

 Who? Wait, was that one of my descendants? I knew I shouldn't have had that one night stand back in '19... What was this? Unhand me, you damn cretin! Nobody except Rache got to pet me! Don't touch me with your filthy, goblin hands, oh... that felt good.

 "Look Jun, he likes me! Can I keep him?"

 No! It was this traitorous body, and automatic infiltration subroutines!

 "Motoko, a cat is a big responsibility, not to mention illegal."

 Hmm, I could use this to my advantage. It might grant me access to where I could not otherwise go, and unearth whatever hidden secrets she had. Yes, the infiltration subroutines were working as intended...

 "Please, cats basically take care of themselves. Say hi to Vik for me, this guy got all soaked somehow, so I'll dry him off at home."

 Fufufufufu. I allowed her to carry me to the car, feeling appropriately smug. All according to my master plan. Maybe she was already infected with toxoplasmosis? Would explain a lot.

 For now, we would see through a glass, darkly.

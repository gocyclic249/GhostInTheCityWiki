The apartment was dark when he finally entered.

 He set a small bag of groceries on the countertop.

 Tonight he would be eating well.

 Before he began, he made his way to a small shrine with two pictures in it, and lit two incense before putting his hand together to pay respect.

 His family all here, he began to cook.

 It was a traditional meal, and he would cook it correctly.

_"I've come a long way, don't you think?"_ he asked.

 He knew the answer, and nodded hearing the retort. '_Not far enough!'

 "It's true! But you shouldn't say it!"_ He laughed.

 Most of the meal was real food, the meat was mostly soy, but it was at least half chicken.

 The soy sauce he already had, what kind of Japanese man doesn't have a bottle of real soy sauce somewhere?

 As he ate his meal. He told his family about his day.

_"That rascal of a Ronin returned today! I had expected her to just practice but she told me to make a bridge, and she held it! I almost had to give her the dojo's plaque! No one could get her to budge until this father drew his blade!"_

 He told them about every battle, and who fought them. He sighed about hopeless cases.

 Anyone caught trying that one stupid move from that kami damned Cowboy Samurai VII would clean the entire Dojo!

_'You cannot cut bullets, stop thinking about trying, you idiots!'_

 Technically you could. But only those fools in the _Divine Wind_ with the speedware and the heat blade had any real chance.

 Some fights were good though. Really good. Those he went into great detail.

 His daughter was a proper _Bushi Warrior_, and would know exactly what he meant. But whenever things got a bit too technical he'd have to go back and re-explain it to his wife. This was fine, he would make a poem to help her understand.

_She'd loved his poetry, it's why she married a guy like him!_

 He finished his meal feeling lighter than he had in months.

 He'd half expected the girl to have disappeared by now

 People like her usually did.

 She went against the grain, and yet was forgiven for it.

 Her _'Gi'_ , her Honesty, at the large meeting did a lot to establish her honor in the eyes of many.

 She would not back down if it meant deception, she was forthright in her beliefs, even in the Tigers Den.

_'But what is her Bushido?'_

 As he cleaned everything up, the shrine last of all. He pondered the question, and the tenets of Bushido.

 He took out an old brush, and ground some ink, the characters already in mind.

_白紙の状態 Hakushi no jōtai_

 "Blank Slate"

 Her style was clean. Effortless, and yet she had no style whatsoever. _Mushin,_ an Emptiness of mind. To strike without thought.

 The kind that his own teacher had said was demanded for any true Iaijutsu duel. Yet it seemed to infuse her entire style.

 It's strange that a girl who lost all memories formed a style so pure, her own lack of connections reflected in a style that embodies no connection to anything.

 Her forms directly countered nothing, and as such, could be countered by nothing.

 He'd used 'Empty' techniques to defeat her. She'd never faced them. Which made sense, because they were, strictly speaking, for dueling.

 He smiled with a small joy _'A style defined by Nothing, is defeated only by a Reflection of Emptiness.'_

 His wife would have loved the poetry of it. His daughter would have call him a fool, and demand to fight.

 He'd never imagined to see such a style.

 A second scroll was produced.

_白紙の剣のスタイル Hakushi no ken no sutairu_

 "Blank Sword Style"

 What a strange dream his student was.

_'The shell of her body contained not a person, but the ghost that haunts it.'

 "Perhaps she is some sort of Kami, come down to humble mortals?"_

 He dismissed the thought immediately. No she was very much a fool.

 The brilliant Blue Oni, to her brothers brutal Red Oni.

 Both monstrous talents, and both fools.

 The old master sighed, _'His fools.'_ He would raise them as best he could, that was the Duty of a Sensei.

 A warrior sworn to nothing...but perhaps her journey?

 A Ronin wandering the City of Dreams.

 He got up and went to the cabinet and pulled out an old bottle of _rice wine_.

 Tomorrow the Dojo would have a new pair of wall scrolls, for those few of spiritual leaning to contemplate.

 Tonight however, would be a good night for poetry.

 Staring up at the glowing skyline, he pondered the earth and the heavens.

* * *

Motoko cosplay second gig.

 Deep beneath the abandoned laundry of a teenager, in the backstreets of a japantown apartment there was, _the list._

 Long abandoned and forgotten, it rested beneath a pile of neon green clothing and cat stickers. On a heavily stained piece of synthpaper, a crudely drawn outfit was etched in pen. Most of _the list_ was frankly illegible, ink having smeared the letters across most of the surface, however by chance the drawing of the outfit. Complete with a mop of purple hair, remained.

 The list would have faded into obscurity. Eventually fading away beneath the discarded garments. Until one day the unthinkable happened. The room was cleaned.

 Unfortunately for the owner, the one doing the cleaning would happen upon _the list_ and stare at the drawing for an unhealthy amount of time. It was only when the door slid open and the creator of _the list_ returned, that the poorly cared for sheet was quickly stuffed into an oversized jacket.

 Time would pass, merriment would be had. The cleaner journeyed home and after dropping _the list_ on top of a table in an even messier room then the one they had just cleaned. The cleaner once again stared at the drawing, this time gleefully rubbing their hands together.

* * *

...It was weeks later when Motoko realized her mistake. Her face slack at the sight in front of her. Cool dialed up as far as possible to keep her image intact. Stretched across the mannequin in front of her and a smirking Hiromi was _the outfit_. The one she had discarded in favor of her leotard. It wasn't that she hadn't liked it, merely that it had been a little too expensive to create when she was just starting out, not to mention it had seemed _wrong_ in some way to wear it without having proven herself. As if she would have been trying too hard.

 So there she stood mortified, her best choom watching her expression like the cat that caught the canary. '_She had seen it, the cringe, the shame. What else had she seen?'_

 "Happy birthday Motokooo!" Hiromi shouted at the top of her lungs. Given the circumstances Motoko had only one logical response to that statement.

 "It's my birthday!?"

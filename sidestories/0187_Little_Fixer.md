Little Fixer​

 Wakako was no stranger to calls from unknown numbers, in her line of work they were even expected. Of course, when it was an unknown number calling on a number she gave to her regular Solos that either meant that they had fucked up bad and a target was calling to make a point, or they freely handed it out to someone else which would earn them a _stern_ talking to. Either way, there was nothing pressing for the moment so she might as well answer it.

 As she did so she activated a program to track the caller's number and get info on them, assuming they were dumb enough to not mask it or weren't afraid to let her know who they were.

 "Who is this?" Wakako asked.

_"Hi! Name's Hiromi Mitsunashi! I'm calling on the behalf of our mutual friend, Motoko,"_ the young voice on the other end of the call said in a chipper tone that immediately annoyed Wakako.

 "Then she will need a stern talking to later," Wakako idly said as she pulled up Mitsunashi's information. Hmmm, child of decently placed Arasaka executives. What an interesting friend Motoko had. "Why have you called me? Seeking to set up a job or just waste my time?"

_"I'm actually calling cause my gonk friend whom I love dearly and the rest of us are gonna be hitting a Scav den soon set up underneath a ripperdoc's clinic, who is almost certainly working with them,"_ Hiromi explained.

 Oh, that was interesting. "Which ripper?"

_"Guy named Charles Bucks in Northren Kabuki,"_ Hiromi freely gave. _"Won't matter much, he'll be out of business soon."_

 "Fair enough. Far be from me to let young people take out the trash on their own imitative," Wakako said evenly. "But why call me?"

_"Cause Motoko wants to loot the place of everything that's its worth... but she doesn't know how to unload it at all,"_ Hiromi admitted.

 "That is not surprising in the slightest," was Wakako's reply.

_"Which is why I convinced her to give me your number to ask you about any contacts of yours that might be interested in a bunch of chrome and medical equipment they can get for way better than market price,"_ Hiromi explained. _"I'm sure you know a few rippers you and your people make good use of that could benefit, and as a result you better results long-term on all your gigs if you people have better access to good cyberwear and medical care. All you need to do is give names and call ahead to them and we'll do the rest. The transporting, the haggling, the selling, the unloading, etc."_

 "And what do I get out of all this?" Wakako asked with a raised eyebrow at the confident little corpo brat.

_"Us not doing the same for a rival Fixer and their support network? A small slice of profits for setting up connections? Motoko's good graces since she is so preem?"_ Hiromi responded in a tone of voice that belonged to a kitsune.

 Wakako considered it for a moment, and finding no downsides decided to throw the brat a bone.

 "I'll send you names, addresses, and call ahead. It's up to you to make anything of it and I expect payment no matter how well or poorly you do," Wakako sent back.

_"Deal!"_ Hiromi said and quickly hung up. Rude little brat, but Wakako was a woman of her word.

 She sent the deets and returned to her work.

 ---

 Later that night Wakako received another call, once more from Hiromi. Faster than she expected.

 She picked it up and spoke, "Finished already?"

_"Yup! Scavs and Scav Ripper dealth with, den and clinic cleaned out, everything loaded then unloaded, products sold, and lots of rippers very happy about their cheap and quality goods,"_ Hiromi said passionately.

 Wakako already knew this of course, all the rippers she sent info on to the girl had contacted her not long after each visit to report on the various deals. They admitted that the corpo brat was good at haggling and would likely make it far in hierarchy when old enough, assuming she wasn't killed first of course. All of the chrome and equipment was decent quality as stated, especially the ripper gear. Would help cut down costs majorly for the next month or so.

 All in all, it was a mutually beneficial deal all around.

 "And my payment?" Wakako said evenly.

_"Sending now!"_ Hiromi said, and indeed Wakako received her fee.

 It was barely a drop in the bucket compared to the rest of her account, not even the biggest one either, but it was the principle of the matter. Good Fixers never did anything for free, after all.

 Honestly, the eddies were worth less than taking a Scav Ripper off streets and getting the ones close to her in her good graces for the extra equipment.

 "Received," Wakako said evenly. "If your foolish friend and the rest of you following her do something as stupid as raid a Scav den again and need assistance once more know my fee will be the same."

_"Will do! Thanks for your support, Lady Wakako. I hope I'm a fraction as badass as you when I take over Night City in the future,"_ Hiromi responded.

 Wakako could not sense any false praise or flattery in her words, only pure belief. What a strange child.

 "That isn't an invitation to call me for nothing though, so don't even think of doing so," Wakako warned.

_"No worries, that's what Motoko is for after all! Have a nice day and talk later! Me and group off to party!"_ Hiromi ended the call by sending Wakako a selfie of her, Motoko, and two young men hanging out in an apartment celebrating their good fortune.

 It has been a long time since Wakako ever received such a frivolous picture from someone else who knew exactly who she was.

 "I now understand how she and Motoko are friends," Wakako sighed as she idly sent the picture to a digital folder.

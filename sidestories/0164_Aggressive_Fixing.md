Aggressive Fixing​

 It would be no surprise to most that most Fixers in Night City knew each other at least professionally. Given the work they did they had to so that the whole system which they worked within didn't collapse. Sure they set up gigs and sent people out that conflicted with each other all the time, but it was no personal, just business. And whoever's people came out ahead were clearly the superior choice that the Fixer in question chose well.

 What might surprise people is that the big name Fixers of Night City did more than just talk just enough to make sure the status quo remained intact. In fact, they could even work together when goals aligned, like when during the Unification War and Night City risked being invaded how all the Fixers got together to how to best apply all their best Solos should the worst happen.

 Of course, that kind of united front was rare and at the end of the day they were all business rivals following unofficial codes to keep things civil.

 But one thing they did do regularly? Trade and barter their agents.

 Most Solos had one Fixer they worked for specifically and no one else, was easier that way, but there were other mercs that were more open-minded and willing to do a wide range of gigs that not any one Fixer can provide at all times. There was nothing wrong with having more than one Fixer after all, assuming you didn't screw one over for the other of course, most Solos were free agents after all and Fixers got and respected that. They were the middlemen and their people their limbs.

 But nothing said the Fixers themselves couldn't take advantage of this and recommend their people to other Fixers looking for specific skills for certain crews and jobs. It can be tough putting a proper team together for a big gig on short notice after all, or maybe a Fixer has a hot gig but none of their usual people is available to do it right.

 Thus, Solo trading was born. And in a secure chatroom once a week all of them got together to talk business.

 Sure they could call each other anytime one on one to search for specific agents, and they did, but this weekly meetings helped keep things open to everyone and barter for hot ticket Solos.

 And one was being highly fought over as Night City's latest up and comer.

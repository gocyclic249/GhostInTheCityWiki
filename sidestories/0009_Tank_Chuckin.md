 Edit:

 "Keep shooting! KEEP SHOOTING!" His voice rang out over the carnage that was unfolding in the compound. 

 His own bullets joined the hailstorm raining down on _her_ to _absolutely no effect!_ Smaller than his own little girl, no obvious cyberware on her, and she walked over the yard like it was a pleasant summer morning. 

 "WE NEED MORE FIREPOWER!"

 The line had barely left his lips, when a rocket flew her way. He breathed a sigh of relief, only for it to catch in his throat, when she _grabbed it out of the air!_

 She crushed the propellant chamber between her fingers, and tossed the rocket over her shoulder, explosion blooming behind her. 

 The sound of an engine cut through the noise of the battlefield. 

 A tank! They were saved! 

 The deafening crack of the cannon ripped through the air, and that bitch just smiled, and tilted her head! The shell must have missed her by a hair, because an explosion rocked the other end of the compound. 

 Before the tank could get another shot of she skipped of the ground, and landed right in front of the vehicle. A moment later, the steel of its armor plating groaned, as _her fingers sank into them_! 

 The tank began to rise in a physics defying display, and she tossed 30 tons of combat vehicle to the side, like a used tissue paper! 

 His comrades next to him went pale, and their faces mirrored his own rising despair. 

 "My God! What _is_ this thing?" 

 He couldn't help but agree. 

 He stopped firing. It was pointless anyway. 

 His eyes were glued to this... This _thing!_ It might as well have been the Grimm reaper for all the certainty of his own demise he could see in her gentle step, and her slight smile. 

 There was no surprise in his heart when she turned invisible. 

 He felt a strange kind of peace, and acceptance even, that he never thought he would feel in his final moments. 

 He felt the cold blade passing through his neck, but there was no pain. A part of his rapidly dimming mind told him that he was in shock, before the darkness of oblivion claimed whatever was left of it.

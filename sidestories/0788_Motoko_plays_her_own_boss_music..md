 Motoko plays her own boss music.

 ***********

 Finally, that gonk of an informant had pulled through and gotten a location for the target. The Oni had torn through a group of 6th Street mooks trying to sell on TC turf, and one of the idiots had been the son of someone important. Everyone knew it wouldn't last, but the guy was mad enough to put out a hit on him and anyone close to him anyway.

 This gig had a time limit, Georg knew it, Ivan knew it, but most importantly, the Oni knew it. The guy should've been bunkered down and ridden it out till it all blew over, but Ivan had actually managed to catch some chatter about one of the Tyger Claw uppercrust with a grudge pulling strings and forcing him to come out of hiding for some kinda meeting. The Oni would be running security for some middleman in one of their glitter den, and this would be the best chance they got before it was called off.

 Georg and his buddies had ridden up in a moving van, opening one of the sliding doors and jumping out by an alley near the back of the building, trying not to attract any unwanted attention. This was very much TC territory, and the last thing they needed was for someone to spot them and set the whole place on high alert. 

 They went up a fire escape on a neighboring building, crawling through a window to an abandoned apartment that had a wall right on the other side of the den. The plan _had_ been for Donny to use thermals and make sure no one was on the other side, then cut through, but when they got there, the wall was already cut open, with at least half a dozen sets of footsteps clear in the dust.

 Georg swore.

 "Fuck. Fucking _fuck!_ Someone beat us here! Oy, Donny, what the fuck is going on on the other side of this?"

 Donny, their runner, shook his head. "No one in that room, and nothing on the security system showing anything off. They're either already dead, or haven't been caught yet."

 It was a risk, but they hadn't wanted to give up quite yet. No one knew how long the gig would stay open, and this was still the best shot they'd get. That there were a _lot_ of eddies on the line probably also helped.

 That was, unfortunately, how they'd gotten into their current situation.

 "I don't fucking like this boss, this place is too fucking quiet. Glitter dens aren't supposed to be like this."

 Georg didn't disagree, especially given they'd not found any more signs of the ones who'd gotten there before them yet, even having moved through nearly an entire floor. Sure, all the usual tells of a den were there, including some of the actual drug itself spilled over the chair next to them right then, but like hell he'd admit to being creeped out.

 "Shut up Kent. Keep moving."

 They found the first sign in the next room. Bullet holes covered the wall in a wild arc, a bloodstain clear on the hideously blue carpet, but no body.

 "Well, someone definitely fought here."

 "No fucking shit Donny. You getting anything from the systems?"

 "Still nothing. Keep your eyes up. Something's not right here."

 More signs of fighting were clear as they made their way through the rest of the floor, bullet holes and broken furniture, and far more blood than the lack of bodies implied.

 "Where the fuck are all the bodies?" Georg asked, starting to feel unnerved.

 "Uhhhh… think I found one."

 Kent had moved to the stairwell entrance, and sure enough, that was where the found the first of whoever had beaten them here. The body of large man in tactical gear with a torn out throat lay facedown on the stairs, blood trail clearly showing he'd slid down them from the floor above.

 "Shit."

 Someone started laughing over the speakers.

 Everyone jumped as the unexpected sound came from seemingly everywhere. "Donny what the fuck is that?" Georg snapped.

 "I don't know! The systems are still showing nothing, but whoever is doing this clearly has control of the sound system, and if they have that, they probably have the cameras too."

 "So they know we're here. Fuck."

 "Yeah. Fuck," Donny agreed.

 Georg grit his teeth and shifted his grip on his rifle. "Alright. To hell with stealth, plan's out the window. We're in it now. Keep your guards up and eyes out, we're going in fast."

 Donny and Kent both nodded, and they moved up the stairs, moving quickly through the first few rooms. They found more bodies in some, but kept moving, until they stopped abruptly when they began to hear [music](https://youtu.be/FRZ9cUEF0NE) from down the next hallway.

 "That's not coming from a speaker…. Someone's down there," Kent whispered.

 "Sound's coming from the room at the end. Two rooms before it, stay quiet and we'll clear them quick in case of an ambush, then take the last door," Georg ordered.

 "Got it."

 They moved quickly, finding nothing in the first, a storage closet, and only another corpse in the second, and hesitated as they stood in front of the last room. Motioning for Kent to cover him, George slowly cracked the door open, a larger apartment than the ones so far coming into view as it silently swung wide. Six bodies lay in the entryway, all with their throats torn out.

 The music was loud, the sound no longer muffled as they approached the wide open living area, finally seeing a young looking girl with purple hair at the piano in the middle of the room, facing away. 

_That… oh shit, that's the sister,_ Georg thought, raising his gun to aim at her back.

 "Took you long enough to get here!" She shouted, not looking back at them, and he almost flinched.

 Kent shouted back from behind him "Playtime's over, kid! Where's your brother?"

 She turned to face them, not seeming nervous in the slightest, as the music somehow kept playing in its own.

 "Oh, he's not here right now. See, that dumbass from 6th street thought he could send a message by messing with my family, so I decided to send a reply! Honestly, I wasn't expecting more to show up after the last group, so you were a bit of a surprise. Hey, weren't there supposed to be three of you?"

 Georg gritted his teeth and spoke "Not falling for that, girl, just tell us –" only to cut off as he heard Kent's voice shout behind him.

 "SHIT, DONNY'S DEAD!"

 Despite himself, he flinched and looked back, seeing Kent pointing his gun back towards the entrance, and Donny– oh fuck, Donny was facedown on the floor with a puddle of blood around his hea–

 Georg snapped his head back around to look at the girl and – she was right in front of him, grinning wide– too wide– and he flinched, stumbling back and firing two rounds straight into her chest.

 "Oh fu–" Kent started to scream but his voice cut off suddenly into a wet gurgle, and Georg whipped around, seeing Kent holding his ripped out throat, blood bubbling out from his lips, and– _where was the girl–_

 Georg threw himself backwards, putting his back to a wall, only to see she hadn't even moved from where she stood, seeming completely unphased, and he fired again and again, at her torso, at her head, screaming. 

 She didn't even flinch as his rifle clicked empty.

 "WHAT ARE YOU?!?!" He yelled, as the girl, no the monster in human form, smiled so widely her cheeks split, blood pouring down her face, spreading her arms wide, hands with too long fingers, too sharp nails, and laughed, and the music that had still been playing all this time suddenly came to an end.

 "It's it obvious? I'm a Ghost! And you, dear messenger, are my reply."

 A clawed hand came from behind and covered his eyes as he felt a hand wrap around his throat, and then there was only pain, and then nothing at all.

 ************

 "Nice!"

 Motoko giggled and cut the BD scroller as the Onryo hack ended, no longer needed as the last man died. She'd finally gotten to play a true boss entrance, and she was sure this one would sell pretty well, so she could cross another thing off the bucket list _and_ Hiromi would be happy. Win-win, really.

 Now to go torment the asshole who'd actually paid for the hit a bit.

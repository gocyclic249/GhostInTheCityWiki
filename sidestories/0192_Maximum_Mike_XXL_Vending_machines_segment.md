 So let me tell you something listeners. A friend of mine was getting himself an XL Burrito from a Vending machine just like anyone else in Night City. He is a Netrunner and as you might guess he hacked the machine to pop one out for him. Now, maybe it was a slow day for him or maybe he had twigged on to something but he took a little bit of extra time to look at the code in the machine. When he dug into it he found out that the machine knew it didn't get its eddies for his oh so delicious XL Burrito yet it still gave him one. The only other thing it did besides recording this was time how long between when he first started on it and when it spat out said XL Burrito. This got him curious so he started to take a closer look. There was nothing special on the machine. It just had some rotating garbage ICE on it for protection. The kind of thing that wouldn't stop someone from getting in but it wasn't ever WET either.

 It wasn't until he looked at what the machine hooked into that things got interesting. The vending machine that gave him his delicious XL Burrito and all other vending machines were hooked up to the own little slice of the net, and it was big enough for him to dive in. Now dear viewer you might be asking why I bring this up. It is, after all, a Netrunner doing what Netrunners do. But let me ask you this: why would vending machines track how long a hack takes and why would they need a strong enough connection to jack in to?

 Maybe it is a way for Netrunners to keep tabs on each other. It could be a way for a Corpo to find and track new Netrunners. For all we know it could be some crazy AI from beyond the Blackwall playing a game. All I know is that he never hacked another vending machine after that. Something to think about.
